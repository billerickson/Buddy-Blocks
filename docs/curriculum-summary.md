# Curriculum Summary

This is a content inventory and strategy guide for canonical curriculum tracks, not a child progress report.

Status key:

- `complete`: an authored track folder exists with `track.yaml`, units, and lessons.
- `planned`: the track is intentionally recommended here but has not been authored yet.
- `empty`: the expected grade or level slot has no authored or planned track.

Scholastic tracks use academic grade placement. Foundation tracks start every student at Level 1 and unlock later levels in sequence. Foundation levels are stored in grade folders for ordering: Level 1 uses `grade-03`, Level 2 uses `grade-04`, Level 3 uses `grade-05`, and Level 4 uses `grade-06`. If a subject needs Level 5, continue the same convention with `grade-07` unless the storage model changes.

## Research Basis

The foundation sequence below is based on these references:

- ACTFL [World-Readiness Standards for Learning Languages](https://www.actfl.org/educator-resources/world-readiness-standards-for-learning-languages) and [NCSSFL-ACTFL Can-Do Statements](https://www.actfl.org/educator-resources/ncssfl-actfl-can-do-statements) for modern language proficiency, communication modes, culture, comparisons, and community use.
- Texas Education Agency [Chapter 114 Languages Other Than English TEKS](https://tea.texas.gov/laws-and-rules/texas-administrative-code/19-tac-chapter-114), including modern and classical language proficiency ranges.
- Common Core [English Language Arts Standards](https://thecorestandards.org/ELA-Literacy/) for grammar, reading literature, writing, speaking, and listening progression.
- Core Knowledge [K-8 Sequence](https://www.coreknowledge.org/core-knowledge-sequence/) for cumulative literature, history, geography, and cultural knowledge.
- NCSS [C3 Framework](https://www.socialstudies.org/system/files/2022/c3-framework-for-social-studies-rev0617.2.pdf) and [National Curriculum Standards for Social Studies](https://www.socialstudies.org/standards/national-curriculum-standards-social-studies-introduction) for inquiry, source use, history, geography, civics, and social studies themes.
- BYU Silva Rhetoricae [Progymnasmata](https://rhetoric.byu.edu/Pedagogy/Progymnasmata/Progymnasmata.htm) and [Rhetorical Exercises](https://rhetoric.byu.edu/Pedagogy/Rhetorical%20Exercises.htm) for the rhetoric sequence.
- OpenStax [logic and argument](https://openstax.org/books/introduction-philosophy/pages/5-summary), [truth tables](https://openstax.org/books/contemporary-mathematics/pages/2-3-constructing-truth-tables), and the Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy [fallacies](https://iep.utm.edu/fallacy/) for logic progression.
- IES What Works Clearinghouse [Organizing Instruction and Study to Improve Student Learning](https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/practiceguide/1) for spaced practice, retrieval, and cumulative memory review.

## Foundation Level Counts

Recommended totals:

- Spanish: 5 levels.
- French: 5 levels.
- Latin: 5 levels.
- Grammar: 4 levels.
- Logic: 4 levels.
- Rhetoric: 5 levels.
- Classical Literature: 5 levels.
- History And Civics: 5 levels.
- Memory Work: 4 levels.

Rationale:

- Modern languages need enough runway to move from Novice Low/Mid communication into Intermediate Mid performance: memorized words, recombined phrases, connected sentences, then short paragraphs and real-world resources.
- Latin needs a comparable five-level runway because grammar, morphology, reading fluency, roots, Roman culture, and classical reception build more slowly than a short enrichment sequence.
- Grammar and Logic can reach a strong middle-school foundation in four levels: concrete recognition, applied usage/reasoning, formal structure, then analysis and editing/argument mapping.
- Rhetoric, Literature, and History And Civics deserve five levels because they form the main classical spine after Level 1: beginner narration and comprehension, ancient/classical work, medieval/early modern work, American/modern work, and capstone seminar or civic application.
- Memory Work should stay shorter and supportive. Four levels are enough for progressive recitation habits, cumulative knowledge pegs, and public performance; later memory items should live inside the subject tracks they support.

## Foundation Strategy

### Spanish

Recommended total: 5 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): Spanish 1, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/03-spanish`, 10 units, 50 lessons.
- Level 2 (`complete`): Spanish 2, `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/03-spanish`, 11 units, 71 lessons.
- Level 3 (`planned`): Spanish 3, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/03-spanish`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): Spanish 4, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/03-spanish`.
- Level 5 (`planned`): Spanish 5, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-07/03-spanish`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Novice entry. Cover greetings, polite phrases, colors, classroom words, numbers, calendar words, family, likes/dislikes, food, places, common actions, high-frequency chunks, and simple sentence meaning. Keep output mostly word, phrase, and modeled sentence level.
- Level 2: Novice Mid expansion. Cover review routines, larger numbers, dates and time, school supplies, classes, people, descriptions, feelings, food requests, directions, common present-tense action patterns, predictable questions, simple culture, short reading, and listening for key words.
- Level 3: Novice High bridge. Cover subject pronouns, regular present-tense `-ar/-er/-ir` patterns, high-frequency irregulars, `ser/estar/tener/hay/ir`, adjective agreement, articles, question formation, daily routines, weather, hobbies, community places, and short connected conversations. Add authentic-looking schedules, notes, maps, messages, and menus.
- Level 4: Intermediate Low communication. Cover preterite and imperfect recognition/use in highly scaffolded narration, near future and simple future expressions, direct object pronouns, comparisons, chores, travel, shopping, health, celebrations, regional culture, and short paragraph writing. Students should narrate simple past events and explain preferences with reasons.
- Level 5: Intermediate Mid capstone. Cover longer connected reading/listening, short presentations, opinions with evidence, polite requests and conditional phrases, commands, present perfect exposure, simple subjunctive chunks for wishes/needs, media and community topics, culture comparisons, and project-based communication. Students should produce short paragraphs and sustain simple conversations across familiar topics.

Authoring notes:

- Every level should include interpretive, interpersonal, and presentational tasks.
- Keep culture embedded in language tasks rather than isolated trivia.
- Continue the flash-card ladder before production prompts in vocabulary-heavy units.

- Level 2: Novice Mid expansion. Cover review routines, larger numbers, time, school supplies, classes, descriptions, feelings, food requests, places, directions, present-tense action chunks, predictable questions, simple authentic resources, reading, and listening.
- Level 3: Novice High bridge. Students should read short authentic resources with support and present on familiar topics.

Authoring notes:

- French needs more explicit sound-spelling and liaison/listening practice than Spanish.
- Keep accent tolerance in scoring, but require model answers with correct accents.
- Culture should include France and broader Francophone communities.

### Latin

Recommended total: 5 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): Latin 1, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/05-latin`, 10 units, 50 lessons.
- Level 2 (`complete`): Latin 2, `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/05-latin`, 10 units, 71 lessons.
- Level 3 (`planned`): Latin 3, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/05-latin`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): Latin 4, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/05-latin`.
- Level 5 (`planned`): Latin 5, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-07/05-latin`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Latin entry. Cover sounds, classroom words, Roman greetings, nouns and gender, simple sentence meaning, family, descriptions, high-frequency verbs, places, food, animals, daily life, mythology, roots, culture, and short adapted readings.
- Level 2: Reading expansion. Cover Latin I review, case roles, noun endings as meaning clues, present and past-time verb forms, people, places, descriptions, Roman daily life, public spaces, mythology, roots, mottoes, adapted passages, and culture/history through text.
- Level 3: Grammar spine. Cover all five declensions at recognition level, nominative/genitive/dative/accusative/ablative functions, adjective agreement, present/imperfect/future/perfect active indicative forms, infinitives, imperatives, personal and relative pronouns, prepositional phrases, and adapted readings tied to Roman history and myth.
- Level 4: Connected reading. Cover passive voice, participles, relative clauses, indirect statement, comparison of adjectives/adverbs, basic subjunctive recognition, purpose/result/cum clauses in controlled contexts, scansion exposure, rhetorical figures, and adapted Caesar, Cicero, Ovid, Vergil, and Vulgate-style excerpts as appropriate.
- Level 5: Classical reading capstone. Cover authentic and lightly adapted author passages by genre, translation commentary, syntax review, morphology mastery checks, meter and style, Latin composition from English prompts, Roman history and reception, classical roots in academic English, and a final translation portfolio.

Authoring notes:

- Latin should emphasize reading and interpretation first, then limited composition for grammar awareness.
- Use English roots, derivatives, and classical culture as recurring transfer tasks.
- Keep grammar terms aligned with Grammar and Logic tracks where possible.

### Grammar

Recommended total: 4 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): Grammar I, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/06-grammar`, 11 units, 11 lessons.
- Level 2 (`planned`): Grammar II, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/06-grammar`.
- Level 3 (`planned`): Grammar III, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/06-grammar`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): Grammar IV, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/06-grammar`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Sentence awareness. Cover parts of speech, nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, prepositions, subjects, predicates, sentence types, capitalization, punctuation, phrases, clauses, fragments, sentence combining, copywork, dictation, and simple diagramming.
- Level 2: Sentence building. Cover common/proper/possessive nouns, personal/possessive/reflexive pronouns, verb tense consistency, helping and linking verbs, direct objects, compound subjects/predicates, prepositional phrases, commas in series and quotations, dialogue punctuation, and diagramming simple expanded sentences.
- Level 3: Clause control. Cover independent/dependent clauses, coordinating/subordinating conjunctions, compound and complex sentences, appositives, adjective/adverb phrases and clauses, pronoun-antecedent agreement, active/passive voice, misplaced modifiers, semicolons, colons, and diagramming compound/complex patterns.
- Level 4: Grammar for style and editing. Cover verb mood and voice, parallel structure, concise modifiers, punctuation for rhythm and clarity, usage and register, sentence variety, grammar in literary sentences, Latin/English grammar connections, copyediting marks, and a final editing portfolio.

Authoring notes:

- Level 1 is currently intentionally compact. If Grammar becomes a daily core habit, expand each unit to 3-5 lessons before building Level 2.
- Tie grammar practice to real sentences from Literature, History, Latin, and student writing.
- Use sentence diagramming as a visual reasoning tool, not a separate worksheet island.

### Logic

Recommended total: 4 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): Logic I, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/07-logic`, 11 units, 11 lessons.
- Level 2 (`planned`): Logic II, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/07-logic`.
- Level 3 (`planned`): Logic III, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/07-logic`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): Logic IV, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/07-logic`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Concrete reasoning. Cover patterns, rules, categories, classification, same/different/opposite, analogies, sequencing, cause/effect, true/false/not enough information, if-then thinking, claims, reasons, and common reasoning mistakes.
- Level 2: Informal logic. Cover statements vs. questions, claims vs. evidence, premises and conclusions, good definitions, examples and counterexamples, analogy strength, generalizations, cause vs. correlation, source reliability, relevant vs. irrelevant reasons, and common fallacies in kid-friendly contexts.
- Level 3: Formal logic I. Cover terms, propositions, categorical statements, square-of-opposition awareness, Venn diagrams, syllogisms, validity vs. truth, soundness, conditionals, necessary and sufficient conditions, and translating simple arguments into standard form.
- Level 4: Formal logic II and applied reasoning. Cover symbolic statements, negation, conjunction, disjunction, conditionals, truth tables, deductive/inductive/abductive reasoning, probability and data claims, scientific reasoning, argument maps, debate case structure, and cumulative analysis of real-world arguments.

Authoring notes:

- Logic should start visual and puzzle-like, then gradually become language and argument analysis.
- Avoid politicized examples; use school, family, literature, science, and history scenarios.
- Link Logic III/IV to Rhetoric evidence work and History source evaluation.

### Rhetoric

Recommended total: 5 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): Rhetoric I, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/08-rhetoric`, 11 units, 11 lessons.
- Level 2 (`planned`): Rhetoric II, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/08-rhetoric`.
- Level 3 (`planned`): Rhetoric III, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/08-rhetoric`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): Rhetoric IV, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/08-rhetoric`.
- Level 5 (`planned`): Rhetoric V, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-07/08-rhetoric`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Clear communication. Cover narration, main idea, summary, strong sentences, paragraph shape, description, explanation, opinion with reasons, compare/contrast, short speeches, revision, and cumulative clear-expression practice.
- Level 2: Progymnasmata I. Cover fable, narrative, anecdote, chreia, proverb, moral, paraphrase, imitation, amplification, reduction, beginning/middle/end structure, oral narration, and short written retellings.
- Level 3: Progymnasmata II. Cover refutation, confirmation, commonplace, praise/blame, encomium, vituperation in age-appropriate form, comparison, evidence selection, audience awareness, transitions, and multi-paragraph compositions.
- Level 4: Progymnasmata III and public argument. Cover impersonation, description/ekphrasis, thesis, law or policy proposal, arrangement of an argument, introduction, narration, proof, refutation, conclusion, ethos, pathos, logos, and delivery.
- Level 5: Rhetoric capstone. Cover rhetorical analysis, speech planning, debate, research-supported argument, literary and civic speeches, style imitation, revision cycles, visual/oral presentation, and a final persuasive or ceremonial oration.

Authoring notes:

- Rhetoric should include both speaking and writing from the beginning.
- Use Literature and History readings as the source material for many prompts.
- Keep assignments short in lessons, but let capstones accumulate across a level.

### Classical Literature

Recommended total: 5 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): Classical Literature I, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/09-literature`, 10 units, 10 lessons.
- Level 2 (`planned`): Classical Literature II, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/09-literature`.
- Level 3 (`planned`): Classical Literature III, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/09-literature`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): Classical Literature IV, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/09-literature`.
- Level 5 (`planned`): Classical Literature V, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-07/09-literature`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Shared story foundation. Cover fables, myths, folk tales, legends, poems, recitation, character, choice, setting, conflict, resolution, beautiful sentences, Latin connections, and cumulative comprehension.
- Level 2: Ancient story world. Cover Aesop review, Greek and Roman mythology, Homeric epic in adapted form, hero patterns, gods and mortals, fate and choice, classical monsters, origin stories, ancient drama concepts, lyric poetry, and comparison of myth variants.
- Level 3: Medieval and Renaissance tradition. Cover quests, Arthurian legend, medieval saints and heroes in culturally sensitive form, allegory, Dante and Chaucer excerpts in adaptation, Shakespeare scenes, sonnets, comedy/tragedy, and the movement from oral tale to literary text.
- Level 4: Early modern and 19th-century classics. Cover Robinsonade/adventure, moral tales, satire, Gothic and romantic elements, American and British poetry, speeches and essays as literature, point of view, symbolism, theme development, and literary influence.
- Level 5: Great Books seminar. Cover selected ancient-to-modern excerpts by genre, epic, tragedy, comedy, novel, essay, speech, and poem. Students should use textual evidence, compare translations/adaptations, discuss moral and civic questions, write literary responses, and build a final commonplace or reading portfolio.

Authoring notes:

- Prefer public-domain texts, adapted excerpts, and short passages that can fit lesson flow.
- Use complete short works when possible: fables, poems, speeches, myths, scenes, and excerpts with enough context.
- Level 1 is compact; expand with 3-5 lessons per unit if Literature becomes a major reading spine.

### History And Civics

Recommended total: 5 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): History And Civics I, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/10-history-civics`, 10 units, 10 lessons.
- Level 2 (`planned`): History And Civics II, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/10-history-civics`.
- Level 3 (`planned`): History And Civics III, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/10-history-civics`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): History And Civics IV, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/10-history-civics`.
- Level 5 (`planned`): History And Civics V, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-07/10-history-civics`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Time, place, and civic orientation. Cover timelines, maps, ancient peoples, Greece and Rome, medieval worlds, explorers and encounters, early America, communities, government, rights, responsibilities, symbols, documents, speeches, and cumulative review.
- Level 2: Ancient and classical world. Cover historical inquiry, primary/secondary sources, geography tools, Mesopotamia, Egypt, Israel, India, China, Greece, Rome, early Christianity in historical context, classical civic ideas, ancient trade, and enduring cultural contributions.
- Level 3: Medieval and early modern world. Cover Byzantine, Islamic, African, Asian, and European medieval worlds; feudalism and towns; medieval learning; Renaissance; Reformation; exploration; encounters and exchange; early nation-states; and the development of law, representation, and religious/cultural pluralism.
- Level 4: American founding and constitutional life. Cover Indigenous North America, colonial regions, Atlantic world, Revolution, founding documents, Constitution, Bill of Rights, branches of government, federalism, westward expansion, reform movements, Civil War, Reconstruction, and civic responsibilities.
- Level 5: Modern world and civic action. Cover industrialization, immigration, imperialism, world wars, totalitarianism and democracy, civil rights, Cold War, globalization, economics basics, media literacy, public policy, local government, and an inquiry project that uses sources and proposes informed action.

Authoring notes:

- Each level should use the C3 inquiry arc: question, disciplinary tools, sources/evidence, and conclusion/action.
- Build timeline and map routines in every level.
- Handle contested topics with age-appropriate framing, multiple sources, and clear distinction between fact, interpretation, and opinion.

### Memory Work

Recommended total: 4 levels.

Inventory:

- Level 1 (`complete`): Memory Work I, `src/content/curriculum/grade-03/11-memory-work`, 8 units, 8 lessons.
- Level 2 (`planned`): Memory Work II, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-04/11-memory-work`.
- Level 3 (`planned`): Memory Work III, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-05/11-memory-work`.
- Level 4 (`planned`): Memory Work IV, recommended folder `src/content/curriculum/grade-06/11-memory-work`.

Level strategy:

- Level 1: Memory habits. Cover short poems, Latin sayings, grammar rules, math facts and measures, geography, history sentences, famous lines and speeches, and cumulative review.
- Level 2: Fluent recitation. Cover longer poems, additional Latin maxims, grammar definitions, geography lists and map pegs, timeline anchors, civic terms, scientific or mathematical formulas where useful, and expressive oral delivery.
- Level 3: Durable knowledge pegs. Cover multi-stanza poems, speech excerpts, Latin paradigms and mottoes, logic definitions, history chronology, geography regions, literary quotations, and cumulative mixed retrieval across subjects.
- Level 4: Capstone recitation. Cover a polished poem or speech, civic document excerpts, Latin passage or motto selections, advanced grammar/logic/rhetoric terms, timeline synthesis, and a final recitation portfolio.

Authoring notes:

- Memory Work should use spaced retrieval: short first exposure, frequent review, mixed recall, and later performance.
- Keep individual lessons brief and satisfying.
- Avoid turning Memory Work into a duplicate content course; it should strengthen recall for the other foundation tracks.
