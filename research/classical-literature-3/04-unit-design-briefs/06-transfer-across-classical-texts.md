# Unit 6: Transfer Across Classical Texts

Track: Classical Literature
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/classical-literature-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students integrate the full Level 3 toolkit on new short passages. They choose an appropriate reading move, support interpretations with evidence, compare passages or interpretations, and correct common analytical mistakes.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit belongs at the end because mastery requires transfer. Students should show they can decide which routine fits a new text rather than simply applying the routine from the most recent lesson.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: transfer, strategy choice, supported interpretation, comparison, misconception correction.
- Vocabulary: reading move, genre signal, form, motif, claim, evidence, explanation, context, comparison, interpretation, misconception, enduring question.
- Representations and models: reading-move menus, claim-evidence-explanation frames, comparison charts, misconception repair prompts.
- Texts and forms: unfamiliar adapted classical passages, short paired passages, mini-dialogues, lyric-like excerpts, and interpretation examples.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the full body of Level 3 work: objective summary, theme, evidence, context, heroic and epic signals, tragic and comic readiness, translation comparison, point of view, imagery, allusion, wisdom forms, and dialogue.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- The newest strategy is the right strategy for every passage.
- Broad topic words are complete themes.
- Evidence can be impressive but irrelevant.
- Context explains everything or nothing.
- Recognizing a term is the same as interpreting the passage.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start by choosing the reading move so students practice diagnosis. Then build a supported interpretation from summary through evidence and explanation. End with comparison and correction tasks that require students to identify weak reasoning and transfer multiple routines at once.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

The unit deliberately interleaves every earlier unit. It should include delayed retrieval of genre terms, motifs, evidence routines, version comparison, close reading, and context boundaries.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares future classical literature levels by making students more independent with short translated excerpts, genre comparison, and concise literary argument. It also clarifies readiness for longer passages and more demanding written analysis.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose a useful reading move for a new passage.
- Build a supported theme, genre, or language interpretation.
- Compare paired passages or interpretations by a specific criterion.
- Correct plot-only, unsupported, symbol-overreach, or context-overreach responses.
- Demonstrate retained understanding of Level 3 motifs and forms.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Choose The Reading Move**: Decide whether a passage calls first for summary, genre, evidence, comparison, language, or context.
2. **Build A Supported Interpretation**: Combine summary, claim, evidence, and explanation on a new passage.
3. **Compare, Correct, And Transfer**: Compare passages or interpretations and repair common analytical mistakes.
