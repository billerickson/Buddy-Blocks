# Unit 2: Heroes, Journeys, And Epic Signals

Track: Classical Literature
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/classical-literature-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students analyze heroic and journey literature by tracking trials, choices, consequences, motifs, and introductory epic signals. By the end of the unit, students should be able to explain how heroic patterns create meaning instead of treating heroic stories as lists of adventures.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Heroic and journey stories are a strong bridge from prior story knowledge into deeper literary analysis. They give students visible structure, memorable motifs, and clear choices while preparing them for later work with tragic consequence, version comparison, and enduring questions.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: heroic code, journey structure, trial, cost, motif, epic signal, fate/free choice.
- Vocabulary: hero, heroic code, epic, invocation, in medias res, epithet, catalog, journey, trial, temptation, helper, hospitality, exile, homecoming, honor, loyalty, fate, free choice.
- Representations and models: journey maps, choice-consequence chains, motif trackers, epic-signal sorts.
- Texts and forms: adapted heroic episodes and short accessible epic-like excerpts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 1 routines for summary, claim, evidence, theme, and context. They should also be able to follow multi-episode stories and recognize character motive and consequence.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Heroes are perfect people.
- A journey is only travel, not a structure of tests and choices.
- Fate means characters have no responsibility.
- Epic signals are decorative labels instead of clues to meaning.
- Homecoming always solves the conflict.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with journey episodes because they are concrete and familiar. Then focus on heroic code so students can analyze choice and cost. Introduce epic signals only after students have a story structure to attach them to. End with motifs that complicate heroic stories, especially hospitality, homecoming, fate, and choice.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson should retrieve Unit 1 habits. Students should summarize the episode before analyzing it, identify the claim being made, and explain evidence rather than merely quote a heroic line.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 3 by foregrounding choice, hubris readiness, reversal, and consequence. It prepares Unit 4 by giving familiar story material that can be retold or translated differently. It prepares Unit 6 by establishing several recurring motifs for transfer.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Sequence the parts of a journey episode.
- Interpret a trial as more than an action scene.
- Explain a heroic-code choice with evidence.
- Recognize introductory epic signals and their purpose.
- Analyze a motif such as hospitality, homecoming, fate, or exile in context.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Journey Episodes And Trials**: Track departure, test, temptation, help, cost, and return or delay.
2. **Heroic Code: Honor, Loyalty, And Cost**: Analyze how a code shapes choices and consequences.
3. **Epic Signals: Invocation, In Medias Res, And Epithets**: Recognize common epic features and connect them to meaning.
4. **Hospitality, Homecoming, Fate, And Choice**: Interpret recurring heroic motifs without flattening them into simple morals.
