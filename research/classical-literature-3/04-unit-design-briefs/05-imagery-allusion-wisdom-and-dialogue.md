# Unit 5: Imagery, Allusion, Wisdom, And Dialogue

Track: Classical Literature
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/classical-literature-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students deepen close reading by analyzing compact language and form: imagery, metaphor, symbol readiness, allusion, repetition, lyric speaker, wisdom sayings, speeches, and dialogues. By the end of the unit, students should connect language choices to meaning without treating figurative language as decoration or a secret code.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Version comparison has already trained students to notice wording. This unit uses that attention for short, rereadable texts where individual images, allusions, repeated phrases, or dialogue turns carry significant meaning.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: imagery, metaphor, symbol readiness, allusion, repetition, speaker, structure, ethical question.
- Vocabulary: image, imagery, metaphor, symbol, allusion, repetition, structure, line, stanza, speaker, audience, speech, dialogue, wisdom saying, proverb, justice, courage, loyalty, piety, friendship.
- Representations and models: image-to-idea charts, allusion clue maps, repetition trackers, dialogue turn maps, ethical-question frames.
- Texts and forms: short lyric-like passages, speeches, adapted wisdom sayings, brief dialogues, and excerpt-like lines with clear allusive or figurative work.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need theme, evidence, version awareness, point of view, speaker/narrator distinction, basic figurative language, and enough classical motifs to recognize allusion with support.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A symbol has one secret answer unrelated to context.
- Allusion is merely a name or reference.
- Imagery and metaphor are decorative extras.
- The speaker is always the author.
- A short wisdom saying is simple because it has few words.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with imagery and metaphor because students can see how concrete language carries ideas. Move to allusion and symbol readiness after they have a context-based close-reading routine. Then examine lyric speaker, repetition, and structure. End with wisdom sayings, speeches, and dialogues, where compact form raises ethical questions through voice and exchange.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review motifs from Units 2-3 through images and allusions. Reuse Unit 4 wording and tone comparison when students analyze repeated words or speaker choices. Keep Unit 1 evidence frames active in every close-reading item.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 6 transfer by adding language-based passages to the final review mix. It also prepares later levels for more independent close reading, fuller poetry study, and more complex philosophical or dramatic texts.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Connect an image or metaphor to a passage idea.
- Identify allusion or symbol readiness from textual clues.
- Distinguish speaker from author.
- Explain the effect of repetition or structure.
- Interpret a wisdom saying, speech, or dialogue through an ethical question.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Imagery And Metaphor In Short Passages**: Explain how concrete language shapes an idea.
2. **Allusion And Symbol Readiness**: Use context clues to interpret references and repeated objects carefully.
3. **Lyric Speaker, Repetition, And Structure**: Notice who is speaking, what repeats, and how form guides meaning.
4. **Wisdom Sayings, Speeches, And Dialogues**: Read compact forms as arguments about ethical questions.
