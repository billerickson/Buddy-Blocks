# Unit 1: Sentences, Subjects, And Predicates

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students build the foundation for formal grammar by recognizing complete sentences, identifying simple subjects and predicates, choosing sentence-ending punctuation, and repairing straightforward fragments and run-ons.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes first because every later grammar target depends on sentence boundaries. Students need to know what makes a sentence complete before parts of speech, punctuation, or editing rules can transfer into writing.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: complete thought, subject/predicate relationship, sentence purpose, sentence boundary, fragment, run-on.
- Vocabulary: sentence, complete thought, fragment, run-on, subject, predicate, statement, question, exclamation, command, period, question mark, exclamation point.
- Representations and models: two-part sentence frames, subject/predicate split lines, punctuation choice charts, fragment/run-on repair choices.
- Texts and forms: short meaningful sentences, student-writing-like fragments, two-sentence run-ons, brief explanation frames.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should be able to read short sentences, understand basic word order, recognize common punctuation marks, and write a simple sentence with support.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A long word group is automatically a sentence.
- A word group with a noun but no predicate is complete.
- The subject is always the first word.
- All sentences end with periods.
- A comma alone can fix a run-on.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with complete thoughts so students focus on meaning before labels. Then split sentences into subject and predicate to show the internal structure of completeness. Next, connect sentence purpose to end punctuation. End by applying those foundations to fragments and run-ons.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This opening unit reviews informal prior knowledge: capital letters, punctuation marks, oral complete thoughts, and simple written sentences.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares noun and pronoun work by making subjects visible, verb work by making predicates visible, mechanics by clarifying sentence boundaries, and cumulative editing by establishing the first diagnostic questions students should ask: `Is it complete?` and `Where should it end?`

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Decide whether a word group is a complete sentence.
- Identify the subject and predicate in a simple sentence.
- Match sentence purpose to end punctuation.
- Repair a fragment by adding the missing sentence part.
- Repair a run-on by separating or correctly connecting complete thoughts.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Complete Thoughts**: Decide whether a word group expresses a complete thought.
2. **Subjects And Predicates**: Identify who or what the sentence is about and what is said about it.
3. **Sentence Types And End Marks**: Match statements, questions, exclamations, and commands to punctuation.
4. **Fragments And Run-Ons**: Repair incomplete and overconnected sentences in clear cases.
