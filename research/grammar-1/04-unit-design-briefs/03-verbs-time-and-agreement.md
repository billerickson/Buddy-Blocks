# Unit 3: Verbs, Time, And Agreement

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students identify verbs as predicate words, use simple verb forms to show time, recognize common irregular verbs, and choose agreement forms that fit singular and plural subjects.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Verbs belong after nouns and sentence foundations because students now understand that a sentence needs both something it is about and something said about it. Verb work turns the predicate into an explicit grammar target.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: action, being, time, regular and irregular forms, subject-verb agreement.
- Vocabulary: verb, action verb, being verb, predicate, tense, past, present, future, regular, irregular, singular, plural, agree/agreement.
- Representations and models: subject+verb frames, time-word timelines, regular/irregular form charts, singular/plural agreement pairs.
- Texts and forms: short action sentences, simple state-of-being sentences, sentence pairs with time clues, correction sentences with one verb target.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should identify subjects, predicates, nouns, pronouns, singular and plural nouns, and complete sentences.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Verbs are only dramatic actions.
- Being verbs do not count as verbs.
- Every past-tense verb ends in `-ed`.
- The nearest noun always controls the verb.
- Agreement is optional if the meaning is understandable.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin by broadening verb recognition from action to being. Then connect verbs to time through past, present, and future forms. Next, teach regular and irregular forms so students avoid overgeneralizing `-ed`. End with subject-verb agreement because it requires students to coordinate subject number and verb form.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review subjects and predicates in every lesson. Use nouns and pronouns from Unit 2 as subjects so agreement practice strengthens earlier noun knowledge.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares adverb identification, sentence expansion, sentence combining, and cumulative editing. It also lays groundwork for Grammar 2 helping/linking verb and tense-consistency work.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify action and being verbs in simple sentences.
- Choose past, present, and future forms from context clues.
- Correct common regular and irregular verb forms.
- Match singular or plural subjects to appropriate verbs.
- Explain that the verb must fit the subject and time.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Action Verbs And Being Verbs**: Recognize verbs that show action or state of being.
2. **Past, Present, And Future Verbs**: Use verb forms and time words to show when something happens.
3. **Regular And Irregular Verb Forms**: Choose common past-tense forms without overusing `-ed`.
4. **Subject-Verb Agreement**: Match simple subjects and verbs in number.
