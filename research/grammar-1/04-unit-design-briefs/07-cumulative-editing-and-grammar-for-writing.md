# Unit 7: Cumulative Editing And Grammar For Writing

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students transfer Grammar 1 knowledge into focused editing, purposeful revision, and short explanations of grammar choices.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit belongs at the end because students need a full set of sentence, word-form, punctuation, and revision tools before mixed editing is fair. The goal is transfer, not another isolated rule.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: targeted editing, revision for clarity, sentence variety, grammar explanation, cumulative transfer.
- Vocabulary: edit, revise, target, convention, clarity, variety, complete thought, fragment, run-on, modifier, phrase, conjunction, capitalization, punctuation, explain, evidence.
- Representations and models: editing target checklists, before/after revision pairs, compact passage markups, one-sentence explanation frames.
- Texts and forms: short student-writing-like passages, sentence-combining pairs, mixed correction tasks, capstone editing checklist.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should bring all earlier Grammar 1 knowledge: sentence boundaries, subject/predicate, parts of speech, word forms, capitalization, punctuation, commas, dialogue signals, expansion, and combining.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Editing means fixing everything at once.
- Revision only means adding more words.
- A grammatically polished sentence can change the writer's meaning.
- Explanations are just labels rather than reasons.
- In mixed review, the newest rule is probably the answer.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with focused editing targets so students learn to choose and apply one convention at a time. Then move to revision for clarity and variety, where students improve sentences rather than only correct errors. End with a compact capstone that mixes targets and asks for explanation.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This unit is entirely cumulative. Each lesson deliberately retrieves sentence completeness, parts of speech, word forms, capitalization, punctuation, modifiers, phrases, conjunctions, and sentence combining.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares Grammar 2 by strengthening habits of evidence-based editing and explanation. Students will be better prepared for compound subjects and predicates, direct objects, expanded phrases, dialogue punctuation, and simple diagramming.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose the relevant editing target in a short sentence or passage.
- Correct targeted errors without changing meaning.
- Revise a sentence for clarity or variety.
- Explain a grammar choice using accurate terms.
- Complete a mixed Grammar 1 capstone edit.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Focused Editing Targets**: Choose one or two grammar targets and edit with purpose.
2. **Revise For Clarity And Variety**: Improve sentences by adding, moving, or combining details.
3. **Grammar 1 Capstone Edit**: Apply sentence, word, and mechanics knowledge in a compact mixed edit.
