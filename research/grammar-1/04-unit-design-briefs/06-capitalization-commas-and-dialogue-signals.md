# Unit 6: Capitalization, Commas, And Dialogue Signals

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use capitalization, commas, and introductory dialogue punctuation to make names, sentence boundaries, lists, dates, places, and speakers clear to readers.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Mechanics receive focused treatment here because students now understand enough sentence and phrase structure to apply punctuation as a meaning signal rather than a decoration.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: capitalization for specific names and beginnings, comma as a separator for specific structures, quotation marks for exact spoken words.
- Vocabulary: capital, uppercase, lowercase, proper noun, title, day, month, comma, series, list, date, place, quotation marks, dialogue, speaker tag, exact words.
- Representations and models: capitalization checklists, list item boxes, date/place pattern frames, dialogue speaker-tag frames.
- Texts and forms: short editing sentences, lists of three or more, simple dates and city/state pairs, one-sentence dialogue examples.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should know complete sentences, proper nouns, phrases, conjunctions, simple sentence combining, and basic end punctuation.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Capitals show excitement or importance rather than rule-governed names and beginnings.
- A comma belongs wherever a reader pauses.
- Two list items need the same commas as three list items.
- Quotation marks go around the speaker tag instead of the exact words.
- Dialogue punctuation can be correct without showing who spoke.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with capitalization because it has been spiraled since Unit 1 and links to proper nouns from Unit 2. Then teach commas in series, followed by dates and places, because both depend on grouping information. End with dialogue punctuation as the most complex mechanics target in the unit.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Capitalization reviews proper nouns and sentence beginnings. Comma lessons review nouns, conjunctions, and phrase grouping. Dialogue sentences review verbs, pronouns, subjects, and end punctuation.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares cumulative editing by adding high-frequency mechanics targets. It also prepares Grammar 2, where quotation punctuation and comma rules become more detailed.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Capitalize sentence beginnings, `I`, names, titles, days, months, and places.
- Use commas in lists of three or more.
- Use commas in simple dates and place phrases.
- Punctuate a simple dialogue sentence with quotation marks and a speaker tag.
- Explain which structure a punctuation mark is signaling.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Capital Letters For Names And Beginnings**: Apply capitalization to sentence starts, `I`, and proper nouns.
2. **Commas In A Series**: Separate three or more words or phrases in a list.
3. **Commas In Dates And Places**: Use commas to group date and place information.
4. **Dialogue Tags And Quotation Marks**: Mark exact spoken words and identify the speaker.
