# Unit 4: Adjectives, Adverbs, And Precise Description

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use modifiers to make sentence meaning more precise, identifying adjectives as noun modifiers and adverbs as verb or modifier helpers in clear contexts.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Modifiers belong after nouns and verbs because students need to know what is being modified. This unit shifts grammar from basic sentence correctness toward precision and revision.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: description, limitation, comparison, action detail, precision.
- Vocabulary: adjective, noun, describe, which one, what kind, how many, comparative, superlative, adverb, verb, how, when, where, precise, detail.
- Representations and models: modifier arrows, noun-detail frames, verb-detail frames, comparison ladders, before/after revision pairs.
- Texts and forms: short descriptive sentences, sentence revisions, clear adjective/adverb contrast examples.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should identify nouns, pronouns, verbs, subjects, predicates, and complete sentences.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Any colorful word is an adjective.
- Adjectives describe actions.
- All adverbs end in `-ly`.
- Comparative and superlative forms can be mixed freely.
- More modifiers always make a sentence clearer.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Teach adjectives first because they build directly on nouns. Then introduce comparison forms as a special adjective job. Finish with adverbs so students can contrast noun description with verb/action description and use modifiers in revision.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson reviews nouns or verbs through the question `Which word is this modifier helping?` Sentence completeness and punctuation continue in all examples.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares sentence expansion, prepositional phrase comparison, revision for clarity, and later style work. It also strengthens students' ability to explain grammar choices by meaning.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify adjectives and the nouns they modify.
- Choose comparative or superlative adjective forms in clear contexts.
- Identify adverbs and the verbs they modify.
- Distinguish adjective jobs from adverb jobs.
- Revise a sentence with a relevant precise detail.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Adjectives Describe Nouns**: Identify and use words that tell which one, what kind, or how many.
2. **Comparatives And Superlatives**: Choose adjective forms for comparing two or more.
3. **Adverbs Describe Actions**: Identify and use words that tell how, when, or where about verbs.
