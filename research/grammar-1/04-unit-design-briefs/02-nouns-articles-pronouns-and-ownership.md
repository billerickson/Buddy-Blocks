# Unit 2: Nouns, Articles, Pronouns, And Ownership

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students understand nouns and noun-related words as tools for naming, pointing, replacing, making plural, and showing ownership in simple sentences.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows sentence foundations because nouns often serve as subjects and anchor the meaning of a sentence. Students can now connect word jobs to complete sentences instead of learning noun labels in isolation.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: common/proper noun distinction, noun signals, singular/plural meaning, ownership, pronoun reference.
- Vocabulary: noun, common noun, proper noun, person, place, thing, idea, article, determiner, singular, plural, irregular plural, possessive, apostrophe, pronoun, antecedent, referent.
- Representations and models: noun sort charts, article/determiner+noun frames, singular/plural pairs, ownership arrows, pronoun-to-noun reference lines.
- Texts and forms: short sentences with clear subjects, mini lists of named people/places/things, simple ownership sentences, two-sentence pronoun contexts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should know complete sentence, subject, predicate, capitalization at sentence beginnings, and basic punctuation.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Only people are nouns.
- Proper nouns are just "important" words.
- Articles and determiners do not matter because they are short.
- Apostrophe-s makes a noun plural.
- Pronouns are clear even when several possible nouns appear nearby.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with nouns and proper capitalization. Add articles and determiners as signals that help students find nouns and choose precise references. Then teach plural forms as number meaning. End with ownership and pronouns because both require students to track relationships between nouns.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson reviews complete sentences and subjects. Proper noun capitalization reviews Unit 1 sentence-beginning capitalization and punctuation in complete sentences.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares verb agreement, adjective modification, pronoun clarity in editing, possessive apostrophes in mechanics, and later Grammar 2 noun/pronoun detail.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify common and proper nouns in sentences.
- Use articles and determiners to point to nouns accurately.
- Form common regular and irregular plurals.
- Distinguish plural nouns from singular possessive nouns.
- Choose a pronoun only when the referent is clear.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Common And Proper Nouns**: Identify naming words and capitalize specific names.
2. **Articles, Determiners, And Noun Signals**: Use small pointing words to find and clarify nouns.
3. **Singular, Plural, And Irregular Nouns**: Show one, more than one, and common irregular plural forms.
4. **Possessives And Clear Pronouns**: Show ownership and keep pronouns connected to clear nouns.
