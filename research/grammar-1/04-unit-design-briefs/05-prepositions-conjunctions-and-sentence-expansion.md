# Unit 5: Prepositions, Conjunctions, And Sentence Expansion

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use phrases and conjunctions to add information and connect related ideas while keeping sentence boundaries clear.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes after core word jobs because phrases and conjunctions combine those words into larger sentence parts. Students are ready to expand sentences, but they still need guardrails against fragments and run-ons.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: phrase as an added word group, location/time details, connection, contrast, choice, reason, sentence combining.
- Vocabulary: preposition, prepositional phrase, object of the preposition as exposure, phrase, where, when, conjunction, connect, and, but, or, because, expand, combine, run-on.
- Representations and models: phrase brackets, sentence core plus add-on details, connector choice charts, two-sentence combining frames.
- Texts and forms: short base sentences, expanded versions, related sentence pairs, clear run-on/non-run-on contrasts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should know sentence completeness, subject and predicate, nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and basic punctuation.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A phrase can be a complete sentence by itself.
- Any connector can join any two ideas clearly.
- `And` is the only useful conjunction.
- `Because` clauses are complete sentences by themselves in every position.
- Combining sentences means simply removing a period.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with prepositional phrases because they add concrete where/when information without changing the sentence core. Then teach conjunctions as connectors with different meanings. After students understand phrases and connectors, ask them to expand sentences deliberately and combine sentences without creating run-ons.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review complete sentence cores before and after adding phrases. Reuse nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs inside expanded sentences.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares comma work in lists, dates, places, and dialogue tags by strengthening grouping awareness. It also prepares final revision work by giving students tools for adding detail and reducing choppiness.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify common prepositions and prepositional phrases.
- Add a prepositional phrase to answer where or when.
- Choose conjunctions that fit addition, contrast, choice, or reason.
- Expand a simple sentence with useful details.
- Combine two related sentences without making a run-on.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Prepositional Phrases Add Where And When**: Use phrase groups to add location or time information.
2. **Conjunctions Connect Words And Ideas**: Choose connectors that match the relationship between words or ideas.
3. **Expand Sentences With Useful Details**: Add details that clarify instead of clutter.
4. **Combine Sentences Without Run-Ons**: Join related sentences with correct connectors and punctuation.
