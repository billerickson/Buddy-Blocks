# Unit 4: Multiplication And Division Strategies

Track: Math
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students build flexible, meaning-based fluency for multiplication and division within 100. They use properties, decompositions, known facts, related facts, and inverse relationships to solve accurately and explain strategy choices.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes after multiplication and division meanings so fluency grows from structure rather than isolated memorization. Students now have enough conceptual grounding to practice facts, compare strategies, and begin the long spaced path toward retention.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: strategy selection, commutativity for multiplication, distributive decomposition, doubles, fives and tens, nines patterns, related facts, inverse operations, fact fluency as accuracy plus flexibility plus retention.
- Vocabulary: commutative property, distributive property, factor, product, quotient, decompose, related fact, known fact, unknown fact, double, multiple, inverse relationship, efficient.
- Representations and models: arrays split into smaller arrays, area-like rectangles, fact-family sets, related-fact chains, strategy tables, number-line jumps for repeated groups.
- Texts and forms: "Which strategy works best?" prompts, short strategy explanations, fact comparisons, worked examples with strategy labels.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need equal-group multiplication, array multiplication, sharing and grouping division, unknown factors, fact families, skip counting, addition/subtraction fluency, and comfort using diagrams to support reasoning.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Fast recall is the only meaning of fluency.
- Every fact should be memorized separately.
- Commutativity works for division because it works for multiplication.
- The distributive property is a written trick rather than splitting a factor or array.
- A strategy that worked for one fact must be best for every fact.
- Correct once means mastered permanently.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Open with low-load facts and patterns that students can anchor confidently, such as 2s, 5s, and 10s. Then introduce commutativity as a way to reduce the fact load for multiplication. Move into distributive decomposition for harder facts, then use related multiplication facts to support division. End with mixed strategy selection and spaced retrieval routines that require students to choose, explain, and retain.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review Unit 2 arrays and equal groups as the visual basis for multiplication strategies. Review Unit 3 unknown-factor reasoning and fact families so division facts remain connected to multiplication.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 5 multiplying one-digit numbers by multiples of 10, Unit 6 multi-step problems, Unit 9 scaled graphs, Unit 10 area, Unit 11 perimeter with equal sides, and the final cumulative unit. Its fluency work should continue across the rest of the level.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Use and explain multiplication strategies for facts within 100.
- Use related multiplication facts to solve division.
- Identify when commutativity applies and when it does not.
- Decompose a factor or array to derive a product.
- Demonstrate retained accuracy through mixed and delayed retrieval.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Anchor Facts: Twos, Fives, And Tens**: Establish reliable facts with visible patterns and skip counting.
2. **Doubles And Near Doubles**: Use known doubles to derive nearby facts.
3. **Turnaround Facts In Multiplication**: Use commutativity to relate products while tracking context.
4. **Break Apart A Factor**: Use distributive reasoning with arrays and equations.
5. **Nines And Neighbor Facts**: Derive facts from tens or known nearby facts.
6. **Division From Related Multiplication**: Solve quotients by finding the missing factor.
7. **Choose The Most Useful Strategy**: Compare strategy efficiency for different facts.
8. **Mixed Fact Retrieval With Meaning**: Practice delayed, mixed multiplication and division facts with explanation support.
