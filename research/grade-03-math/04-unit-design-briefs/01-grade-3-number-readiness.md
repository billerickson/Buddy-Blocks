# Unit 1: Grade 3 Number Readiness

Track: Math
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students refresh and stabilize the Grade 2 number, operation, and representation knowledge that Grade 3 depends on. By the end of the unit, students should use place-value language, addition and subtraction strategies, open number lines, strip diagrams, equations, skip counting, equal groups, and arrays to represent and check simple problems.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes first because Grade 3 introduces several new demands at once: multiplication, division, fractions as numbers, multi-step problems, and more independent representation choice. Students need a short but serious launch that makes prerequisite knowledge visible, repairs fragile understandings, and establishes the course habit of modeling before calculating.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: place value, composing and decomposing numbers, addition/subtraction as joining, separating, and comparing, skip-counting structure, early multiplicative structure, answer reasonableness.
- Vocabulary: place value, digit, value, hundreds, tens, ones, expanded form, compose, decompose, sum, difference, compare, estimate, reasonable, inverse operation, equation, equal groups, row, column, array.
- Representations and models: base-ten drawings, open number lines, number paths, strip diagrams, equations with blanks or boxes, skip-counting sequences, equal-group drawings, arrays.
- Texts and forms: short one-step arithmetic contexts, comparison statements, equation-to-story matching, brief explanation stems such as "I know this is reasonable because..."

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should enter with developing fluency for addition and subtraction within 20, strategies for addition and subtraction within 100, basic three-digit place value, skip counting by 2s, 5s, and 10s, simple word-problem experience, equal-share language, and familiarity with rows, columns, graphs, clocks, coins, and standard measurement tools.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A digit's value is the same no matter where it appears.
- Regrouping or decomposing is a set of digit moves rather than trading units.
- Counting by ones is the only reliable way to find a total.
- The equals sign means "the answer comes next."
- A model is decorative instead of a tool for understanding the problem.
- Any close-looking answer is acceptable if the computation felt familiar.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with place-value structure because it supports almost every later Grade 3 domain. Then revisit addition and subtraction through models and checking strategies. After additive reasoning is active, move into skip counting, equal groups, and arrays as readiness for formal multiplication. End with mixed representation choice so students practice deciding whether a number line, diagram, equation, or array helps most.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This is the opening unit, so review draws from Grade 2 rather than earlier Grade 3 units. It should deliberately retrieve Grade 2 place value, addition/subtraction, skip counting, arrays, time/money/measurement vocabulary, and simple graph-reading without treating any one review topic as a full new course.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Units 2-4 by making equal groups, arrays, and skip counting visible. It prepares Unit 5 through place value and estimation. It prepares Unit 6 through strip diagrams, equations, and answer-checking habits. It also gives later fraction, measurement, data, and area work a stable number-line and representation foundation.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Read, write, compare, and decompose three-digit numbers.
- Add and subtract within 100 fluently and within 1,000 with models or strategies.
- Match simple contexts to equations and diagrams.
- Use skip counting, equal groups, and arrays as structured representations.
- Explain whether an answer is reasonable using magnitude, inverse operations, or context.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Hundreds, Tens, And Ones**: Rebuild place-value language and decompositions for three-digit numbers.
2. **Numbers On Open Number Lines**: Use number-line distance and benchmarks to compare and locate numbers.
3. **Addition Strategies That Still Work**: Refresh make-ten, place-value, and number-line strategies for sums.
4. **Subtraction As Difference And Take-Away**: Review subtraction meanings and connect subtraction to addition checks.
5. **Equations, Boxes, And Balance**: Treat equations as relationships, with unknowns in different positions.
6. **Skip Counting With Structure**: Connect skip counting to equal jumps, equal groups, and repeated addition.
7. **Rows, Columns, And Arrays**: Name and interpret array structure before formal multiplication.
8. **Choose A Model And Check**: Mix number lines, strip diagrams, equations, and arrays to build representation choice.
