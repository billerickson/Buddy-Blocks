# Unit 10: Area, Arrays, And Multiplication

Track: Math
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students understand area as square-unit covering and connect rectangular area to arrays and multiplication. They count unit squares, tile rectangles, use side lengths to reason about area, and decompose supported rectilinear figures.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Area belongs after multiplication, measurement, and unit reasoning are established. This placement lets students see area as both geometric measurement and an application of arrays, without confusing it too early with perimeter.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: area as covering, square units, tiling without gaps or overlaps, rectangular arrays, side lengths as rows and columns, decomposing figures into rectangles.
- Vocabulary: area, square unit, unit square, tile, cover, rectangle, rows, columns, side length, array, product, square inch, square centimeter, square foot, decompose, compose.
- Representations and models: tiled rectangles, grid diagrams, arrays, rectangles labeled by side length, composed and decomposed rectilinear figures.
- Texts and forms: area measurement prompts, diagram explanations, "Which method finds area?" decisions, equation-to-rectangle matches.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need multiplication arrays, rows and columns, multiplication facts within 100, measurement units, rectangle attributes, grid-reading, and the ability to interpret side lengths.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Area is found by counting the boundary.
- Any grid, even with gaps or overlaps, shows valid area units.
- Side length and area are the same kind of measure.
- Area is only a formula to memorize.
- A rectangle with larger perimeter always has larger area.
- Decomposed pieces can be added without preserving square units.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with physical or visual covering using unit squares so area is an attribute, not a formula. Move to counting squares efficiently in rows and columns, then connect rows and columns to multiplication. Use side lengths only after students understand the tiled structure those lengths imply. End with supported decomposition of rectilinear figures and early distinction prompts that name area as square-unit covering.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review Unit 2 arrays, Unit 4 multiplication facts, Unit 9 measurement units, and Unit 6 modeling explanations. Use facts and arrays in service of measuring area, not as separate drills.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 11 perimeter distinction by making area stable first. It also supports Unit 12 transfer tasks and Grade 4 area/perimeter work with larger numbers and more complex figures.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Explain area as square-unit covering.
- Count square units in tiled figures.
- Use multiplication to find rectangular area.
- Match side lengths, arrays, equations, and area values.
- Decompose supported figures and add component areas.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Cover A Region With Square Units**: Define area through tiling without gaps or overlaps.
2. **Count Squares Efficiently**: Move from one-by-one counting to row and column structure.
3. **Arrays Inside Rectangles**: Connect rectangular tiling to multiplication.
4. **Use Side Lengths For Area**: Reason from labeled sides to rows, columns, and products.
5. **Unknown Side Or Area In A Rectangle**: Use multiplication or unknown-factor reasoning in area contexts.
6. **Decompose Figures Into Rectangles**: Find area of supported composite shapes by splitting and adding.
