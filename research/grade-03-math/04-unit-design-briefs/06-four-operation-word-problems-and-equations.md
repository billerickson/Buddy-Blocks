# Unit 6: Four-Operation Word Problems And Equations

Track: Math
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students model and solve one-step and accessible two-step word problems using all four operations. They learn to identify quantities, choose representations, write or match equations, track intermediate answers, and explain final answers with units and reasonableness.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit sits after students have refreshed whole-number computation and built multiplication/division foundations. It consolidates arithmetic into modeling habits before the course begins the major fraction and measurement strands, where students will need to interpret contexts and choose representations with more independence.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: operation choice by structure, unknowns in varied positions, intermediate quantity, two-step reasoning, answer units, reasonableness.
- Vocabulary: operation, equation, unknown, box, blank, intermediate quantity, total, difference, product, quotient, compare, more than, fewer than, times as many, reasonable, units, model.
- Representations and models: strip diagrams, arrays, equal-group drawings, number lines, tables, equations, input-output style tables when controlled, annotated work.
- Texts and forms: short one-step and two-step word problems, equation-to-situation matches, worked examples, concise written explanations.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need addition/subtraction within 1,000, multiplication/division meanings and facts within 100, rounding and estimation, inverse checks, strip diagrams, arrays, number lines, and equation reading.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Key words determine the operation.
- Two-step problems can be solved by combining all numbers at once.
- The first answer found is automatically the final answer.
- A correct computation is enough even if the equation does not match the situation.
- Units and context phrases are optional.
- If numbers are larger, the operation must be more complex.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with sorting and representing one-step problem types so students attend to structure. Then mix operations in controlled contexts and require students to choose a model. Introduce two-step problems by making the intermediate quantity explicit. Move from guided diagrams to equation writing, then finish with mixed modeling tasks that emphasize checking and explaining.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review Units 1 and 5 through addition/subtraction and estimation contexts. Review Units 2-4 through equal-group, array, division, and unknown-factor problems. Use previously learned representations so the new work is deciding and sequencing, not learning every model from scratch.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares students to solve fraction, measurement, data, area, perimeter, geometry, and financial contexts later in the course. It also establishes the final unit's transfer routine: understand the situation, choose a model, solve, check, and explain.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose the operation or operations that match a context.
- Represent one-step and two-step problems with diagrams and equations.
- Identify and use an intermediate quantity.
- Solve accurately with all four operations within Grade 3 expectations.
- Explain the answer in context and judge reasonableness.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Read For Structure, Not Key Words**: Sort situations by mathematical relationship.
2. **One-Step Problems With All Four Operations**: Match contexts to operation, model, and equation.
3. **Unknowns In Different Places**: Solve equations where the blank is not always at the end.
4. **Represent Compare Problems**: Use strip diagrams for more than, fewer than, and times as many.
5. **Find The Hidden First Step**: Identify intermediate quantities in two-step problems.
6. **Write Equations For Two-Step Problems**: Connect diagrams and equations to each step.
7. **Check Units And Reasonableness**: Evaluate answers for size, operation fit, and context.
8. **Mixed Modeling Practice**: Choose a representation independently for varied Grade 3 contexts.
