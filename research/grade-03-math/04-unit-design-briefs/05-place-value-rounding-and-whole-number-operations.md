# Unit 5: Place Value, Rounding, And Whole-Number Operations

Track: Math
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use place value to round, estimate, add, subtract, regroup, check answers, and multiply one-digit numbers by multiples of 10. Procedures should be tied to units, digit values, benchmarks, and reasonableness.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit returns to whole-number operations after multiplication/division meanings and early fluency are underway. Students can now connect place value to both additive computation and multiplicative reasoning, giving them tools for larger numbers before the course shifts into multi-step modeling and fractions.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: rounding by nearest benchmark, estimation before and after computation, regrouping as trading units, addition/subtraction within 1,000, inverse checks, multiplying by multiples of 10.
- Vocabulary: round, nearest ten, nearest hundred, benchmark, estimate, reasonable, regroup, compose, decompose, expanded form, inverse operation, multiple of 10, place value, digit value.
- Representations and models: base-ten blocks or drawings, open number lines, vertical algorithms with place-value explanations, expanded notation, benchmark number lines, partial products for one-digit times multiples of 10.
- Texts and forms: estimation prompts, "Is this answer reasonable?" contexts, computation explanations, check-your-answer routines.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need three-digit place value, addition/subtraction strategies, open number lines, multiplication facts from Unit 4, skip counting by tens and hundreds, comparison language, and understanding of inverse operations.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Rounding is changing a number to any nearby "nice" number.
- Rounding can be done by a digit rule without understanding nearest benchmarks.
- Regrouping means moving digits instead of trading units.
- Estimation is only something to do after exact computation.
- 6 x 40 has no connection to 6 x 4.
- The size of an answer does not need to fit the original problem.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with rounding on number lines so students see magnitude before digit shortcuts. Use estimation to set expectations for sums and differences, then revisit addition and subtraction with regrouping and explicit unit trades. After additive operations are stable, connect multiplication facts to multiples of 10 through place value. End with mixed computation and reasonableness tasks that ask students to choose exact calculation, estimation, or checking.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review Unit 1 place value, number lines, addition/subtraction, and inverse checks. Review Unit 4 multiplication facts when multiplying by multiples of 10, especially the relationship between basic facts and tens.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 6 two-step problems by strengthening computation and checking. It supports Unit 9 measurement estimation and scaled graphs, Unit 10 area calculations with larger side lengths, and Unit 12 cumulative problem solving.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Round numbers to the nearest ten or hundred using benchmarks.
- Estimate and compute sums and differences within 1,000.
- Explain regrouping with place-value language.
- Check addition/subtraction using inverse operations.
- Multiply one-digit numbers by multiples of 10 using known facts and place value.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Nearest Ten On A Number Line**: Round two- and three-digit numbers by distance to benchmarks.
2. **Nearest Hundred And Reasonableness**: Extend rounding to hundreds and use estimates to predict size.
3. **Add Within 1,000 With Place Value**: Connect models, expanded form, and written methods.
4. **Subtract Within 1,000 With Regrouping**: Make trades of hundreds, tens, and ones visible.
5. **Use Inverse Operations To Check**: Verify sums and differences by reversing the operation.
6. **Multiply By Multiples Of 10**: Connect facts like 4 x 3 to 4 x 30 through tens.
7. **Estimate, Compute, Or Check?**: Decide which whole-number tool fits a given task.
