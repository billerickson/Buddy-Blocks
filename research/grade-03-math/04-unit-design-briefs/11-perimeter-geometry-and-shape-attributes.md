# Unit 11: Perimeter, Geometry, And Shape Attributes

Track: Math
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students understand perimeter as boundary length, distinguish perimeter from area, and reason about shapes by defining attributes. They classify quadrilaterals, recognize category relationships, and connect equal-area partitions to fraction language.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows area so students can contrast two different measurements of shapes after area is conceptually secure. It also closes the main geometry strand before the cumulative mastery unit, giving students a chance to integrate measurement, fractions, and attributes.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: perimeter as distance around, area versus perimeter, side-length addition, equal sides, defining and non-defining attributes, quadrilateral categories, equal-area partitions.
- Vocabulary: perimeter, boundary, distance around, side length, area, square unit, attribute, defining attribute, non-defining attribute, quadrilateral, rectangle, square, rhombus, polygon, partition, equal area, category, subcategory.
- Representations and models: labeled polygons, grid shapes, perimeter paths, shape attribute charts, Venn-style category sorts, partitioned rectangles and other shapes.
- Texts and forms: shape classification claims, area/perimeter decision prompts, brief explanations using attribute evidence.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need area as square-unit covering, addition and multiplication fluency, measurement units, side length, rectangle and polygon vocabulary, fraction partitioning, and comparison language.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Area and perimeter are interchangeable because both involve shapes.
- Perimeter is found by counting inside squares.
- Area is found by adding side lengths.
- Shape category depends on orientation or appearance rather than attributes.
- A square is not a rectangle because it has a more specific name.
- Equal-area parts must be congruent or symmetric.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with perimeter as walking or measuring around a boundary. Then compare perimeter and area with side-by-side representations and tasks that force students to choose the attribute. Move into shape attributes and quadrilateral classification after measurement distinction is clearer. End by partitioning shapes into equal-area parts, connecting geometry back to fractions and same-whole reasoning.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review Unit 10 area continually through contrast prompts. Review Unit 7 fractions through equal partitions, Unit 5 addition/regrouping for perimeter sums, Unit 4 multiplication when equal side lengths make shortcuts useful, and Unit 6 explanation routines.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 12 cumulative transfer by requiring students to choose among area, perimeter, fraction, and shape-attribute reasoning. It prepares Grade 4 geometry and measurement work with angles, lines, symmetry, more complex perimeter/area problems, and expanded fraction reasoning.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Find perimeter from side lengths or grid paths.
- Decide whether a task asks for area or perimeter and justify the choice.
- Classify shapes by defining attributes.
- Recognize quadrilateral subcategories in Grade 3 terms.
- Partition shapes into equal-area parts and name the fractions.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Perimeter Is Distance Around**: Trace and measure boundaries with side lengths.
2. **Add Side Lengths Carefully**: Find perimeter of polygons, including missing repeated sides in controlled cases.
3. **Area Or Perimeter?**: Choose square-unit covering or boundary length based on the question.
4. **Attributes That Define Shapes**: Separate defining attributes from color, size, or orientation.
5. **Quadrilateral Categories**: Classify rectangles, squares, rhombuses, and other quadrilaterals by attributes.
6. **Equal-Area Partitions**: Partition shapes and connect equal parts to fraction names.
7. **Geometry And Measurement Mix**: Solve tasks that combine perimeter, area, attributes, and fractions.
