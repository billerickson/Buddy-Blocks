# Unit 8: Geometry, Measurement, And Coordinate Figures

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-06-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students solve Grade 6 geometry and measurement problems by choosing the correct measure, model, formula, and unit. By the end of the unit, students should find area of polygons, volume of rectangular prisms with fractional edge lengths, surface area from nets, and distances or areas on the coordinate plane.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes after rational numbers, expressions, and equations because geometry now uses coordinate signs, formulas, variables, fractions, decimals, and unit analysis. It gives students a concrete setting to apply symbolic reasoning.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: decomposing and composing shapes, base-height relationships, dimensional units, volume as filling, surface area as total face area, coordinate distance parallel to axes.
- Vocabulary: area, square units, base, height, triangle, parallelogram, polygon, decompose, volume, cubic units, rectangular prism, fractional edge length, net, surface area, face, coordinate polygon.
- Representations and models: grids, polygon decompositions, formula diagrams, rectangular prisms, nets, coordinate planes, labeled units, expression/formula substitutions.
- Texts and forms: floor-plan, package, garden, map, and design contexts with diagrams carrying most of the information.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Grade 5 area and volume foundations, multiplication with fractions and decimals, expression evaluation, coordinate plotting in all four quadrants, and number-line distance.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Area, perimeter, surface area, and volume are interchangeable.
- Slanted side length can be used as height even when it is not perpendicular to the base.
- Fractional edge lengths cannot produce fractional volumes.
- Surface area counts only visible faces.
- Coordinate distance is found by adding coordinate values rather than counting or subtracting along an axis.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with area formulas for parallelograms and triangles, using visual decomposition so formulas are not memorized without meaning. Then apply decomposition to polygons. Move into volume with fractional edge lengths, then nets and surface area. Finish with coordinate-plane polygon tasks and a measure-selection lesson that forces students to distinguish area, surface area, volume, perimeter, and distance.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review fraction multiplication/division from Unit 4, signed coordinates from Unit 5, expression evaluation from Unit 6, and equations from Unit 7. Use ratio and unit-rate review when measurement conversions or scale-like comparisons appear.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares cumulative modeling, data/geometry transfer, and Grade 7 scale drawings, circles, composite area, and more advanced surface area/volume problems.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Find area of parallelograms, triangles, and decomposed polygons.
- Use formulas with correct base-height pairs.
- Find volume of rectangular prisms with fractional dimensions.
- Use nets to find surface area.
- Solve coordinate-plane polygon problems.
- Choose the correct measure and unit for a situation.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Area Of Parallelograms And Triangles**: Connect formulas to decomposing and rearranging shapes.
2. **Decompose Polygons For Area**: Break polygons into rectangles and triangles to find total area.
3. **Volume With Fractional Edge Lengths**: Use unit cubes and multiplication to find prism volume.
4. **Nets And Surface Area**: Use nets to total the areas of all faces.
5. **Coordinate Plane Polygon Problems**: Use coordinates to find side lengths and areas of polygons.
6. **Choose The Right Measure And Unit**: Decide whether a task asks for perimeter, area, surface area, volume, or distance.
