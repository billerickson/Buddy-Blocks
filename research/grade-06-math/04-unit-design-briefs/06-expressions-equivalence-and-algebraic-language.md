# Unit 6: Expressions, Equivalence, And Algebraic Language

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-06-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to treat algebraic expressions as meaningful descriptions of quantities. By the end of the unit, students should interpret variables, evaluate expressions, use exponents and order of operations, write expressions from words, and identify equivalent expressions using properties.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows rational numbers because students now have a broader number system and are ready to describe patterns and quantities symbolically. It precedes equations, inequalities, geometry formulas, and variable relationships, all of which require expression fluency.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: variable as quantity, expression as calculation plan, substitution, order of operations, exponent meaning, equivalent expressions, properties of operations.
- Vocabulary: variable, expression, numerical expression, algebraic expression, term, factor, coefficient readiness, evaluate, substitute, exponent, base, property, equivalent, distribute.
- Representations and models: expression cards, tables of values, area models for distribution, verbal-to-symbolic translations, substitution tables, equation-free expression comparisons.
- Texts and forms: short phrases, pattern descriptions, geometry/readiness formulas, shopping or rate contexts, explanation prompts for expression meaning.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need operation fluency, multiplication meanings, variables as unknowns from earlier grades, rational-number readiness, and careful reading of mathematical phrases.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A variable is a label for an object or unit, not a number.
- `5m` means only "5 meters," not five times an unknown.
- Exponents mean multiply by the exponent.
- Order of operations is optional when numbers are easy.
- Expressions are equivalent when they have the same numbers, even if operations differ.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with variables as quantities so notation has meaning. Then evaluate expressions with substitution before adding exponents and order of operations. Move from words to expressions, then use properties and area models to show equivalent expressions. End with distribution because it bridges arithmetic structure and future algebra.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review operation order, multiplication/division meanings, fraction and decimal substitution, and ratio/rate contexts. Continue checking whether expression values are reasonable.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares equations, inequalities, dependent/independent relationships, geometry formulas, statistics expressions, financial models, and Grade 7 algebra.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Interpret variables and expressions in context.
- Evaluate expressions accurately after substitution.
- Apply order of operations and whole-number exponents.
- Write expressions from verbal descriptions.
- Identify and explain equivalent expressions using properties.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Variables As Quantities**: Interpret letters as numbers that are unknown or changing.
2. **Evaluate Expressions With Substitution**: Replace variables with values and compute accurately.
3. **Exponents And Order Of Operations**: Use exponents with whole-number bases and apply operation order.
4. **Write Expressions From Words**: Translate concise verbal descriptions into algebraic expressions.
5. **Properties And Equivalent Expressions**: Use operation properties to decide whether expressions have the same value.
6. **Distribute To Show Equivalent Forms**: Use area or grouping models to connect `a(b + c)` with `ab + ac`.
