# Unit 3: Unit Rates, Percents, And Applied Rates

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-06-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students extend ratio reasoning to rates, unit rates, unit prices, and percent as rate per 100. By the end of the unit, students should use labels to decide which division is needed, connect percent to fractions and decimals, and solve applied rate or percent problems in Grade 6 contexts.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows equivalent ratios because unit rates and percents are special ratio relationships. Students need the multiplicative structure from Unit 2 before working with "per 1" and "per 100" language.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: rate, unit rate, unit price, percent as per 100, benchmark percent, percent of a quantity, rate comparison.
- Vocabulary: rate, unit rate, per, per 1, per 100, percent, benchmark, unit price, quantity, measurement unit, better buy, reasonable.
- Representations and models: ratio tables, double number lines, percent bars, labeled equations, unit-rate comparison tables, simple budget or price scenarios.
- Texts and forms: shopping, speed, recipe, reading-rate, measurement, and financial decision contexts with limited reading load.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need equivalent ratios, multiplication/division, fraction and decimal benchmarks, table reasoning, and careful unit interpretation from Units 1-2.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Unit rate order does not matter.
- The larger total is always the better deal.
- Percent always means a count out of exactly 100 objects.
- A percent sign makes a number bigger or smaller by magic.
- Benchmark percents are separate facts rather than connected to fractions and decimals.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with rate language and units, then teach how to find a unit rate with attention to which quantity is per 1. Move to unit price because it is a concrete application of unit rate. Then introduce percent as per 100 using bars and double number lines before solving benchmark percent tasks. End with mixed decision problems where students must choose a rate or percent model and explain the result.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review equivalent ratios, ratio tables, double number lines, fraction benchmarks, decimal operations, and estimation from Units 1-2.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares decimal and fraction operation applications, algebraic relationships, statistics comparisons, geometry measurement rates, personal finance, and Grade 7 proportional reasoning.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Interpret rate statements with correct units.
- Compute and use unit rates and unit prices.
- Represent percent as per 100.
- Find benchmark percents of a quantity.
- Solve applied rate and percent decisions with reasonableness checks.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Rate Language And Units**: Interpret "per" statements and explain what each unit means.
2. **Find The Unit Rate**: Divide to find a quantity per 1 and label it correctly.
3. **Unit Prices And Better Buys**: Compare prices by finding cost per unit.
4. **Percent Means Per 100**: Connect percent language to ratios, fractions, decimals, and double number lines.
5. **Benchmark Percents Of A Quantity**: Use 10%, 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% to reason about amounts.
6. **Rate And Percent Decision Problems**: Apply rates and percents in compact real-world decisions.
