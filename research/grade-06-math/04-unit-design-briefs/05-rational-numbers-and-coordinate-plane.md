# Unit 5: Rational Numbers And The Coordinate Plane

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-06-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students extend the number system to include positive and negative rational numbers. By the end of the unit, students should interpret signed numbers in context, locate and compare rational numbers, explain opposites and absolute value, and plot ordered pairs in all four quadrants.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows fraction and decimal fluency because students need magnitude sense before adding negative numbers to the number line. It also comes before algebraic relationships and geometry because those units use coordinate grids, signed values, and graph interpretation.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: signed quantities, opposites, distance from zero, rational-number order, coordinate-plane location, quadrant interpretation.
- Vocabulary: positive, negative, rational number, opposite, absolute value, distance, origin, x-axis, y-axis, quadrant, ordered pair, coordinate, elevation, debt, credit, temperature.
- Representations and models: horizontal and vertical number lines, context tables, comparison statements, coordinate grids, ordered pair notation.
- Texts and forms: temperature, elevation, account balance, game score, map, and coordinate geometry readiness contexts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need number-line reasoning, fraction and decimal magnitude, comparison symbols, first-quadrant coordinate plotting, and the habit of connecting numbers to context.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- The negative number with the larger numeral is greater.
- Absolute value means "make the number positive" in every context.
- Opposites are the same as reciprocals.
- The first coordinate tells vertical movement.
- Points with one negative coordinate are always in the same quadrant.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with real contexts so signed numbers have meaning. Move to opposites on number lines and absolute value as distance before comparing and ordering rational numbers. Then extend coordinate plotting to all four quadrants and end with contexts where coordinates or signed quantities must be interpreted rather than merely plotted.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review fraction and decimal magnitude from Units 1 and 4, number-line benchmarks, and coordinate-plane readiness from Grade 5. Use rate or financial contexts from Unit 3 when signed account changes make sense.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares variable relationship graphs, coordinate-plane geometry, inequalities on number lines, data displays with signed values, and later Grade 7 signed-number operations.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match signed numbers to real-world contexts.
- Locate opposites and explain absolute value as distance from zero.
- Compare and order positive and negative rational numbers.
- Plot ordered pairs in all four quadrants.
- Interpret coordinates and signs in context.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Positive And Negative Contexts**: Use signed numbers to describe direction or position from a reference point.
2. **Opposites On Number Lines**: Locate numbers and their opposites on horizontal and vertical number lines.
3. **Absolute Value As Distance**: Interpret absolute value as distance from zero and connect it to context.
4. **Compare And Order Rational Numbers**: Order positive and negative fractions and decimals using number-line reasoning.
5. **Plot Points In All Four Quadrants**: Graph ordered pairs and identify quadrants by coordinate signs.
6. **Interpret Coordinates In Context**: Use signed coordinates to answer questions about maps, movement, and distance.
