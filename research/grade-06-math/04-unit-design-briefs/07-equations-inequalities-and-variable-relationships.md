# Unit 7: Equations, Inequalities, And Variable Relationships

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-06-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use equations, inequalities, and variable relationships to represent and solve Grade 6 contexts. By the end of the unit, students should solve and check one-variable equations, interpret inequality solution sets, graph inequalities on number lines, and connect tables, graphs, verbal rules, and equations for related quantities.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows expression work because students need to understand variables and expressions before solving equations or describing relationships. It also uses rational-number number lines from Unit 5 and prepares cumulative modeling in geometry, statistics, and finance.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: equality, inverse operations, solution, checking, inequality solution set, boundary values, dependent and independent variables, relationship across representations.
- Vocabulary: equation, solve, solution, inverse operation, equality, inequality, solution set, greater than, less than, at least, at most, dependent variable, independent variable, rule.
- Representations and models: balanced equations, tape diagrams, number-line inequality graphs, tables, coordinate graphs, verbal rules, simple equations.
- Texts and forms: one-step equations, compact word problems, constraints, savings or distance scenarios, table-to-graph interpretation prompts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need expression evaluation, operation fluency, variables, number-line reasoning, rational-number ordering, coordinate plotting, and unit-rate/table reasoning.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- The equals sign means "the answer comes next."
- Solving means moving numbers without preserving equality.
- A proposed solution does not need to be checked.
- An inequality has exactly one answer.
- "At least" and "at most" are interchangeable.
- Dependent and independent variables can be swapped without changing meaning.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with equations as balanced statements before formal solving. Then solve one-step equations and check solutions in context. Move to inequalities and solution sets, then graph those sets on number lines. End by connecting variables across tables, graphs, equations, and verbal rules, which pulls together arithmetic, ratios, and coordinate-plane work.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review variables and substitution from Unit 6, signed number lines from Unit 5, unit rates and tables from Unit 3, and reasonableness checks from Unit 1.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares geometry formulas, data and financial models, and Grade 7 proportional relationships and multi-step equations.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Explain equality and solve one-step equations.
- Check whether a number is a solution in context.
- Interpret inequalities and solution sets.
- Graph inequalities correctly on number lines.
- Connect tables, graphs, equations, and verbal rules for dependent and independent variables.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Equations As Balanced Statements**: Interpret equations as statements that two expressions have the same value.
2. **Solve One-Step Equations**: Use inverse operations to solve equations within Grade 6 expectations.
3. **Check A Solution In Context**: Substitute a proposed value and decide whether it answers the question.
4. **Inequalities And Solution Sets**: Interpret inequality symbols and describe many possible solutions.
5. **Graph Inequalities On A Number Line**: Show solution sets with open or closed circles and direction.
6. **Tables, Graphs, And Variable Relationships**: Connect dependent and independent variables across representations.
