# Unit 4: Fraction Division And Decimal Fluency

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-06-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students develop meaning-based fluency with fraction division and strengthen decimal and multi-digit operation fluency. By the end of the unit, students should interpret division situations, connect fraction-division models to equations and procedures, compute decimal quotients with place-value reasoning, and use estimates to check results.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes after ratios, rates, and percent because students now have several contexts where quotients and per-unit reasoning matter. Fraction division is a major Grade 6 abstraction jump and deserves its own focused unit before rational numbers and algebra increase symbolic demands.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: measurement division, sharing division, quotient interpretation, reciprocal, fraction division procedure, decimal quotient, algorithm, reasonableness.
- Vocabulary: divisor, dividend, quotient, reciprocal, divide, fraction division, measurement division, sharing division, area model, tape diagram, decimal place, estimate.
- Representations and models: number lines, tape diagrams, area models, fraction bars, equations, standard algorithms, estimate-check statements.
- Texts and forms: sharing, packing, recipe, measurement, and rate contexts with diagrams before procedure-heavy items.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need fraction magnitude, fraction multiplication, common denominators, multiplication and division meanings, decimal place value, whole-number division, and unit-rate language.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Division always makes smaller.
- Keep-change-flip is the concept rather than a procedure connected to meaning.
- The reciprocal can be applied to the wrong number.
- Fraction quotients do not need context labels.
- Decimal quotients can be checked by digit pattern instead of place value or estimation.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with division situations involving fractions before any procedure. Separate measurement division ("how many groups?") from sharing division ("how much in each group?"). Then connect visual models to equations and the efficient fraction-division procedure. Close with decimal division and mixed operation checks so students can compute fluently without abandoning reasonableness.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review Unit 1 division meanings, fraction benchmarks, decimal operations, and estimation. Use Unit 3 rate contexts where a quotient has clear units.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares rational-number comparison, algebraic equations involving rational quantities, geometry with fractional dimensions, statistics computations, and financial decisions involving decimals and rates.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify what a fraction-division quotient represents.
- Model fraction division with number lines, tape diagrams, or area models.
- Connect models to equations and efficient procedures.
- Divide whole numbers and decimals accurately.
- Use estimates and units to check exact answers.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Division Situations With Fractions**: Interpret fraction-division stories before computing.
2. **How Many Fraction Groups?**: Use measurement division to find how many fractional groups fit.
3. **Share Fractions Equally**: Use sharing division to find the size of each equal share.
4. **Connect Models To Divide Fractions**: Link diagrams, equations, and the reciprocal procedure.
5. **Decimal Division With Place Value**: Divide decimals while tracking place value and units.
6. **Multi-Digit And Decimal Operation Checks**: Use estimation, inverse operations, and context to verify results.
