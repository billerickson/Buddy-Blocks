# Unit 9: Statistics, Financial Decisions, And Cumulative Modeling

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-06-math/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students reason with data, financial contexts, and cumulative Grade 6 models. By the end of the unit, students should identify statistical questions, describe distributions with center and variability, make simple financial decisions using rates, decimals, percents, and constraints, and complete mixed modeling tasks that require representation choice and explanation.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This final unit belongs at the end because statistics and financial modeling require the whole Grade 6 toolkit: operations, ratios, rates, percents, rational numbers, expressions, equations, inequalities, geometry, and careful interpretation. It also gives a natural cumulative review before Stage 7 QA and later implementation.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: statistical variability, distribution shape, center, spread, outliers, financial constraints, budget tradeoffs, model choice, cumulative transfer.
- Vocabulary: statistical question, variability, distribution, dot plot, histogram, box plot, mean, median, range, interquartile range, mean absolute deviation, cluster, gap, outlier, budget, income, expense, unit price, debit, credit, savings goal, model.
- Representations and models: dot plots, histograms, box plots, summary tables, budget tables, unit-price comparisons, inequalities, equations, ratio tables, coordinate or geometry diagrams when needed.
- Texts and forms: class data questions, compact financial scenarios, worked examples with errors, short capstone prompts, explanation and decision statements.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the full Grade 6 sequence: number operations, ratios, unit rates, percent, fraction division, decimals, rational numbers, coordinate-plane reasoning, expressions, equations, inequalities, geometry, and explanation routines.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Any question with a number is statistical.
- Mean is always the best measure of typical value.
- Spread and outliers do not matter if the centers are close.
- A budget is only arithmetic, not a set of constraints and choices.
- Unit price, percent, and total cost can be compared without attending to units.
- The most recent model is the correct model for every problem.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with statistical questions and variability, then data displays and distribution shape. Next compare mean, median, and outliers, followed by spread measures. Move into financial decisions where rates, percents, decimals, inequalities, and data matter. Then use mixed modeling with data and finance before ending with a compact Grade 6 mastery task.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This unit reviews all earlier units. Ratios, rates, and percents appear in financial and data contexts. Decimal and fraction operations appear in calculations. Rational numbers appear in change, balance, and coordinate contexts. Algebra appears in budget constraints and rules. Geometry appears in cumulative modeling.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Grade 7 proportional reasoning, percent applications, probability, sampling, comparative inference, signed-number operations, multi-step equations, and more independent modeling.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Distinguish statistical and nonstatistical questions.
- Interpret dot plots, histograms, and box plots.
- Choose and explain mean or median in context.
- Describe spread using range, interquartile range, and mean absolute deviation at a Grade 6 level.
- Make financial decisions with unit prices, budgets, percents, income, and expenses.
- Solve cumulative modeling tasks with representation choice, computation, and explanation.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Statistical Questions And Variability**: Decide whether a question anticipates varying data.
2. **Dot Plots, Histograms, And Shape**: Interpret displays and describe distribution shape, clusters, gaps, and peaks.
3. **Mean, Median, And Outliers**: Choose a measure of center and explain how outliers affect it.
4. **Range, IQR, And MAD As Spread**: Use spread measures to describe how data vary.
5. **Budgets, Unit Prices, And Choices**: Make concrete financial decisions with rates, decimals, percents, and constraints.
6. **Mixed Modeling With Data And Finance**: Choose representations and operations for applied data and money scenarios.
7. **Grade 6 Math Mastery Task**: Complete a cumulative task requiring model choice, computation, explanation, and reasonableness checks.
