# Grade 6 Math Course Map

Track: Math
Level: Grade 6
Sources: Accepted research brief at `research/grade-06-math/01-research-brief.md` and level blueprint at `research/grade-06-math/02-level-blueprint.md`

## Course Design Notes

This course map orders Grade 6 Math from number readiness into ratios, rates, fraction and decimal operations, rational numbers, algebra, geometry, statistics, and cumulative application. The sequence is intentionally uneven: ratio reasoning and fraction division need time for models and misconception repair, while later units use compact lessons to connect representations and preserve review. Personal financial literacy is treated as an applied modeling strand that reinforces rates, decimals, percents, data, and reasonableness rather than as a separate economics course.

## Unit 1: Operation Readiness And Number Sense

### Unit Purpose

Stabilize the arithmetic and magnitude knowledge students need for Grade 6 work: decimal place value, whole-number division, fraction magnitude, fraction multiplication readiness, estimation, and reasonableness checks.

### Student Outcomes

- Use place value, estimation, and inverse reasoning to check whole-number and decimal calculations.
- Interpret fractions and decimals by magnitude, benchmark, and number-line location.
- Connect multiplication and division meanings to later fraction division and rate reasoning.
- Choose whether exact calculation or estimation is needed.
- Explain why an answer is reasonable using size, units, or a model.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Place value, benchmark, magnitude, estimate, reasonable, quotient, divisor, dividend, product, factor, multiple, reciprocal readiness, numerator, denominator, decimal place, number line, inverse operation.

### Prerequisites

Students should enter with Grade 5 experience in whole-number and decimal operations, fraction addition/subtraction with unlike denominators, fraction multiplication, unit-fraction division exposure, volume, coordinate grids in the first quadrant, and simple expressions.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit connects Grade 5 arithmetic to every later Grade 6 domain. It prepares ratio scaling, fraction division, decimal fluency, rational-number number lines, algebraic formulas, geometry measurement, and statistics computations.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Treating procedures as digit tricks without considering magnitude.
- Believing division always makes a number smaller.
- Comparing fractions or decimals by surface features rather than value.
- Estimating only after exact calculation.
- Dropping units or labels when checking reasonableness.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

4-6 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Keep operation and magnitude review short and frequent in every unit. Later review should embed calculations in ratio, geometry, data, and financial contexts rather than isolated drills only.

## Unit 2: Ratio Language And Equivalent Ratios

### Unit Purpose

Introduce ratios as multiplicative comparisons that can be described with words, tape diagrams, tables, double number lines, and equations. Students learn to build and interpret equivalent ratios before moving into unit rates or percents.

### Student Outcomes

- Recognize ratio situations and describe what the quantities compare.
- Distinguish part-to-part, part-to-whole, and rate-like comparisons.
- Represent ratios with tape diagrams, tables, double number lines, and equations.
- Generate equivalent ratios by multiplying or dividing both quantities by the same factor.
- Reject additive reasoning when it does not preserve a ratio.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Ratio, for every, to, part-to-part, part-to-whole, equivalent ratio, multiplicative comparison, scale factor readiness, tape diagram, ratio table, double number line, equation.

### Prerequisites

Students need multiplication/division meanings, fraction comparison readiness, table reading, number-line reasoning, and short word-problem interpretation.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit builds from Unit 1 multiplication and fraction magnitude. It prepares Unit 3 rates and percents, later geometry scale contexts, statistics comparisons, and financial modeling.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Thinking a ratio is just a fraction with no context.
- Making equivalent ratios by adding the same number to both quantities.
- Confusing part-to-part and part-to-whole ratios.
- Matching tables by totals instead of multiplicative relationships.
- Assuming order in a ratio does not matter.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

5-7 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Interleave multiplication facts, division meanings, and fraction language while introducing ratios. Revisit equivalent ratios in every later unit where scaling, rate, percent, or comparison appears.

## Unit 3: Unit Rates, Percents, And Applied Rates

### Unit Purpose

Extend ratio reasoning to rates, unit rates, and percent as rate per 100. Students interpret units carefully, use tables and double number lines, and solve concrete applied problems without crossing into later Grade 7 proportional relationship procedures.

### Student Outcomes

- Interpret rates such as miles per hour, dollars per pound, or pages per minute.
- Find and use unit rates while preserving which quantity is per 1.
- Represent percent as a rate per 100 and connect common percents to fractions and decimals.
- Solve percent-of-a-quantity and benchmark percent problems in Grade 6 contexts.
- Use rates and percents in unit price, measurement, and simple financial decisions.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Rate, unit rate, per, per 1, per 100, percent, benchmark percent, quantity, unit price, double number line, ratio table, equation, measurement unit.

### Prerequisites

Students need equivalent ratios, multiplication/division, decimal and fraction benchmarks, table reasoning, and careful unit language.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit depends on Unit 2 ratio structure and prepares fraction/decimal operation applications, financial literacy, statistics comparisons, and Grade 7 proportional relationships without teaching constant of proportionality as a major objective.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Reversing a unit rate, such as dollars per pound versus pounds per dollar.
- Dividing the wrong way because unit labels were ignored.
- Treating percent as always a whole-number count out of exactly 100 objects.
- Solving percent problems by memorized tricks without understanding per 100.
- Thinking the lowest total price is always the best deal regardless of amount.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

5-7 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Review equivalent ratios, benchmark fractions, decimal place value, and estimation. Later units should revisit rates and percent in budgets, geometry measurements, data summaries, and cumulative modeling.

## Unit 4: Fraction Division And Decimal Fluency

### Unit Purpose

Develop conceptual and procedural fluency for fraction division and multi-digit decimal operations. Students connect models, stories, equations, algorithms, and estimates so operations retain meaning.

### Student Outcomes

- Interpret division of fractions through measurement and sharing contexts.
- Use tape diagrams, number lines, and area models to reason about fraction division.
- Connect fraction-division models to equations and efficient procedures.
- Divide multi-digit whole numbers and operate with decimals using standard algorithms.
- Estimate products and quotients to check whether exact answers are reasonable.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Fraction division, divisor, dividend, quotient, reciprocal, measurement division, sharing division, tape diagram, area model, decimal quotient, standard algorithm, estimate, reasonableness.

### Prerequisites

Students need fraction magnitude, multiplication and division meanings, common denominators, fraction multiplication, decimal place value, and whole-number division.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit extends Unit 1 readiness and uses ratio/rate contexts from Units 2-3. It prepares geometry with fractional dimensions, statistics computations, equations involving rational numbers, and financial modeling.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Believing division always makes smaller.
- Treating keep-change-flip as the meaning of fraction division.
- Losing track of what the quotient represents in a story.
- Placing decimals in quotients by visual habit instead of place value.
- Accepting exact answers that conflict with estimates.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

6-8 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Use fraction division in short applied contexts after the unit ends. Space decimal operation review through rate, geometry, statistics, and financial tasks.

## Unit 5: Rational Numbers And The Coordinate Plane

### Unit Purpose

Extend the number system to positive and negative rational numbers, opposites, absolute value, ordering, and all four quadrants of the coordinate plane. Students interpret signs and magnitude in real contexts.

### Student Outcomes

- Interpret positive and negative numbers in contexts such as temperature, elevation, debt, and account balance.
- Locate rational numbers on horizontal and vertical number lines.
- Identify opposites and absolute value as distance from zero.
- Compare and order positive and negative rational numbers.
- Plot and interpret ordered pairs in all four quadrants.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Positive, negative, rational number, opposite, absolute value, distance from zero, greater than, less than, horizontal number line, vertical number line, coordinate plane, x-axis, y-axis, origin, quadrant, ordered pair.

### Prerequisites

Students need number-line reasoning, fraction and decimal magnitude, coordinate plotting in the first quadrant, context interpretation, and comparison symbols.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit depends on earlier magnitude work and prepares coordinate-plane geometry, graph interpretation, variable relationships, and later signed-number operations in Grade 7.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Thinking the negative number with the larger numeral is greater.
- Treating absolute value as "make it positive" instead of distance from zero.
- Confusing opposites with reciprocals.
- Reversing x- and y-coordinates.
- Assuming a point's quadrant does not depend on both signs.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

5-7 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Continue short review of fractions, decimals, and number lines. Revisit coordinate-plane and signed-number ideas in geometry, graphing relationships, and data interpretation.

## Unit 6: Expressions, Equivalence, And Algebraic Language

### Unit Purpose

Move students from arithmetic expressions into algebraic structure. Students interpret variables, evaluate expressions, use exponents with whole-number bases, apply properties, and recognize equivalent expressions.

### Student Outcomes

- Interpret variables as unknown or changing quantities, not labels.
- Write and evaluate expressions from words, tables, or contexts.
- Use order of operations and whole-number exponents in numerical expressions.
- Use properties of operations to generate and identify equivalent expressions.
- Explain what an expression means in context.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Numerical expression, algebraic expression, variable, coefficient readiness, term, factor, exponent, base, evaluate, substitute, order of operations, equivalent expressions, property, distribute.

### Prerequisites

Students need operation fluency, equation familiarity, multiplication as groups, area model reasoning, and attention to mathematical language.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit uses operations and ratio contexts from earlier units. It prepares equations, inequalities, variable relationships, geometry formulas, and data/financial modeling.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Reading a variable as a unit label, such as `5m` meaning five meters only.
- Evaluating expressions from left to right without order of operations.
- Treating exponents as multiplication by the exponent.
- Assuming expressions are equivalent because they look similar.
- Distributing or combining terms without preserving value.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

5-7 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Review arithmetic facts, fraction/decimal substitution, and ratio meanings in expression contexts. Later units should retrieve expression evaluation inside formulas, equations, and relationships.

## Unit 7: Equations, Inequalities, And Variable Relationships

### Unit Purpose

Use equations, inequalities, and variable relationships to represent contexts and solution meanings. Students solve one-variable equations and inequalities within Grade 6 expectations and connect dependent/independent variables across tables, graphs, equations, and verbal rules.

### Student Outcomes

- Solve one-variable equations by reasoning about inverse operations and equality.
- Check whether a value is a solution to an equation.
- Interpret inequalities and solution sets in context.
- Graph simple inequalities on a number line.
- Identify dependent and independent variables and connect tables, graphs, equations, and verbal rules.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Equation, solution, solve, inverse operation, equality, inequality, solution set, greater than, less than, at least, at most, number-line graph, independent variable, dependent variable, table, graph, rule.

### Prerequisites

Students need expression evaluation, variables, operation meanings, number-line reasoning, and coordinate-plane plotting.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit builds on Unit 6 expressions and Unit 5 number lines. It prepares data modeling, geometry formulas, financial constraints, and Grade 7 equation and proportional relationship work.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Thinking the equals sign means "calculate the left side."
- Treating solving as moving symbols without preserving equality.
- Believing an inequality has one answer.
- Reversing "at least" and "at most."
- Swapping dependent and independent variables in tables or graphs.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

5-7 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Interleave expression evaluation, rational-number order, and coordinate plotting. Later geometry, statistics, and finance lessons should use equations or inequalities as concise models.

## Unit 8: Geometry, Measurement, And Coordinate Figures

### Unit Purpose

Apply area, volume, surface area, nets, coordinate-plane reasoning, formulas, and unit analysis to Grade 6 geometry problems. Students distinguish dimensions and choose the correct measure for a task.

### Student Outcomes

- Find area of triangles, parallelograms, and polygons by decomposing or using formulas.
- Find volume of rectangular prisms with whole-number or fractional edge lengths.
- Use nets to reason about surface area of rectangular prisms.
- Solve coordinate-plane polygon problems using distances parallel to axes.
- Distinguish area, surface area, volume, and perimeter by units and meaning.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Area, square units, base, height, triangle, parallelogram, polygon, decompose, compose, coordinate polygon, volume, cubic units, rectangular prism, fractional edge length, net, surface area, face, formula.

### Prerequisites

Students need multiplication, fraction multiplication/division readiness, expression evaluation, coordinate plotting in all four quadrants, and Grade 5 area/volume foundations.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit uses rational numbers, expressions, equations, and operation fluency from prior units. It prepares cumulative modeling and Grade 7 geometry with scale, circles, and more complex surface area/volume.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Confusing area, surface area, volume, and perimeter.
- Using slanted side length as triangle or parallelogram height.
- Applying a formula without matching units or dimensions.
- Counting only visible faces in a net or solid.
- Finding coordinate distances by adding coordinates rather than measuring horizontal or vertical distance.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

5-7 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Review fraction multiplication, decimal operations, coordinate signs, and expression evaluation in geometry tasks. Revisit ratio and unit rate when measurement units appear.

## Unit 9: Statistics, Financial Decisions, And Cumulative Modeling

### Unit Purpose

Develop data reasoning and end-of-level transfer. Students distinguish statistical questions, analyze distributions with center and variability, make simple financial decisions using rates, decimals, percents, and data, and complete cumulative modeling tasks.

### Student Outcomes

- Decide whether a question is statistical by checking whether it anticipates variability.
- Represent and interpret data with dot plots, histograms, box plots, and summary measures.
- Describe distributions using center, spread, shape, clusters, gaps, and outliers.
- Compare mean and median and choose a useful measure for a context.
- Make simple financial decisions involving income, expenses, budgets, unit prices, debit/credit choices, and savings goals.
- Solve cumulative modeling tasks that interleave major Grade 6 domains.

### Key Concepts And Vocabulary

Statistical question, variability, distribution, dot plot, histogram, box plot, mean, median, range, interquartile range, mean absolute deviation, cluster, gap, outlier, budget, income, expense, unit price, debit, credit, savings goal, model, reasonableness.

### Prerequisites

Students need the full Grade 6 toolkit: ratios, rates, percents, decimal and fraction operations, rational-number interpretation, expressions, equations, inequalities, geometry, coordinate reasoning, and explanation routines.

### Connections To Prior And Later Units

This unit uses every earlier unit and prepares Grade 7 proportional reasoning, probability, sampling, comparative inference, percent applications, signed-number fluency, and more advanced modeling.

### Misconceptions To Address

- Thinking any question with numbers is statistical.
- Choosing mean as the best typical value in every situation.
- Ignoring spread, outliers, or shape when comparing data.
- Treating a budget as a list of numbers without constraints or tradeoffs.
- Choosing a model by recent habit rather than problem structure.

### Suggested Lesson Count Range

6-8 lessons.

### Review, Spacing, Or Interleaving Notes

Use this unit as a cumulative review cycle. Items should require students to decide which representation, operation, or measure fits, then explain why the result makes sense in context.
