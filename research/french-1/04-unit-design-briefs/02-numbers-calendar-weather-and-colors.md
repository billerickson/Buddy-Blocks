# Unit 2: Numbers, Calendar, Weather, And Colors

Track: French  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/french-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students build high-reuse language for numbers, age, dates, weather, and colors. By the end of the unit, they should understand and produce short phrases that can recur in daily classroom routines and later personal, school, and food contexts.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Numbers, calendar, weather, and colors are frequent, concrete, and easy to recycle. Placing them early gives later units useful raw material for age, birthdays, descriptions, quantities, schedules, prices, and simple opinions.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: number recognition and production, age as avoir, date and birthday phrasing, weather expressions, noun-color phrases.
- Vocabulary and forms: numbers 0-31 and tens to 100 as exposure; Quel âge as-tu? J'ai ___ ans; days, months, aujourd'hui, demain, hier; Quelle est la date? Mon anniversaire est...; il fait beau/froid/chaud/mauvais; il pleut; il neige; colors.
- Representations and texts: number cards, birthday/calendar cards, weather icons, color-object labels, short weather reports.
- Forms: C'est lundi; aujourd'hui c'est...; j'ai ___ ans; le cahier bleu; la trousse rouge.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 1 greetings, classroom phrases, listening habits, and willingness to repeat short phrases. They bring English concepts for numbers, calendar, weather, and color.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Age should use être because English says "I am twelve."
- A number that sounds close is close enough.
- Weather phrases translate word for word from English.
- French colors always come before nouns like many English adjectives.
- The same color form fits every noun without attention to gender or number.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit opens with numbers because age and dates depend on them. Dates and birthdays then put numbers into a personal and calendar frame. Weather follows as a short daily routine that can be reviewed often. Colors come last because they introduce noun-adjective order and agreement exposure after students already know a few nouns.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Each lesson reuses greetings and classroom phrases from Unit 1. Students answer short routine questions after greeting: name, age, date, weather, or color choice.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Numbers support age in Unit 3, family ages in Unit 4, school schedules in Unit 6, and prices in Unit 7. Colors prepare adjective placement and agreement for family descriptions and school-object descriptions.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match number words to numerals and type controlled number answers.
- Use j'ai ___ ans for age.
- Interpret and build simple date and birthday phrases.
- Match weather phrases to meanings or icons.
- Use a color with a familiar noun while noticing article and agreement clues.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Numbers For Age And Classroom Counts**: Recognize and use numbers in age and quantity phrases.
2. **Dates, Days, And Birthdays**: Use days, months, dates, and birthday phrases in short contexts.
3. **Weather Windows**: Interpret and produce common weather phrases.
4. **Colors With Nouns**: Use colors in simple noun phrases and notice adjective order and agreement.
