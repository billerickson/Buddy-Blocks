# Unit 5: Likes, Activities, And Opinions

Track: French  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/french-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students express likes, dislikes, favorite activities, and simple reasons using high-frequency preference chunks. They learn ne...pas as a useful negation frame and practice answering preference questions in short interpersonal exchanges.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Students can now identify themselves and describe people. Preferences give them a more personal reason to communicate and introduce activity chunks without requiring a full verb-system survey.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: preference, intensity, negation, activity chunks, simple reasons, short opinion exchange.
- Vocabulary and forms: j'aime, j'adore, je n'aime pas, je déteste; jouer au foot, jouer aux jeux vidéo, écouter de la musique, regarder un film, lire, chanter, danser, étudier, parler français; Qu'est-ce que tu aimes? Est-ce que tu aimes...? Pourquoi? parce que; et, mais; amusant, intéressant, difficile, super.
- Representations and texts: activity cards, preference scales, mini-dialogues, simple survey results, cloze preference paragraphs.
- Forms: j'aime + infinitive chunk; ne...pas around the verb; parce que + short reason.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need question-answer routines, j'aime exposure from earlier communication if any, personal identity phrases, être chunks for simple adjectives, and enough vocabulary confidence to work with short activity phrases.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- J'aime translates word-for-word as "I like to" in every part.
- Pas alone is enough for the target negation frame in controlled writing.
- Activity chunks after j'aime should be conjugated.
- Est-ce que questions require a long answer.
- Parce que must introduce a complex sentence.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit opens with positive preference chunks to build confidence. It then introduces negation as a frame around the verb. The third lesson teaches preference questions and short answers. The final lesson adds simple reasons and connectors so students can say more without overloading grammar.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students reuse greetings, question words, tu/vous in informal preference questions, adjectives from people descriptions, and pronunciation patterns in activity words.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Preferences transfer directly to school subjects, food, places, weather, and routines. Negation and parce que return in school mini-dialogues and capstone production.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match activity chunks to meanings.
- State likes and strong likes with appropriate chunks.
- Use je n'aime pas as a complete negation frame.
- Choose or build fitting preference questions and answers.
- Add a simple reason with parce que.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **J'aime And J'adore Activity Chunks**: State positive preferences with familiar activity phrases.
2. **Je N'aime Pas: Negation As A Frame**: Use ne...pas to make controlled negative preference statements.
3. **Asking About Preferences**: Ask and answer short questions about likes and dislikes.
4. **Simple Reasons With Parce Que**: Add brief reasons and connectors to preference statements.
