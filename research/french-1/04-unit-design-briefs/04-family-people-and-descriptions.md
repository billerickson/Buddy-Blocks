# Unit 4: Family, People, And Descriptions

Track: French  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/french-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students identify family members and familiar people, use articles and possession chunks, describe people simply, and interpret short descriptions. The unit makes gender, number, and agreement visible through meaning.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Students have already introduced themselves. The next natural communication step is talking about people around them. This unit also gives articles, adjective agreement, and il y a a concrete reason to matter before those forms return with objects, food, places, and routines.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: family and people nouns, articles, grammatical gender, possession chunks, existence with il y a, adjective agreement and placement.
- Vocabulary and forms: la famille, la mère, le père, les parents, le frère, la soeur, le grand-père, la grand-mère, un ami, une amie; un, une, le, la, l', les; mon, ma, mes; il y a; grand/grande, petit/petite, brun/brune, blond/blonde, sympa, drôle, intelligent/intelligente.
- Representations and texts: family trees, people cards, description labels, short family profiles.
- Forms: C'est mon frère; Il y a trois personnes; Ma soeur est drôle; Mon ami est grand.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need self-introduction language, être and avoir chunks, age numbers, question words, color-adjective exposure, and the idea that articles carry information.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Gender in grammar is the same as biological gender.
- Articles can be omitted whenever English omits them.
- Mon/ma depends on the speaker instead of the noun that follows.
- Adjectives never change form.
- Il y a means "he has" because il and a appear inside it.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit starts with family nouns and articles so students hear nouns with their common companions. It then uses il y a to describe who is present. The third lesson focuses on adjective descriptions and agreement. The final lesson adds mon, ma, and mes as practical possession chunks that also reinforce noun gender and number.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students reuse numbers for age and family counts, identity phrases for people, question words for who/what questions, and colors as familiar adjective examples.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares school-object and food nouns with articles, location descriptions with il y a, preference statements about people and activities, and cumulative self/family profile tasks.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match family nouns with accurate meanings and articles.
- Use il y a to describe people or things present.
- Choose adjective forms that match familiar nouns.
- Use mon, ma, and mes with controlled nouns.
- Interpret a short family or friend description.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Family Words Need Articles**: Learn family nouns with le, la, l', un, une, and les.
2. **Il Y A In My Family And World**: Use il y a to say who or what is present.
3. **Describing People With Matching Adjectives**: Notice adjective placement and agreement in simple descriptions.
4. **My, Your? Mon, Ma, Mes In Context**: Use possession chunks with familiar people nouns.
