# Unit 6: School, Objects, And Everyday Places

Track: French  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/french-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students communicate about school objects, subjects, classroom locations, and everyday places. They reuse articles, colors, il y a, preferences, and question words while adding basic prepositions and controlled aller/faire chunks.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

School is a familiar context where earlier language can be recombined naturally. Students can now talk about what they have, where things are, what they like, where they go, and what they do, without needing advanced grammar.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: school nouns with articles, subject preferences, classroom location, existence vs location, going to places, school routine chunks.
- Vocabulary and forms: le stylo, le crayon, le cahier, le livre, le sac, la trousse, la classe, l'école, les devoirs; le français, les maths, l'histoire, les sciences, l'art, la musique; dans, sur, sous, devant, derrière, à côté de; la bibliothèque, le café, le parc, le cinéma, le musée; je vais à..., je fais mes devoirs, je fais du sport.
- Representations and texts: classroom maps, backpack inventories, school schedules, town-place cards, short school dialogues.
- Forms: Il y a un livre; Le stylo est dans le sac; J'aime les sciences; Je vais à la bibliothèque.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need articles, colors, il y a, j'aime/je n'aime pas, question words, numbers, and basic adjective awareness.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- School nouns can be used without articles.
- Il y a and est are interchangeable.
- Prepositions can be ignored because the object words are familiar.
- Je vais means a complete idea without a destination in these tasks.
- À always maps one-to-one to English "to" or "at."

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit begins with school objects and subjects so students can reuse articles and likes. It then moves to il y a and location prepositions in a classroom context. The third lesson expands outward to places using je vais. The final lesson combines objects, subjects, places, likes, and politeness in short school mini-dialogues.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students reuse colors from Unit 2, articles and il y a from Unit 4, preferences and negation from Unit 5, and tu/vous from Units 1 and 3.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Places and polite mini-dialogues prepare food-ordering and routine contexts in Unit 7. The distinction between existence, location, liking, and going supports the capstone's mixed interpretation tasks.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify school objects and subjects with articles.
- Use il y a for classroom inventory and est with location phrases.
- Match prepositions to simple visual or textual locations.
- Use je vais à... with familiar places.
- Build short school dialogues with appropriate questions and replies.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Classroom Objects And School Subjects**: Use articles with school nouns and state simple school preferences.
2. **Il Y A And Where Things Are**: Distinguish what exists from where it is located.
3. **Going Places With Je Vais**: Use je vais à... with school and town destinations.
4. **School Mini-Dialogues**: Combine school vocabulary, preferences, places, and polite exchanges.
