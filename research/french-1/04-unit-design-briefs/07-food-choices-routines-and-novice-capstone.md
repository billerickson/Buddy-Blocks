# Unit 7: Food, Choices, Routines, And Novice Capstone

Track: French  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/french-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use French to make simple food choices, request politely, describe a small routine, interpret short cultural and everyday texts, and complete a cumulative novice communication task.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Food, choices, and routines are familiar, communicative, and naturally cumulative. They let students reuse politeness, numbers, articles, likes, places, question words, and basic verb chunks while staying within novice limits.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: polite request, want/prefer, price and quantity, routine chunks, cultural comparison, cumulative novice communication.
- Vocabulary and forms: le pain, le fromage, le croissant, le sandwich, la pomme, la salade, l'eau, le jus, le chocolat chaud; je voudrais, je veux, s'il vous plaît, c'est combien?; le matin, l'après-midi, le soir; je vais, je fais, j'aime, je mange, je bois as formulaic exposure.
- Representations and texts: café menus, food cards, price labels, routine strips, short place/food dialogues, capstone profile and exchange.
- Cultural content: francophone places and everyday practices presented through short respectful comparison prompts, not trivia lists.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need greetings, politeness, numbers, articles, likes/dislikes, school and place language, je vais and je fais chunks, question words, and basic pronunciation habits.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Je veux and je voudrais have the same pragmatic effect in every setting.
- Food articles should become a full partitive grammar system in this level.
- C'est combien? can be answered without listening for the number.
- Culture learning means memorizing facts about France only.
- Capstone output should be long, even if accuracy and comprehensibility suffer.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit begins with foods and polite requests because students can immediately reuse articles and politeness. It then adds prices and choices with numbers. The third lesson uses familiar verb chunks to describe simple routines. The final capstone lesson mixes interpretive, interpersonal, and presentational tasks across the level.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson reviews greetings, politeness, numbers, articles, question words, likes, places, and pronunciation patterns. The capstone deliberately revisits identity, family, school, food, weather, routines, and culture in short tasks.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This is the final French 1 unit. It prepares French 2 by showing students how to recombine known chunks, read short contextualized texts, participate in controlled exchanges, and produce connected novice sentences without needing full grammar mastery.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify common food and drink words with articles.
- Use je voudrais for polite requests and interpret c'est combien?
- Choose appropriate food or place replies in short exchanges.
- Describe a simple routine with familiar chunks and time-of-day phrases.
- Complete a cumulative novice task with interpretive, interpersonal, and presentational evidence.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Foods, Drinks, And Polite Requests**: Use food vocabulary with articles and polite request language.
2. **Je Voudrais, Prices, And Choices**: Combine wanting, numbers, and polite choice-making.
3. **Simple Routines With Familiar Chunks**: Describe a daily routine with known present-tense chunks.
4. **French 1 Capstone Exchange**: Recombine familiar language in a compact interpretive, interpersonal, and presentational task.
