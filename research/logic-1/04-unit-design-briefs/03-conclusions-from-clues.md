# Unit 3: Conclusions From Clues

Track: Logic  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/logic-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students practice drawing conclusions that stay within the information given. By the end of the unit, they can identify known facts, distinguish supported conclusions from guesses, use clues in short passages, and explain which clue makes a conclusion follow.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit belongs after relevance because students need to choose useful information before they can infer from it. The work shifts from evaluating someone else's support to controlling their own conclusions.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: given information, clues, supported conclusions, unsupported guesses, text evidence.
- Vocabulary: conclusion, clue, given, follow, support, guess, infer, know, cannot know yet, evidence.
- Representations and models: "we know / we cannot know" charts, clue-to-conclusion arrows, short evidence tables, conclusion choice frames.
- Texts and forms: two- to four-sentence passages, simple classroom scenarios, small tables, short rules, and sentence frames such as "This follows because ___."

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 1 argument-part language and Unit 2 relevance judgment. They should be able to read a short passage and answer literal questions about details.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A possible answer is the same as a supported conclusion.
- The most interesting idea is the best conclusion.
- Real-world knowledge can replace the information in the prompt.
- One clue supports every related conclusion.
- "Cannot know yet" means "probably false."

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with identifying the information actually given. Then contrast supported conclusions with guesses. The third lesson applies the routine to short passages, and the fourth lesson requires students to name the clue that supports a conclusion so their reasoning is visible.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Lessons should continue asking which information is relevant to the claim or question. Claim/reason language can appear in passage prompts where a character makes a small argument and the student must decide what follows.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 4 by establishing the general "what follows?" habit needed for if-then rules, cause/effect, and category statements. It also prepares Unit 6 reading transfer tasks.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify given facts in a short context.
- Select the supported conclusion from several choices.
- Reject a guess that adds information not given.
- Use passage clues to answer a reasoning question.
- Explain the clue that supports a conclusion.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **What Information Is Given?**: Separate stated facts from outside ideas.
2. **Supported Conclusion Or Guess?**: Choose conclusions that follow from the information.
3. **Conclusions From Short Passages**: Use passage clues for simple inference.
4. **Explain The Clue**: Name the clue that makes a conclusion reasonable.
