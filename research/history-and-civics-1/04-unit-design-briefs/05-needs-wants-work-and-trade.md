# Unit 5: Needs, Wants, Work, And Trade

Track: History And Civics
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/history-and-civics-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn basic economic ideas as tools for understanding daily life and community choices. By the end of the unit, students can distinguish needs and wants, identify goods and services, explain producer/consumer roles, and describe simple trade, money, scarcity, and choice scenarios.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows civic life because communities make decisions about needs, resources, work, and trade. It also prepares students to understand daily life and culture as practical as well as symbolic.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: needs, wants, scarcity, goods, services, production, consumption, work, trade, money, choice, cost/benefit at an introductory level.
- Vocabulary: need, want, good, service, producer, consumer, work, trade, money, scarcity, resource, choice, cost, benefit.
- Representations and models: needs/wants sort, goods/services sort, producer-consumer pairs, trade scenario cards, choice charts.
- Texts and forms: short market, family, school, and historical daily-life scenarios.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need sorting skills, short scenario reading, community vocabulary, and a basic understanding that people use resources to meet needs and wants.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Wants are bad or unimportant because they are not needs.
- Scarcity means there is none of something.
- Producers only make objects, not services.
- Money and trade mean exactly the same thing.
- People in the past made choices without limits.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with needs, wants, and scarcity because scarcity explains why choices exist. Then sort goods, services, producers, and consumers so students can name economic roles. End with trade, money, and choices so students apply vocabulary in daily-life and historical contexts.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review community problems from Unit 4, source details from Unit 3, maps for trade and movement from Unit 2, and timeline language when comparing economic choices over time.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares culture and daily-life comparisons, historical stories about work and invention, symbols/documents tied to community values, and capstone inquiry tasks involving choices and evidence.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Distinguish needs and wants with context.
- Explain scarcity as limited resources, not total absence.
- Classify goods and services.
- Identify producers and consumers.
- Explain trade or money as a way people exchange goods and services.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Needs, Wants, And Scarcity**: Explain why people make choices when resources are limited.
2. **Goods, Services, Producers, And Consumers**: Identify what is exchanged and the roles people play in economic life.
3. **Trade, Money, And Choices**: Describe how people exchange and decide among options in simple community or historical scenarios.
