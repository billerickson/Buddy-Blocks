# Unit 8: Inquiry Capstone: Claims With Evidence

Track: History And Civics
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/history-and-civics-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students combine the level's tools in short inquiry tasks. By the end of the unit, students can ask or answer a focused historical or civic question, use a timeline, map, and source together, and make a simple claim supported by relevant evidence.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes last because it requires cumulative transfer. Students must use time, place, sources, civics, economics, culture, and respectful explanation together rather than as separate vocabulary topics.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: inquiry, focused question, claim, relevant evidence, source set, conclusion, context check.
- Vocabulary: inquiry, question, claim, evidence, source, timeline, map, cause, effect, change, continuity, place, community, choice, responsibility, conclusion.
- Representations and models: mini source packets, timeline-map-source triads, claim/evidence tables, checklist for time-place-source fit.
- Texts and forms: one- or two-source inquiry prompts, selected evidence explanations, short constructed responses.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need all prior unit knowledge: time sequence, map and place vocabulary, source details, civic/economic concepts, cultural respect, symbols/documents, and concise evidence sentence frames.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A claim can be supported by any detail.
- Evidence may be invented when a source is present.
- Maps and timelines are extra decoration.
- A longer answer is always stronger.
- One source can answer every question.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin by asking and choosing focused inquiry questions so students know what kind of evidence is needed. Then integrate timeline, map, and source evidence. End with claim writing and evidence checking, which is the mastery target for the level.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This unit intentionally reviews every earlier unit. It retrieves time words, map tools, source evidence, civic roles, economic choices, cultural respect, and symbol/document meaning through mixed tasks.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares later History And Civics levels by establishing the inquiry arc in miniature: focused question, disciplinary tools, evidence, claim, and conclusion.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose or write a focused inquiry question.
- Identify which source, map, or timeline detail is relevant.
- Combine timeline, map, and source information.
- Make a claim that answers the question.
- Support the claim with one clear piece of evidence.
- Check whether the answer respects time, place, source details, and context.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Ask A Focused Inquiry Question**: Distinguish broad curiosity from a question that can be answered with available sources.
2. **Use Timeline, Map, And Source Together**: Combine multiple tools to answer one historical or civic question.
3. **Make A Claim With Evidence**: Write or choose a clear claim and support it with relevant evidence.
