# Unit 3: Word Relationships And Precision

Track: Vocabulary
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students deepen semantic reasoning by sorting words, identifying relationships, comparing near-synonyms, and choosing precise words in context. By the end of the unit, students should explain how related words are alike, different, and useful in particular sentences.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

After students can infer and verify meanings, they are ready to compare meanings. This unit strengthens the semantic precision students need before morphology, multiple meanings, academic words, and written sentence use become more demanding.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: category membership, synonymy, antonymy, contrast, shades of meaning, intensity, word choice, revision.
- Vocabulary: synonym, antonym, category, member, related word, shade of meaning, precise, vague, stronger, milder, intensity, feeling, formal.
- Representations and models: category maps, word sorts, synonym pairs in sentences, antonym contrast charts, intensity scales, sentence-revision frames.
- Texts and forms: short sentence contexts, classroom directions, simple story sentences, revision prompts, oral and written explanation frames.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need common category language, Grade 2 synonym and antonym readiness, Unit 1 context habits, and Unit 2 reference awareness when a word meaning is uncertain.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Synonyms always mean exactly the same thing.
- Antonym means any word that is not the target word.
- The strongest word is always the best word.
- Words belong together if they look or sound alike.
- Precise word choice means using the longest or hardest word.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with categories because grouping words by meaning is concrete and supports later word-family work. Then review synonyms and antonyms in context, not as isolated pairs. Move to shades of meaning so students compare intensity and feeling. End with sentence revision, where precision becomes an expressive skill rather than a recognition task.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Context clues remain active because synonym and antonym choices must fit the sentence. Reference checks can appear when two words are close but one is not precise enough. Students should continue explaining the clue or sentence fit behind each choice.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 4 word families, Unit 5 multiple-meaning decisions, Unit 6 nonliteral phrase interpretation, and Unit 7 academic vocabulary where direction words have precise meanings.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Sort words by category and identify an outlier.
- Match synonyms or antonyms when the sentence supports the relationship.
- Choose among near-synonyms based on intensity or feeling.
- Revise a vague word into a more precise word.
- Explain why one related word fits better than another.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Sort Words Into Categories**: Group words by shared meaning and reject look-alike or sound-alike distractors.
2. **Synonyms In Context**: Choose same-or-similar meaning words that fit a sentence.
3. **Antonyms And Contrasts**: Use opposite and contrasting meanings to understand sentences.
4. **Shades Of Meaning**: Order and choose words by strength, feeling, or precision.
5. **Revise Vague Words**: Replace broad words with clearer words in short sentences.
