# Unit 5: Multiple Meanings, Homophones, And Homographs

Track: Vocabulary
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students resolve words that share spelling, sound, or form but differ in meaning. By the end of the unit, students should use context, spelling, pronunciation clues when available, and reference entries to choose the intended meaning.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows context, reference, semantic precision, and word parts because confusing word forms require all four habits. Students are now ready to see that a word's meaning is not fixed by spelling or sound alone.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: context-specific meaning, same spelling/different meaning, same sound/different spelling, pronunciation shift, definition fit.
- Vocabulary: multiple meaning, homophone, homograph, spelling, sound, pronounce, context, definition, meaning fit, sentence clue, entry.
- Representations and models: two-meaning sentence pairs, homophone choice frames, simplified multi-definition entries, meaning-fit charts.
- Texts and forms: short sentences, dialogue lines, informational snippets, entry comparison tasks, one-sentence explanations.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 1 context reasoning, Unit 2 reference-entry habits, Unit 3 precision, and common Grade 2 exposure to words such as bark, light, right, see, and here.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A word has one real meaning.
- The familiar meaning is always correct.
- Sound-alike words can be swapped freely.
- Spelling is unimportant if the word sounds right.
- Homographs always sound the same.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with multiple-meaning words because they rely on context and definition fit. Move to homophones, where spelling and meaning both matter. Then introduce homographs in accessible cases. End with reference confirmation so students practice selecting the fitting entry meaning after using context.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Each lesson reviews Unit 1 context clues and Unit 2 definition fit. Unit 3 precision helps students reject near-fit meanings. Unit 4 word parts can be mentioned only when helpful; this unit should not turn into morphology practice.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 6 nonliteral language by showing that literal word meanings may not be enough. It prepares Unit 7 content vocabulary where common words such as table, map, scale, and measure take subject-specific meanings.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose the correct meaning of a multiple-meaning word in context.
- Select the correct homophone for a sentence.
- Interpret common homographs using context.
- Match definitions to context sentences.
- Explain why a wrong meaning or sound-alike word does not fit.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Multiple-Meaning Words In Context**: Use sentence clues to pick the intended meaning.
2. **Homophones Need Sentence Clues**: Choose the spelling and meaning that fit the sentence.
3. **Homographs Can Shift Meaning**: Use context to interpret same-spelling words with different meanings.
4. **Use Entries To Confirm Word Meanings**: Verify confusing word meanings with simplified reference entries.
