# Unit 8: Cumulative Word-Solving Transfer

Track: Vocabulary
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students bring the full level together by solving unfamiliar words in mixed contexts. By the end of the unit, students should choose an effective strategy, combine evidence when needed, explain the clue or word part that supports their answer, and use vocabulary precisely in a short response.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

The final unit belongs after all major strategy clusters have been taught. It prevents students from treating vocabulary as separate tricks and instead asks them to decide what kind of evidence a new word or phrase calls for.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: strategy choice, mixed review, evidence, transfer, meaning fit, explanation, precise production.
- Vocabulary: strategy, routine, clue, context, word part, reference, definition, fit, evidence, precise, multiple meaning, nonliteral, academic word, transfer.
- Representations and models: mixed-strategy charts, clue/word-part/reference comparison tables, short passage tasks, revision frames.
- Texts and forms: literary snippets, informational passages, classroom directions, content-area captions, short constructed responses, sentence revisions.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need all prior units: context clues, reference tools, word relationships, word parts, multiple meanings, nonliteral language, and academic/content vocabulary. They should also be comfortable with short explanation frames.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Use the most recent strategy for every word.
- A word part is enough evidence even when the sentence disagrees.
- A dictionary definition is correct even if it does not fit the context.
- Explaining the clue is optional.
- A sentence with a new word is successful just because it includes the word.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with explicit strategy choice so students compare when context, word parts, or references help most. Then combine evidence sources in cases where one clue is not enough. Move to explaining evidence because it is the heart of mastery. End with a cumulative transfer lesson using mixed contexts and short vocabulary production.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson reviews multiple prior units. Context, references, word parts, precision, multiple meanings, nonliteral phrases, and academic words should be interleaved rather than blocked.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Grade 4 by strengthening flexible strategy transfer. Students will be better prepared for longer paragraphs, more complex affixes, stronger reference skills, idioms/adages/proverbs, and more formal academic discussion.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose a fitting word-solving strategy for an unfamiliar word.
- Combine context, word parts, and reference information.
- Explain evidence for a meaning choice.
- Revise sentences for precise vocabulary use.
- Demonstrate mixed end-of-level vocabulary transfer.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Choose The Best Word-Solving Strategy**: Decide whether context, word parts, references, or another strategy should lead.
2. **Combine Clues, Word Parts, And References**: Use more than one evidence source when a word is challenging.
3. **Explain Your Evidence**: Give a short explanation that connects the meaning to the clue, word part, or reference.
4. **Cumulative Vocabulary Transfer**: Solve and use vocabulary in mixed new contexts from across the level.
