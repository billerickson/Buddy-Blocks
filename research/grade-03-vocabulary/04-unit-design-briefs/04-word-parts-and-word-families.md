# Unit 4: Word Parts And Word Families

Track: Vocabulary
Level: Grade 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grade-03-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use common prefixes, suffixes, base words, known roots, and word families to infer meaning while checking context. By the end of the unit, students should explain what a meaningful word part contributes and avoid false word-part guesses.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Morphology comes after context, reference, and semantic relationships because word parts are powerful but easy to overapply. Students now have enough meaning-fit habits to use morphology as evidence rather than as a trick.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: base word, affix meaning, word-family meaning, root as shared meaning, context check, false word parts.
- Vocabulary: base word, root, prefix, suffix, affix, word part, word family, syllable, meaning, `un-`, `re-`, `pre-`, `mis-`, `-ful`, `-less`, `-ly`, `-er`, `-est`, `-ness`.
- Representations and models: word-part boxes, word sums, family webs, base-plus-affix cards, sentence context checks.
- Texts and forms: short sentences with target words, paired examples and nonexamples, explanatory stems such as "The prefix ___ means ___, so..."

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need familiarity with compound words and common endings, Unit 1 context clues, Unit 2 reference checks, and Unit 3 related-word reasoning.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Every word beginning is a prefix.
- Words that look alike always share meaning.
- The prefix or suffix alone gives the whole meaning.
- Suffixes all do the same job.
- If a word has a word part, context no longer matters.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with base words and meaningful parts so students know what they are looking for. Teach prefixes and suffixes separately because they raise different misconceptions. Then broaden to word families and known roots. End with a context-check lesson that deliberately includes false or incomplete word-part guesses.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students should use context before and after each word-part inference. Semantic precision from Unit 3 supports comparing related words such as help, helpful, helpless, and helper. Reference checks can confirm a word part when the sentence is not enough.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 7 academic/content vocabulary by giving students tools for words such as reread, unknown, prediction, careful, careless, classify, and observation. It also prepares Grade 4-5 work with more complex Greek and Latin affixes and roots.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify base words in familiar derived words.
- Explain common prefix meanings.
- Explain common suffix meanings or uses.
- Group related words by shared meaning.
- Check a word-part guess against context and reject false matches.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Base Words And Meaning Parts**: Identify the meaningful base in familiar words.
2. **Prefixes That Change Meaning**: Use common prefixes to infer meaning.
3. **Suffixes That Change Meaning Or Use**: Use common suffixes to infer meaning or word use.
4. **Word Families And Known Roots**: Connect related words by shared base or root meaning.
5. **Check Word Parts With Context**: Test morphology guesses against the sentence.
