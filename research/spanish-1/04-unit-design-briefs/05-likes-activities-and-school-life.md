# Unit 5: Likes, Activities, And School Life

Track: Spanish  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/spanish-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students talk about preferences, activities, school subjects, schedules, and simple school actions. They use `me gusta`, `me gustan`, `no me gusta`, `quiero`, connectors, opinions, and selected present-tense chunks to communicate about school and free time.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

After students can introduce and describe people, they are ready to say what they like and what they do. School and activity language is personal, high frequency, and useful for building longer novice messages without requiring full verb paradigms.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: preference, singular/plural liking, activity as infinitive, opinion, schedule sequence, limited present-tense action.
- Vocabulary: activities, school subjects, schedule words, opinion adjectives, connectors.
- Forms: `me gusta`, `me gustan`, `no me gusta`, `¿Que te gusta?`, `quiero`, `tambien`, `pero`, `primero`, `despues`, `estudio`, `hablo`, `leo`, `escribo`, `practico`.
- Representations and texts: preference surveys, class schedules, school ID cards, short texts about a school day, mini interviews.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 4 identity and description language, numbers, days, time, adjective agreement, question-response routines, and readiness to learn formulaic chunks before full grammar analysis.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- `Me gusta` is a word-for-word match for "I like."
- `Me gustan` is unnecessary for plural nouns.
- Infinitives and conjugated forms are interchangeable.
- School-subject opinions do not need agreement.
- Every verb should be learned as a full chart before being used.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit starts with one clear liking pattern, then adds plural and negative forms. Activity and opinion language follows so students can say more than one preference. School schedules give a reading and time context for the language. The unit closes with mixed school and free-time questions so students practice short interpersonal exchanges.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Lessons revisit identity, age, days, time, articles, adjectives, and `ser` descriptions. Students use numbers and calendar language in schedules and reuse family/person descriptions in preference comparisons.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Preference language prepares food choices, place choices, and capstone opinions. Schedule and activity language prepare simple routines. Connectors and question words support longer novice messages in Unit 6.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Use `me gusta` for one noun or activity.
- Use `me gustan` for plural liked things.
- Express dislikes with `no me gusta`.
- Interpret school subjects, opinions, days, and times in schedules.
- Use selected action chunks in first-person school contexts.
- Build or answer short preference and school-life dialogues.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Me Gusta For One Like**: Use `me gusta` with one activity or singular noun.
2. **Me Gustan And No Me Gusta**: Express plural likes and dislikes with clear cues.
3. **Activities And Opinions**: Combine activity words, opinion adjectives, and simple connectors.
4. **School Subjects And Schedules**: Read and talk about classes, days, and times.
5. **Asking About School And Free Time**: Build short dialogues about preferences, classes, and activities.
