# Unit 3: Classroom Objects And Descriptions

Track: Spanish  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/spanish-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use the concrete classroom setting to identify objects, choose articles, form simple plurals, describe objects with colors and size words, and say what exists or where something is. By the end of the unit, students understand that articles and endings carry useful gender and number information.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

After students can manage greetings, numbers, dates, and simple questions, they need a concrete domain for early grammar patterns. Classroom objects are visible, familiar, and reusable, which makes article, plural, and adjective patterns easier to teach without abstract grammar overload.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: noun gender, articles, singular/plural, adjective placement, adjective agreement, existence, location.
- Vocabulary: common classroom objects, colors, size words, position words.
- Forms: `el`, `la`, `los`, `las`, plural endings, `hay`, `no hay`, `esta`, `estan`, `en`, `cerca de`, `al lado de`, `encima de`, `debajo de`.
- Representations and texts: labeled classroom scenes, object inventories, simple maps, noun phrase cards, find-the-object prompts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 1 classroom language, Unit 2 numbers, and readiness to notice that Spanish articles, noun endings, and word order can differ from English.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Grammatical gender refers to people only.
- Articles can be ignored without changing meaning.
- Plural nouns only need a final `s`; articles do not change.
- Color adjectives always come before nouns as in English.
- `Hay` and `esta/estan` do the same job in every sentence.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

First, students learn classroom nouns with articles as whole chunks so gender is attached to the noun from first exposure. Then they use numbers to notice singular and plural changes. Color and adjective agreement come after students understand article-noun pairing. Location and `hay` close the unit by putting objects into short scene descriptions.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

The unit reviews classroom commands, numbers, pronunciation clues, and basic questions. Students use numbers to count objects, greetings to begin partner exchanges, and date or weather warm-ups for cumulative retrieval.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Articles, plural forms, and adjective agreement prepare students to describe people, family members, food, places, and school subjects. `Hay` and location language return in classroom scenes, city maps, and capstone descriptions.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match classroom nouns with the correct article.
- Change familiar nouns and articles from singular to plural.
- Describe familiar objects with colors or size words that agree.
- Interpret and produce `hay` statements about a scene.
- Choose a location phrase that matches object placement.
- Correct common article, plural, or adjective-placement errors.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Classroom Nouns And Articles**: Learn familiar object nouns with `el` and `la` as meaningful chunks.
2. **Singular, Plural, And How Many**: Use numbers and articles to describe one or more classroom objects.
3. **Colors And Adjective Agreement**: Describe objects with color words that fit gender and number.
4. **Hay And Object Locations**: Say what exists and where familiar objects are in a classroom scene.
