# Unit 6: Food, Places, Routines, And Novice Capstone

Track: Spanish  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/spanish-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students integrate Level 1 language in everyday contexts: food, drink, polite wants, meals, hunger and thirst, places, going somewhere, simple routines, mixed questions, and a compact capstone presentation. The unit emphasizes transfer across interpretive, interpersonal, and presentational novice tasks.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This final unit works as an application and consolidation space. Students already have greetings, numbers, dates, descriptions, family, likes, school language, and time; food, places, and routines let them recombine those pieces without moving into later-level grammar systems.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: preference in a food context, polite request, hunger/thirst, location versus destination, simple routine sequence, mixed novice question handling.
- Vocabulary: foods, drinks, meals, places, routine actions, review question words and connectors.
- Forms: `quiero`, `quisiera` as polite exposure, `tengo hambre`, `tengo sed`, `me gusta`, `no me gusta`, `estoy en`, `esta en`, `voy a`, `vas a`, `va a`, time phrases, `primero`, `despues`, `por la mañana`, `por la tarde`.
- Representations and texts: menus, community maps, daily schedules, short routine notes, mixed dialogues, capstone planning frames.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need all prior Level 1 language: greetings, politeness, numbers, dates, weather, time, articles, adjective agreement, `hay`, `ser`, `estar`, `tener`, family, likes, school activities, question words, and short production routines.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- `Quiero` is always the most polite request in every context.
- `Estoy en` and `voy a` are interchangeable.
- A destination phrase can omit `a` because English sometimes does.
- Routine writing should become a long paragraph.
- Capstone success means using lots of new words instead of accurate familiar language.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Food and polite wants come first because they extend likes and politeness. Hunger and thirst add `tener` review. Places with `estar` and `ir a` contrast location and destination. Routines combine time, activities, and connectors. Mixed questions prepare students for the final capstone, which samples reading, dialogue, writing, and optional speaking.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson in this unit should retrieve earlier language: greetings, numbers, dates, time, articles, adjective agreement, family, school subjects, likes, question words, `ser`, `estar`, `tener`, and `hay`. Review should feel embedded in meaningful contexts rather than detached.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares Spanish Level 2 by solidifying novice interpretive strategies, short interpersonal exchanges, and presentational sentence frames. It deliberately avoids full past tense, full command systems, object-pronoun placement, and extended unscripted conversation.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify and request foods or drinks politely in simple contexts.
- Use `tener` chunks for hunger and thirst.
- Distinguish location with `estar en` from destination with `ir a`.
- Sequence a simple daily routine with time phrases and connectors.
- Ask and answer mixed novice questions across learned domains.
- Produce a compact integrated novice presentation using accurate familiar language.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Food, Drinks, And Polite Wants**: Identify foods and drinks and make simple polite requests.
2. **Meals, Hunger, And Thirst**: Use food vocabulary with `tengo hambre`, `tengo sed`, and meal contexts.
3. **Places With Estar And Ir A**: Distinguish where someone is from where someone is going.
4. **Simple Daily Routines**: Sequence familiar actions with time phrases and connectors.
5. **Mixed Questions In Conversation**: Ask and answer cumulative novice questions in short exchanges.
6. **Novice Capstone Presentation**: Produce a compact self, family, school, likes, place, and routine presentation.
