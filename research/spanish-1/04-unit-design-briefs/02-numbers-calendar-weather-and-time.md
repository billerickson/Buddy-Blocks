# Unit 2: Numbers, Calendar, Weather, And Time

Track: Spanish  
Level: Level 1  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/spanish-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use high-frequency numeric and calendar language to answer everyday questions about age, dates, birthdays, weather, seasons, and time. By the end of the unit, students can interpret and produce short statements that anchor communication in when and what conditions.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Numbers, dates, weather, and time have high reuse across the entire level. Placing them early gives later lessons a durable retrieval base for family size, schedules, food prices, classroom quantities, routine times, and capstone presentations.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: number meaning in context, age formula, date order, weather chunks, time statements.
- Vocabulary: numbers 0-31, days, months, seasons, `hoy`, `mañana`, birthday words, weather expressions, time phrases.
- Forms: `Tengo... años`, `¿Cuantos años tienes?`, `¿Que dia es hoy?`, `Es el...`, `Mi cumpleaños es...`, `Hace...`, `Llueve`, `Nieva`, `Es la una`, `Son las...`.
- Representations and texts: calendar pages, birthday notes, weather cards, simple schedules, mini interviews.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 1 greetings, question punctuation, polite response routines, pronunciation awareness, and comfort with short chunks. They should know English number, date, weather, and clock concepts.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- `Tengo... años` can be built like the English phrase "I am ... years old."
- Spanish date order and capitalization match English exactly.
- Similar number words can be guessed without attention to sound and spelling.
- Weather expressions are word-for-word English translations.
- `Es la una` and `Son las dos` follow one identical pattern.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with numbers in useful contexts rather than isolated counting. Use numbers immediately for age with `tener`, then extend into calendar dates and birthdays. Weather and seasons add daily context with fixed expressions. Basic time comes last because it combines numbers with a new singular/plural pattern and prepares schedules in Unit 5.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson should reuse greetings, classroom phrases, question marks, pronunciation clues, and polite interaction from Unit 1. Mini interviews should begin and end politely, so Unit 1 routines become automatic.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Numbers return in object quantities, family descriptions, schedules, prices, routines, and capstone tasks. Dates and weather support short warm-ups and context-setting. Time prepares school schedule and daily routine work.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Recognize and use numbers 0-31 in age, date, and time contexts.
- Ask and answer age with `tener`.
- Interpret calendar and birthday statements.
- Choose accurate weather or season statements from context.
- Distinguish `Es la una` from `Son las...`.
- Combine time, date, or weather with a simple social exchange.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Numbers 0-31 In Useful Contexts**: Recognize and retrieve numbers through ages, dates, and quantities.
2. **Age With Tener**: Ask and answer age using `¿Cuantos años tienes?` and `Tengo... años`.
3. **Days, Months, And Dates**: Read and state days, months, dates, and birthdays.
4. **Weather And Seasons**: Interpret and produce common weather and season statements.
5. **Telling Basic Time**: Use `Es la una` and `Son las...` for simple times and schedules.
