# Unit 3: Description And Amplification

Track: Rhetoric
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/rhetoric-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to expand short writing so it becomes clearer, more vivid, and more useful for the audience. By the end of the unit, they can choose details that serve the purpose, expand bare sentences, use precise words and sensory clues, and add examples without burying the main idea.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

After students can track audience, purpose, and story point, they need a way to make ideas fuller. Amplification comes before claims and arguments because students will later need specific examples and details to support ideas.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: details should serve a purpose; expansion can clarify who, what, when, where, why, or how; precise words and examples help the audience understand.
- Vocabulary: describe, detail, useful detail, distracting detail, sensory detail, precise verb, expand, amplify, example, clarify, order, emphasis, main idea.
- Representations and models: bare-to-better sentence ladder, detail sort, five-question expansion frame, before-and-after paragraph model.
- Texts and forms: single-sentence expansions, two- to four-sentence paragraphs, description snippets, model examples and non-examples.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need basic sentence awareness, familiar nouns, verbs, and adjectives, ability to read short paragraphs, and the Unit 1 habit of asking what a communication is trying to do for an audience.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- More detail is always better.
- Any adjective improves a sentence.
- A precise word must be long or fancy.
- Examples are decorative instead of clarifying.
- Added details do not need to stay connected to the main idea.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with the judgment skill: useful detail versus distracting detail. Then teach simple expansion with who, what, when, and where before adding why and how. Next, focus on precise words and sensory clues so students revise at the word and phrase level. End with examples that clarify the main idea, bridging into claim support.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Use audience and purpose from Unit 1 as the test for detail quality. Use story events from Unit 2 as content for expansion so students keep narrative order and story point active.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 4 by making examples and details available as support. It prepares Unit 6 by giving students concrete revision targets: add a useful detail, choose a precise word, or clarify with an example.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose useful details and reject distracting details.
- Expand a sentence with relevant who, what, when, where, why, or how information.
- Choose precise verbs or descriptive words that fit the purpose.
- Add an example that clarifies a main idea.
- Improve a short paragraph without losing focus.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Useful Details, Not Just More Details**: Decide whether a detail helps the audience understand the point.
2. **Expand With Who, What, When, And Where**: Add basic information to make a sentence clearer.
3. **Precise Words And Sensory Clues**: Choose vivid but fitting words that improve meaning.
4. **Examples That Clarify The Main Idea**: Add examples that explain or support without distracting.
