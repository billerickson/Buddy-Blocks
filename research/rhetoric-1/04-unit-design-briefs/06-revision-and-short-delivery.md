# Unit 6: Revision And Short Delivery

Track: Rhetoric
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/rhetoric-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn revision as purposeful improvement and oral delivery as audience-aware communication. By the end of the unit, they can choose one focused revision target, improve a short response, and prepare or deliver a brief oral response with clear voice and respectful listener awareness.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Revision and delivery come after students have meaningful content to improve: retellings, descriptions, claims, examples, and comparisons. The unit teaches students that rhetoric is made stronger through choices, feedback, and rehearsal, not just by producing a first answer.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: revision improves meaning, order, support, word choice, and audience fit; delivery makes ideas understandable to listeners.
- Vocabulary: revise, edit, clarity, word choice, order, stronger reason, specific example, conclusion, audience fit, oral response, voice, pace, listener, respectful.
- Representations and models: one-target revision checklist, before-and-after response pairs, order strip, audience-fit word choice chart, speaking note card.
- Texts and forms: short student-like drafts, one-paragraph responses, oral response frames, brief speaking prompts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need audience-purpose analysis, retelling, useful details, claim-reason-example support, and comparison/evaluation language.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Revision means spelling and punctuation only.
- Revision should try to fix everything at once.
- Longer revision is automatically better revision.
- A memorized speech is the only good speech.
- Speaking choices do not depend on the listener.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin by separating revision from editing. Then practice clear order, which supports retellings and paragraphs. Next, revise reasons and examples, then word choice for audience and purpose. End with short delivery so students apply audience awareness beyond writing.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every revision target reviews prior content: order from Unit 2, useful detail from Unit 3, reasons and examples from Unit 4, respectful wording from Unit 5, and audience-purpose fit from Unit 1.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares the capstone studio by giving students a menu of improvement moves. It also prepares Rhetoric 2, where students will imitate, expand, reduce, paraphrase, and orally narrate with more control.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify whether a change is revision or editing.
- Choose a focused revision target for a weak response.
- Improve order, support, word choice, or conclusion.
- Select audience-fit language.
- Prepare or complete a brief oral response with clear delivery criteria.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Revision Is More Than Editing**: Distinguish meaning-focused revision from surface editing.
2. **Revise For Clear Order**: Reorder sentences or events so the response is easier to follow.
3. **Revise For Stronger Reasons And Examples**: Improve support by replacing vague or irrelevant material.
4. **Word Choice For Audience And Purpose**: Choose words that fit the listener and goal.
5. **Prepare And Deliver A Short Response**: Use simple notes and delivery criteria for a brief oral response.
