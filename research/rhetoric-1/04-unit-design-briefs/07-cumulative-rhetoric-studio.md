# Unit 7: Cumulative Rhetoric Studio

Track: Rhetoric
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/rhetoric-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students integrate Rhetoric 1 skills in mixed mini-tasks. By the end of the unit, they can decide which rhetorical move a task calls for, diagnose a weak response, and produce a short written or oral response that fits audience and purpose.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

The capstone unit belongs last because it asks students to transfer rather than repeat a single pattern. Students must select from retell, describe, support, compare, revise, and present, then explain how the choice fits the task.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: different tasks call for different rhetorical moves; strong responses fit audience, purpose, message, and support.
- Vocabulary: rhetorical move, retell, describe, explain, claim, reason, example, compare, revise, present, audience, purpose, message, support, conclusion.
- Representations and models: move-choice menu, weak-response diagnosis chart, short planning frame, final mini-performance checklist.
- Texts and forms: short fables, everyday scenarios, student-like drafts, one-paragraph written responses, brief oral presentations.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the full Rhetoric 1 body of knowledge: audience-purpose-message, story order and point, useful details, claim-reason-example support, comparison with criteria, respectful evaluation, focused revision, and short delivery.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- The newest skill is the right move for every task.
- A response is strong if it is long.
- Audience and purpose no longer matter once the answer is correct.
- Revision is separate from communication.
- Oral and written rhetoric use completely different skills.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with move selection so students learn to diagnose the task. Then diagnose weak responses to name what is missing. End with a compact mini-performance that combines planning, production, revision, and delivery without becoming a long essay or speech.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This unit reviews every prior unit. Items should deliberately mix story retelling, audience-purpose checks, details, claims, examples, comparisons, revision decisions, and short oral delivery.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares Rhetoric 2 by giving students confidence with the basic moves that later become more formal progymnasmata exercises: fable, narrative, paraphrase, amplification, reduction, proverb, and oral narration.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose the fitting rhetorical move for a task.
- Name what is weak or missing in a response.
- Combine two or more Level 1 skills.
- Produce a compact written or oral response.
- Explain how the response fits audience and purpose.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Choose The Rhetorical Move**: Decide whether a task asks for retelling, describing, supporting, comparing, revising, or presenting.
2. **Fix The Weak Response**: Diagnose missing audience fit, order, detail, support, comparison, or revision.
3. **Level 1 Mini Performance**: Plan, revise, and present a short response that uses multiple Rhetoric 1 skills.
