# Unit 4: Validity, Truth, And Soundness

Track: Logic
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/logic-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students separate three judgments that are often confused: whether a proposition is true, whether an argument is valid, and whether an argument is sound. They learn to test structure before using factual knowledge.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit follows syllogisms because validity must have a concrete meaning before students compare it with truth and soundness. It prevents the common Logic 3 error of calling any true-sounding conclusion "valid."

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: truth value of propositions, validity of argument structure, soundness as valid plus true premises, unsound arguments.
- Vocabulary: true, false, valid, invalid, sound, unsound, premise truth, conclusion truth, structure, factual accuracy, persuasive, follows.
- Representations and models: two-column truth/structure checks, valid-but-false examples, invalid-but-true-sounding examples, soundness decision steps.
- Texts and forms: short invented arguments, familiar factual claims, standard-form syllogisms with obviously true or false content.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 3 validity testing, premise and conclusion identification, and enough factual common sense to identify simple true and false propositions.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Valid means true.
- A true conclusion makes the argument valid.
- A false premise makes the argument invalid.
- Sound means emotionally convincing.
- Factual disagreement is the same as finding an invalid structure.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The first lesson isolates validity as a structure test with content that may be silly or invented. The second lesson examines truth as a property of individual propositions. The third lesson combines the two into soundness, giving students a decision routine: valid structure first, then true premises.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Lessons should review standard-form syllogisms, middle terms, and Venn checks. Quantifier precision remains important because a small wording change can alter validity.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Unit 5 uses "must follow" thinking for conditionals. Unit 6 asks students to decide whether a natural-language argument problem is asking about truth, validity, or soundness.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Classify propositions as true, false, or not judged by structure alone.
- Classify arguments as valid or invalid.
- Identify arguments that are valid but unsound because a premise is false.
- Identify why a true conclusion does not rescue invalid reasoning.
- Apply a soundness decision routine in simple cases.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Validity Is About Structure**: Judge whether the conclusion must follow, apart from factual content.
2. **Truth Of Premises And Conclusions**: Separate proposition truth from argument validity.
3. **Sound Or Unsound?**: Decide whether an argument is both valid and built from true premises.
