# Unit 4: Quests, Tests, And Heroic Choices

Track: Classical Literature  
Level: Level 2  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/classical-literature-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students read heroic and journey episodes as structured stories with tasks, obstacles, helpers, choices, costs, and outcomes. By the end of the unit, students should identify a quest pattern, interpret heroic choices with evidence, and compare two choices without reducing characters to simple labels.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Heroic episodes ask students to combine nearly every earlier skill: sequence, motive, consequence, myth background, pattern recognition, character interpretation, and evidence. They belong after myth-cycle orientation but before final comparison and transfer.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: quest structure, journey, test, helper, obstacle, cost, return, heroic choice, character complexity.
- Vocabulary: quest, journey, task, obstacle, helper, monster, hero, cost, return, test, turning point, courage, pride, cleverness, wisdom, restraint, loyalty.
- Representations and models: quest map, obstacle-helper-cost chart, heroic choice scale, two-choice comparison frame.
- Texts and forms: adapted heroic or journey episodes with clear tasks and consequences.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need Unit 3 myth patterns and Unit 1-2 tools for sequence, motive, consequence, lesson, and evidence.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A hero is always good in every choice.
- Strength is the same as wisdom.
- A helper is unimportant because the hero gets the fame.
- A monster or opponent has no story function.
- A quest ends when the task is finished, even if there is a cost or return to explain.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin by mapping the quest shape. Then add obstacles, helpers, and costs so students see that a quest is more than a task list. Next, interpret character qualities such as courage, pride, cleverness, and wisdom in context. End by comparing heroic choices to prepare the final unit's broader comparison work.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Review myth motifs from Unit 3 and motive-consequence reasoning from Units 1-2. Include brief reminders that fables, folktales, myths, and epic episodes can share patterns while keeping different genre jobs.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 5 by giving students story images and voices that short poems can evoke. It prepares Unit 6 by requiring focused comparison, evidence use, and transfer of pattern language to a new episode.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify quest elements in order.
- Match obstacles, helpers, and costs to their story function.
- Choose the best evidence for a claim about courage, pride, cleverness, wisdom, restraint, or loyalty.
- Compare two heroic choices with one focus.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Map A Quest**: Identify task, journey, obstacle, helper, choice, cost, and return.
2. **Obstacles, Helpers, And Costs**: Explain how difficulty and aid shape the outcome.
3. **Courage, Pride, Cleverness, Wisdom**: Interpret heroic qualities using story evidence.
4. **Compare Heroic Choices**: Compare two choices by motive, risk, help, consequence, or restraint.
