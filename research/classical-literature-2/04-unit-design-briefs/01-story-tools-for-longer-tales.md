# Unit 1: Story Tools For Longer Tales

Track: Classical Literature  
Level: Level 2  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/classical-literature-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students refresh and extend core story-reading tools so they can handle longer adapted classical and traditional tales. By the end of the unit, students should retell linked events in order, notice cause and effect, distinguish trait, feeling, motive, choice, and consequence, and choose short evidence for a theme or moral.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes first because Level 2 increases text length, unfamiliar names, and interpretive demand. Students need common language for sequence, motive, consequence, theme, and evidence before fables, myths, quests, poetry, comparison, and drama ask them to use those tools with less support.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: retelling, key events, cause and effect, turning points, character interpretation, theme and moral support, evidence choice.
- Vocabulary: retell, sequence, episode, key event, cause, effect, turning point, resolution, trait, feeling, motive, choice, consequence, moral, theme, evidence.
- Representations and models: event chain, cause-and-effect chain, character choice map, theme-and-evidence frame.
- Texts and forms: short adapted tales or story excerpts with two or three linked episodes; selected evidence statements.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should already know basic story elements, beginning-middle-end order, simple genre labels, character and setting, and the idea that some stories teach lessons.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A good retelling includes every detail.
- A theme is the same as one event.
- A character trait is any feeling a character has.
- Motive, action, and consequence all mean the same thing.
- Evidence is any detail from the story, whether or not it supports the answer.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with retelling because sequence is the foundation for motive, consequence, and theme. Then sharpen cause-and-effect thinking so students see why later events depend on earlier choices. Next, separate character terms that are often blurred. End with theme and evidence so students can use the earlier story map to support a larger claim.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This opening unit reviews Level 1 work with fable, myth, folktale, legend, poem, character, setting, moral, and beginning-middle-end retelling. Review is brief and practical, not a separate survey.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares Unit 2 fable and folktale moral reasoning, Unit 3 myth-cycle consequence tracking, Unit 4 heroic choice analysis, Unit 5 poetry evidence, and Unit 6 comparison and transfer.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Select key events for a retelling.
- Order events in a longer tale.
- Match cause to effect.
- Distinguish trait, feeling, motive, choice, and consequence.
- Choose evidence that supports a theme or moral.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Build A Strong Retelling**: Select and order key events instead of retelling every detail.
2. **Cause, Effect, And Turning Points**: Explain how one event leads to another and where the story changes direction.
3. **Trait, Feeling, Motive, Choice**: Separate common character-reading terms and use them accurately.
4. **Evidence For Themes And Morals**: Choose story details that support a larger lesson or theme.
