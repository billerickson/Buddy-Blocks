# Unit 3: Parallelism, Modifiers, And Concision

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 4
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-4/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students revise sentences for balance and precision. By the end of the unit, they can repair faulty parallelism, tighten comparisons and paired structures, move or rewrite modifiers for clarity, and reduce wordiness without cutting necessary meaning.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Unit 2 introduces style through sentence shape. Unit 3 turns that style work toward precision: patterns must match, modifiers must land clearly, and concise writing must still say enough.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: grammatical balance, equal ideas in equal forms, modifier attachment, sentence focus, redundancy, essential detail.
- Vocabulary: parallel structure, series, paired construction, comparison, modifier, misplaced modifier, dangling modifier, concise, wordy, precise, redundant.
- Representations and models: list-pattern frames, comparison frames, modifier arrows, cut/keep/move revision charts.
- Texts and forms: sentences with lists and comparisons, short revision pairs, compact paragraphs with unnecessary repetition or detail.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need parts of speech, phrase recognition, sentence boundaries, sentence combining, basic comparison language, and enough reading stamina to compare several possible sentence revisions.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A list is parallel if all items relate to the same topic.
- Parallel structure is only for long formal writing.
- A modifier can describe any noun in the sentence.
- Concision means making the sentence as short as possible.
- Wordy writing sounds more mature because it is longer.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with lists because they make parallel structure visible. Then extend parallelism to paired ideas and comparisons, where the pattern is less obvious but the reader need is stronger. Move to modifier placement because it asks students to track what words describe. End with concision so students use precision and pattern awareness to decide what to cut, move, or keep.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students revisit Unit 1 sentence boundaries and Unit 2 sentence combining during every revision. Faulty parallelism and modifier errors are presented as clarity problems, not only correctness labels.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 4 because register and voice choices often become wordy or imbalanced. It prepares Unit 5 because punctuation around lists, interrupters, and modifiers depends on meaning. It prepares Unit 6 because focused edit passes need categories such as parallelism, modifier, and concision.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose or produce a parallel version of a list.
- Repair paired constructions and comparisons with matching forms.
- Identify what a modifier appears to describe and revise when needed.
- Choose a concise revision that keeps the original meaning.
- Reject revisions that sound polished but remove important detail or shift meaning.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Lists That Stay Parallel**: Make items in a series use matching grammatical forms.
2. **Paired Ideas And Comparisons**: Keep both sides of a comparison or paired construction balanced.
3. **Modifiers Near What They Describe**: Place modifiers so readers know exactly what is being described.
4. **Concision Without Losing Meaning**: Cut redundancy and wordiness while preserving useful detail.
