# Unit 6: Editing Systems And Grammar In Context

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 4
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-4/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students bring Grammar 4 skills together through focused edit passes, copyediting marks, short grammar-in-context analysis, and revision justification. By the end of the unit, they can choose a focused target, revise short passages, and explain why the revision improves clarity, rhythm, register, or meaning.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

The final unit asks students to transfer grammar knowledge rather than answer isolated rule prompts. It belongs at the end because students now have enough targets to decide what kind of edit a passage needs.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: focused edit pass, copyediting instruction, cumulative review, author choice, preserve meaning, revision justification, portfolio evidence.
- Vocabulary: copyediting mark, delete, insert, transpose, capitalize, lowercase, proofread, revise, edit pass, portfolio, grammar in context, justify.
- Representations and models: mark-to-action matching, edit-pass checklists, short passage revision grids, before/after portfolio comparisons.
- Texts and forms: student-like paragraphs, literary and civic sentences, compact academic passages, constrained revision explanations.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the full Grammar 4 sequence: sentence control, sentence variety, parallelism, modifiers, concision, mood, voice, register, punctuation, and short-passage revision routines.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Copyediting means fixing everything at once.
- A revision is better if it sounds more formal, even when it changes the meaning.
- Literary grammar has no rules or patterns.
- Editing marks are labels rather than directions for a change.
- A passage is done after one error is fixed.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with copyediting marks and focused passes so students have a disciplined editing system. Then analyze literary and civic sentences to connect grammar choices to reading. Move into passage revision in multiple passes so students practice sequencing targets. End with portfolio-style revision justification that asks students to choose, compare, and explain final edits.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson reviews multiple earlier targets. Unit 1 sentence control, Unit 3 precision, Unit 4 voice/register, and Unit 5 punctuation all appear in mixed but controlled passages.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This is the capstone for Grammar 4 and prepares later grammar, composition, literature, and civic reading work. It builds the habit of choosing a grammar tool for a specific reading or writing purpose.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match copyediting marks to actions.
- Choose a focused edit pass for a passage problem.
- Analyze how grammar choices shape tone or emphasis in author sentences.
- Revise short passages across several targets without changing intended meaning.
- Select the best justification for a revision.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Copyediting Marks And Focused Passes**: Use editing marks and choose one target at a time.
2. **Literary Sentences Under The Grammar Lens**: Notice how sentence structure, repetition, punctuation, and voice shape meaning.
3. **Revise A Passage In Passes**: Improve a short passage through ordered sentence-boundary, clarity, style, and punctuation passes.
4. **Portfolio Check: Justify The Revision**: Compare before/after revisions and choose the clearest reason for the change.
