# Unit 2: Sentence Variety, Rhythm, And Emphasis

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 4
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-4/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to revise sentence structure for flow, emphasis, and reader attention. By the end of the unit, they can vary sentence length and openings, combine related ideas without crowding, and use appositives, interrupters, and repetition for controlled effects.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

After Unit 1 secures boundaries, students are ready to see grammar as choice. This unit makes style concrete through sentence shape rather than vague advice to "make it better."

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: rhythm, emphasis, flow, sentence length, sentence opening, sentence combining, apposition, interruption, purposeful repetition.
- Vocabulary: simple sentence, compound sentence, complex sentence, compound-complex sentence, opener, appositive, interrupter, repetition, style, emphasis.
- Representations and models: sentence-length patterns, opening labels, before/after combinations, punctuation frames for appositives and interrupters.
- Texts and forms: short paragraphs, paired revisions, literary or civic model sentences, sentence-order and revision-choice items.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need reliable clause and sentence-boundary control, basic coordination and subordination, comma use in familiar cases, and enough revision experience to compare two versions of a sentence or short paragraph.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Style means adding impressive words.
- Longer sentences are always better.
- Combining sentences always improves writing.
- Every sentence should begin the same way for consistency.
- Repetition is always an error rather than sometimes a purposeful pattern.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with sentence length and rhythm so students hear structure before manipulating it. Then revise openings because opening variety is a practical and visible style move. Move into sentence combining to join related ideas without losing clarity. End with appositives, interrupters, and emphasis as more advanced but controlled sentence-shaping tools.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson reviews Unit 1 boundaries. Sentence-combining tasks deliberately include fragment and comma-splice distractors so style choices stay grammatically secure.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Unit 2 prepares Unit 3 by showing that balanced patterns help readers. It prepares Unit 5 by connecting punctuation to rhythm and interruption. It prepares Unit 6 by giving students multiple valid revision options to evaluate.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify the effect of sentence length and sentence order in a short passage.
- Choose a stronger sentence opening without changing meaning.
- Combine related sentences into one clear sentence.
- Use appositives and interrupters with controlled punctuation.
- Distinguish purposeful repetition from accidental repetition.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Sentence Length And Rhythm**: Compare short, medium, and long sentences for pace and emphasis.
2. **Strong Sentence Openings**: Revise repetitive openings while keeping meaning clear.
3. **Combine Without Crowding**: Join related ideas when combining improves flow and leave them separate when clarity needs space.
4. **Appositives, Interrupters, And Emphasis**: Add or interpret appositives and interrupters as controlled style tools.
