# Unit 1: Sentence Control For Purposeful Revision

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 4
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-4/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students refresh the sentence-control knowledge that supports all later style and revision work. By the end of the unit, students can identify clauses and phrases, repair fragments and run-ons, join independent clauses correctly, and revise pronouns or modifiers when clarity breaks down.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Grammar 4 asks students to make purposeful choices, but purposeful choices depend on knowing where sentences begin, end, and connect. This unit comes first so later work on rhythm, punctuation, voice, register, and copyediting does not rest on fragile sentence boundaries.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: complete thought, clause independence, phrase support, sentence boundary, coordination, subordination, agreement, modifier placement, clarity.
- Vocabulary: independent clause, dependent clause, phrase, fragment, run-on, comma splice, coordinating conjunction, subordinating conjunction, semicolon, pronoun, antecedent, modifier.
- Representations and models: clause labels, boundary marks, repair choices, short sentence pairs, before/after revisions.
- Texts and forms: single sentences, two-sentence pairs, short student-like paragraph excerpts, revision-choice items.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should know subjects, verbs, basic parts of speech, simple/compound/complex sentence exposure, common punctuation marks, pronoun agreement in simple cases, and the idea that modifiers describe nearby words or phrases.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A sentence is complete if it is long enough.
- Any clause with a verb is a full sentence.
- A comma can join two complete thoughts without another connector.
- Pronouns are clear if the writer knows what they mean.
- Modifiers can sit anywhere as long as the reader can guess.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin by separating clauses, phrases, and complete thoughts because this diagnostic language is needed for every repair. Then repair fragments and run-ons so students practice boundary decisions. Next, join independent clauses with semicolons, conjunctions, or sentence breaks. End with pronoun and modifier clarity so students see sentence control as reader support, not only punctuation correctness.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This is the opening unit, so review draws from earlier grammar levels: parts of speech, sentence types, punctuation, pronouns, modifiers, and compound/complex sentence exposure.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Unit 2 by making sentence variety safe, Unit 3 by making modifier and parallel revision precise, Unit 5 by grounding punctuation choices in clause structure, and Unit 6 by giving students focused edit categories.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Classify a word group as phrase, dependent clause, independent clause, or complete sentence.
- Choose a valid repair for a fragment, run-on, or comma splice.
- Select the correct way to join independent clauses.
- Revise an unclear pronoun or misplaced modifier in a finite exact way.
- Explain the clarity problem behind a sentence-control error through selected responses.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Clause, Phrase, Or Complete Thought?**: Sort word groups by whether they can stand alone and why.
2. **Repair Fragments And Run-Ons**: Choose sentence-boundary repairs that create complete, clear sentences.
3. **Joining Independent Clauses**: Use semicolons, coordinating conjunctions, or sentence breaks for closely related complete thoughts.
4. **Pronouns And Modifiers For Clarity**: Revise unclear pronouns and misplaced modifiers that confuse the reader.
