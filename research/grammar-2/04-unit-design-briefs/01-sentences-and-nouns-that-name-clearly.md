# Unit 1: Sentences And Nouns That Name Clearly

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 2
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students stabilize complete sentence awareness and use nouns to name clearly. By the end of the unit, students should identify subjects and predicates, distinguish common and proper nouns, form regular plurals, and use possessive nouns to show ownership or relationship.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes first because every later Grammar 2 skill depends on knowing what the sentence is about and which words name people, places, things, or ideas. Noun control must be secure before students can judge pronoun clarity, verb agreement, possession, dialogue speaker tags, and diagram relationships.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: complete sentence, naming, noun category, number, ownership, capitalization.
- Vocabulary: sentence, fragment, subject, predicate, common noun, proper noun, singular noun, plural noun, possessive noun, owner, apostrophe.
- Representations and models: subject/predicate frames, noun category charts, plural/possessive contrast pairs, sentence edit marks.
- Texts and forms: short natural sentences, paired examples, focused correction prompts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should know that sentences begin with capital letters and end with punctuation, that nouns name people, places, and things, and that verbs often show action.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A group of words is a sentence if it starts with a capital letter.
- The subject is always the first word.
- Proper nouns are any nouns that feel important.
- Plural `s` and possessive `-'s` do the same job.
- An apostrophe can be added to any noun ending in `s`.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with complete sentences so students have a stable frame for every noun. Then move into common and proper nouns, because capitalization is a visible cue but not the whole concept. Plural nouns come before possessives so students can contrast "more than one" with "belongs to." The unit ends with possessive nouns because that skill will be repeatedly reviewed with pronouns, punctuation, and editing.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This is the opening unit, so review comes from prior sentence knowledge: capitalization, end punctuation, nouns, verbs, adjectives, and basic subject/predicate ideas.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Clear nouns prepare students to identify antecedents in Unit 2, subjects in Unit 3, direct objects and phrase objects in Unit 4, speaker tags in Unit 5, and diagram parts in Unit 6.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify complete sentences, subjects, and predicates.
- Sort common and proper nouns.
- Choose or form singular and plural nouns.
- Distinguish plural and possessive forms.
- Correct a focused capitalization or apostrophe error.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Complete Sentences Need Subjects And Predicates**: Rebuild the sentence frame students will use for the rest of the level.
2. **Common And Proper Nouns Name Precisely**: Distinguish general names from specific names and apply capitalization.
3. **Singular And Plural Nouns**: Use noun number to tell whether a sentence names one or more than one.
4. **Possessive Nouns Show Ownership**: Use apostrophes to show who or what owns or is connected to something.
