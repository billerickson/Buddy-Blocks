# Unit 6: Visual Grammar And Cumulative Editing

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 2
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use simple sentence diagrams and focused editing routines to show cumulative Grammar 2 mastery. They read and build simple diagrams, edit one named target at a time, and choose the relevant grammar tool in mixed review.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit belongs last because diagramming and mixed editing require the whole level: subjects, predicates, nouns, pronouns, verbs, direct objects, complements, modifiers, punctuation, and reader clarity. It consolidates the course without introducing advanced clause analysis.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: visual relationship, focused target, revision for clarity, transfer.
- Vocabulary: sentence diagram, subject line, verb line, direct object, complement, modifier, prepositional phrase, edit, revise, target.
- Representations and models: simple subject-verb-object diagrams, subject-linking verb-complement diagrams, modifier branches, target checklists.
- Texts and forms: short sentences, two- to four-sentence editing passages, mixed review prompts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the full Grammar 2 sequence: sentence parts, noun forms, pronouns, verb phrases, sentence expansion, commas, and dialogue punctuation.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A diagram is a decoration rather than a meaning model.
- Every word must be diagrammed before the main sentence parts can be understood.
- Editing means fixing everything at once.
- The newest grammar rule is the answer in every mixed prompt.
- Longer revision is automatically clearer.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Students first read diagrams so the visual model is not a production burden. They then build simple diagrams from sentences with familiar patterns. Focused editing follows because students can use sentence relationships to locate a named target. The final lesson mixes targets and asks students to choose the relevant tool, which is the strongest readiness evidence for Stage 7 QA and later implementation.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

The unit reviews all prior units. Diagram lessons retrieve subjects, verbs, direct objects, complements, and modifiers. Editing lessons retrieve noun forms, pronoun clarity, tense consistency, commas, and dialogue.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares later grammar levels for subordinate clauses, more complex diagrams, advanced punctuation, and paragraph-level revision by preserving the habit of identifying the sentence relationship before editing.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match simple diagrams to sentences.
- Build a simple diagram for subject-verb-object or subject-linking verb-complement patterns.
- Edit a named target without changing unrelated correct parts.
- Choose the grammar concept needed in a mixed item.
- Explain why a correction or revision improves clarity.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Read A Simple Sentence Diagram**: Interpret a diagram as a picture of sentence relationships.
2. **Build A Simple Sentence Diagram**: Place subject, verb, object or complement, and one modifier in a simple diagram.
3. **Edit One Target At A Time**: Use a named target to make a focused correction.
4. **Choose The Grammar Tool In Mixed Review**: Decide which Grammar 2 concept a sentence needs before answering.
