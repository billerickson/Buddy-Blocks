# Unit 3: Verb Phrases And Time

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 2
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students recognize and use verb phrases with control. They identify action verbs, helping verbs, and linking verbs, then keep simple verb tense consistent in short contexts.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Verb control follows nouns and pronouns because students now have stable subjects to connect to predicates. The unit prepares students for direct objects, compound predicates, sentence diagrams, and editing tasks that depend on finding the verb accurately.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: action, helping, linking, main verb, time signal, consistency.
- Vocabulary: verb, action verb, helping verb, main verb, verb phrase, linking verb, complement, present tense, past tense, future tense, consistent.
- Representations and models: subject-verb frames, underlined verb phrases, verb function contrast charts, two-sentence tense checks.
- Texts and forms: short sentences, paired mini-passages, focused verb-form edits.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should identify complete sentences, subjects, predicates, nouns, pronouns, and basic action verbs.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- The only verb is the action word at the end of the sentence.
- Helping verbs are optional decorations.
- Linking verbs show action.
- Any past-tense verb must end in `ed`.
- Tense can shift as long as each sentence is correct by itself.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with action verbs because they are the most concrete. Then add helping verbs so students see that a verb phrase can have more than one word. Linking verbs come next as a contrast: they connect instead of showing action. The unit ends with tense consistency, requiring students to apply verb recognition across more than one sentence.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Subjects, predicates, nouns, and pronouns are reviewed in every lesson. Pronoun lessons supply clear subjects for verb phrases and tense practice.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Students need accurate verb recognition to find direct objects, compound predicates, prepositional phrases, adverbs, and sentence diagram relationships.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify action verbs and verb phrases.
- Choose helping verbs that complete a sentence.
- Distinguish linking verbs from action verbs.
- Choose present, past, or future forms.
- Correct tense shifts in short contexts.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Action Verbs And Verb Phrases**: Identify the words that tell what the subject does.
2. **Helping Verbs Work With Main Verbs**: Recognize verb phrases such as is running, has finished, and will visit.
3. **Linking Verbs Connect The Subject**: Distinguish linking verbs from action verbs and identify what they connect.
4. **Keep Verb Tense Consistent**: Keep verb time steady across short sentences unless the meaning changes.
