# Unit 5: Punctuation That Shows Structure

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 2
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use punctuation to show list structure and speaker meaning. They apply commas in series, quotation marks for exact spoken words, commas with simple speaker tags, and capitalization and end punctuation inside simple dialogue.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Punctuation is placed after sentence expansion because commas and quotation marks make more sense when students can see the structure being marked. This unit also relies on proper nouns, pronouns, verbs, and sentence boundaries from earlier units.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: separation, exact words, speaker tag, sentence boundary inside a quotation.
- Vocabulary: comma, series, list item, quotation marks, dialogue, speaker tag, exact words, capitalization, end punctuation.
- Representations and models: list-item brackets, quotation mark frames, speaker tag arrows, punctuation checklists.
- Texts and forms: list sentences, simple one-sentence quotations, speaker-tag-before and speaker-tag-after dialogue.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need complete sentence control, noun capitalization, sentence expansion, and basic punctuation from earlier units.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Commas show every place a reader pauses.
- Two list items need a comma just because three items do.
- Quotation marks belong around the speaker tag.
- The comma after a speaker tag can be replaced by any punctuation.
- Dialogue punctuation can be corrected without checking exact spoken words.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with commas in a series because students already built expanded lists in Unit 4. Then isolate quotation marks around exact words. Add speaker tags and dialogue commas after students can identify the exact words. End with capitalization and end punctuation in dialogue so students combine all pieces in focused edits.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Commas in a series review nouns, adjectives, and compound parts. Dialogue sentences review proper nouns, pronouns, verb tense, and subject/predicate boundaries.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Punctuation editing feeds directly into Unit 6 mixed review. Students will need to choose whether a sentence needs a comma for a list, quotation marks for exact words, capitalization, or a different grammar correction.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Place commas in lists of three or more items.
- Identify exact spoken words in a dialogue sentence.
- Choose correct quotation mark placement.
- Use commas with simple speaker tags.
- Correct capitalization and end punctuation in simple dialogue.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Commas In A Series**: Use commas to separate three or more words or phrases in a list.
2. **Quotation Marks For Exact Words**: Put quotation marks around only the words a speaker says.
3. **Speaker Tags And Dialogue Commas**: Use commas to connect simple speaker tags and quoted words.
4. **Capitalization And Punctuation In Dialogue**: Combine capitalization, end punctuation, and quotation marks in simple dialogue.
