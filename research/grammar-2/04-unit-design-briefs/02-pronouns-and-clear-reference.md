# Unit 2: Pronouns And Clear Reference

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 2
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use pronouns as clear reader-facing replacements for nouns. They connect pronouns to antecedents, choose subject and object pronoun forms, distinguish possessive pronouns from possessive adjectives, and use reflexive pronouns only when they point back to the subject.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Pronouns belong after noun work because a pronoun is only useful when the noun it replaces or points to is clear. This placement lets students practice clarity before later sentences become longer and more punctuation-heavy.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: replacement, reference, sentence job, possession, same subject.
- Vocabulary: pronoun, antecedent, personal pronoun, subject pronoun, object pronoun, possessive pronoun, possessive adjective, reflexive pronoun, clear reference.
- Representations and models: noun-to-pronoun arrows, subject/object sentence slots, pronoun charts, two-sentence reference checks.
- Texts and forms: short paired sentences, focused pronoun edits, contrast examples.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should identify nouns, subjects, predicates, proper nouns, plural nouns, and possessive nouns from Unit 1.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A pronoun is clear because the writer knows the noun.
- `I` and `me` are interchangeable.
- Object pronouns can be used as subjects if they sound casual.
- Possessive pronouns need apostrophes because possessive nouns do.
- Reflexive pronouns can replace any object pronoun.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit starts with antecedents and personal pronouns so students understand the replacement relationship. It then separates subject and object pronouns because sentence job affects form. Possessive forms come next to connect back to Unit 1 possessive nouns while avoiding apostrophe transfer errors. Reflexive pronouns close the unit because they require students to track whether the pronoun points back to the same subject.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson reviews noun identification and subject/predicate structure. Possessive pronoun work deliberately contrasts with possessive nouns from Unit 1.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Pronoun clarity supports verb tense contexts, sentence expansion, dialogue speaker tracking, and cumulative editing. Students will later revise sentences where a pronoun could refer to more than one noun.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match pronouns to clear antecedents.
- Choose subject or object pronouns by sentence job.
- Distinguish possessive pronouns and possessive adjectives.
- Use reflexive pronouns only for same-subject reference.
- Correct unclear pronoun references in focused sentences.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Personal Pronouns And Antecedents**: Connect a pronoun to the noun it replaces or points to.
2. **Subject And Object Pronouns**: Choose pronoun forms by their job in the sentence.
3. **Possessive Pronouns And Possessive Adjectives**: Show ownership without confusing pronoun forms with possessive nouns.
4. **Reflexive Pronouns Point Back**: Use myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, and themselves only when they point back to the subject.
