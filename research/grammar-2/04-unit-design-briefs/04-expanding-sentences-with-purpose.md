# Unit 4: Expanding Sentences With Purpose

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 2
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-2/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students expand sentences with purpose while keeping relationships clear. They identify direct objects, compound subjects, compound predicates, prepositional phrases, adjectives, and adverbs, then explain how the added part changes meaning.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Sentence expansion belongs after verb work because students need to find the subject and verb before they can tell what receives the action, what is compounded, or what is being modified. The unit also gives punctuation and diagramming real sentence structures to work with.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: action receiver, combining related parts, phrase detail, modifier precision.
- Vocabulary: direct object, compound subject, compound predicate, conjunction, preposition, prepositional phrase, object of the preposition, adjective, adverb, modifier.
- Representations and models: action-verb question frames, sentence combining frames, preposition phrase brackets, modifier arrows, simple diagram previews.
- Texts and forms: short expanded sentences, revision choices, one-target edits.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need stable subjects, predicates, nouns, pronouns, action verbs, helping verbs, linking verbs, and basic adjective/adverb awareness.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Any word after a verb is a direct object.
- A linking verb can take a direct object.
- Compound subjects and compound predicates create a compound sentence.
- Every phrase that begins with a small word is a prepositional phrase.
- More detail always makes writing better.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Direct objects come first because they depend most directly on action verbs from Unit 3. Compound subjects and predicates follow as sentence-building tools that still keep one sentence frame. Prepositional phrases are introduced before adjective/adverb precision because they add where and when details in a visible chunk. The unit ends with adjectives and adverbs so students can compare different kinds of modifiers and choose details for clarity.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students review nouns and pronouns as subjects, objects, and objects of prepositions. Verb function is reviewed when distinguishing direct objects from complements. Plural, possessive, and pronoun clarity appear in expansion sentences.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Expanded lists prepare commas in series. Phrases and modifiers prepare simple diagrams. Sentence combining and revision prepare cumulative editing.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify direct objects after action verbs.
- Combine related subjects and predicates correctly.
- Identify prepositional phrases and their objects.
- Choose adjectives or adverbs that fit the word being modified.
- Select an expansion that improves clarity without changing meaning.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Direct Objects Receive The Action**: Ask whom or what after an action verb to find the receiver.
2. **Compound Subjects**: Combine two related subjects that share a predicate.
3. **Compound Predicates**: Combine two related actions or verb phrases that share a subject.
4. **Prepositional Phrases Add Where And When**: Use prepositions and objects of prepositions to add clear setting or time details.
5. **Adjectives And Adverbs Add Precise Detail**: Choose modifiers that answer the right question and sharpen meaning.
