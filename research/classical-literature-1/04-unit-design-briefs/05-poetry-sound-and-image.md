# Unit 5: Poetry, Sound, And Image

Track: Classical Literature
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/classical-literature-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to hear poetry and oral language as meaningful form: lines, stanzas, rhyme, rhythm, repetition, image, simile, personification, speaker, and mood work together to help a short poem or lyrical passage stay memorable.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Students have already practiced repeated language in folktales and vivid images in myths and heroic tales. Poetry now shifts attention from what happens to how language sounds, pictures, and feels while still using evidence.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: oral language, poetic form, sound pattern, image, figurative language, speaker, mood.
- Vocabulary: poem, line, stanza, rhyme, rhythm, repetition, image, sound, speaker, simile, personification, mood, recitation, oral language.
- Representations and models: line-and-stanza markup, sound/repetition highlighting, image sketch notes, speaker-evidence chart.
- Texts and forms: short poems, chants, short speeches, and adapted image-rich lines connected to Level 1 story worlds.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need listening stamina for short texts, evidence habits, awareness of repeated language, and willingness to read or hear a line more than once.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Poetry is only rhyme.
- Repetition is always accidental or only decorative.
- The speaker is always the author.
- Images are literal pictures printed beside a poem.
- A simile or personification must be explained as if it were factual.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with line, stanza, sound, and repetition because students can hear them. Then introduce image and mood so students connect words to mental pictures and feeling. Next, teach simile and personification as meaning-making comparisons. End with speaker and recitation to prepare for dialogue and drama.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

The unit reviews folktale repetition, mythic images, heroic emotion, and evidence language. Students continue using "because" explanations, but evidence may be a word, phrase, sound, or image instead of only a plot event.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Attention to speaker, line, voice, and oral performance prepares students for narrator, dialogue, scene, and drama. Poetry vocabulary also supports later lyric poetry, speeches, and literary comparison.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify line, stanza, rhyme, rhythm, repetition, image, simile, personification, and speaker in short texts.
- Explain how a sound, repeated word, or image supports meaning or mood.
- Distinguish speaker from author at an introductory level.
- Use oral reading or recitation cues to notice sound and sense.
- Support an interpretation with a word or phrase from the poem.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Lines, Stanzas, And Sound**: Notice how a poem is arranged and heard.
2. **Repetition And Rhyme Help Memory**: Explain how repeated words or sounds make language memorable.
3. **Images, Similes, And Personification**: Interpret vivid pictures and comparisons without literalizing them.
4. **Speaker, Mood, And Recitation**: Read short lines aloud and infer who is speaking and how the words feel.
