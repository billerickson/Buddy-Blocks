# Unit 4: Heroes, Quests, And Consequences

Track: Classical Literature
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/classical-literature-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to follow heroic and journey tales by tracking goals, tasks, obstacles, helpers, choices, admirable traits, flaws, costs, rewards, and consequences.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Heroic material belongs after myths because many Level 1 heroic episodes draw on mythic names, places, and patterns. Students are ready to go beyond "the hero wins" and ask how courage, pride, curiosity, help, and cost shape the story.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: quest structure, heroic test, helper, obstacle, flaw, cost, reward, journey as change.
- Vocabulary: hero, heroine, quest, journey, task, obstacle, helper, monster, courage, flaw, pride, choice, cost, reward, consequence, legend, epic tale.
- Representations and models: quest path, obstacle-helper chart, trait-choice-outcome chain, hero comparison frame.
- Texts and forms: adapted heroic episodes, short legends, and journey tales with controlled background.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need myth vocabulary, pattern recognition, retelling, evidence language, and character-choice-consequence reasoning.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A hero is perfect.
- A quest is any trip.
- Obstacles matter only because they are exciting.
- A helper makes the hero less heroic.
- A flaw means the character cannot be admirable.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with the visible map of a quest: goal, path, obstacle, and outcome. Then analyze helpers and tests so students see structure beyond action. Next, focus on heroic traits and flaws, which complicates character judgment. End with cost and reward to prepare students for later epic and tragedy-adjacent ideas without introducing advanced literary theory.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

The unit reviews fable consequences, trickster choices, folktale tests, mythic warnings, and respectful use of names and traditions. It continues comparison practice through hero patterns.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Hero work prepares students for Level 2 adapted epic, monster episodes, fate and choice, and ancient drama concepts. It also supports poetry and dialogue lessons where character voice and image often draw on heroic material.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify quest parts in a short heroic episode.
- Match an obstacle or helper to its role in the story.
- Explain a hero's trait, flaw, or choice with evidence.
- Distinguish a quest from an ordinary trip.
- Compare two hero or journey moments using one criterion.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **A Quest Has A Goal**: Identify goal, path, obstacle, and outcome in a journey tale.
2. **Obstacles, Helpers, And Tests**: Explain how helpers and obstacles shape the hero's task.
3. **Heroes Can Have Flaws**: Connect admirable traits and flaws to choices and consequences.
4. **Cost, Reward, And Return**: Track how a journey changes a character or community.
