# Unit 3: Academic Vocabulary For Reading, Tasks, And Discussion

Track: Vocabulary  
Level: Grade 6  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/grade-06-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn high-utility academic words used in directions, reading responses, evidence explanations, relationships, and discussion so they can understand tasks and participate with precision.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

After students learn how to solve words and analyze morphology, they need the language that appears across every subject. This unit improves access to later domain, argument, research, and revision tasks.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: task verbs, evidence and inference, relationships among ideas, discussion moves, sentence-frame use.
- Vocabulary: analyze, infer, evaluate, summarize, cite, support, evidence, reason, explanation, compare, contrast, cause, effect, sequence, clarify, elaborate, qualify, challenge, synthesize.
- Representations and models: task-verb decision charts, evidence/reason/explanation strips, compare/contrast tables, cause/effect arrows, discussion-turn frames.
- Texts and forms: directions, short reading-response prompts, partner-discussion lines, brief evidence statements.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should know basic words such as answer, explain, compare, different, same, because, and evidence. They should be able to write or choose a simple reason for an answer.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Analyze means answer quickly.
- Infer means guess without evidence.
- Evidence and reason mean the same thing.
- Compare means only similarities and contrast means only differences.
- Challenge means attack another person instead of questioning an idea respectfully.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with academic verbs in directions so students can decode what tasks ask them to do. Then separate evidence, inference, and explanation because these words drive reading responses. Next teach relationship language for comparing, contrasting, cause, and effect. End with discussion moves so students use academic vocabulary in social learning, not only in worksheets.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students should use Unit 1's routine for unfamiliar academic terms and Unit 2's morphology when words such as evaluate, explanation, or synthesize include meaningful parts.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

These words become the response language for domain vocabulary, connotation, figurative language, argument, media, research, and cumulative transfer. Later prompts should keep them active.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match academic verbs to the expected student action.
- Distinguish evidence from inference and explanation.
- Use compare/contrast and cause/effect terms in context.
- Choose a discussion move that fits a conversation goal.
- Complete short sentence frames using academic vocabulary accurately.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Academic Verbs In Directions**: Decode what tasks ask students to do.
2. **Evidence, Inference, And Explanation**: Distinguish proof, thinking, and reasoning in reading responses.
3. **Compare, Contrast, Cause, And Effect**: Use relationship words to track how ideas connect.
4. **Discussion Moves For Stronger Thinking**: Practice clarify, elaborate, qualify, challenge, and synthesize in short exchanges.
