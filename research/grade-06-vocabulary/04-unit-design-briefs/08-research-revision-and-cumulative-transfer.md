# Unit 8: Research, Revision, And Cumulative Transfer

Track: Vocabulary  
Level: Grade 6  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/grade-06-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students integrate the full level toolkit through research vocabulary, paraphrase and synthesis language, revision terms, word-learning portfolio habits, and cumulative transfer to unfamiliar contexts.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This capstone unit comes last because students must choose among strategies independently. It uses context, morphology, academic vocabulary, domain vocabulary, connotation, figurative language, argument terms, and source vocabulary in mixed tasks.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: inquiry, keyword search, note-taking, paraphrase, synthesis, citation, revision, portfolio learning, transfer.
- Vocabulary: inquiry, research, source, keyword, note, paraphrase, quote, cite, synthesize, conclusion, revise, refine, transition, portfolio, context, morphology, definition, connotation, example, non-example, original sentence.
- Representations and models: research-note cards, paraphrase comparison charts, synthesis sentence frames, portfolio-entry templates, cumulative strategy checklists.
- Texts and forms: short source notes, paired mini sources, weak and improved paraphrases, sentence-revision samples, cumulative passages.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the full set of prior units: word-learning routine, morphology, academic verbs, disciplinary vocabulary, connotation, figurative language, argument/source terms, and brief written-response skills.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Research means finding any website quickly.
- Paraphrase means changing one or two words.
- Synthesize means list facts separately.
- Citation words are only formatting rules.
- A portfolio entry is copied dictionary text.
- Strategies only work in the unit where they were taught.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with inquiry and source terms so students can name the research process. Then teach paraphrase, synthesis, and citation as language actions. Next build portfolio habits as a long-term learning structure. End with mixed transfer so students must decide which strategy applies without being told.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This unit explicitly reviews all prior units. Each lesson should require students to cite strategy evidence: context clue, word part, reference entry, domain context, connotation, figurative clue, source clue, or revision purpose.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares Grade 7 vocabulary and cross-subject learning by strengthening independent strategy choice, concise explanation, source awareness, and durable word collection habits.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Use research and source terms accurately.
- Distinguish paraphrase, quotation, citation, and synthesis.
- Complete a meaningful word-learning portfolio entry.
- Choose and explain an appropriate strategy for an unfamiliar word or phrase.
- Transfer vocabulary knowledge across mixed reading, writing, and source contexts.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Inquiry And Source Terms**: Name the research actions and source features students need for Grade 6 inquiry.
2. **Paraphrase, Synthesize, And Cite**: Use academic language for restating, combining, and crediting ideas.
3. **Word-Learning Portfolio Habits**: Record words in a way that supports long-term learning and accurate use.
4. **Cumulative Vocabulary Transfer Challenge**: Apply the full strategy toolkit to unfamiliar mixed contexts.
