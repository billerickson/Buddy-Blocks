# Unit 1: Strategic Word Learning In Context

Track: Vocabulary  
Level: Grade 6  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/grade-06-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students establish a repeatable word-learning routine: inspect context, analyze word parts when useful, connect to known words, verify with a reference, and test whether a meaning fits the sentence or passage.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes first because every later vocabulary strand depends on independent word solving. Before students study roots, academic language, connotation, figurative language, or source vocabulary, they need a shared process for forming and checking meaning hypotheses.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: context evidence, inference, reference verification, part-of-speech fit, meaning substitution, strategy choice.
- Vocabulary: context, clue, infer, verify, reference, dictionary entry, glossary, part of speech, definition, example, morphology, meaning fit, strategy, hypothesis.
- Representations and models: clue charts, sentence frames, dictionary-entry snapshots, substitution tests, short passage contexts.
- Texts and forms: one-sentence and paragraph contexts, glossary snippets, multiple-definition entries, short explanations of clue use.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should enter with elementary context-clue experience, basic dictionary/glossary familiarity, recognition of common prefixes and suffixes, and the ability to explain a clue in a brief sentence.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Context always gives the exact dictionary definition.
- The first dictionary definition is always the right one.
- A word part alone is enough evidence.
- Similar-looking words always share a useful meaning.
- A guessed meaning is finished before it has been tested in the original sentence.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with the full routine so students know the target process. Then isolate context clues and reference selection because these are common failure points in middle-school reading. End with meaning-fit testing so students learn to revise an initial guess when the sentence or part of speech does not support it.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This is the opening unit, so review draws from elementary vocabulary habits: synonyms, context clues, basic word parts, dictionary entries, and simple sentence explanations.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The routine becomes the recurring method for morphology, academic vocabulary, technical words, connotation, figurative language, source terms, and final transfer. Later lessons should ask which part of the routine helped most.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify context clues and explain their role.
- Distinguish supported inferences from unsupported guesses.
- Select a reference definition that fits part of speech and sentence context.
- Substitute a proposed meaning and judge whether it fits.
- Use more than one evidence source when a word is unfamiliar.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Build A Word-Learning Routine**: Introduce the five-step process students will reuse throughout the level.
2. **Use Context Without Overguessing**: Use nearby words and sentences as evidence while recognizing context limits.
3. **Choose The Right Reference Entry**: Select the dictionary or glossary entry that matches part of speech and context.
4. **Test Meaning For Fit**: Substitute and revise meanings so the final interpretation works in the original sentence.
