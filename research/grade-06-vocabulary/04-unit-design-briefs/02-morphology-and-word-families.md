# Unit 2: Morphology And Word Families

Track: Vocabulary  
Level: Grade 6  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/grade-06-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use prefixes, suffixes, roots, word families, cognates, and false-pattern checks as evidence for word meaning while continuing to verify morphology-based guesses in context.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Morphology follows the word-learning routine because students need to treat word parts as evidence, not shortcuts. Placing it early gives students a reusable tool for academic, disciplinary, argument, and research vocabulary.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: affix meaning, root meaning, part-of-speech shifts, related words, cognate awareness, false pattern checks.
- Vocabulary: prefix, suffix, root, base word, affix, word family, part of speech, noun, verb, adjective, adverb, cognate, etymology, false pattern.
- Representations and models: root webs, prefix/suffix maps, word-family trees, word-part plus context evidence charts.
- Texts and forms: short academic sentences, dictionary origin notes, cognate examples, sentence pairs showing part-of-speech changes.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need basic affix awareness, context clue use, reference entry selection, and familiarity with common word families from earlier grades.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Every root or prefix has one fixed meaning.
- Words with the same root can be used interchangeably.
- A suffix only changes spelling.
- A familiar-looking letter group is always a meaningful root.
- Cognates are always accurate guides.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with prefixes because they give clear meaning shifts. Move to suffixes because they often change part of speech, which connects morphology to sentence fit. Then study a small set of high-utility Greek and Latin roots in academic words. Finish with word families, cognates, and false patterns so students learn both the power and limits of morphology.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Unit 1's context, reference, and fit-check routine should appear in every lesson. Students should explain when morphology helped and when they needed context or a reference to finish the meaning.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Morphology supports academic verbs, discipline-specific terms, argument vocabulary, research language, and revision words. Part-of-speech awareness also supports precise word choice in Unit 5.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Infer meaning from a common prefix or suffix.
- Identify part-of-speech shifts caused by suffixes.
- Use roots to explain a word family.
- Reject a false morphology clue when context or reference evidence does not support it.
- Combine word-part evidence with context evidence.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Prefixes That Shift Meaning**: Use prefixes to infer direction, repetition, negation, order, or relationship.
2. **Suffixes And Parts Of Speech**: Show how suffixes can change a word's role and meaning in a sentence.
3. **Greek And Latin Roots In Academic Words**: Use selected roots to unlock related Grade 6 academic words.
4. **Word Families, Cognates, And False Patterns**: Build related-word awareness while checking for misleading look-alikes.
