# Unit 5: Connotation, Denotation, And Precise Word Choice

Track: Vocabulary  
Level: Grade 6  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/grade-06-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students distinguish dictionary meaning from association, intensity, formality, and precision, then apply those distinctions to reading, tone, audience, purpose, and sentence revision.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Students have already worked with context, morphology, academic words, and domain precision. This unit uses that foundation to show why words with similar definitions cannot always be swapped.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: denotation, connotation, synonym choice, intensity scale, formal and informal register, audience, purpose, revision.
- Vocabulary: denotation, connotation, synonym, precise, vague, intensity, formal, informal, neutral, positive, negative, tone, audience, purpose, revise, word choice.
- Representations and models: connotation scales, synonym ladders, audience-purpose charts, sentence revision comparisons.
- Texts and forms: short descriptive sentences, announcement samples, dialogue snippets, mini revision tasks.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should know synonyms, antonyms, shades of meaning, dictionary definitions, basic tone words, and simple revision language.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Connotation only means positive or negative.
- Any synonym with the same definition will work.
- A more intense word is always better.
- Formal language is always clearer.
- Revision means fixing mechanics only.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with denotation so the dictionary meaning is stable. Then add connotation, feeling, and formality. Next rank intensity and precision among near-synonyms. End with revision because students need to choose words for a purpose, not only label them.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students should use dictionary entries from Unit 1, part-of-speech knowledge from Unit 2, academic purpose/audience language from Unit 3, and technical precision from Unit 4.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Connotation and precision prepare figurative-language interpretation, tone/mood work, media framing, argument language, and final revision tasks.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify denotation and connotation in context.
- Choose a word that fits tone, formality, or audience.
- Rank near-synonyms by intensity.
- Explain why a synonym swap changes meaning.
- Revise vague or mismatched wording in a short sentence.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Denotation And Dictionary Meaning**: Anchor word meaning in precise definitions before studying associations.
2. **Connotation, Feeling, And Formality**: Identify emotional, social, and register effects of word choice.
3. **Intensity And Precision In Synonyms**: Compare near-synonyms by strength and exactness.
4. **Revise For Exact Word Choice**: Improve sentences by choosing words that fit audience, purpose, and tone.
