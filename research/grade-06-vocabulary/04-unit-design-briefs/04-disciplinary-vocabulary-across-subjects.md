# Unit 4: Disciplinary Vocabulary Across Subjects

Track: Vocabulary  
Level: Grade 6  
Stage: Unit Design Brief  
Source: `research/grade-06-vocabulary/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students interpret selected subject-area words in compact math, science, history/social studies, literature, and source-analysis contexts, with special attention to technical meanings that differ from everyday meanings.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes after academic vocabulary because students now have the task language needed to read short content-area contexts. It gives them practice transferring word-learning strategies beyond ELA-like sentences.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: domain-specific meaning, technical precision, everyday versus academic use, diagrams and tables as vocabulary evidence.
- Vocabulary: discipline, domain-specific, technical, model, factor, variable, expression, estimate, observe, classify, data, conclusion, source, region, culture, economy, citizen, perspective, glossary.
- Representations and models: math expressions, graphs, data tables, maps, source excerpts, glossary entries, label-and-clue charts.
- Texts and forms: short explanations, lab-note snippets, map captions, primary/secondary source statements, brief literature or informational contexts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the word-learning routine, academic verbs, basic graph/table/map reading, and enough subject familiarity to understand brief Grade 6 contexts.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Everyday meanings always work in academic contexts.
- A diagram or table is not a vocabulary clue.
- Technical terms can be memorized without context.
- Source only means a website.
- Model always means a small copy of an object.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with math because common words such as factor and variable need precise meanings. Move to science process/data words, then history/geography/civics language, then mixed short sources where students choose the subject-specific meaning from context.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students should continue to cite clues, use morphology when useful, and distinguish task verbs in directions. Evidence, model, source, and perspective should also connect back to Unit 3.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Technical precision prepares Unit 5's word-choice work. Source and perspective language prepares Unit 7. Reading vocabulary in diagrams and excerpts prepares the cumulative transfer unit.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Choose a subject-specific meaning that fits a context.
- Use diagrams, tables, maps, or source excerpts as vocabulary evidence.
- Explain a difference between everyday and technical meanings.
- Match content-area words to concise definitions or examples.
- Use a glossary entry without ignoring context.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Math Words With Precise Meanings**: Interpret common math terms that need exact subject meanings.
2. **Science Process And Data Words**: Use observation, classification, variable, data, and conclusion language in brief science contexts.
3. **History, Geography, And Civics Terms**: Interpret source, region, culture, economy, citizen, and perspective in social studies contexts.
4. **Technical Meanings In Short Sources**: Choose meanings that fit mixed diagrams, excerpts, and glossary entries.
