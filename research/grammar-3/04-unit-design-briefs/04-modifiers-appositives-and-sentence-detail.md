# Unit 4: Modifiers, Appositives, And Sentence Detail

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to add detail without losing the sentence core. They identify adjective and adverb phrases, use introductory elements, recognize appositives, and punctuate nonessential appositives in controlled sentences.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Modifier and appositive work belongs after clause work because students must know the main clause before deciding which words are added detail. This prevents students from mistaking every phrase for a clause.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: sentence core, added detail, description, modification, renaming, essential and nonessential information.
- Vocabulary: modifier, adjective phrase, adverb phrase, introductory phrase, introductory clause, appositive, rename, comma pair.
- Representations and models: core sentence brackets, modifier arrows, appositive equals sign, introductory element plus main clause frame.
- Texts and forms: short descriptive sentences, sentence expansion choices, appositive punctuation choices, phrase-vs-clause contrasts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need sentence core recognition, prepositional phrases, nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, clauses, and introductory dependent clause punctuation.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Any extra detail must be a dependent clause.
- Modifiers can be placed far from the word they describe.
- An appositive is any phrase after a noun.
- All appositives need commas, or no appositives need commas.
- Added detail should be kept even when it makes the sentence unclear.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit starts by separating the sentence core from added detail. It then teaches adjective and adverb phrases as modifiers. Introductory elements follow because they depend on the same core-plus-detail idea. The unit ends with appositives because students must understand renaming and comma pairs.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Lessons review independent clauses, dependent clauses, and introductory dependent clauses. Phrase work is explicitly contrasted with clause work so students do not overextend the clause label.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares students to correct misplaced modifiers in Unit 5 and to use commas for appositives and introductory elements in Unit 6.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify the sentence core in a sentence with added detail.
- Distinguish phrases from clauses in clear cases.
- Choose the word or idea a modifier describes.
- Recognize appositives that rename nouns.
- Punctuate nonessential appositives with comma pairs.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Keep The Core, Add The Detail**: Separate main sentence parts from descriptive additions.
2. **Adjective And Adverb Phrases Modify Meaning**: Identify what descriptive phrases tell and what they modify.
3. **Introductory Elements Lead Into The Main Clause**: Use introductory phrases and clauses as setup for the main idea.
4. **Appositives Rename Nouns**: Identify and punctuate nonessential appositives.
