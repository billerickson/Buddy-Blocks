# Unit 2: Coordination And Compound Sentences

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to join related independent clauses as compound sentences when the ideas have equal weight. They choose fitting coordinating conjunctions, use comma plus conjunction punctuation, and repair comma splices and run-ons.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Students have just learned how to identify independent clauses. Coordination is the first sentence-combining move because it uses two complete clauses side by side before students handle dependency and clause hierarchy.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: equal ideas, related independent clauses, coordination, sentence combining, sentence boundary repair.
- Vocabulary: compound sentence, coordinating conjunction, FANBOYS, comma splice, run-on, semicolon, equal importance.
- Representations and models: two independent-clause boxes, comma-plus-conjunction frame, meaning relationship labels, semicolon bridge model.
- Texts and forms: paired short sentences, controlled compound sentence choices, comma splice corrections, run-on repairs.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need to identify independent clauses, subjects and verbs, complete thoughts, and fragments. They should know basic comma and period use.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Any sentence with `and` is compound.
- A comma alone can join two complete sentences.
- Coordinating conjunctions can be selected by sound alone.
- Semicolons can join dependent clauses or unrelated ideas.
- Combining sentences always improves writing.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit first defines compound sentences as two independent clauses joined correctly. It then focuses on meaning relationships because students must choose between `and`, `but`, `or`, `so`, and other coordinators deliberately. After correct joining is established, students compare comma-plus-conjunction with semicolons. The unit ends with repair of comma splices and run-ons.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson revisits the independent-clause test from Unit 1. Fragment examples are contrasted with comma splices so students do not treat every sentence-boundary error the same way.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Coordination prepares students to compare equal relationships with dependent relationships in Unit 3. It also gives them a structure to revisit during semicolon and cumulative punctuation work in Unit 6.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify whether both sides of a joined sentence are independent clauses.
- Choose a coordinating conjunction that fits the meaning.
- Punctuate compound sentences with comma plus conjunction.
- Use a semicolon only between closely related independent clauses.
- Correct comma splices and run-ons in finite, controlled sentences.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Two Independent Clauses, One Compound Sentence**: Build compound sentences from two complete related ideas.
2. **Choose The Best Coordinator**: Match coordinating conjunctions to addition, contrast, choice, reason, and result.
3. **Comma Plus Conjunction Or Semicolon**: Compare two correct ways to join related independent clauses.
4. **Fix Comma Splices And Run-Ons**: Repair sentence-boundary errors without creating fragments.
