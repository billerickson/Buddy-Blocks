# Unit 5: Agreement, Voice, And Modifier Clarity

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students edit sentences for clarity by tracking pronoun reference, agreement, action, and modifier placement. They learn that grammar choices help readers know who or what is being discussed.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes after students have studied clauses and modifiers because agreement, voice, and modifier placement all require attention to relationships across the sentence. Students are ready to see grammar as a clarity tool.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: reference, agreement, actor and receiver, clear placement, revision for reader understanding.
- Vocabulary: pronoun, antecedent, singular, plural, agreement, unclear reference, active voice, passive voice, actor, receiver, misplaced modifier.
- Representations and models: pronoun-to-antecedent arrows, actor-action-receiver chart, modifier arrow, before/after revision pairs.
- Texts and forms: short sentence edits, paired active/passive sentences, pronoun agreement choices, finite modifier placement corrections.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need nouns, pronouns, singular/plural number, verbs, sentence core, direct objects, modifiers, and clause recognition.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A pronoun is correct if the reader can guess the noun.
- Singular and plural pronouns can be mixed freely.
- Passive voice is always incorrect.
- The actor is always the grammatical subject.
- Misplaced modifiers are just awkward style, not meaning errors.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit begins with pronoun-antecedent agreement because it is a concrete reference relationship. It then adds clear pronoun reference, which requires more reader-awareness. Active and passive voice follow as a way to track actor and receiver. The final lesson returns to modifiers and teaches placement as a clarity decision.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Lessons review sentence core, clauses, modifier arrows, and appositives from earlier units. Short repair items may include compound or complex sentences only when the targeted clarity issue remains obvious.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares students for cumulative editing in Unit 6, where pronoun, voice, modifier, punctuation, and sentence-combining decisions may appear in short focused sets.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match pronouns to singular or plural antecedents.
- Choose or type a clear pronoun replacement in finite cases.
- Identify active and passive voice by actor/action/receiver.
- Choose active or passive voice when given a clear purpose.
- Correct clear misplaced modifiers by moving the modifier near the word it describes.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Pronouns Must Match Their Antecedents**: Maintain singular and plural agreement.
2. **Make Pronoun References Clear**: Revise unclear pronouns so the reader knows the antecedent.
3. **Active And Passive Voice Show Action Differently**: Recognize actor, action, and receiver in clear cases.
4. **Place Modifiers Where They Belong**: Move modifiers close to the words they describe.
