# Unit 3: Subordination And Complex Sentences

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to use dependent clauses to show relationships such as time, cause, condition, contrast, and concession. They build and punctuate complex sentences while keeping the main clause clear.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Students now know how to join equal independent clauses. Subordination gives them a different sentence-combining tool: making one idea depend on or frame another idea.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: dependency, main idea, meaning relationship, clause order, introductory dependent clause punctuation.
- Vocabulary: complex sentence, subordinating conjunction, dependent clause, main clause, time, cause, condition, contrast, concession, introductory clause.
- Representations and models: main-clause anchor line, dependent-clause tag, movable clause cards, cause/time/condition relationship labels.
- Texts and forms: paired idea sets, complex sentence frames, clause-order comparisons, comma choices based on clause position.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need independent/dependent clause distinction, compound sentence coordination, subject/verb tests, and basic comma use.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A subordinating conjunction can be chosen without considering meaning.
- Dependent clauses become independent when they are long.
- Sentences beginning with `because` are always incorrect.
- A comma is always needed before `because`.
- Complex sentences should replace all simple or compound sentences.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit begins by naming subordinating conjunctions as dependency makers. It then teaches meaning groups so students choose conjunctions by logic. Next, students build complex sentences in two clause orders. The final lesson focuses on comma use when a dependent clause comes first.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

The unit repeatedly contrasts dependent clauses with independent clauses from Unit 1 and compound sentences from Unit 2. Students continue to ask whether ideas are equal or whether one frames the other.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Subordination prepares students for introductory clauses and phrases in Unit 4, modifier clarity in Unit 5, and cumulative sentence-combining choices in Unit 6.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify subordinating conjunctions in short sentences.
- Match subordinators to time, cause, condition, contrast, or concession.
- Classify main and dependent clauses.
- Build complex sentences from two simple ideas.
- Punctuate introductory dependent clauses correctly.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Subordinating Words Make Clauses Dependent**: Identify how words like `because`, `when`, and `although` change a clause.
2. **Show Time, Cause, Condition, And Contrast**: Choose subordinators that fit the relationship between ideas.
3. **Build Complex Sentences Around A Main Clause**: Combine ideas by placing the dependent clause before or after the main clause.
4. **Punctuate Introductory Dependent Clauses**: Use commas when a dependent clause comes before the main clause.
