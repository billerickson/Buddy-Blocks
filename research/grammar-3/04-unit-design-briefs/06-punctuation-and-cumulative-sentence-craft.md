# Unit 6: Punctuation And Cumulative Sentence Craft

Track: Grammar
Level: Level 3
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/grammar-3/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use punctuation and sentence-combining choices to show relationships among ideas. They apply Grammar 3 skills in short cumulative editing and revision tasks.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit belongs at the end because punctuation only makes sense when students can identify the structure being marked. Students now have tools for clauses, coordination, subordination, modifiers, appositives, agreement, voice, and clarity.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: punctuation as signal, sentence relationship, revision choice, cumulative editing, clarity.
- Vocabulary: comma, semicolon, colon, introductory element, appositive, list, explanation, closely related independent clauses, sentence combining.
- Representations and models: punctuation decision tree, clause boxes, appositive comma pair, colon-introducer frame, revision choice chart.
- Texts and forms: two-idea sentence combining, short targeted editing passages, punctuation choices, sentence variety comparisons.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need all prior units: clause classification, compound and complex sentences, modifiers, appositives, agreement, voice, and clear modifier placement.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A punctuation mark should be placed wherever a reader pauses.
- Semicolons are stronger commas.
- A colon can come right after any verb before a list.
- The best revision always combines ideas.
- Editing means searching for every possible error at once.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

The unit begins with comma patterns students have already met so punctuation is anchored to known structures. It then isolates semicolons and colons because both are often misused. The third lesson asks students to choose among sentence-combining options. The final lesson uses short cumulative edits with a named focus for each question.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This unit reviews every previous unit, but each item remains targeted. Students retrieve clause tests, coordination, subordination, appositives, pronoun agreement, active/passive voice, and modifier placement in spaced, mixed contexts.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares later grammar levels by making students more independent sentence editors. It also prepares future writing work that will involve style, paragraph revision, parallelism, and longer passages.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Select the punctuation mark that matches the sentence structure.
- Use commas for introductory clauses/phrases and nonessential appositives.
- Use semicolons between related independent clauses.
- Use colons after a complete introductory clause before a list or explanation.
- Choose the best sentence-combining revision for clarity and meaning.
- Edit a short passage for targeted Grammar 3 conventions.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Commas Mark Introductions And Appositives**: Review commas that signal setup and renaming detail.
2. **Semicolons And Colons Have Specific Jobs**: Distinguish closely related clauses from introductions to lists or explanations.
3. **Choose The Best Sentence Combination**: Decide whether ideas should stay separate, coordinate, subordinate, or use apposition.
4. **Edit A Short Passage With Grammar 3 Tools**: Apply targeted cumulative editing without overloading the task.
