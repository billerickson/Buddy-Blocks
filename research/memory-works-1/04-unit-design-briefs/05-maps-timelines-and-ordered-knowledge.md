# Unit 5: Maps, Timelines, And Ordered Knowledge

Track: Memory Works
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/memory-works-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn to memorize categories and ordered knowledge without mixing them up. They practice geography categories, compass directions, simple map cues, and timeline pegs as meaningful lists and sequences.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes after students have practiced words, lines, facts, and rules. Maps and timelines introduce spatial and chronological memory, which require careful attention to category and order.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: category memory, ordered recall, compass direction, map location cue, chronological sequence, wrong-category errors.
- Vocabulary: category, list, order, sequence, map, compass, north, east, south, west, continent, ocean, timeline, chronology, peg.
- Representations and models: category charts, compass rose, simple world-map cue descriptions, timeline strip, sequence cards.
- Texts and forms: continent and ocean lists, compass direction order, simplified history pegs, mixed category prompts.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need category language, simple order words, basic map familiarity, the Unit 1 practice routine, and experience correcting swapped or missing chunks.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Ordered lists can be recalled in any order.
- Continents and oceans are interchangeable kinds of places.
- Compass directions are labels only, not memory cues.
- A timeline is a random list of famous names or dates.
- Wrong-category errors should be practiced the same way as missing-word errors.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with geography categories so students can separate continents from oceans before memorizing lists. Then introduce compass directions as stable cues. After spatial order is active, move to history pegs in chronological order. End by mixing list types so students identify the category before recalling.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Earlier sayings, poem lines, fact families, measures, and grammar definitions return in small review moments. The focus remains on choosing the right memory method for the target type.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit supplies map, list, and timeline targets for the final cumulative challenge. It also prepares later levels where students will memorize longer geography sequences, historical chronology, and knowledge pegs.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Sort targets into geography categories.
- Recall a short list in a stable order.
- Use compass directions as cues.
- Order simple history pegs chronologically.
- Diagnose whether an error is missing, swapped, or wrong-category.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Continents And Oceans As Categories**: Keep geography memory targets sorted by kind.
2. **Compass Directions As Cues**: Use north, east, south, and west as stable map cues.
3. **History Pegs In Order**: Memorize a short timeline sequence with chronology.
4. **Keep Lists From Getting Mixed**: Identify the target type before recalling a list.
