# Unit 4: Facts, Forms, And Patterns

Track: Memory Works
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/memory-works-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students apply memory routines to compact academic targets: math fact families, measurement equivalences, grammar definitions, and simple language forms. They learn to connect facts and rules to examples so recall has meaning.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

After language-rich sayings and poems, students need to see that memory work also supports other subjects. This unit keeps academic targets short and structured so students can focus on cue choice, categories, and useful review rather than heavy new content.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: fact family structure, unit equivalence, definition plus example, retrieval before checking, category cues.
- Vocabulary: fact family, equivalence, measure, unit, definition, example, rule, form, category, flash card, front, back.
- Representations and models: fact-family triangles, measure cards, definition-example pairs, flash cards, category charts.
- Texts and forms: multiplication/division fact family such as 3, 4, 12; measures such as 12 inches = 1 foot; grammar definitions such as noun and verb.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need basic multiplication readiness, familiarity with common measures, awareness of nouns and verbs, and the memory routines from the first three units.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- A fact family is a set of unrelated answers.
- Units can be left off because the number is enough.
- A grammar definition is known even if the student cannot identify an example.
- Flash cards are useful when flipped immediately without trying to recall.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with fact families because the relationships among numbers make the target more meaningful. Then move to measures, where the unit is part of the memory target. End with grammar definitions, which require students to recall a short rule and recognize examples.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students continue using chunking, first cues, exact wording, paraphrase, and delayed review. Short poem or saying retrieval appears between fact targets so students practice shifting modes.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares mixed-review work by adding fact, unit, and rule targets to the cumulative pool. It also prepares students to recognize different target forms in Unit 6.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Match related facts in a fact family.
- Recall measure equivalences with correct units.
- Use a flash card by retrieving before checking.
- Match grammar definitions to examples.
- Choose a cue that fits the target type.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Math Facts As Families**: Recall related facts as connected chunks, not isolated answers.
2. **Measures Worth Knowing**: Memorize high-use measurement equivalences with their units.
3. **Grammar Definitions With Examples**: Recall short definitions and connect them to examples.
