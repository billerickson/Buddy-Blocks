# Unit 1: Memory Practice Habits

Track: Memory Works
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/memory-works-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students learn the basic memory-work routine: understand the target, break it into chunks, recall it without looking, check against a model, correct the missed part, and review it again later. By the end of the unit, students should know that active retrieval and spaced review are stronger than simply rereading.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit comes first because Memory Works is partly about what to memorize and partly about how to memorize. Students need a shared practice language before they meet poems, sayings, fact families, rules, maps, timelines, and performance tasks.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: active retrieval, recognition, cued recall, independent recall, chunking, checking, targeted correction, spaced review.
- Vocabulary: study, recognition, recall, retrieval, cue, chunk, model, cover, check, correct, error, spaced review, cumulative review.
- Representations and models: covered-word prompts, first-word cues, chunk boxes, model-and-attempt comparison, simple review calendar.
- Texts and forms: one-sentence sayings, short lists, two-line practice targets, brief student explanations.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students should be able to repeat a short phrase, read or listen to a short target, follow a multi-step routine with support, and compare two short pieces of text for differences.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Looking again is the same as remembering.
- Recognition is the same as recall.
- Cues are cheating rather than temporary supports.
- A mistake means the whole target is ruined.
- Same-day recall proves long-term mastery.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin by contrasting passive study with active recall. Then teach chunking so students can make short targets manageable. Once chunks are visible, introduce the cover-recall-check cycle. End with targeted correction so mistakes become information about what to practice next.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

This is the opening unit, so review comes from familiar oral repetition, short directions, simple sayings, counting patterns, and classroom routines rather than earlier Memory Works units.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Every later unit uses this routine. Sayings require exact wording, poems require chunked lines and recitation, academic facts require cue choice, ordered knowledge requires sequence checks, and the final unit requires students to pick a practice step independently.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Distinguish recognition from recall.
- Identify useful chunks in a short target.
- Use a cover-recall-check routine.
- Locate the missed part of an attempt.
- Choose a next practice step based on an error.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Study, Recognition, And Recall**: Compare looking again, recognizing an answer, and pulling an answer from memory.
2. **Chunk The Target**: Break a short target into meaningful parts before practicing.
3. **Cover, Recall, Check**: Practice a target without looking, then compare it with the model.
4. **Correct The Missed Part**: Use errors to choose the next focused practice step.
