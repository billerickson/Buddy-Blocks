# Unit 6: Cumulative Recall And Confident Performance

Track: Memory Works
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/memory-works-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students bring the level together through mixed retrieval, cue fading, targeted correction, short recitation planning, and an end-of-level challenge. They show that they can use memory routines across many target types.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

This unit belongs at the end because memory mastery depends on delayed and mixed recall. Students now have a cumulative pool of routines and targets, so they can practice choosing the right method rather than following a single lesson pattern.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: interleaving, cue fading, independent recall, rehearsal planning, performance readiness, transfer.
- Vocabulary: interleave, mixed review, independent recall, cue fading, rehearsal, recitation plan, performance, mastery, transfer.
- Representations and models: mixed review deck, cue ladder, practice plan, recitation checklist, final challenge grid.
- Texts and forms: sayings, poem chunks, fact families, measures, grammar definitions, geography lists, compass directions, timeline pegs.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the full Memory Works 1 routine and prior exposure to all target types from Units 1-5.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Mixed review is unfair because the topic changes.
- Cues should never be faded once they help.
- Performance means perfection rather than prepared communication.
- Mastery means never needing review.
- A mistake during review proves the target was never learned.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin with interleaving so students practice switching among target types. Then fade cues gradually toward independent recall. After that, students plan and rehearse a short recitation. End with a mixed challenge that includes recall, explanation, correction, and transfer.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Every lesson in this unit retrieves earlier units. The review should include the practice routine, sayings, poem lines, fact families, measures, grammar definitions, geography categories, compass directions, and history pegs.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

This unit prepares Memory Works 2 by giving students a more independent practice stance. Future levels can lengthen selections, add more knowledge pegs, and expect stronger oral fluency because students know how to practice.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify a target type and choose a matching practice strategy.
- Recall older targets after a delay.
- Fade from strong cue to lighter cue to independent recall.
- Build a short recitation plan.
- Complete mixed recall and explain how to correct an error.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Interleave Old And New Targets**: Mix memory types and choose the right approach for each.
2. **Fade Cues Toward Independent Recall**: Move from heavy support to lighter cues and full recall.
3. **Plan And Practice A Short Recitation**: Prepare a short oral performance with rehearsal steps.
4. **End-Of-Level Mixed Memory Challenge**: Demonstrate recall, explanation, correction, and transfer.
