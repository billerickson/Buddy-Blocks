# Unit 2: Sayings, Meaning, And Exact Words

Track: Memory Works
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/memory-works-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students memorize short sayings with exact wording and connect them to meaning. They learn that a saying is not just a string of words; it carries a compact idea that can guide recall and application.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Sayings are short, memorable, and meaning-rich, making them a natural first content form after the practice routine. They let students practice exact wording without the load of a long poem or unfamiliar academic list.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: exact wording, paraphrase, meaning as a memory support, example and non-example, near-wording errors.
- Vocabulary: saying, proverb, exact words, paraphrase, meaning, situation, example, non-example, near wording.
- Representations and models: saying cards, missing-word prompts, meaning matches, situation cards, model-and-attempt comparison.
- Texts and forms: brief sayings such as "Well begun is half done," "A stitch in time saves nine," and "Measure twice, cut once."

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the Unit 1 retrieval routine, basic sentence comprehension, and the ability to explain a familiar idea in their own words.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Exact wording does not matter if the idea is close.
- A paraphrase should copy the original wording.
- A saying can fit any situation that shares one word.
- Meaning is separate from memorization.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Start with memorizing one very short saying exactly. Then add paraphrase work so meaning supports recall. End by asking students to choose which saying fits a situation, because application shows that the saying was understood rather than merely echoed.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students repeatedly use chunking, cover-recall-check, first-word cues, and targeted correction from Unit 1. Near-wording mistakes provide a natural reason to practice only the missed chunk.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

The unit prepares poem recitation by establishing exact wording and meaning. It also prepares grammar, history, and civic memory targets where students must recall a compact statement and use it in context.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Recall a short saying exactly.
- Supply a missing word from a saying.
- Paraphrase a saying accurately.
- Match a saying to a fitting situation.
- Identify and repair near-wording errors.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Learn A Short Saying**: Memorize exact wording for one-sentence sayings using chunks and first-word cues.
2. **Put The Meaning In Your Own Words**: Paraphrase sayings so meaning supports memory.
3. **Match Sayings To Situations**: Choose which remembered saying fits a short scenario.
