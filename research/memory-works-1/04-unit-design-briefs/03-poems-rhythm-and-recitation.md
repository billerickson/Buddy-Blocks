# Unit 3: Poems, Rhythm, And Recitation

Track: Memory Works
Level: Level 1
Stage: Unit Design Brief
Source: `research/memory-works-1/03-course-map.md`

## 1. Unit Goal

Students use rhythm, repeated sound, line breaks, and phrase chunks to memorize short poem selections and recite them with clear voice, steady pacing, and attention to meaning.

## 2. Why This Unit Belongs Here

Poems extend the exact-language work of sayings into oral performance. The unit is placed after sayings so students already know that wording matters, and before academic facts so memory work does not become only lists and definitions.

## 3. Key Concepts, Vocabulary, Representations, Texts, Forms, Or Models

- Concepts: rhythm as memory support, line chunks, first-word cues, oral rehearsal, expressive recitation, delayed poem review.
- Vocabulary: poem, line, couplet, stanza, rhythm, rhyme, repeated sound, first-word cue, recitation, voice, pacing, expression.
- Representations and models: line breaks, couplet cards, phrase ladders, first-word cue lists, recitation checklist.
- Texts and forms: short original practice couplets and brief public-domain-style lines chosen for humane, age-appropriate recitation.

## 4. Prerequisite Knowledge

Students need the Unit 1 routine, exact-word attention from Unit 2, basic rhyme or rhythm awareness, and willingness to speak short lines aloud.

## 5. Common Misconceptions

- Recitation means saying words as fast as possible.
- Rhythm helps only songs, not poem memory.
- A missed word ruins the whole attempt.
- Expression can be ignored until recall is perfect.

## 6. Lesson Sequence Rationale

Begin by noticing rhythm and repeated sound. Then teach a two-line selection through phrase chunks. After the words are becoming stable, add delivery choices: voice, pacing, pauses, and meaning. End with delayed review so students experience keeping a poem alive over time.

## 7. Review Connections To Earlier Units

Students revisit Unit 1 chunks and cues and Unit 2 exact wording. Sayings return as quick oral review before poem work begins, reinforcing that meaning and wording support each other.

## 8. Preparation For Later Units

Poem recitation prepares the final performance unit and supports later literature, rhetoric, language, and public-speaking work. It also gives students a memorable example of why spacing matters.

## 9. Assessment Targets

- Identify a rhythm, rhyme, repeated word, or line break that supports memory.
- Put a short poem target in correct line order.
- Recall missing words from a couplet.
- Choose a clear delivery choice for recitation.
- Explain how to repair a missed line after delay.

## 10. Recommended Lesson Titles

1. **Use Rhythm And Repeated Sound**: Notice sound patterns that make a poem easier to remember.
2. **Learn A Couplet By Chunks**: Memorize two lines by phrase chunks and first-word cues.
3. **Recite With Voice And Pacing**: Practice clear, meaningful oral delivery.
4. **Review A Poem After A Delay**: Bring back a poem selection later and repair missed words.
