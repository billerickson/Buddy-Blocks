## Teaching Goal

Show how headlines and loaded words can frame a topic for readers.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify framing words and explain how they influence reader response.

## Misconception Checks

Headlines are neutral labels; framing means lying; changing one loaded word cannot change reader response.

## Teaching Note

Compare "Students request longer lunch" with "Students demand schedule change" and discuss effect.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l04_q01_framing_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word choice to its likely effect.
pairs:
  - left: requested
    right: makes the action sound polite
  - left: demanded
    right: makes the action sound forceful
  - left: admitted
    right: suggests someone was hiding or resisting the truth
  - left: announced
    right: sounds more neutral about sharing information
explanation: Framing can happen through verbs that guide reader response.
hint: Notice which words sound neutral and which carry judgment.
```

```question
key: u07_l04_q02_headline_compare
type: passage-question
prompt: Compare the headlines.
passageTitle: Two Headlines
passage: |
  Headline A: Students request longer lunch period.
  Headline B: Students demand schedule change.
question: Which word in Headline B frames the students as more forceful?
choices:
  - demand
  - students
  - schedule
  - change
correctAnswer: demand
explanation: Demand has a stronger, more forceful connotation than request.
hint: Compare the verb in each headline.
```

```question
key: u07_l04_q03_neutral_headline
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which headline sounds most neutral?
choices:
  - City Council Approves New Park Plan
  - City Council Finally Fixes Boring Empty Lot
  - City Council Wastes Space On Park Plan
  - City Council's Amazing Park Saves The Day
correctAnswer: City Council Approves New Park Plan
explanation: The first headline reports the action without loaded praise or criticism.
hint: Choose the wording with the least judgment.
```

```question
key: u07_l04_q04_loaded_word_cloze
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the more neutral word.
sentenceBefore: The committee
sentenceAfter: the proposal after a long discussion.
choices:
  - rejected
  - trashed
  - wrecked
  - destroyed
correctAnswer: rejected
explanation: Rejected reports the decision; trashed, wrecked, and destroyed add stronger judgment.
hint: Choose the word a neutral news report might use.
```

```question
key: u07_l04_q05_frame_or_lie
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement about framing is best?
choices:
  - Framing can guide how readers see an event through emphasis or word choice
  - Framing always means a headline is completely false
  - Framing only happens in photographs
  - Framing means using no words at all
correctAnswer: Framing can guide how readers see an event through emphasis or word choice
explanation: Framing affects interpretation, even when the basic facts are true.
hint: Think about how request and demand can describe similar actions differently.
```
