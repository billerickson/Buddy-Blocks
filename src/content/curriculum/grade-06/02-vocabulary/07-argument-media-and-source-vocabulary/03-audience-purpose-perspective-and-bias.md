## Teaching Goal

Interpret why a source was made, who it addresses, and how viewpoint may shape it.

## Student Outcome

The student can use audience, purpose, perspective, and bias accurately without treating bias as automatic falsehood.

## Misconception Checks

Bias means false; perspective means random opinion; audience is everyone who might read the source.

## Teaching Note

Compare a park flyer and a city report about the same event.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l03_q01_source_terms_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each source term to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: audience
    right: who a message is for
  - left: purpose
    right: why a message was made
  - left: perspective
    right: the viewpoint shaped by experience or position
  - left: bias
    right: a slant or preference that shapes presentation
explanation: These terms help readers think about how and why a source communicates.
hint: Audience is who; purpose is why.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q02_audience_choice
type: passage-question
prompt: Identify the audience.
passageTitle: Flyer Text
passage: |
  Join us after school for beginner guitar lessons. No instrument needed. Parents can sign permission forms in the office.
question: Who is the main audience for this flyer?
choices:
  - students who might join the lessons
  - parents looking for office permission forms
  - students already taking advanced guitar
  - teachers choosing music textbooks
correctAnswer: students who might join the lessons
explanation: The flyer invites students to join after-school lessons.
hint: Ask who the message is trying to reach.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q03_purpose_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What is the likely purpose of a poster that says, 'Bring a reusable water bottle to reduce plastic waste'?
choices:
  - to persuade people to bring reusable bottles
  - to entertain with a fictional story
  - to define every kind of plastic
  - to describe a basketball game
correctAnswer: to persuade people to bring reusable bottles
explanation: The poster asks readers to take an action.
hint: Look for what the source wants readers to do.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q04_bias_not_false
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement about bias is accurate?
choices:
  - Bias means a source has a slant or preference that readers should evaluate
  - Bias means every fact in a source is false
  - Bias means the source has no audience
  - Bias means the source is always a dictionary
correctAnswer: Bias means a source has a slant or preference that readers should evaluate
explanation: Bias affects presentation, but readers still evaluate evidence instead of automatically rejecting everything.
hint: Bias is about slant, not automatic falsehood.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q05_perspective_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the source context.
passageTitle: Two School Lunch Comments
passage: |
  A student athlete says the new lunch menu needs more filling meals. The school nurse says the menu has more vegetables than before.
question: What does perspective explain here?
choices:
  - Why people in different roles may notice different parts of the menu
  - Which person is lying
  - How many vegetables are in every meal
  - The exact price of lunch
correctAnswer: Why people in different roles may notice different parts of the menu
explanation: Their roles shape what each person emphasizes.
hint: Perspective is viewpoint shaped by position or experience.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q06_explain_bias
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A club flyer says, 'Our club is the only fun choice after school.' Which explanation best shows why this wording may show bias?
choices:
  - The phrase 'only fun choice' shows a one-sided preference for this club over other clubs.
  - The wording is unbiased because flyers can never have a point of view.
  - The wording is biased only because it is about after-school activities.
  - The phrase proves every other club is boring and does not need checking.
correctAnswer: The phrase 'only fun choice' shows a one-sided preference for this club over other clubs.
explanation: The wording favors one club strongly and does not fairly consider other choices.
hint: Look for loaded or one-sided words.
```
