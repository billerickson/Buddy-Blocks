## Teaching Goal

Distinguish proof from thinking and from the reasoning that connects them.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify evidence, inference, reason, and explanation in a short response.

## Misconception Checks

Evidence and reason are identical; inference is a wild guess; explanation means repeat the evidence.

## Teaching Note

Use a three-part frame: "I infer __ because the text says __. This shows __."

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l02_q01_evidence_choice
type: passage-question
prompt: Choose the best evidence.
passageTitle: After Practice
passage: |
  Luis stayed after practice to take ten more free throws. The next day, he arrived early and practiced again before the gym opened.
question: Which evidence best supports the inference that Luis is determined?
choices:
  - He stayed after practice and arrived early the next day
  - The gym opened the next day
  - He plays basketball
  - There were free throws
correctAnswer: He stayed after practice and arrived early the next day
explanation: Those details show repeated effort, which supports the inference that he is determined.
hint: Look for the detail that proves effort over time.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q02_label_roles
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each term to its role in a reading response.
pairs:
  - left: evidence
    right: the detail from the text
  - left: inference
    right: what the reader figures out
  - left: explanation
    right: how the evidence supports the idea
  - left: reason
    right: why the idea makes sense
explanation: These words work together, but they do not name the same thing.
hint: Separate what the text says from what the reader thinks.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q03_sentence_frame
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the evidence-based response.
parts:
  - "I "
  - " that the trail is difficult because the text "
  - " hikers had to climb over rocks."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: infer
    choices:
      - infer
      - copy
      - define
  - correctAnswer: says
    choices:
      - says
      - guesses
      - hides
explanation: The frame separates the reader's inference from the text evidence.
hint: Use the word for figuring out, then the word that introduces evidence.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q04_inference_not_guess
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement best describes an inference?
choices:
  - A conclusion based on evidence and reasoning
  - A random guess with no support
  - A copied sentence from the text
  - A dictionary definition
correctAnswer: A conclusion based on evidence and reasoning
explanation: An inference goes beyond exact words, but it must still be supported by evidence.
hint: An inference is not stated directly, but it is not random.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q05_fix_evidence_reason
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which response separates evidence and explanation?
choices:
  - My evidence is that her hands shake. This shows she is nervous.
  - My evidence is that the character is nervous because she is nervous.
  - The character is nervous because nervous means nervous.
  - I infer she is nervous because I infer it.
correctAnswer: My evidence is that her hands shake. This shows she is nervous.
explanation: The corrected response uses a text detail as evidence and explains the inference.
hint: Use something the text shows, not the same idea repeated.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q06_short_inference
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and choose the response that makes an inference with evidence.
passageTitle: A Cold Morning
passage: |
  The sidewalk sparkled, and Mia pulled her scarf tighter.
question: Which response includes both an inference and evidence?
choices:
  - It is probably cold because Mia pulled her scarf tighter and the sidewalk sparkled.
  - Mia has a scarf, and the sidewalk is outside.
  - It is probably summer because the sidewalk sparkled.
  - The weather is cold because I like snowy days.
correctAnswer: It is probably cold because Mia pulled her scarf tighter and the sidewalk sparkled.
explanation: The scarf and sparkling sidewalk suggest cold weather, likely ice or frost.
hint: Choose the answer that figures something out and names sentence clues.
```
