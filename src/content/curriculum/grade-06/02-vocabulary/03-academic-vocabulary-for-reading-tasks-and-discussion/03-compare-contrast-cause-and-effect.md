## Teaching Goal

Use relationship vocabulary to track how ideas are alike, different, or connected by cause and effect.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose and use compare, contrast, cause, effect, sequence, and relationship terms in context.

## Misconception Checks

Compare means only similarities; contrast means any disagreement; cause and effect can be reversed.

## Teaching Note

Use a simple chart with four relationship types and one example sentence for each.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l03_q01_relationship_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each relationship word to what it signals.
pairs:
  - left: compare
    right: show similarities
  - left: contrast
    right: show differences
  - left: cause
    right: why something happens
  - left: effect
    right: what happens as a result
explanation: Relationship words tell how ideas are connected.
hint: Cause comes before the result; effect is the result.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q02_cause_effect
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the sentence 'Because the road flooded, the bus took a different route,' what is the effect?
choices:
  - the bus took a different route
  - the road flooded
  - because
  - the sentence is about a bus
correctAnswer: the bus took a different route
explanation: The flooded road is the cause. The changed route is what happened as a result.
hint: Ask what happened because of the flooded road.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q03_signal_word_cloze
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the relationship word that fits.
sentenceBefore: The two articles both discuss recycling, but they
sentenceAfter: in their opinions about plastic bags.
choices:
  - differ
  - repeat
  - cause
  - summarize
correctAnswer: differ
explanation: The word but signals contrast, so differ fits.
hint: Look at the signal word but.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q04_sequence_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the sequence words in an order that makes sense.
items:
  - finally
  - first
  - next
  - then
correctOrder:
  - first
  - next
  - then
  - finally
explanation: Sequence words help readers follow the order of events or steps.
hint: Start with the word that begins a process.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q05_compare_or_contrast
type: passage-question
prompt: Identify the relationship.
passageTitle: Two Notes
passage: |
  Both notes ask students to bring a notebook. However, the science note asks for safety goggles, while the history note asks for a map.
question: Which relationship is strongest in the second sentence?
choices:
  - contrast
  - cause and effect
  - definition
  - sequence
correctAnswer: contrast
explanation: However and while show differences between the two notes.
hint: Look for words that show difference.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q06_explain_relation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which sentence uses cause and effect to connect these ideas: 'The power went out. The class used flashlights'?"
choices:
  - Because the power went out, the class used flashlights to see.
  - Because the class used flashlights, the power went out.
  - The power went out, and the class used flashlights.
  - The class used flashlights, but the power outage did not matter.
correctAnswer: Because the power went out, the class used flashlights to see.
explanation: A cause/effect sentence makes the relationship between the two events clear.
hint: The cause is the power outage; the effect is using flashlights.
```
