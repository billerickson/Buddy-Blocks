## Teaching Goal

Teach academic discussion words as purposeful moves in a conversation.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose lines that clarify, elaborate, qualify, challenge, or synthesize respectfully.

## Misconception Checks

Challenge means attack; qualify means make an answer weaker; synthesize means repeat the last speaker.

## Teaching Note

Model one claim and several possible next turns, naming the purpose of each.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l04_q01_discussion_move_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each discussion move to its purpose.
pairs:
  - left: clarify
    right: make an idea clearer
  - left: elaborate
    right: add more detail
  - left: qualify
    right: limit or adjust a statement
  - left: synthesize
    right: combine ideas into a new whole
explanation: Academic discussion words name what a speaker is doing with ideas.
hint: Think about the goal of each move.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q02_dialogue_clarify
type: dialogue-builder
prompt: Choose the response that clarifies.
turns:
  - speaker: Ava
    line: The source is useful, but only partly.
  - speaker: Noah
    line: Can you...
choices:
  - explain which part is useful?
  - change the topic?
  - repeat my answer instead?
  - stop using evidence?
correctAnswer: explain which part is useful?
explanation: Clarifying asks for a clearer or more specific explanation.
hint: A clarify move should make the idea easier to understand.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q03_challenge_respectfully
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which line respectfully challenges an idea?
choices:
  - I see your point, but what evidence shows the source is recent?
  - That is a terrible idea.
  - I do not have to explain my answer.
  - Everyone should just agree with me.
correctAnswer: I see your point, but what evidence shows the source is recent?
explanation: A respectful challenge questions an idea using evidence, not insults.
hint: Look for a line that questions the idea and stays academic.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q04_qualify_cloze
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the discussion move. 'That is true for the first paragraph, but not for the whole article.'
sentenceBefore: This speaker is trying to
sentenceAfter: the claim.
choices:
  - qualify
  - copy
  - erase
  - pronounce
correctAnswer: qualify
explanation: Qualify means limit or adjust a claim so it is more accurate.
hint: The speaker says the claim is partly true, not always true.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q05_synthesize_dialogue
type: dialogue-builder
prompt: Choose the response that synthesizes two ideas.
turns:
  - speaker: Mia
    line: The graph shows more students rode bikes in May.
  - speaker: Owen
    line: The article says the school added bike racks in April.
  - speaker: Sam
    line: So...
choices:
  - the new bike racks may have helped more students ride bikes in May.
  - Mia said graph and Owen said article.
  - bike racks are made of metal.
  - May comes after April on the calendar.
correctAnswer: the new bike racks may have helped more students ride bikes in May.
explanation: Synthesize means combine ideas into a connected understanding.
hint: The best answer connects the graph and article.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q06_use_discussion_move
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which sentence elaborates on this idea: 'The character is careful'?"
choices:
  - The character is careful because she checks the map twice before leaving.
  - The character is careful.
  - The character is careful because careful characters are careful.
  - The character is careful, but the sentence does not need more detail.
correctAnswer: The character is careful because she checks the map twice before leaving.
explanation: Elaborating adds detail that develops the original idea.
hint: Look for the sentence that adds a connected detail or example.
```
