## Teaching Goal

Connect author word choice to tone, mood, and sensory imagery.

## Student Outcome

The student can distinguish tone from mood and identify imagery that supports a feeling or attitude.

## Misconception Checks

Tone and mood are interchangeable; imagery means any adjective; one word can prove tone without context.

## Teaching Note

Read a two-sentence description and ask how the author's word choice makes the scene feel.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l04_q01_tone_mood_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each term to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: tone
    right: author's attitude
  - left: mood
    right: feeling created for the reader
  - left: imagery
    right: sensory language
  - left: word choice
    right: the specific words an author uses
explanation: Tone, mood, and imagery are connected, but they name different parts of author craft.
hint: Tone belongs to the author; mood is what the reader feels.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q02_tone_choice
type: passage-question
prompt: Choose the best tone word.
passageTitle: A Broken Window
passage: |
  I stared at the cracked window and sighed. Of course the ball had found the one fragile pane in the whole yard.
question: Which word best describes the tone?
choices:
  - frustrated
  - joyful
  - proud
  - curious
correctAnswer: frustrated
explanation: The sigh and the phrase 'Of course' suggest the speaker is frustrated.
hint: Look for words that show the speaker's attitude.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q03_mood_evidence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which words best create a peaceful mood?
choices:
  - soft rain tapped the leaves
  - sirens screamed in the street
  - doors slammed down the hall
  - the crowd shoved forward
correctAnswer: soft rain tapped the leaves
explanation: Soft rain and leaves create a calm, peaceful feeling.
hint: Mood is the feeling the scene creates for the reader.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q05_tone_vs_mood
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement is correct?
choices:
  - Tone is the author's attitude; mood is the feeling created for the reader.
  - Tone and mood always mean the same thing.
  - Mood is only about weather.
  - Tone is only the loudness of a sound in reading.
correctAnswer: Tone is the author's attitude; mood is the feeling created for the reader.
explanation: In literary vocabulary, tone and mood are related but different.
hint: Author attitude and reader feeling are not the same role.
```
