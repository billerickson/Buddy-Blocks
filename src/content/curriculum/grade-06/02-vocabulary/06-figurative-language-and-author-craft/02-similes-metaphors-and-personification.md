## Teaching Goal

Interpret comparison and human-quality language as tools for meaning and effect.

## Student Outcome

The student can interpret a simile, metaphor, or personification in a short context.

## Misconception Checks

Simile and metaphor labels matter more than meaning; personification means any animal action; comparisons must match in every way.

## Teaching Note

Ask what quality is shared in "The hallway was a river of students."

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l02_q01_device_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each example to the kind of figurative language.
pairs:
  - left: The clouds were like gray blankets.
    right: simile
  - left: The classroom was a beehive of activity.
    right: metaphor
  - left: The old stairs groaned.
    right: personification
  - left: The joke spread like wildfire.
    right: simile with like
explanation: "Labels help only when they lead to meaning: comparison or human qualities."
hint: Look for like/as, direct comparison, or human action.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q02_metaphor_meaning
type: passage-question
prompt: Interpret the metaphor.
passageTitle: Passing Period
passage: |
  When the bell rang, the hallway became a river of students flowing toward the cafeteria.
question: What does the metaphor suggest?
choices:
  - Many students moved together in one direction
  - The hallway was filled with actual water
  - Students were silent and still
  - The cafeteria was closed
correctAnswer: Many students moved together in one direction
explanation: The river metaphor emphasizes movement, flow, and a crowd.
hint: Ask what quality of a river is being compared to the students.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q03_personification
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses personification?
choices:
  - The wind whispered through the trees.
  - The wind moved through the trees.
  - The wind was cold.
  - The wind blew from the north.
correctAnswer: The wind whispered through the trees.
explanation: Whispered gives the wind a human action.
hint: Personification gives a nonhuman thing a human quality or action.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q04_quality_compared
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the shared quality in the simile.
sentenceBefore: In 'Her idea was as bright as a lighthouse,' bright suggests the idea was
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - clear and helpful
  - made of glass
  - near the ocean
  - painted white
correctAnswer: clear and helpful
explanation: The simile borrows the lighthouse's helpful brightness, not every detail of a lighthouse.
hint: A comparison usually shares one important quality, not all qualities.
```

