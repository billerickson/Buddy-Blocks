## Teaching Goal

Name common research actions and source features students need for Grade 6 inquiry.

## Student Outcome

The student can use inquiry, keyword, source, note, question, topic, and conclusion vocabulary accurately.

## Misconception Checks

Research means copying facts from a website; a keyword is a full question; a conclusion is just the last sentence.

## Teaching Note

Use one research question and show how it becomes keywords, notes, and a conclusion.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l01_q01_research_terms_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each research term to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: inquiry
    right: a process of asking and investigating questions
  - left: keyword
    right: an important search word or phrase
  - left: source
    right: where information comes from
  - left: note
    right: a brief record of useful information
explanation: Research vocabulary names actions and tools for finding and using information.
hint: Think about what a researcher does first, searches with, reads, and records.
```

```question
key: u08_l01_q02_keyword_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Research question: 'How do school gardens affect student learning?' Which keyword set is most useful?"
choices:
  - school gardens student learning
  - I wonder if gardens are nice
  - green fun outside
  - student school how do affect
correctAnswer: school gardens student learning
explanation: Good keywords capture the main ideas without extra question words.
hint: Choose the important nouns and idea words.
```

```question
key: u08_l01_q03_source_note
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the source note.
passageTitle: Source Card
passage: |
  Title: Trees Around Our School
  Author: City Parks Department
  Date: 2025
  Topic: Tree types and shade in local schoolyards
question: For a report on shade trees near schools, what kind of information does this source card provide?
choices:
  - source details that help judge usefulness
  - a finished conclusion paragraph
  - an idiom definition
  - a fiction plot summary
correctAnswer: source details that help judge usefulness
explanation: The title, author, date, and topic help a researcher decide whether the source fits the question.
hint: Look at what the card records.
```
