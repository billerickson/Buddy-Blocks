## Teaching Goal

Teach portfolio entries as durable word-learning tools rather than copied dictionary notes.

## Student Outcome

The student can complete a portfolio entry with context, morphology, fitting definition, connotation, example, non-example, and original sentence.

## Misconception Checks

A copied definition is enough; examples and non-examples are optional decoration; original sentences can ignore the word's actual meaning.

## Teaching Note

Show a weak portfolio card and revise it into a learning tool with evidence.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l03_q01_portfolio_parts_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each portfolio part to its purpose.
pairs:
  - left: context
    right: where the word appeared
  - left: morphology
    right: useful word parts
  - left: non-example
    right: what the word does not mean
  - left: original sentence
    right: student's own accurate use
explanation: A portfolio entry should help a student remember and use the word later.
hint: Think about what each part records for future learning.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q02_best_example
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Portfolio word: scarce, meaning 'not enough or hard to find.' Which is the best example?"
choices:
  - Clean water was scarce after the long drought.
  - The shelves were full of every kind of notebook.
  - The dog barked loudly at the gate.
  - The painting used bright blue paint.
correctAnswer: Clean water was scarce after the long drought.
explanation: A drought can make water hard to find, so the sentence shows scarce accurately.
hint: Choose the sentence that shows not enough of something.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q03_nonexample_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Portfolio word: fragile, meaning easily broken. Which is the best non-example?"
choices:
  - a steel bridge built to hold heavy trucks
  - a thin glass ornament
  - a dry leaf that crumbles
  - a cracked eggshell
correctAnswer: a steel bridge built to hold heavy trucks
explanation: A strong steel bridge is not easily broken, so it is a non-example of fragile.
hint: A non-example should clearly not fit the meaning.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q04_entry_cloze

parts:
  - "Word part clue: bene means "
  - ". Fitting definition: "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: good
    acceptedAnswers:
      - good
      - well
  - correctAnswer: helpful
    acceptedAnswers:
      - helpful
      - producing good results
      - good for someone or something
explanation: Beneficial means helpful or producing good results, and bene gives a good/well clue.
hint: Use the root clue from Unit 2.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q05_fix_original_sentence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence shows the meaning of accurate?
choices:
  - My answer was accurate because all the facts were correct.
  - My answer was accurate because it was very colorful.
  - My answer was accurate because it was written quickly.
  - My answer was accurate because the paper was neat.
correctAnswer: My answer was accurate because all the facts were correct.
explanation: Accurate means correct or exact, not colorful.
hint: Use a context involving correctness or exactness.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q06_original_sentence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which original sentence best shows the meaning of verify?
choices:
  - I checked two sources to verify the date of the event.
  - I guessed the date to verify it.
  - I decorated the poster to verify it.
  - I used verify as the title of my story.
correctAnswer: I checked two sources to verify the date of the event.
explanation: Verify means check or confirm that something is true or accurate.
hint: Think about checking an answer, date, source, or fact.
```
