## Teaching Goal

Teach students vocabulary for restating, combining, and crediting ideas.

## Student Outcome

The student can distinguish paraphrase, quote, cite, and synthesize in short research contexts.

## Misconception Checks

Paraphrase means changing a few words; synthesize means list facts separately; citing is only punctuation.

## Teaching Note

Compare copied, weakly changed, and truly paraphrased versions of the same source sentence.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l02_q01_action_terms_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each research action to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: paraphrase
    right: restate an idea in your own words
  - left: quote
    right: use the source's exact words
  - left: cite
    right: give credit to the source
  - left: synthesize
    right: combine ideas from more than one place
explanation: These actions help writers use sources responsibly and clearly.
hint: Paraphrase changes wording; quote keeps exact wording.
```

```question
key: u08_l02_q02_best_paraphrase
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Source sentence: 'Honeybees communicate the location of food through movements called waggle dances.' Which is the best paraphrase?"
choices:
  - Honeybees use special movements, called waggle dances, to show other bees where food is.
  - Honeybees communicate the location of food through movements called waggle dances.
  - Bees dance because food is fun.
  - Honeybees move.
correctAnswer: Honeybees use special movements, called waggle dances, to show other bees where food is.
explanation: The best paraphrase keeps the meaning but changes the wording and structure.
hint: A paraphrase should not copy the sentence or change the meaning.
```

```question
key: u08_l02_q03_fix_weak_paraphrase
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Source: 'Deserts receive very little rainfall.' Which paraphrase uses your own words?"
choices:
  - Deserts are dry places because they do not get much rain.
  - Deserts get very little rainfall.
  - Deserts receive very little rainfall.
  - Deserts receive very much rainfall.
correctAnswer: Deserts are dry places because they do not get much rain.
explanation: A stronger paraphrase changes wording and structure while keeping the meaning.
hint: Do more than swap receive for get.
```

```question
key: u08_l02_q04_synthesis_choice
type: passage-question
prompt: Choose the synthesis.
passageTitle: Two Notes
passage: |
  Note 1: School gardens can teach students how plants grow.
  Note 2: School gardens can provide vegetables for cooking lessons.
question: Which sentence synthesizes the notes?
choices:
  - School gardens can support learning in both science and cooking.
  - School gardens can teach students how plants grow.
  - School gardens can provide vegetables for cooking lessons.
  - Gardens are outside.
correctAnswer: School gardens can support learning in both science and cooking.
explanation: The synthesis combines the ideas from both notes.
hint: Look for the answer that joins both notes into one idea.
```

```question
key: u08_l02_q05_cite_reason
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why should a writer cite a source?
choices:
  - To give credit and show where information came from
  - To make every sentence longer
  - To avoid understanding the source
  - To turn a paraphrase into an opinion
correctAnswer: To give credit and show where information came from
explanation: Citing helps readers trace information and gives credit to the source.
hint: Cite is connected to credit and source.
```

```question
key: u08_l02_q06_write_synthesis
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Note 1: 'Walking to school can add exercise to a student's day.' Note 2: 'Fewer car trips can reduce traffic near school.' Which sentence synthesizes the notes?"
choices:
  - Walking to school can help students get exercise while also reducing traffic near the school.
  - Walking to school can add exercise to a student's day.
  - Fewer car trips can reduce traffic near school.
  - Traffic near school can be noisy in the morning.
correctAnswer: Walking to school can help students get exercise while also reducing traffic near the school.
explanation: A synthesis joins information from more than one note into a larger idea.
hint: Use a connector such as while, and, or also.
```
