## Teaching Goal

Have students choose among the full set of vocabulary strategies in unfamiliar mixed contexts.

## Student Outcome

The student can infer, verify, explain, revise, and apply words across context, morphology, reference, connotation, figurative, source, and research tasks.

## Misconception Checks

Each strategy works only in its original unit; one clue is enough for every word; transfer means memorizing old answers.

## Teaching Note

Frame the lesson as a mixed strategy challenge: "What kind of word problem is this?"

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l04_q01_strategy_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word problem to the best first strategy.
pairs:
  - left: A word has the prefix pre-
    right: check word parts
  - left: A word has four dictionary definitions
    right: use context and part of speech
  - left: Two headlines use request and demand
    right: compare connotation and framing
  - left: A phrase says the room turned icy
    right: interpret figurative meaning
explanation: Independent word learners choose the strategy that fits the problem.
hint: Ask what kind of evidence the problem gives you.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q02_mixed_context_word
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the best vocabulary strategy.
passageTitle: A New Plan
passage: |
  The original schedule was impractical because buses could not arrive before sunrise. The committee designed a feasible plan with a later start.
question: What does feasible most nearly mean?
choices:
  - possible to do
  - written in code
  - very old
  - full of jokes
correctAnswer: possible to do
explanation: The contrast with impractical and the later workable plan show feasible means possible to do.
hint: Use the contrast between impractical and the new plan.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q03_morphology_transfer
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the word transcription, the root script most helps you infer a connection to which idea?
choices:
  - writing
  - water
  - sound volume
  - speed
correctAnswer: writing
explanation: Script means write. A transcription is a written version of spoken words or other text.
hint: Think of manuscript and inscription.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q04_word_choice_transfer
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which revision best fits a neutral report?
choices:
  - The students requested more time for lunch.
  - The students whined for more time for lunch.
  - The students heroically battled for more lunch.
  - The students threw a ridiculous lunch fit.
correctAnswer: The students requested more time for lunch.
explanation: Requested is the most neutral verb. The other choices frame the students with stronger judgment.
hint: Choose the least loaded word.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q05_reference_transfer
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the definition of volume that fits a library sentence.
sentenceBefore: In 'The library ordered the final volume of the series,' volume means
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - a book in a set
  - how loud a sound is
  - the amount of space something takes up
  - a measure of liquid only
correctAnswer: a book in a set
explanation: The library and series context points to the book meaning of volume.
hint: "Use the subject context: library and series."
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q06_cumulative_explanation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Read: 'The speaker's cautious tone helped the audience trust the warning.' Which answer gives the meaning, strategy, and evidence?"
choices:
  - Cautious means careful; context and meaning fit help because a careful tone would make a warning trustworthy.
  - Cautious means careful; the word part alone is enough, so the warning context does not matter.
  - Cautious means loud; the evidence is that the sentence mentions an audience.
  - Cautious means funny; the best strategy is to ignore the sentence.
correctAnswer: Cautious means careful; context and meaning fit help because a careful tone would make a warning trustworthy.
explanation: Cautious means careful or showing care to avoid mistakes. The warning and trust clues support that meaning.
hint: Ask what kind of tone would help people trust a warning.
```
