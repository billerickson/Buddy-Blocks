## Teaching Goal

Interpret common math words whose technical meanings are narrower than everyday meanings.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain words such as factor, variable, expression, model, estimate, and equivalent in math contexts.

## Misconception Checks

Factor means anything that affects something; variable means something "varies" in a vague way; model only means a physical object.

## Teaching Note

Compare the everyday word factor with factor in "3 is a factor of 12."

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l01_q01_math_term_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each math term to its precise meaning.
pairs:
  - left: factor
    right: a number multiplied to get a product
  - left: variable
    right: a symbol for a value that can change or is unknown
  - left: expression
    right: numbers, symbols, and operations without an equals sign
  - left: equivalent
    right: having the same value
explanation: Math words often need more exact meanings than their everyday uses.
hint: Think about how each word works in equations or number relationships.
```

```question

  - The factor was very exciting.
  - I factor my backpack before school.
correctAnswer: Three is a factor of twelve because 3 x 4 = 12.
explanation: The math meaning of factor is a number that is multiplied to get a product.
```
