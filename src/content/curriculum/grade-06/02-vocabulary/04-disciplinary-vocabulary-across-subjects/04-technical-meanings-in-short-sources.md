## Teaching Goal

Help students choose technical meanings that fit mixed short sources, diagrams, and glossary entries.

## Student Outcome

The student can decide whether a word is being used in an everyday or discipline-specific way.

## Misconception Checks

A familiar everyday meaning is always enough; glossary definitions can be copied without checking the source; visuals are optional.

## Teaching Note

Present a word like model in a science source and a literature sentence, then compare meanings.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l04_q01_technical_source
type: passage-question
prompt: Choose the technical meaning that fits.
passageTitle: Science Diagram
passage: |
  The diagram shows a model of the water cycle. Arrows show how water moves from oceans to clouds and back to land.
question: What does model mean here?
choices:
  - a representation that helps explain a process
  - a person wearing clothes for photos
  - a perfect copy of every detail on Earth
  - a rule that must be followed
correctAnswer: a representation that helps explain a process
explanation: A science model represents the water cycle so it is easier to understand.
hint: The diagram explains a process.
```

```question
key: u04_l04_q02_everyday_vs_technical
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses volume as a science or math term?
choices:
  - The graduated cylinder measured the liquid's volume.
  - Please lower the volume on the speaker.
  - The library has the second volume of the novel.
  - His voice had a lot of volume.
correctAnswer: The graduated cylinder measured the liquid's volume.
explanation: In science and math, volume can mean the amount of space something takes up.
hint: Look for a measurement context.
```

```question
key: u04_l04_q03_glossary_fit
type: fill-blank
prompt: A glossary says scale can mean 'a set of marks used for measuring' or 'the size relationship between a map and a real place.' In a map caption, scale most likely means
sentenceBefore: In this map context, scale means
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - the size relationship between a map and a real place
  - a set of marks used for measuring weight
  - a musical pattern
  - a fish covering
correctAnswer: the size relationship between a map and a real place
explanation: The subject context is a map, so the map definition fits.
hint: "Use the source type: a map caption."
```

```question
key: u04_l04_q05_correct_technical_meaning
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the definition choice.
sentence: In 'The graph shows a positive trend,' trend means a popular fashion style.
acceptedAnswers:
  - In 'The graph shows a positive trend,' trend means a general pattern or direction in the data.
  - Trend means a pattern in the data.
  - In this graph context, trend means the direction the data are moving.
explanation: In a graph, trend means a pattern or direction in data, not a fashion style.
hint: Use the graph context.
```

  - the basic unit of a living thing, because biology and plants are life science contexts
  - a spreadsheet box, because articles can include tables

explanation: The subject context tells which technical definition fits.
