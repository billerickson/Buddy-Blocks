## Teaching Goal

Teach students to use sentence and paragraph clues while recognizing that context may narrow rather than fully define a word.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a useful context clue and avoid unsupported over-specific guesses.

## Misconception Checks

Any nearby word is a clue; context always provides an exact definition; a familiar topic makes every guess valid.

## Teaching Note

Compare two possible meanings for one word and ask which is actually supported by the sentence.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l02_q01_context_meaning
type: passage-question
prompt: Use context to choose the best meaning.
passageTitle: A Fair Judge
passage: |
  The judge was impartial. She listened carefully to both teams and did not favor her own school.
question: What does impartial most nearly mean?
choices:
  - fair
  - excited
  - popular
  - confused
correctAnswer: fair
explanation: The clue 'did not favor her own school' supports the meaning fair or unbiased.
hint: Look at what the judge did with both teams.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q02_name_the_clue
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the sentence 'The judge was impartial; she did not favor either side,' which words are the strongest clue for impartial?
choices:
  - did not favor either side
  - The judge
  - was
  - the sentence has a semicolon
correctAnswer: did not favor either side
explanation: That phrase explains what impartial looks like in the sentence.
hint: Find the phrase that tells what impartial means, not just where it appears.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q03_context_limits
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Read the sentence: 'The machine emitted a faint hum.' What is the safest context-supported meaning of faint?"
choices:
  - soft or weak
  - blue and shiny
  - broken beyond repair
  - made in another country
correctAnswer: soft or weak
explanation: Hum is a sound, and faint describes a sound that is not strong.
hint: Use only what the sentence supports.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q04_clue_type
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the clue type. 'Unlike the noisy hallway, the library was tranquil.'
sentenceBefore: The word unlike signals a
sentenceAfter: clue.
choices:
  - contrast
  - example
  - definition
  - part-of-speech
correctAnswer: contrast
explanation: Unlike shows that noisy hallway and tranquil library are being contrasted.
hint: Ask whether the sentence shows similarity or difference.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q05_explain_scarce
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the sentence 'After weeks without rain, clean water became scarce,' which explanation is best supported?
choices:
  - Scarce means not enough or hard to find; the clue is 'weeks without rain.'
  - Scarce means clean; the clue is 'clean water.'
  - Scarce means raining often; the clue is 'without rain.'
  - Scarce means easy to find; the clue is that water is familiar.
correctAnswer: Scarce means not enough or hard to find; the clue is 'weeks without rain.'
explanation: Weeks without rain would make water limited, so scarce means not enough or hard to find.
hint: Pick the answer that gives both a meaning and a sentence clue.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q06_unsupported_guess
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which answer is an unsupported overguess for the word frigid in 'The frigid wind stung our faces'?
choices:
  - The wind was exactly 10 degrees
  - The wind was very cold
  - The wind felt uncomfortable
  - The wind stung their faces
correctAnswer: The wind was exactly 10 degrees
explanation: The context supports cold and uncomfortable, but it does not give an exact temperature.
hint: Avoid adding a detail that the sentence never gives.
```
