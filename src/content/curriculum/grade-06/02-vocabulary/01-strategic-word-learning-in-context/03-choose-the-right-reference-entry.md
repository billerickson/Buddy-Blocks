## Teaching Goal

Help students select the definition, part of speech, or glossary entry that matches the context.

## Student Outcome

The student can use part of speech and sentence meaning to choose the correct reference entry.

## Misconception Checks

The first definition is always correct; every definition for a word fits every sentence; part of speech can be ignored.

## Teaching Note

Show a two-entry word such as conduct and test each definition in a sentence.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_conduct_entry
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Choose the reference meaning that fits: 'The scientist will conduct the experiment on Friday.'"
choices:
  - to lead or carry out
  - behavior
  - the way heat or electricity moves through something
  - a musical performance
correctAnswer: to lead or carry out
explanation: In this sentence, conduct is a verb meaning to carry out an experiment.
hint: Ask what the scientist will do.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q02_pos_clues
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each sentence clue to the part of speech it suggests.
pairs:
  - left: The word follows the article 'a'
    right: noun
  - left: The word describes a noun
    right: adjective
  - left: The word shows an action
    right: verb
  - left: The word describes how an action happens
    right: adverb
explanation: Part-of-speech clues help you choose the correct reference entry.
hint: Look at what job the word is doing in the sentence.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q03_complex_entry
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which entry fits the sentence: 'The apartment complex has three buildings'?"
choices:
  - "noun: a group of related buildings"
  - "adjective: not simple"
  - "verb: to join together"
  - "adjective: easy to understand"
correctAnswer: "noun: a group of related buildings"
explanation: The word follows apartment and names a place, so the noun entry fits.
hint: Does complex describe something, or does it name a thing?
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q04_draft_definition
type: fill-blank
prompt: "Choose the meaning of draft that fits: 'Nina revised the first draft of her essay.'"
sentenceBefore: In this sentence, draft means
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - an early version of writing
  - a current of air
  - to choose someone for a team
  - a deep drink
correctAnswer: an early version of writing
explanation: The clue revised and essay shows that draft means an early version of writing.
hint: Use the words revised and essay.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q05_correct_bad_entry
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the bad reference choice.
sentence: In 'The team will draft a plan,' draft means 'a current of air.'
acceptedAnswers:
  - In 'The team will draft a plan,' draft means to write or create an early version.
  - Draft means to write an early version of the plan.
  - In this sentence, draft means to create an early version.
explanation: The team is creating a plan, so the verb meaning 'write or create an early version' fits.
hint: Ask what action the team will do to the plan.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q06_explain_entry_fit
type: multiple-choice
prompt: The word minute can mean 'sixty seconds' or 'very small.' In 'The artist painted minute details,' which explanation best fits?
choices:
  - Minute means very small because it describes the details in a painting.
  - Minute means sixty seconds because that is the more familiar meaning.
  - Minute means sixty seconds because details is a noun.
  - Minute means very small because it is always the first dictionary definition.
correctAnswer: Minute means very small because it describes the details in a painting.
explanation: Minute describes details, so the adjective meaning 'very small' fits.
hint: Notice whether minute names time or describes details.
```
