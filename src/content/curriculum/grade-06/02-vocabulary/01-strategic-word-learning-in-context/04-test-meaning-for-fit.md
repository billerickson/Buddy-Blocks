## Teaching Goal

Teach students to substitute a proposed meaning into the sentence and revise when the meaning does not fit.

## Student Outcome

The student can decide whether a meaning fits the context and improve an inaccurate first guess.

## Misconception Checks

A partly related definition is close enough; a word-part clue cannot be contradicted; checking means repeating the same guess.

## Teaching Note

Write a sentence with a wrong substitution and let students hear how it breaks the meaning.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l04_q01_fit_reluctant
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which meaning best fits the sentence: 'Maya was reluctant to speak first, so she waited until two classmates had shared'?"
choices:
  - not eager or willing
  - very loud
  - already finished
  - full of energy
correctAnswer: not eager or willing
explanation: Waiting until others spoke shows Maya was not eager to speak first.
hint: Substitute each meaning into the sentence and see which one makes sense.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q02_fix_bad_substitution
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "In 'The route was direct, with no turns,' which meaning of direct fits?"
choices:
  - Straight or without extra turns
  - Confusing
  - Expensive
  - Crowded
correctAnswer: Straight or without extra turns
explanation: The clue 'with no turns' shows direct means straight or not roundabout.
hint: Use the phrase 'with no turns' to test the meaning.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q03_replacement_word

acceptedAnswers:
  - careful
  - watchful
  - careful driver

hint: The icy road explains why the driver would not be reckless.

choices:
  - possible to do
  - expensive to buy
  - hard to remember

explanation: The paragraph explains whether the meeting plan can actually work.
hint: Ask which plan can be arranged.
```
