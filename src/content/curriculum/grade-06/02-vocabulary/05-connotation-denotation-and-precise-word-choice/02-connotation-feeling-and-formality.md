## Teaching Goal

Teach connotation as association, feeling, formality, and social effect beyond denotation.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify positive, negative, neutral, formal, and informal connotations in context.

## Misconception Checks

Connotation is only positive or negative; informal words are always wrong; formal words are always better.

## Teaching Note

Compare childlike, youthful, and immature as words with related denotations but different effects.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l02_q01_connotation_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word has the most negative connotation?
choices:
  - stubborn
  - determined
  - persistent
  - committed
correctAnswer: stubborn
explanation: All four words can relate to not giving up, but stubborn suggests an unreasonable refusal to change.
hint: Look for the word that makes the trait sound like a problem.
```

  - kids
  - youngsters
  - minors
correctOrder:
  - kids
  - youngsters
  - children
  - minors
