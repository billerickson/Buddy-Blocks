## Teaching Goal

Anchor students in denotation as the direct dictionary meaning of a word.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify denotation and choose definitions that fit context.

## Misconception Checks

Denotation means the same thing as connotation; all definitions in an entry fit equally; denotation includes every feeling a word suggests.

## Teaching Note

Compare the denotation of home and house before discussing associations.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l01_q01_denotation_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word to its denotation.
pairs:
  - left: fragile
    right: easily broken
  - left: ancient
    right: very old
  - left: assist
    right: help
  - left: ordinary
    right: not special or unusual
explanation: Denotation is the direct meaning of a word.
hint: Choose the plain definition, not a feeling about the word.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q02_denotation_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice gives the denotation of aroma?
choices:
  - a smell
  - a pleasant memory
  - something fancy
  - a loud sound
correctAnswer: a smell
explanation: Aroma denotes a smell, though it often has a pleasant connotation.
hint: Look for the basic definition before feelings or associations.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q03_definition_fit
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the direct definition that fits.
sentenceBefore: In 'The solution was simple,' simple denotes
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - not complicated
  - boring and useless
  - very expensive
  - full of color
correctAnswer: not complicated
explanation: The direct meaning of simple is not complicated, even though context can add tone.
hint: Do not add a judgment unless the sentence supports it.
```

```question

  The committee selected a compact design for the playground because the space was small.

choices:
  - taking little space
  - very loud
  - hard to understand
  - made in a hurry
correctAnswer: taking little space
explanation: The clue 'the space was small' supports the direct meaning taking little space.

```

```question
key: u05_l01_q05_denotation_vs_connotation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement best separates denotation from connotation?
choices:
  - Denotation is direct meaning; connotation is the feeling or association a word suggests.
  - Denotation is always negative; connotation is always positive.
  - Denotation is slang; connotation is formal.
  - Denotation and connotation are the same thing.
correctAnswer: Denotation is direct meaning; connotation is the feeling or association a word suggests.
explanation: A word can have a direct definition and also carry associations.
hint: One is definition; the other is effect or association.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q06_explain_denotation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: The words home and residence can both denote a place where someone lives. Which explanation best shows what denotation means here?
choices:
  - Denotation is direct meaning, so both words can mean a place where someone lives.
  - Denotation is the feeling only, so home and residence must feel exactly the same.
  - Denotation means one word is always informal and the other is always wrong.
  - Denotation means the words have no dictionary meaning.
correctAnswer: Denotation is direct meaning, so both words can mean a place where someone lives.
explanation: Both words have a similar denotation even though their connotations can differ.
hint: Focus only on the shared dictionary meaning.
```
