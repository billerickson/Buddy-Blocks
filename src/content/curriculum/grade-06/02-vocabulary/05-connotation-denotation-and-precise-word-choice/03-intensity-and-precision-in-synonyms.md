## Teaching Goal

Help students choose among near-synonyms by strength and exact meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can rank or select words by intensity and precision for a specific context.

## Misconception Checks

All synonyms can replace one another; intense words always improve writing; vague words are safer because they fit many contexts.

## Teaching Note

Build an intensity ladder such as notice, examine, inspect, scrutinize.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l03_q01_intensity_order
type: order-items
prompt: Order the words from least intense to most intense.
items:
  - furious
  - annoyed
  - angry
  - irritated
correctOrder:
  - annoyed
  - irritated
  - angry
  - furious
explanation: The words all show negative feeling, but angry is stronger than irritated and furious is strongest.
hint: Start with the mildest feeling.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q02_precise_verb
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the most precise verb.
sentenceBefore: The scientist did not just look at the sample; she
sentenceAfter: it through a microscope.
choices:
  - examined
  - saw
  - glanced at
  - noticed
correctAnswer: examined
explanation: Examined means looked at carefully, which fits the microscope context.
hint: Use the context clue 'through a microscope.'
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q03_too_intense
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which word is too intense for this sentence: 'I felt ___ when I misplaced my pencil for one minute'?"
choices:
  - devastated
  - annoyed
  - bothered
  - slightly upset
correctAnswer: devastated
explanation: Devastated means extremely upset, which is too strong for briefly misplacing a pencil.
hint: Match the strength of the word to the size of the problem.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q04_vague_revision
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which revision uses a more precise verb for moved fast?
choices:
  - The athlete sprinted across the finish line.
  - The athlete moved fast across the finish line.
  - The athlete went across the finish line.
  - The athlete did across the finish line.
correctAnswer: The athlete sprinted across the finish line.
explanation: Sprinted, raced, or dashed gives a more precise sense of fast movement.
hint: Choose a verb that already includes the idea of moving fast.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q05_context_precision
type: passage-question
prompt: Choose the word that best fits the context.
passageTitle: Museum Rule
passage: |
  The sign reminded visitors not to touch the glass cases because even a small fingerprint could damage the display.
question: Which word best describes the rule?
choices:
  - strict
  - careless
  - playful
  - uncertain
correctAnswer: strict
explanation: The rule is exact and serious because small actions could cause damage.
hint: Think about the reason the sign gives.
```

```question

  - Stroll means walk slowly and calmly, so it tells how they went through the park.
  - Stroll means run quickly, so it makes the action faster.
  - Stroll and go give exactly the same amount of detail.
  - Stroll is more precise only because it sounds more formal.
correctAnswer: Stroll means walk slowly and calmly, so it tells how they went through the park.
explanation: Stroll gives information about the action's speed and mood that go does not give.
```
