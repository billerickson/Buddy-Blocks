## Teaching Goal

Use selected roots to unlock related academic words without turning roots into isolated memorization.

## Student Outcome

The student can use roots such as `scrib/script`, `mit/miss`, `jur/jus`, `bene`, `man`, and `vac` to support meaning.

## Misconception Checks

Same root means same full definition; a root clue is enough by itself; unrelated look-alikes are always root relatives.

## Teaching Note

Build a word web for `scrib/script` with describe, manuscript, inscription, and transcript.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l03_q01_root_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each root to its useful meaning clue.
pairs:
  - left: scrib / script
    right: write
  - left: mit / miss
    right: send
  - left: jur / jus
    right: law or right
  - left: bene
    right: good or well
explanation: These roots appear in many academic words.
hint: Think of describe, submit, justice, and benefit.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q02_script_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the root and context.
passageTitle: Museum Label
passage: |
  The historian studied the inscription carved into the stone wall. The written message named the people who built the monument.
question: What does inscription most nearly mean?
choices:
  - words written or carved on something
  - a person who studies weather
  - a broken piece of stone
  - a law about buildings
correctAnswer: words written or carved on something
explanation: The root script means write, and the passage says the message was carved into stone.
hint: Use both the root and the phrase 'written message.'
```
