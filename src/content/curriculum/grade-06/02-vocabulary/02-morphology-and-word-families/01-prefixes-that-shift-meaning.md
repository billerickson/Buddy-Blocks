## Teaching Goal

Use high-utility prefixes to infer direction, repetition, negation, order, or relationship.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain how a prefix changes the meaning of a base word or academic word.

## Misconception Checks

A prefix always has one exact meaning; `un-`, `in-`, and `non-` are interchangeable; prefix meaning replaces sentence context.

## Teaching Note

Use a small prefix map and show how `pre-` changes view into preview.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_prefix_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each prefix to a useful meaning clue.
pairs:
  - left: pre-
    right: before
  - left: re-
    right: again or back
  - left: sub-
    right: under or below
  - left: inter-
    right: between or among
explanation: These prefixes can guide meaning, but the whole word still needs context.
hint: Think of a familiar word with each prefix, such as preview or interact.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q02_preview_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A teacher says, 'Preview the chapter before reading it closely.' What does preview most likely mean?
choices:
  - look at it before the full reading
  - read it again after finishing
  - hide it under another book
  - argue against its main idea
correctAnswer: look at it before the full reading
explanation: The prefix pre- means before, and the sentence says before reading closely.
hint: Use both the prefix and the phrase 'before reading it closely.'
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q03_prefix_sentence_choice
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the word that fits the sentence.
sentenceBefore: After the first design failed, the team had to
sentenceAfter: the bridge model.
choices:
  - redesign
  - subdesign
  - interdesign
  - predesign
correctAnswer: redesign
explanation: Redesign means design again, which fits after the first design failed.
hint: Which prefix can mean again?
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q04_prefix_limit
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement about prefixes is most accurate?
choices:
  - A prefix gives a clue, but context helps confirm the full meaning
  - A prefix always gives the complete definition
  - A word with a prefix never needs a dictionary
  - Prefixes only matter in spelling lessons
correctAnswer: A prefix gives a clue, but context helps confirm the full meaning
explanation: Prefixes are useful evidence, but full word meaning depends on the whole word and context.
hint: Think about the routine from Unit 1.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q05_transfer_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the prefix and context to answer.
passageTitle: Moving A Seedling
passage: |
  The class decided to transplant the seedling from a small cup into the garden bed, where its roots would have more room.
question: What does transplant most nearly mean?
choices:
  - move from one place to another
  - plant before anything else
  - remove every leaf
  - water again and again
correctAnswer: move from one place to another
explanation: Trans- can mean across or from one place to another, and the seedling moves from the cup into the garden bed.
hint: Use both trans- and the movement described in the sentence.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q06_explain_prefix
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which explanation best shows how the prefix re- helps with reconsider in this sentence: 'After hearing new evidence, Jonah had to reconsider his opinion'?"
choices:
  - Re- means again, and Jonah has to think about his opinion again.
  - Re- means before, and Jonah is predicting his opinion.
  - Re- means under, and Jonah is hiding his opinion.
  - Re- means not, and Jonah refuses to think about his opinion.
correctAnswer: Re- means again, and Jonah has to think about his opinion again.
explanation: Reconsider means to consider or think about something again, especially after new information.
hint: Use both the prefix and the new-evidence clue.
```
