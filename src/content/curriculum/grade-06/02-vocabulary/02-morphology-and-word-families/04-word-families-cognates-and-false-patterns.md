## Teaching Goal

Build flexible awareness of related words while warning students against misleading look-alikes.

## Student Outcome

The student can group related words, use reliable cognates cautiously, and reject false morphology clues.

## Misconception Checks

Every similar spelling signals shared meaning; all cognates mean exactly the same thing; word-family members have identical connotations.

## Teaching Note

Contrast helpful relatives such as inspect and inspection with a misleading look-alike that shares letters but not useful meaning.

```question

  Before the bus trip, the driver inspected each tire. The inspection helped the school know the bus was safe.
question: Which explanation best connects inspected and inspection?
choices:
  - Both relate to looking carefully to check something
  - Both mean the bus moved quickly
  - Both mean the tires were replaced
  - Both are unrelated because one is longer
correctAnswer: Both relate to looking carefully to check something
explanation: The related words share a meaning, but inspected is an action and inspection is a noun.
hint: Ask what the driver did and what the school used.
```
