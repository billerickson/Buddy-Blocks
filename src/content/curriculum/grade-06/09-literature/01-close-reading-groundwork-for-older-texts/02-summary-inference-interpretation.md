## Teaching Goal

Help students distinguish literal summary, reasonable inference, interpretation, and unsupported opinion.

## Student Outcome

The student can classify reading statements and improve a weak interpretation by tying it to evidence.

## Misconception Checks

Treating any personal reaction as interpretation; thinking summary is less important; calling every guess an inference.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u01_l02_q01_statement_types
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each statement type to its job.
pairs:
  - left: summary
    right: tells what happens or what the passage says
  - left: inference
    right: uses clues to read what is implied
  - left: interpretation
    right: explains a larger meaning supported by evidence
  - left: unsupported opinion
    right: states a reaction or claim without textual support
explanation: Each type of statement has a different job in literary reading.
hint: Ask whether the statement reports, reasons from clues, explains meaning, or leaves the text behind.
```

```question
key: cl4_u01_l02_q02_best_summary
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the best summary.
passageTitle: The Cup
passage: |
  Mira set the broken cup beside the hearth. Her brother reached for the pieces, but she shook her head.
  "Leave them there," she said. "Let Father see what his anger has done."
question: Which sentence is the best summary?
choices:
  - Mira leaves a broken cup where her father will see the result of his anger.
  - Mira forgives her father because family peace matters most.
  - The broken cup symbolizes the end of all hope in the house.
  - Mira's brother is the main cause of the conflict.
correctAnswer: Mira leaves a broken cup where her father will see the result of his anger.
explanation: A summary reports the literal action without adding an unsupported meaning.
hint: Pick the choice that tells what happens, not what the passage might mean.
```

```question
key: cl4_u01_l02_q03_reasonable_inference
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the strongest inference.
passageTitle: The Cup
passage: |
  Mira set the broken cup beside the hearth. Her brother reached for the pieces, but she shook her head.
  "Leave them there," she said. "Let Father see what his anger has done."
question: Which inference is best supported?
choices:
  - Mira wants her father to face the damage caused by his anger.
  - Mira plans to hide the cup before evening.
  - Mira believes cups are more valuable than people.
  - Mira's brother broke the cup on purpose.
correctAnswer: Mira wants her father to face the damage caused by his anger.
explanation: Mira says the broken cup should be left where Father can see what his anger has done.
hint: Use Mira's words as clues.
```

```question
key: cl4_u01_l02_q04_unsupported_opinion
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement is an unsupported opinion about the cup passage?
choices:
  - Mira is the most annoying character in all old stories.
  - Mira wants Father to see the broken cup.
  - The cup may represent damage caused by anger.
  - Mira stops her brother from moving the pieces.
correctAnswer: Mira is the most annoying character in all old stories.
explanation: That statement gives a reaction but no evidence from the passage.
hint: Find the choice that cannot be checked against the passage.
```

```question
key: cl4_u01_l02_q05_ladder_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the reading statements from most literal to most interpretive.
items:
  - The broken cup may show the harm anger causes.
  - Mira leaves the broken cup by the hearth.
  - Mira seems to want Father to notice the damage.
  - The cup is broken.
correctOrder:
  - The cup is broken.
  - Mira leaves the broken cup by the hearth.
  - Mira seems to want Father to notice the damage.
  - The broken cup may show the harm anger causes.
explanation: The statements move from a simple detail to summary, inference, and interpretation.
hint: Start with the statement that only reports one visible fact.
```

```question
key: cl4_u01_l02_q06_repair_overclaim
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the overclaim so it stays supported by the passage.
sentence: The broken cup proves that Mira's father will never change.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The broken cup suggests that Mira wants her father to see the harm caused by his anger.
  - The broken cup may show the harm caused by Father's anger.
  - The broken cup suggests the damage caused by Father's anger.
explanation: The passage supports harm caused by anger, but it does not prove Father's whole future.
hint: Keep the broken cup and anger, but remove the claim about what will always happen.
```
