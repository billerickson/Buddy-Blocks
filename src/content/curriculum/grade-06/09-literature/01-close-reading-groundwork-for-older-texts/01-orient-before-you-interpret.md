## Teaching Goal

Teach students to enter a difficult older passage by identifying speaker, situation, genre clues, and literal action before making an interpretation.

## Student Outcome

The student can complete a basic orientation for a short passage and reject an interpretation that ignores the literal situation.

## Misconception Checks

Interpreting from the first striking word; treating narrator, speaker, author, and character as identical; guessing genre from topic alone.

```question
key: cl4_u01_l01_q03_speaker_situation
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and identify the speaker's situation.
passageTitle: At The Gate
passage: |
  At dawn, the old herald lifted the bronze staff and faced the silent gate.
  "Open," he called, "not for my honor, but for the children waiting behind me."
  The guards looked toward the towers, where no banner moved.
question: Which orientation statement best fits the passage?
choices:
  - A herald asks guards to open a gate for the sake of waiting children.
  - A king celebrates a victory in front of a cheering city.
  - A poet describes a peaceful morning with no conflict.
  - The author explains how bronze staffs were made.
correctAnswer: A herald asks guards to open a gate for the sake of waiting children.
explanation: The passage shows a herald speaking to guards at a gate, with children waiting behind him.
hint: Name who speaks, whom he addresses, and what he asks.
```

```question
key: cl4_u01_l01_q04_genre_clue

passageTitle: At The Gate

  At dawn, the old herald lifted the bronze staff and faced the silent gate.
  "Open," he called, "not for my honor, but for the children waiting behind me."
  The guards looked toward the towers, where no banner moved.
question: Which detail most suggests an older heroic or public setting?
choices:
  - the herald lifting a bronze staff before a guarded gate
  - the passage beginning at dawn
  - the children waiting
  - the guards looking
correctAnswer: the herald lifting a bronze staff before a guarded gate
explanation: A herald, bronze staff, gate, guards, and towers suggest a formal public scene.
hint: Look for a detail that points to social role and setting, not just time of day.
```
