## Teaching Goal

Help students compare a source pattern with a transformation and identify changed emphasis.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain how a changed speaker, setting, conflict, or ending changes meaning.

## Misconception Checks

An adaptation has the same meaning as its source; any difference is a mistake; comparison is only listing similarities.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u06_l02_q01_source_adaptation_compare
type: passage-question
prompt: Compare the source pattern and adaptation.
passageTitle: The Refused Prize
passage: |
  Source pattern: A warrior refuses a public prize and asks that the honor be given to a fallen companion.

  Adaptation: At the school assembly, Mara refuses the solo medal and asks that the team name be engraved instead.
question: What stays most similar across the source and adaptation?
choices:
  - A public honor is redirected from one person toward others.
  - Both scenes happen after a sea voyage.
  - Both characters are warriors on a battlefield.
  - The medal and prize are made from the same metal.
correctAnswer: A public honor is redirected from one person toward others.
explanation: Both passages share a pattern of refusing individual honor to honor someone else or a group.
hint: Look for the shared action and value, not the surface setting.
```
