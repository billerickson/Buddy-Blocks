## Teaching Goal

Teach students that allusion carries meaning from a source pattern into a new passage.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a source echo and explain what idea or association it brings.

## Misconception Checks

Allusion is only name-dropping; recognizing the source is enough; any shared object proves an allusion.

## Questions

```question

prompt: What is an allusion?
choices:
  - An echo of another text, story, person, or tradition that carries meaning
  - A summary of every event in a passage
  - A random name with no connection to meaning
  - A stage direction spoken by a chorus
correctAnswer: An echo of another text, story, person, or tradition that carries meaning
explanation: An allusion matters because it brings meaning from a source into a new passage.
hint: The key idea is an echo that adds meaning.
```

```question
key: cl4_u06_l01_q03_thread_allusion
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the source note and passage to interpret the allusion.
passageTitle: The Thread In The Archive
passage: |
  Source note: In an old maze story, a thread helps a trapped hero find the way out.

  In the ruined archive, Leona tied a red thread to the doorpost before entering the dark rows of shelves.
  "If the old records confuse us," she said, "we will follow this back to daylight."
question: What meaning does the thread echo carry into this passage?
choices:
  - Guidance through confusion toward escape or clarity
  - A promise that books are always dangerous
  - Proof that Leona is the same person as the old hero
  - A comic complaint about sewing
correctAnswer: Guidance through confusion toward escape or clarity
explanation: The source note links thread with escape from a maze, and Leona uses thread to navigate confusing shelves.
hint: Connect the source function of the thread to Leona's action.
```

```question
key: cl4_u06_l01_q05_best_carried_meaning
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the best restrained claim about the allusion.
passageTitle: The Thread In The Archive
passage: |
  Source note: In an old maze story, a thread helps a trapped hero find the way out.

  In the ruined archive, Leona tied a red thread to the doorpost before entering the dark rows of shelves.
  "If the old records confuse us," she said, "we will follow this back to daylight."
question: Which claim is best supported?
choices:
  - The thread allusion presents reading the archive as a maze-like search that needs guidance.
  - The passage proves that every archive contains a monster.
  - Leona is foolish because thread can never guide anyone.
  - The old maze story and Leona's situation are identical in every detail.
correctAnswer: The thread allusion presents reading the archive as a maze-like search that needs guidance.
explanation: The passage adapts the thread's guiding function to intellectual confusion in the archive.
hint: Choose a claim about the shared function, not total sameness.
```

```question
key: cl4_u06_l01_q06_repair_allusion_claim
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the overclaim.
sentence: Because of the thread, the passage must include the whole old maze story.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Because of the thread, the passage echoes the old maze story's idea of guidance through confusion.
  - The thread allusion carries the idea of finding a way through confusion.
  - The passage borrows the thread pattern to suggest guidance, not the whole old story.
explanation: An allusion can carry one meaningful pattern without repeating the whole source.
hint: Keep the thread and guidance; remove the claim that everything must be copied.
```
