## Teaching Goal

Assess whether students can transfer the level's reading routines to an unfamiliar classical-style passage.

## Student Outcome

The student can orient, summarize, identify genre, analyze a theme or idea, and support a concise claim from a new passage.

## Misconception Checks

Transfer means guessing from a genre label; the first interesting detail proves the claim; unfamiliar passages require outside knowledge first.

```question
key: cl4_u06_l04_q02_transfer_orientation
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the unfamiliar passage and choose the best orientation.
passageTitle: The Silent Bell
passage: |
  At evening the council gathered beneath the silent bell. No one rang it, for the city had promised peace.
  A stranger entered carrying a cracked shield painted with the city's old sign.
  "I bring no war," she said, "only the names of those who kept your promise when fear made it costly."
  The eldest councilor covered his face, and the youngest asked why honor sounded so much like grief.
question: Which orientation statement best fits the passage?
choices:
  - A stranger brings names connected to the city's costly promise of peace before the council.
  - A comic inspector judges handwriting in a schoolroom.
  - A sailor celebrates a quick and easy homecoming.
  - A teacher defines courage through a dialogue about stones.
correctAnswer: A stranger brings names connected to the city's costly promise of peace before the council.
explanation: The passage shows a council, a stranger, a promise of peace, and names of those who kept it at cost.
hint: Name the people, setting, and main action.
```
