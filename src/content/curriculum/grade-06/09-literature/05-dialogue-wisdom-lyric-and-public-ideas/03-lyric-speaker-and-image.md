## Teaching Goal

Help students distinguish lyric speaker from author and interpret imagery as a path to idea or emotion.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify speaker/addressee and explain how an image shapes meaning.

## Misconception Checks

The speaker is automatically the poet; images are decorative; emotion alone is the interpretation.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u05_l03_q01_speaker_author
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement is most accurate when reading a lyric poem?
choices:
  - The speaker is the voice in the poem and is not automatically the author.
  - The speaker and author must always be the same person.
  - A lyric has no speaker because it has images.
  - The author is always a character inside the poem.
correctAnswer: The speaker is the voice in the poem and is not automatically the author.
explanation: Lyric reading begins by distinguishing the voice in the poem from the real writer.
hint: Ask who is speaking inside the text.
```
