## Teaching Goal

Teach students to interpret aphorisms or proverbs in relation to surrounding situation and evidence.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain a wisdom saying's meaning in context and avoid treating it as a free-floating slogan.

## Misconception Checks

A proverb means the same thing in every situation; short sayings are easy and need no evidence; any wise-sounding phrase is the theme.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u05_l02_q01_wisdom_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and interpret the saying in context.
passageTitle: The Lamp
passage: |
  The young judge condemned the whole town before hearing one witness. His teacher set a lamp before him.
  "Hold it high," she said. Smoke darkened the judge's sleeve.
  "A lamp lifted to expose others first shows its own smoke."
question: What does the saying mean in this context?
choices:
  - A person eager to judge others may reveal his own fault first.
  - Lamps are useful only outdoors.
  - Smoke proves that the town is innocent of everything.
  - Judges should never use light.
correctAnswer: A person eager to judge others may reveal his own fault first.
explanation: The judge condemns others too quickly, and the lamp image exposes his own fault.
hint: Connect the saying to the judge's action before it.
```
