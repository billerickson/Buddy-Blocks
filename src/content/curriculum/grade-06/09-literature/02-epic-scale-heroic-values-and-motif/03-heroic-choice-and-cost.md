## Teaching Goal

Help students analyze heroic choices as moments that reveal both values and consequences.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain what a heroic choice values and what cost or risk it brings.

## Misconception Checks

A hero's choice is automatically right; cost means the choice was wrong; action alone proves theme without motive or consequence.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u02_l03_q01_choice_and_cost
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and identify both value and cost.
passageTitle: The Bridge
passage: |
  The bridge ropes snapped in the flood. Damas tied the last rope around his waist and crossed first,
  though the far bank was dark with enemy fires. "If one of us must test the way," he said,
  "let it be the one who gave the order."
question: Which answer best describes Damas's choice?
choices:
  - He accepts personal risk because he values responsible leadership.
  - He avoids danger by ordering others to cross first.
  - He crosses because he has forgotten the enemy.
  - He proves that leaders should never give orders.
correctAnswer: He accepts personal risk because he values responsible leadership.
explanation: Damas tests the dangerous way himself because he gave the order.
hint: Look for what he risks and why he says he takes the risk.
```
