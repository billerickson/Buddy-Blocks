## Teaching Goal

Teach students to interpret epic simile and repeated language as meaning-making style.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain how a simile, epithet, or repeated phrase emphasizes a character, action, or theme.

## Misconception Checks

Figurative language is decoration; an epithet is just a nickname; repeated language means the author ran out of words.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u02_l04_q01_simile_identify
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and identify the epic simile.
passageTitle: The Shield Wall
passage: |
  The warriors closed their shields. As an oak holds its leaves against the first hard rain,
  so the line held under the arrows. Again the captain cried, "Stand fast, wall of bronze."
question: Which phrase is the simile?
choices:
  - As an oak holds its leaves against the first hard rain
  - The warriors closed their shields
  - Again the captain cried
  - wall of bronze
correctAnswer: As an oak holds its leaves against the first hard rain
explanation: The passage compares the shield line to an oak holding its leaves.
hint: Look for the comparison introduced with 'as' and completed by 'so.'
```
