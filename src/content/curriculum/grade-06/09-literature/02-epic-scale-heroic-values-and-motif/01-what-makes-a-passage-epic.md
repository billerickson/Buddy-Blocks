## Teaching Goal

Introduce epic genre signals and help students see epic as a form with public stakes, elevated action, and repeated patterns.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify epic signals in a passage and explain why they matter for reading.

## Misconception Checks

Epic means any exciting or long story; a list of events is enough to identify genre; genre labels are decorative.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u02_l01_q01_epic_signals
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each epic signal to what it helps show.
pairs:
  - left: a formal call to remember a great action
    right: invocation or elevated opening
  - left: a hero's action affects a whole city
    right: public stakes
  - left: a repeated title such as 'breaker of gates'
    right: epithet
  - left: a battle compared to a storm at sea
    right: extended simile
explanation: Epic signals help readers recognize scale, formality, repetition, and large comparison.
hint: Think about what each feature does for the size and style of the passage.
```

```question
key: cl4_u02_l01_q02_epic_orientation
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the best genre description.
passageTitle: The Captain At Dawn
passage: |
  Remember the captain who stood before the harbor wall when the black ships gathered.
  Behind him waited the city, its lamps hidden under clay. He lifted his spear and called
  each guard by the name of his father.
question: Which description best fits the passage?
choices:
  - An epic-style passage with public stakes, formal memory, and heroic action
  - A private diary entry about one person's quiet morning
  - A comic scene about mistaken identity
  - A recipe explaining how to make clay lamps
correctAnswer: An epic-style passage with public stakes, formal memory, and heroic action
explanation: The passage asks readers to remember a captain, shows a threatened city, and presents formal heroic action.
hint: "Look for scale: who is affected by the action?"
```

```question
key: cl4_u02_l01_q03_public_stakes
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which detail from the captain passage most clearly shows public stakes?
choices:
  - Behind him waited the city
  - at dawn
  - clay
  - his spear
correctAnswer: Behind him waited the city
explanation: The city waiting behind the captain shows that more than one private person is affected.
hint: Public stakes involve a community, city, family, or people beyond one individual.
```

```question

  - epithet
  - opinion

  - ending
```

```question
key: cl4_u02_l01_q05_genre_effect
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the best explanation of the epic effect.
passageTitle: The Captain At Dawn
passage: |
  Remember the captain who stood before the harbor wall when the black ships gathered.
  Behind him waited the city, its lamps hidden under clay. He lifted his spear and called
  each guard by the name of his father.
question: How does the epic style affect the scene?
choices:
  - It makes the captain's action feel tied to memory, ancestors, and the city's fate.
  - It makes the captain seem bored by an ordinary chore.
  - It proves that the guards' fathers are all present in the harbor.
  - It removes all conflict from the scene.
correctAnswer: It makes the captain's action feel tied to memory, ancestors, and the city's fate.
explanation: The call to remember, the threatened city, and the fathers' names give the action public and ancestral weight.
hint: Connect the style to memory and community.
```
