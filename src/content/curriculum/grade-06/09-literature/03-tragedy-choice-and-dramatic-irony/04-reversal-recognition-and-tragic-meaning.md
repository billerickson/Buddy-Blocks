## Teaching Goal

Teach students how reversal and recognition reshape a tragic scene or passage.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a turning point and explain how new knowledge changes the meaning of earlier action.

## Misconception Checks

Reversal is any event; recognition is just feeling sad; the final event alone contains the theme.

```question
key: cl4_u03_l04_q02_reversal_identify
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and identify the reversal.
passageTitle: The Letter
passage: |
  The ruler tore the messenger's cloak. "Liar," he said. Then the queen's old ring fell from the hidden fold.
  The hall went silent. The ruler whispered, "She trusted you, and I did not."
question: Which moment is the reversal?
choices:
  - The queen's ring falls from the hidden fold and changes the ruler's understanding.
  - The ruler tears the cloak before anything changes.
  - The hall is silent before the scene begins.
  - The messenger is never accused.
correctAnswer: The queen's ring falls from the hidden fold and changes the ruler's understanding.
explanation: The ring reverses the ruler's judgment of the messenger.
hint: Find the moment that changes the direction of the scene.
```
