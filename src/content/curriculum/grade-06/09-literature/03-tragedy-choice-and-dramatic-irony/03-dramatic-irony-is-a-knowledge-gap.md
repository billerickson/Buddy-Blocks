## Teaching Goal

Define dramatic irony as a gap between what the audience knows and what a character knows or believes.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a dramatic irony situation and explain how the gap changes audience understanding.

## Misconception Checks

Irony means sarcasm; any surprise is irony; dramatic irony means the character is lying.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u03_l03_q01_irony_definition
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What is dramatic irony?
choices:
  - A gap between what the audience knows and what a character knows or believes
  - Any sentence that sounds rude or sarcastic
  - A surprise that no one in the audience could predict
  - A joke spoken by the author
correctAnswer: A gap between what the audience knows and what a character knows or believes
explanation: Dramatic irony depends on audience knowledge differing from a character's knowledge.
hint: The key word is knowledge.
```

```question
key: cl4_u03_l03_q02_knowledge_gap
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scene and identify the dramatic irony.
passageTitle: The False Seal
passage: |
  The audience has already seen the servant replace the queen's seal with a false one.

  Queen: This seal will prove my command is true.
  Messenger: Then no one can doubt you, my lady.
question: Where is the knowledge gap?
choices:
  - The audience knows the seal is false, but the queen believes it proves her command.
  - The queen knows the seal is false, but the audience does not.
  - The messenger and queen both know everything the audience knows.
  - The seal is false, but no character mentions a command.
correctAnswer: The audience knows the seal is false, but the queen believes it proves her command.
explanation: The prompt gives the audience knowledge that the queen does not have.
hint: Compare audience knowledge with the queen's belief.
```

```question
key: cl4_u03_l03_q05_not_dramatic_irony
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which situation is NOT dramatic irony?
choices:
  - A character and the audience both learn the truth at the same moment.
  - The audience knows a letter is forged, but the king trusts it.
  - The audience sees a hidden sword before a character enters.
  - The audience knows the traveler is the lost son, but the father does not.
correctAnswer: A character and the audience both learn the truth at the same moment.
explanation: If audience and character learn together, there is no knowledge gap.
hint: Dramatic irony needs the audience to know something the character does not.
```
