## Teaching Goal

Teach students to orient to a dramatic scene by identifying speakers, immediate conflict, and scene pressure.

## Student Outcome

The student can describe who is speaking, what is at stake, and what conflict drives a short scene.

## Misconception Checks

Dialogue is easy because characters speak directly; the loudest character controls the meaning; stage conflict is the same as physical fighting.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u03_l01_q01_drama_terms
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each dramatic term to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: scene
    right: a focused unit of staged action
  - left: conflict
    right: the pressure or clash driving the exchange
  - left: motive
    right: what drives a character to speak or act
  - left: addressee
    right: the person being spoken to
explanation: These terms help readers orient to drama before interpreting a scene.
hint: Think about what helps you answer who, what, and why in a scene.
```

```question
key: cl4_u03_l01_q02_scene_conflict
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scene and identify the main conflict.
passageTitle: Before The Decree
passage: |
  Nera: The law is posted, but my brother still lies beyond the wall.
  Guard: The ruler forbids burial. I am ordered to watch the gate.
  Nera: Then watch me pass. A law that forgets the dead forgets the living too.
question: What conflict drives the scene?
choices:
  - Nera's family duty clashes with the ruler's law.
  - The guard wants to become ruler of the city.
  - Nera cannot remember where the wall is.
  - The ruler asks Nera to write a new law.
correctAnswer: Nera's family duty clashes with the ruler's law.
explanation: Nera wants to bury her brother, while the guard cites the ruler's command.
hint: Look for what Nera wants and what blocks her.
```

```question
key: cl4_u03_l01_q03_speakers_wants
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each figure to the role or pressure the scene shows.
pairs:
  - left: Nera
    right: family duty pressing against the law
  - left: Guard
    right: the posted law being enforced at the gate
  - left: Ruler
    right: the authority forbidding burial beyond the wall
  - left: Brother
    right: the dead family member Nera wants to honor
explanation: A dramatic situation becomes clearer when readers track what each figure does, represents, or pressures another character to do.
hint: Use what each named figure is connected to in the scene.
```

```question
key: cl4_u03_l01_q04_scene_turns
type: order-items
prompt: Put the scene turns in order.
passageTitle: Before The Decree
passage: |
  Nera: The law is posted, but my brother still lies beyond the wall.
  Guard: The ruler forbids burial. I am ordered to watch the gate.
  Nera: Then watch me pass. A law that forgets the dead forgets the living too.
items:
  - Nera says the law forgets the dead and living
  - The guard says he is ordered to watch the gate
  - Nera says her brother lies beyond the wall
  - Nera declares that she will pass
correctOrder:
  - Nera says her brother lies beyond the wall
  - The guard says he is ordered to watch the gate
  - Nera declares that she will pass
  - Nera says the law forgets the dead and living
explanation: Ordering the turns shows how the conflict sharpens from situation to defiance to principle.
hint: Follow the lines of dialogue in order.
```
