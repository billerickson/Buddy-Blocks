## Teaching Goal

Help students infer character motive from speech and action, then connect motive to tragic conflict.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify what a character wants and how a choice reveals a value or pressure.

## Misconception Checks

Motive must be stated directly; one character is wholly right and the other wholly wrong; conflict can be solved by choosing the most emotional line.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u03_l02_q01_motive_from_speech
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scene and identify the speaker's motive.
passageTitle: The Crowned Door
passage: |
  Elder: If you open the sealed door, the ruler will call it treason.
  Prince: If I leave it closed, the plague-sick will call it justice denied.
  Elder: You could wait until morning.
  Prince: Fever does not wait for sunrise.
question: What motive most clearly drives the prince?
choices:
  - He wants to help the sick despite political danger.
  - He wants to impress the elder with a joke.
  - He wants to protect the sealed door from everyone.
  - He wants to prove that sunrise is unimportant.
correctAnswer: He wants to help the sick despite political danger.
explanation: The prince contrasts treason charges with justice for the plague-sick and says fever cannot wait.
hint: Look for the need he thinks is urgent.
```

```question
key: cl4_u03_l02_q06_repair_motive_overclaim
type: error-correction

  - The prince considers opening the door because he thinks the sick need help before morning.
  - The prince is willing to risk punishment because he values urgent mercy for the sick.
```
