## Teaching Goal

Help students explain how a gap between appearance and reality creates comic audience effect.

## Student Outcome

The student can name the apparent situation, the real situation, and the effect on the audience.

## Misconception Checks

Audience effect is just "it is funny"; appearance/reality gaps always create tragedy; the audience's knowledge does not matter.

## Questions

```question
key: cl4_u04_l02_q01_appearance_reality_pairs
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each appearance to its reality.
pairs:
  - left: The boastful judge appears wise.
    right: He cannot answer the simple riddle.
  - left: The servant appears confused.
    right: She understands the plan before the masters do.
  - left: The feast appears generous.
    right: The host serves empty plates to praise thrift.
  - left: The traveler appears lost.
    right: He is testing whether the gatekeeper is honest.
explanation: Comedy often creates meaning through the gap between appearance and reality.
hint: Pair what seems true with what the scene reveals.
```

```question
key: cl4_u04_l02_q02_audience_gap
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and identify the audience's advantage.
passageTitle: The Empty Feast
passage: |
  The audience sees the servants carrying covered trays with nothing on them.
  Host: Behold my generosity! No guest in this hall will be burdened by too much food.
  Guest: Your kindness is lighter than air.
question: What does the audience know that creates the comic effect?
choices:
  - The trays are empty while the host praises his generosity.
  - The guest has already eaten a large meal.
  - The servants are carrying treasure.
  - The host is secretly a great cook.
correctAnswer: The trays are empty while the host praises his generosity.
explanation: The gap between empty trays and boastful generosity creates the humor.
hint: Compare what the audience sees with what the host claims.
```
