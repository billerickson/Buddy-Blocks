## Teaching Goal

Connect area formulas for parallelograms and triangles to visual decomposition.

## Student Outcome

The student can find area using base and height and explain why the formula fits the shape.

## Key Ideas

Parallelograms can be rearranged into rectangles, and triangles are half of related parallelograms or rectangles.

## Misconception Checks

Use any side as height; forget the one-half for triangles; confuse area with perimeter.

## Teaching Note

Cut and move a triangle from a parallelogram to form a rectangle, then compare areas.

## Questions

```question
choices:
  - 16 square units
  - 30 square units
  - 60 square units
  - 100 square units
correctAnswer: 30 square units
explanation: Triangle area is 1/2 x base x height, so 1/2 x 10 x 6 = 30.
hint: A triangle is half of a related parallelogram or rectangle.
```
