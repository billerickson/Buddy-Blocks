## Teaching Goal

Teach students to retell a longer tale by choosing key events and placing them in order.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify which events belong in a strong retelling and order them accurately.

## Misconception Checks

Including every detail; omitting the turning point; confusing event order after a flashback-like explanation or repeated episode.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l01_q01_key_event
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A tale begins with Mara finding a locked bronze gate. Which detail most belongs in a strong retelling?
choices:
  - Mara found a locked bronze gate.
  - The gate had three scratches near the handle.
  - Mara's cloak was dark green.
  - A cricket chirped beside the road.
correctAnswer: Mara found a locked bronze gate.
explanation: A locked gate can shape the problem of the story, so it belongs in a retelling.
hint: Choose the detail that helps explain what changes in the story.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q02_event_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the events in a strong retelling order.
items:
  - The shepherd returns the lost lyre.
  - The village hears music again.
  - A shepherd finds a lyre under an olive tree.
  - The lyre's owner promises to teach the shepherd a song.
correctOrder:
  - A shepherd finds a lyre under an olive tree.
  - The shepherd returns the lost lyre.
  - The lyre's owner promises to teach the shepherd a song.
  - The village hears music again.
explanation: The retelling follows the discovery, the choice to return the lyre, the promise, and the result.
hint: Start with the first event that creates the problem or opportunity.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q03_minor_detail
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is a minor detail that could usually be left out of a short retelling?
choices:
  - The messenger warned the prince before sunset.
  - The prince ignored the warning and opened the door.
  - The door's brass hinge shone in the sun.
  - A storm began after the door opened.
correctAnswer: The door's brass hinge shone in the sun.
explanation: The shiny hinge describes the door, but it does not explain the main events.
hint: A strong retelling keeps details that affect the problem, choice, or ending.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q04_retelling_passage
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the tale summary and choose the best retelling.
passageTitle: The Cup Of Rain
passage: |
  A dry village waited for rain. Lio found a silver cup beside an old spring.
  A traveler told him to pour the last water from the cup onto the dusty ground.
  Lio obeyed, even though he was thirsty. Clouds gathered, and rain filled the spring.
question: Which retelling keeps the key events in order?
choices:
  - A thirsty boy found a silver cup, poured out the last water after a traveler told him to, and rain filled the spring.
  - A silver cup was shiny, a traveler walked by, and the village had dusty roads.
  - Rain came first, then Lio found a cup and decided not to listen.
  - A traveler took the cup away because Lio was thirsty.
correctAnswer: A thirsty boy found a silver cup, poured out the last water after a traveler told him to, and rain filled the spring.
explanation: This retelling includes the problem, advice, choice, and result in the correct order.
hint: Look for the answer that explains the change from dry village to rain.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q05_retelling_parts
type: match-pairs

pairs:
  - left: Problem
    right: The city gates were closed before the children returned.
  - left: Important choice
    right: The older child decided to ask the gatekeeper for help.
  - left: Turning point
    right: The gatekeeper recognized the family ring.
  - left: Resolution
    right: The children entered safely before nightfall.
explanation: A retelling often tracks the problem, choice, turning point, and resolution.
hint: Ask what job each event does in the story.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q06_best_omission

  - Nera promised not to open the small box.
  - Nera opened the box when she heard a whisper.

  - A bright moth flew out and led Nera to the cave.
```
