## Teaching Goal

Help students explain how one story event leads to another and how a turning point changes the direction of the story.

## Student Outcome

The student can match causes to effects and identify the event where the story changes.

## Misconception Checks

Treating every earlier event as the cause; confusing the most exciting event with the turning point; reading effects as random.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l02_q01_cause_effect
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each cause to its effect.
pairs:
  - left: The prince broke the promise.
    right: The hidden door opened.
  - left: The girl shared her bread.
    right: The stranger showed her the safe path.
  - left: The sailor ignored the warning cloud.
    right: His boat was caught in a storm.
  - left: The youngest brother listened carefully.
    right: He answered the riddle.
explanation: Cause and effect show how one event leads to another.
hint: Look for what happened because of each action.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q02_turning_point
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the summary and find the turning point.
passageTitle: The Locked Orchard
passage: |
  Three sisters searched for fruit in a locked orchard.
  They tried to climb the wall, but the stones were too smooth.
  The youngest sister noticed a bird dropping seeds near a narrow crack.
  She planted the seeds, and vines grew into a ladder by morning.
question: Which event is the turning point?
choices:
  - The sisters searched for fruit.
  - The stones were too smooth to climb.
  - The youngest sister noticed seeds near a crack.
  - The orchard had fruit inside.
correctAnswer: The youngest sister noticed seeds near a crack.
explanation: That moment changes the story from being stuck to finding a possible solution.
hint: The turning point is the moment that sends the story in a new direction.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q03_because_so
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the cause-and-effect sentence.
parts:
  - "Because the gatekeeper recognized the ring, "
  - " so the children "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: he opened the gate
    acceptedAnswers:
      - he opened the gate
  - correctAnswer: entered safely
    acceptedAnswers:
      - entered safely
      - got inside safely
explanation: The recognized ring causes the gatekeeper to open the gate, which lets the children enter safely.
hint: Use the cause to explain the next action, then the result.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q04_best_cause
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In a tale, the traveler receives help after giving water to a stranger. What caused the traveler to receive help?
choices:
  - The traveler gave water to a stranger.
  - The traveler owned a blue cup.
  - The road was dusty.
  - The stranger had a long cloak.
correctAnswer: The traveler gave water to a stranger.
explanation: The act of sharing water is the cause that leads to help later.
hint: Choose the action that explains why the later event happened.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q05_not_cause
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is not a cause-and-effect claim?
choices:
  - Because the lamp went out, the children could not see the path.
  - Since Orin lied, no one trusted his next warning.
  - The cave was dark, cold, and quiet.
  - When the bird sang, the hidden door opened.
correctAnswer: The cave was dark, cold, and quiet.
explanation: That sentence describes the cave, but it does not show one event causing another.
hint: Look for a sentence that has no because, since, when, or event result idea.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q06_sequence_chain
type: order-items
prompt: Order the cause-and-effect chain.
items:
  - The bell woke the sleeping guard.
  - The hero pulled the rope.
  - The guard opened the tower door.
  - The hero escaped before dawn.
correctOrder:
  - The hero pulled the rope.
  - The bell woke the sleeping guard.
  - The guard opened the tower door.
  - The hero escaped before dawn.
explanation: Each event causes or makes possible the next event.
hint: Start with the action that begins the chain.
```
