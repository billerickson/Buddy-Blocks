## Teaching Goal

Teach students to support a theme or moral with specific story evidence.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose a theme or moral that fits a story and match it with supporting evidence.

## Misconception Checks

Choosing a theme from personal opinion; using an unrelated detail as evidence; confusing one event with the theme.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l04_q01_theme_not_event
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is a theme or moral, not just an event?
choices:
  - Promises should be kept even when they become difficult.
  - The boy crossed the bridge at sunset.
  - The old woman gave the boy a ring.
  - The gate closed before the horse arrived.
correctAnswer: Promises should be kept even when they become difficult.
explanation: That sentence states a larger lesson. The other choices are events.
hint: A theme or moral is a larger idea that could fit more than one story.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q02_best_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the tale summary and choose the evidence.
passageTitle: The Empty Net
passage: |
  A fisher caught only one small fish. The fish begged to be released.
  The fisher let it go, even though he was hungry.
  The next morning, the fish led many fish toward the fisher's net.
question: Which detail best supports the moral 'Mercy can bring unexpected good'?
choices:
  - The fisher let the small fish go.
  - The fisher owned a net.
  - The next morning came after night.
  - The fish was small.
correctAnswer: The fisher let the small fish go.
explanation: The fisher shows mercy by releasing the fish, and that choice leads to good later.
hint: Find the detail that shows mercy.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q03_theme_evidence_sentence
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the theme-and-evidence sentence.
parts:
  - "The story shows that "
  - " because "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: kindness can be rewarded
    acceptedAnswers:
      - kindness can be rewarded
      - kindness may be rewarded
  - correctAnswer: the traveler helps the old man and later receives help
    acceptedAnswers:
      - the traveler helps the old man and later receives help
      - the traveler helped the old man and later received help
explanation: The first blank is the larger idea. The second blank gives story evidence.
hint: Put the lesson first, then the event that proves it.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q04_unsupported_theme
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A story says a proud runner refuses help, gets lost, and learns to listen. Which theme is not supported?
choices:
  - Listening to wise advice can prevent trouble.
  - Pride can lead to mistakes.
  - Refusing help may have consequences.
  - The fastest runner always wins.
correctAnswer: The fastest runner always wins.
explanation: The story says the proud runner gets lost, so this theme does not fit.
hint: Reject the answer that goes against the story events.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q05_moral_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each story detail to the moral it best supports.
pairs:
  - left: A child shares the last lantern and everyone finds the path.
    right: Shared help can guide many people.
  - left: A boastful archer misses after refusing to practice.
    right: Pride without practice can lead to failure.
  - left: A quiet listener solves the riddle after hearing every clue.
    right: Careful listening can reveal the answer.
  - left: A trader cheats once and loses every customer's trust.
    right: Dishonesty can cost more than it gains.
explanation: The evidence and moral should fit each other closely.
hint: Look for the moral that names the lesson shown by the event.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q06_best_theme
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the best theme.
passageTitle: The Two Torches
passage: |
  Two travelers entered a tunnel. One carried a bright torch and rushed ahead.
  The other walked slowly and held her smaller torch near the ground.
  When the first traveler tripped on a loose stone, the second saw it and helped him out.
question: Which theme best fits the passage?
choices:
  - Careful attention can be more useful than rushing.
  - Bright torches are always dangerous.
  - Tunnels should never be entered.
  - The second traveler wanted to win a race.
correctAnswer: Careful attention can be more useful than rushing.
explanation: The careful traveler notices the danger and helps, while the rushed traveler trips.
hint: Choose the larger idea supported by both travelers' actions.
```
