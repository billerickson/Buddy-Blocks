## Teaching Goal

Separate common character-reading terms so students can interpret characters precisely.

## Student Outcome

The student can distinguish a trait, feeling, motive, choice, and consequence in a short story situation.

## Misconception Checks

Calling any emotion a trait; giving an action when asked for motive; calling a consequence a punishment even when it is neutral.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_term_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each character-reading term to the best example.
pairs:
  - left: Trait
    right: patient
  - left: Feeling
    right: worried for a moment
  - left: Motive
    right: wanted to protect his sister
  - left: Choice
    right: hid the key under a stone
  - left: Consequence
    right: the door stayed locked
explanation: Each term does a different job when we read a character.
hint: Trait tells what someone is often like; motive tells why someone acts.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q02_trait_not_feeling
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word is a character trait rather than a temporary feeling?
choices:
  - generous
  - startled
  - sleepy
  - nervous
correctAnswer: generous
explanation: Generous describes what a person is often like. The others can be momentary feelings or states.
hint: Ask which word could describe a steady quality.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q03_motive_passage
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the story moment and answer.
passageTitle: The Hidden Loaf
passage: |
  Tessa saw that her neighbor's basket was empty.
  She placed half of her own loaf by the neighbor's door before sunrise.
question: What is Tessa's most likely motive?
choices:
  - She wants to help her neighbor.
  - She wants to hide from the sunrise.
  - She wants the basket for herself.
  - She wants to win a race.
correctAnswer: She wants to help her neighbor.
explanation: Tessa sees a need and shares her bread, so helping is the best-supported motive.
hint: Look at what Tessa notices before she acts.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q04_choice_consequence
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the character sentence.
parts:
  - "Niko's choice was to "
  - ", and the consequence was that "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: return the lost coin
    acceptedAnswers:
      - return the lost coin
      - give back the lost coin
  - correctAnswer: the merchant trusted him
    acceptedAnswers:
      - the merchant trusted him
      - the merchant trusted Niko
explanation: The choice is what Niko does. The consequence is what happens because of that choice.
hint: First name the action, then name what followed.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q05_evidence_for_trait
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which detail best supports the trait 'brave'?
choices:
  - Lena walked into the dark hall to lead her younger brother out.
  - Lena liked red apples more than green apples.
  - Lena's sandals were new.
  - Lena heard music in the market.
correctAnswer: Lena walked into the dark hall to lead her younger brother out.
explanation: Facing danger to help someone supports the trait brave.
hint: Choose the action that shows the trait.
```
