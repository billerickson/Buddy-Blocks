## Teaching Goal

Compare two fables or folktales by one focus, especially choice, consequence, or lesson.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose a focused comparison statement and avoid unfocused lists.

## Misconception Checks

Comparing every detail at once; assuming similar events create identical morals; ignoring evidence from one story.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l05_q01_focus_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which is the best comparison focus for two fables?
choices:
  - How each main character's choice leads to the moral
  - Every animal, object, and color in both stories
  - Which story has the shorter title
  - How many letters are in each character's name
correctAnswer: How each main character's choice leads to the moral
explanation: A strong focus helps compare meaning, not random details.
hint: Choose a focus that helps explain the lesson.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q02_paired_morals
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the paired summaries and choose the best comparison.
passageTitle: Two Short Tales
passage: |
  Tale A: A proud rooster refuses to share grain and is left alone when the storm comes.
  Tale B: A quiet ant stores grain with others and survives the winter with the colony.
question: Which focused comparison fits both tales?
choices:
  - Both tales connect sharing or cooperation to survival.
  - Both tales say storms are always good.
  - Both tales have the same ending for the main character.
  - Both tales are about learning to sing.
correctAnswer: Both tales connect sharing or cooperation to survival.
explanation: The rooster's refusal and the ant's cooperation lead to different survival outcomes.
hint: Compare the choice and consequence in both tales.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q03_comparison_sentence
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: "Use these paired tales to complete the comparison sentence. Tale A: A runner boasts that advice is for slow feet, ignores a guide's warning about the marsh, and loses the race. Tale B: A singer mocks every other voice, refuses to practice with the chorus, and is left singing alone."
parts:
  - "Both tales warn against "
  - ", but Tale A shows it through "
  - " while Tale B shows it through "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: pride
    acceptedAnswers:
      - pride
      - boasting
  - correctAnswer: a runner who refuses advice
    acceptedAnswers:
      - a runner who refuses advice
      - the runner refusing advice
  - correctAnswer: a singer who mocks others
    acceptedAnswers:
      - a singer who mocks others
      - the singer mocking others
explanation: The focus is pride, but each tale uses different evidence.
hint: Keep the same focus in the first blank, then give one detail from each tale.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q04_bad_comparison
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which comparison is too unfocused?
choices:
  - Both stories have animals, trees, stones, talking, walking, and different endings.
  - Both stories show greed leading to loss.
  - Both stories use a repeated test to reveal kindness.
  - Both stories connect a small choice to a large consequence.
correctAnswer: Both stories have animals, trees, stones, talking, walking, and different endings.
explanation: That answer lists details without a clear focus.
hint: A strong comparison names one idea or pattern.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q05_match_focus_claim
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each comparison focus to a fitting claim.
pairs:
  - left: Choice
    right: Both characters decide whether to share food.
  - left: Consequence
    right: One character loses trust, while the other gains help.
  - left: Moral
    right: Both tales warn that greed can cost more than it gains.
  - left: Trick
    right: Each story uses deception to reveal a hidden flaw.
explanation: The claim should match the selected comparison focus.
hint: Ask what the claim is comparing.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q06_one_story_only
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the paired summaries and choose the weak comparison.
passageTitle: Fox And Turtle
passage: |
  Tale A: A fox brags that speed is all that matters and loses when he ignores the path signs.
  Tale B: A turtle moves slowly, reads each sign, and reaches the feast safely.
question: Which comparison is weak because it fits only one tale?
choices:
  - Both tales value careful attention over speed alone.
  - Both tales include a journey toward a feast.
  - Both tales use path signs as important details.
  - Both tales show a fox reading carefully.
correctAnswer: Both tales show a fox reading carefully.
explanation: Only Tale A has a fox, and the fox ignores the signs.
hint: A comparison must fit both stories.
```
