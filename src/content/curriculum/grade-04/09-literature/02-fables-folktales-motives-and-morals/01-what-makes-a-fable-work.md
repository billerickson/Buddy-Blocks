## Teaching Goal

Identify fable features and connect a compact animal or human tale to its moral.

## Student Outcome

The student can recognize fable signals and choose a moral that fits the whole story.

## Misconception Checks

Calling every animal story a fable; choosing a moral because it sounds wise; ignoring the consequence.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_fable_signal
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which detail is the strongest signal that a story may be a fable?
choices:
  - A short tale shows a fox's greedy choice and ends with a lesson.
  - A long poem describes the sea in twelve stanzas.
  - A scene has three speakers arguing on a stage.
  - A history note lists the dates of two kings.
correctAnswer: A short tale shows a fox's greedy choice and ends with a lesson.
explanation: Fables are often short tales that teach a moral through a character's choice and consequence.
hint: Look for a short story with a lesson.
```

  - Many kinds of stories can include animals.

  - left: Simple character type
    right: The greedy crow wants every shiny thing.
  - left: Choice
    right: The crow refuses to share the found coin.

```question
key: u02_l01_q04_moral_from_fable
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the fable and choose the moral.
passageTitle: The Crow And The Pebbles
passage: |
  A crow found a jar with a little water at the bottom.
  She dropped pebbles into the jar one by one until the water rose.
  A thirsty dog laughed at her slow work and ran off.
  By noon, the crow drank, and the dog was still searching.
question: Which moral best fits the fable?
choices:
  - Patient effort can solve a hard problem.
  - Jars should be kept indoors.
  - Dogs are always wiser than crows.
  - Pebbles are more valuable than water.
correctAnswer: Patient effort can solve a hard problem.
explanation: The crow's patient work raises the water and solves the problem.
hint: Look at the character's choice and the result.
```

  - A proud donkey mocks a quiet mule, then needs the mule's help and learns humility.
  - A traveler lists every town he visited over ten years.

  - Two actors speak lines in a market scene.
correctAnswer: A proud donkey mocks a quiet mule, then needs the mule's help and learns humility.
explanation: This is a short tale with character behavior, consequence, and a clear lesson.

```question
key: u02_l01_q06_moral_evidence

passageTitle: The Boastful Reed
passage: |
  A reed boasted that it never bent in the wind.
  When a storm came, the reed stood stiff and snapped.
  The grasses bent low and rose again when the storm passed.
question: Which detail best supports the moral 'Flexibility can be stronger than stubbornness'?
choices:
  - The grasses bent low and rose again.
  - The reed grew near grass.
  - The storm came in the evening.
  - The reed had a tall shadow.
correctAnswer: The grasses bent low and rose again.
explanation: The grasses survive because they bend, while the stiff reed snaps.
hint: Choose the detail that shows flexibility helping.
```
