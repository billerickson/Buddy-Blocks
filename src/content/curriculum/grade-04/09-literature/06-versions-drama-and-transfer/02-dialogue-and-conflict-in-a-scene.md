## Teaching Goal

Read a short drama scene by tracking speakers, conflict, and choice.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify who speaks, what the conflict is, and which choice changes the scene.

## Misconception Checks

Reading all dialogue as the narrator; missing conflict because no one explains it directly; choosing a line without noting who said it.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l02_q01_speaker_scene
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scene and answer.
passageTitle: At The Gate
passage: |
  Lina: Open the gate before the storm reaches us.
  Guard: I cannot open it without the bronze token.
  Lina: Then take my father's ring. It bears the same mark.
question: Who says the gate should be opened before the storm arrives?
choices:
  - Lina
  - Guard
  - Lina's father
  - The storm
correctAnswer: Lina
explanation: Lina's first line asks for the gate to be opened before the storm.
hint: Look at the name before the line.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q02_conflict_identify
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scene and identify the conflict.
passageTitle: At The Gate
passage: |
  Lina: Open the gate before the storm reaches us.
  Guard: I cannot open it without the bronze token.
  Lina: Then take my father's ring. It bears the same mark.
question: What is the conflict?
choices:
  - Lina needs the gate opened, but the guard requires proof.
  - The guard wants to sing a poem.
  - Lina cannot remember her own name.
  - The ring has no mark.
correctAnswer: Lina needs the gate opened, but the guard requires proof.
explanation: The scene's tension comes from the need to enter and the guard's rule.
hint: Ask what problem the characters are trying to solve.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q03_best_next_line
type: dialogue-builder
prompt: Choose the best next line for the scene.
turns:
  - speaker: Lina
    line: Open the gate before the storm reaches us.
  - speaker: Guard
    line: I cannot open it without the bronze token.
  - speaker: Lina
    line: Then take my father's ring. It bears the same mark.
choices:
  - "Guard: The mark is true. I will open the gate."
  - "Guard: I forgot how storms work."
  - "Guard: Let us discuss a poem about apples."
  - "Guard: The ring proves there is no gate."
correctAnswer: "Guard: The mark is true. I will open the gate."
explanation: The line responds to Lina's evidence and resolves the gate conflict.
hint: Choose the line that follows from the ring's mark.
```

```question

  - Lina asks for the gate to open.
  - The guard says he needs the bronze token.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q06_choice_changes_scene
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scene and choose the turning choice.
passageTitle: The Broken Oath
passage: |
  Mira: I promised not to speak the passphrase.
  Daro: Without it, the prisoners stay locked inside.
  Mira: Then I will speak it once and accept the cost.
question: Which choice changes the scene?
choices:
  - Mira decides to speak the passphrase and accept the cost.
  - Daro says the prisoners are locked inside.
  - Mira remembers a promise.
  - The passphrase has a name.
correctAnswer: Mira decides to speak the passphrase and accept the cost.
explanation: That choice changes what will happen next and creates a consequence.
hint: Find the action a character decides to take.
```
