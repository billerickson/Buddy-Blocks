## Teaching Goal

Distinguish narrator, poem speaker, and character dialogue across prose, poetry, and drama.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify who tells, who speaks, and how voice shapes meaning.

## Misconception Checks

Using narrator and speaker interchangeably; assuming every "I" is the author; ignoring how voice reveals attitude.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l03_q01_voice_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each voice term to the best example.
pairs:
  - left: Narrator
    right: The voice tells what happened in a prose story.
  - left: Poem speaker
    right: The voice says, 'I wait beside the sea.'
  - left: Character dialogue
    right: "Mira: I will guard the door."
  - left: Stage direction
    right: "[The lamp flickers as the guard enters.]"
explanation: Different forms show voice in different ways.
hint: Look at whether the voice tells, speaks in a poem, speaks in dialogue, or gives scene action.
```
