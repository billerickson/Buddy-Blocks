## Teaching Goal

Apply motif language to a new adapted tale while rejecting unsupported labels.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a supported motif and explain which story event shows it.

## Misconception Checks

Forcing a familiar motif onto any old story; choosing a motif because of one word; ignoring counterevidence.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l04_q01_motif_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the new tale summary and identify the motif.
passageTitle: The Stranger's Bowl
passage: |
  A stranger asked three houses for a bowl of soup.
  The first two houses shut their doors. The third house shared what little it had.
  By morning, the third house's empty garden was full of green shoots.
question: Which motif is best supported?
choices:
  - Hospitality tested and rewarded
  - Transformation into a bird
  - A hero refusing every helper
  - A poem's speaker addressing the sea
correctAnswer: Hospitality tested and rewarded
explanation: The third house welcomes the stranger and receives a reward.
hint: Look for welcome or refusal followed by consequence.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q02_reject_forced_motif
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A tale includes a locked gate, a careful promise, and a returned key. Which motif is least supported?
choices:
  - Transformation
  - Promise
  - Forbidden choice
  - Reward or consequence
correctAnswer: Transformation
explanation: Nothing in the summary says a character or object changes form.
hint: Reject the label that has no event to prove it.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q03_motif_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each motif to evidence from a new tale.
pairs:
  - left: Promise
    right: The singer vows to return before moonrise.
  - left: Quest
    right: The child travels to find the healing leaf.
  - left: Transformation
    right: The greedy sailor becomes a shell on the shore.
  - left: Trickster
    right: The hare uses a false roar to expose the lion's pride.
  - left: Hospitality
    right: The poor host shares fire with a cold stranger.
explanation: A motif must be connected to the event that shows it.
hint: Pair the motif name with the clearest evidence.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q04_motif_cloze
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the motif-evidence check.
parts:
  - "The motif is "
  - " because the story shows "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: forbidden choice
    acceptedAnswers:
      - forbidden choice
      - a forbidden choice
  - correctAnswer: the child opening the sealed box after being warned not to
    acceptedAnswers:
      - the child opening the sealed box after being warned not to
      - the child opens the sealed box after being warned not to
      - the sealed box is opened after a warning
explanation: The evidence shows the motif because a warning is followed by the forbidden action.
hint: Name the motif, then point to the event that proves it.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q05_multiple_motifs
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the tale summary and choose the best answer.
passageTitle: The Moonlit Road
passage: |
  A traveler promised to carry a silver branch to the temple.
  On the road, a fox offered a shortcut if the traveler shared bread.
  The traveler shared, followed the shortcut, and reached the temple before moonset.
question: Which two motifs are supported?
choices:
  - Promise and helper
  - Transformation and stanza
  - Forbidden choice and personification
  - Trickster punishment and drama scene
correctAnswer: Promise and helper
explanation: The traveler has a promise, and the fox helps with a shortcut after receiving bread.
hint: Look for a vow and useful aid.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q06_overclaim
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which answer avoids overclaiming?
choices:
  - This tale may show a helper motif because the owl guides the lost child.
  - This tale proves every owl in every story is a helper.
  - This tale has all classical motifs because it is old.
  - This tale must be a myth because it has a road.
correctAnswer: This tale may show a helper motif because the owl guides the lost child.
explanation: The answer names a motif and gives specific evidence without making a huge claim.
hint: Choose the answer that stays close to the story evidence.
```
