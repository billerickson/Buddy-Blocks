## Teaching Goal

Show how promises, warnings, and forbidden choices shape mythic consequences.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify the warning or promise and explain how a choice leads to the outcome.

## Misconception Checks

Treating warnings as background details; calling the consequence unfair without tracking the choice; missing the promise that sets up the conflict.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l02_q01_warning_chain
type: order-items
prompt: Order the warning-choice-consequence chain.
items:
  - The singer looks back before reaching the sunlight.
  - The companion is lost again.
  - The guide warns the singer not to look back.
  - The singer begins the climb from the underworld.
correctOrder:
  - The guide warns the singer not to look back.
  - The singer begins the climb from the underworld.
  - The singer looks back before reaching the sunlight.
  - The companion is lost again.
explanation: The warning sets up the forbidden choice, which leads to the consequence.
hint: Start with the warning.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q02_promise_identify
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the myth moment and answer.
passageTitle: The Golden Oar
passage: |
  Mira promised the river god that she would return the golden oar before sunset.
  When a crowd praised the oar, she kept it one more night.
  At dawn, the river withdrew from the city.
question: Which event is the broken promise?
choices:
  - Mira keeps the oar one more night.
  - The crowd praises the oar.
  - The river god owns the oar.
  - Sunset comes before dawn.
correctAnswer: Mira keeps the oar one more night.
explanation: She promised to return the oar before sunset, but she keeps it.
hint: Compare the promise to what Mira does.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q03_forbidden_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A character is told, 'Do not open the jar until the new moon.' Which later action is the forbidden choice?
choices:
  - Opening the jar that afternoon
  - Carrying the jar carefully
  - Asking who gave the warning
  - Waiting for the new moon
correctAnswer: Opening the jar that afternoon
explanation: The warning forbids opening the jar before the new moon.
hint: Find the action that goes against the warning.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q04_consequence_support
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the summary and choose the explanation.
passageTitle: The Silent Lyre
passage: |
  A musician promised to play only one song on a sacred lyre.
  The crowd begged for more, and the musician played until midnight.
  The lyre fell silent for a year.
question: Why does the lyre fall silent?
choices:
  - The musician breaks the promise by playing more than one song.
  - The crowd dislikes music.
  - The lyre was never sacred.
  - Midnight is always unlucky in every story.
correctAnswer: The musician breaks the promise by playing more than one song.
explanation: The consequence follows from the broken promise.
hint: Look for the action that goes beyond the promise.
```

```question
    acceptedAnswers:

  - correctAnswer: touching the golden fruit
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q06_promise_or_background
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence sets up a promise that may shape later events?
choices:
  - Damon swore to return before the third torch burned out.
  - Damon's cloak was blue at the edge.
  - The courtyard stones were warm.
  - A bird flew above the gate.
correctAnswer: Damon swore to return before the third torch burned out.
explanation: A sworn promise creates an expectation that later events can fulfill or break.
hint: Look for words like promised, swore, or vowed.
```
