## Teaching Goal

Teach students to recognize welcome, refusal, aid, and consequence patterns in myths.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a hospitality or helper pattern and explain its effect on the story.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking helpers are minor extras; missing that hospitality can be a test; treating reward or consequence as random.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l04_q01_hospitality_pattern
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A stranger asks for shelter during a storm. Only one cottage welcomes him, and that cottage is protected from the flood. Which pattern appears?
choices:
  - Hospitality or welcome is tested and rewarded.
  - A poem repeats a line.
  - A hero refuses every helper.
  - A myth has no consequence.
correctAnswer: Hospitality or welcome is tested and rewarded.
explanation: The welcome matters because it leads to protection later.
hint: Look for welcome, refusal, and consequence.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q03_refusal_consequence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the myth moment and answer.
passageTitle: The Guest At Dusk
passage: |
  At dusk, an old guest asked two brothers for a seat by the fire.
  The older brother pushed him away. The younger brother shared his stool.
  By morning, the younger brother's fire still burned, but the older brother's hearth was cold.
question: Which choice leads to the consequence?
choices:
  - The older brother refuses the guest, while the younger brother welcomes him.
  - Dusk happens before morning.
  - The guest has no name.
  - The stool is near the fire.
correctAnswer: The older brother refuses the guest, while the younger brother welcomes him.
explanation: The different treatment of the guest leads to different results.
hint: Compare how each brother treats the guest.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q04_helper_not_solution

prompt: Why is a helper not the same as the hero doing nothing?
choices:
  - A helper may give aid, but the main character still must choose and act.
  - A helper always solves every problem alone.
  - A helper proves the quest was fake.
  - A helper only appears in poems.
correctAnswer: A helper may give aid, but the main character still must choose and act.
explanation: Helpers make success possible, but they do not erase the character's choices.
hint: Look for both aid and action.
```
