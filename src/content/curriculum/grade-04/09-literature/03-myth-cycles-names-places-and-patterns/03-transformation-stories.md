## Teaching Goal

Read transformation as a meaningful story event, not just a magical surprise.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain what changes, why it changes, and what the transformation shows.

## Misconception Checks

Treating transformation as decoration; focusing only on the new shape; ignoring what the change reveals about choice, warning, or theme.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l03_q01_transformation_parts
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each transformation part to the example.
pairs:
  - left: Before
    right: A proud hunter mocked the forest spirits.
  - left: Cause
    right: He broke the warning not to strike the sacred deer.
  - left: New form
    right: His hands became antlers.
  - left: Meaning
    right: His pride made him resemble what he hunted.
explanation: Transformation stories often connect the change to a choice and meaning.
hint: Find what happens before, why the change occurs, and what it shows.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q02_transformation_cause
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the myth moment and answer.
passageTitle: The Laurel Shade
passage: |
  Calla begged to escape a pursuer who would not listen.
  The earth opened softly around her feet, and she changed into a laurel tree.
  Her leaves whispered whenever someone spoke her name.
question: What causes the transformation in this adapted moment?
choices:
  - Calla begs for escape from the pursuer.
  - Someone counts the leaves.
  - The tree grows near a wall.
  - The pursuer names every flower.
correctAnswer: Calla begs for escape from the pursuer.
explanation: The change answers Calla's plea to escape.
hint: Look for the need or request right before the change.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q03_before_after
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: In a transformation story, a sailor steals pearls from the sea god and becomes a shell fixed to the shore. Complete the transformation statement.
parts:
  - "Before the change, the sailor was "
  - "; after the change, he became "
  - "; the change showed "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: greedy for the sea god's pearls
    acceptedAnswers:
      - greedy for the sea god's pearls
      - greedy
  - correctAnswer: a shell fixed to the shore
    acceptedAnswers:
      - a shell fixed to the shore
      - a shell
  - correctAnswer: his greed trapped him
    acceptedAnswers:
      - his greed trapped him
      - greed trapped him
explanation: The new form connects to the character's greedy choice and consequence.
hint: The meaning should explain why this form fits the character's choice.
```

```question

prompt: Why should readers ask what a transformation shows?
choices:
  - Because the new form often reveals a choice, warning, or theme.
  - Because transformations never affect the plot.
  - Because only the color of the new form matters.
  - Because every transformation has the same moral.

```

```question
key: u03_l03_q05_transformation_meaning
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the summary and choose the meaning.
passageTitle: The Echoing Girl
passage: |
  A girl repeated every secret she heard, even after her friends begged her to stop.
  At last, her voice faded until she could only repeat the last words spoken near her.
question: What does the transformation show?
choices:
  - Her habit of repeating others' words becomes her whole voice.
  - Her friends wanted her to sing louder.
  - Secrets are always easy to keep.
  - A quiet forest has no sounds.
correctAnswer: Her habit of repeating others' words becomes her whole voice.
explanation: The change fits the earlier choice to repeat what others said.
hint: Look for how the new form matches the old behavior.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q06_transformation_order
type: order-items
prompt: Order the transformation story shape.
items:
  - A character ignores a warning.
  - The character changes form.
  - The new form shows the consequence.
  - A warning or need is introduced.
correctOrder:
  - A warning or need is introduced.
  - A character ignores a warning.
  - The character changes form.
  - The new form shows the consequence.
explanation: Many transformation stories move from warning or need to choice, change, and meaning.
hint: Start before the character makes the choice.
```
