## Teaching Goal

Orient students to myths as stories where names, places, and objects matter because of their story roles.

## Student Outcome

The student can match a mythic name, place, or object to what it does in the story.

## Misconception Checks

Memorizing names without roles; confusing Greek/Roman context with any old story; losing the main plot in unfamiliar vocabulary.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l01_q01_story_role
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In a myth summary, Ariadne gives Theseus a thread before he enters the labyrinth. What is the thread's story role?
choices:
  - It helps Theseus find his way back.
  - It proves Theseus is a poet.
  - It names the weather in Athens.
  - It shows the labyrinth is a river.
correctAnswer: It helps Theseus find his way back.
explanation: The thread matters because it helps solve the maze problem.
hint: Ask what the object does in the story.
```

```question
  The oracle warned Calla that a bronze sandal would mark the stranger who could open the sealed spring.
  Years later, a traveler arrived wearing one bronze sandal and asked for water.
question: Why does the bronze sandal matter?
choices:
  - It connects the traveler to the oracle's warning.
  - It proves the traveler owns a shop.
  - It shows the spring is made of bronze.
  - It tells the reader the traveler's favorite color.
correctAnswer: It connects the traveler to the oracle's warning.
explanation: The sandal is a story clue that links the traveler to the earlier warning.
hint: Look back to the oracle's message.
```
