## Teaching Goal

Distinguish vivid image, simile, and personification in short poetic lines.

## Student Outcome

The student can classify a line and explain what it helps the reader picture or understand.

## Misconception Checks

Calling any image a simile; calling any animal action personification; identifying the term but not the meaning.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l03_q01_term_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each term to the best description.
pairs:
  - left: Image
    right: words that help the reader picture or sense something
  - left: Simile
    right: a comparison using like or as
  - left: Personification
    right: giving human action or feeling to something nonhuman
  - left: Literal description
    right: words that mean exactly what they say
explanation: These terms help readers talk about how language creates meaning.
hint: Look for the special job of each term.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q02_simile_identify
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which line is a simile?
choices:
  - The shield flashed like morning sun.
  - The shield was bronze.
  - The shield waited bravely.
  - The shield hung on the wall.
correctAnswer: The shield flashed like morning sun.
explanation: The line compares the shield to morning sun using like.
hint: A simile uses like or as to compare.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q03_personification_identify
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which line uses personification?
choices:
  - The old gate groaned and begged the wind to stop.
  - The old gate was made of cedar.
  - The old gate stood near the road.
  - The old gate had iron hinges.
correctAnswer: The old gate groaned and begged the wind to stop.
explanation: The gate is given human-like action by begging.
hint: Look for a nonhuman thing acting like a person.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q04_image_meaning
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the line and choose what it helps the reader picture.
passageTitle: Line
passage: |
  The road curled like a gray ribbon around the hill.
question: What does the image help the reader picture?
choices:
  - A winding gray road around a hill
  - A straight road under the sea
  - A red ribbon tied to a door
  - A hill with no road
correctAnswer: A winding gray road around a hill
explanation: The comparison to a ribbon helps the reader picture the road's curved shape.
hint: Use the words curled, gray ribbon, and around the hill.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q05_classify_lines
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each line to the best label.
pairs:
  - left: The sea whispered against the stones.
    right: personification
  - left: The moon was as thin as a silver thread.
    right: simile
  - left: The red cloak hung beside the door.
    right: literal description
  - left: Cold mist covered the black pines.
    right: image

hint: Look for like/as, human action, exact description, or sensory picture.
```
