## Teaching Goal

Connect poetic features to theme, mood, or remembered story pattern.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain how speaker, repetition, image, simile, or personification supports meaning.

## Misconception Checks

Listing a feature without connecting it to meaning; treating mood as the same as theme; ignoring the story pattern behind an image.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l04_q01_feature_effect
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the lines and choose the best explanation.
passageTitle: The Returning Sail
passage: |
  White sail, white sail, rise from the foam,
  bring our long-lost brother home.
question: How does the repetition of 'white sail' affect the meaning?
choices:
  - It emphasizes hope as the family watches for return.
  - It proves the poem is about a locked door.
  - It shows the speaker dislikes the sea.
  - It removes the idea of waiting.
correctAnswer: It emphasizes hope as the family watches for return.
explanation: The repeated image of the sail focuses attention on hope for someone coming home.
hint: Connect the repeated words to the wish in the next line.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q02_mood_from_image

choices:
  - lonely
  - silly
  - crowded

hint: Think about the feeling created by cold and empty.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q03_feature_meaning_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each feature to its likely meaning effect.
pairs:
  - left: A repeated warning line
    right: makes danger feel important
  - left: A simile comparing a shield to sunrise
    right: makes the shield seem bright and hopeful
  - left: Personification of a tired road
    right: makes the journey feel long
  - left: A speaker addressing a lost friend
    right: creates longing or grief
explanation: Features matter because they shape meaning or mood.
hint: Ask how the feature changes what the reader feels or understands.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q04_story_pattern_image
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the lines and choose the story pattern they recall.
passageTitle: Three Gates
passage: |
  First gate of iron, second gate of flame,
  third gate opens when the traveler speaks her name.
question: Which story pattern do the lines most clearly recall?
choices:
  - A repeated or rising test
  - A list of unrelated colors
  - A fable moral stated at the end
  - A narrator giving a date
correctAnswer: A repeated or rising test
explanation: The three gates suggest a sequence of tests that build toward success.
hint: Notice the first, second, and third structure.
```

```question

type: passage-question
prompt: Read the lines and choose the best explanation.
passageTitle: The Gate Remembers
passage: |
  The gate remembers every hand
  that trembled there at dusk.
question: Why is the personification meaningful?
choices:
  - It makes the gate seem like a witness to many fearful choices.
  - It proves the gate is a human character.
  - It means every hand belongs to the same person.
  - It shows dusk never matters in stories.
correctAnswer: It makes the gate seem like a witness to many fearful choices.
explanation: The gate is given memory, which makes it feel like it has witnessed repeated human fear.
hint: Ask what human ability the gate receives and why that matters.
```
