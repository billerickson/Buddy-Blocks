## Teaching Goal

Show how repeated language and rhythm help meaning, mood, or memory.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify repeated words or rhythmic patterns and explain their effect.

## Misconception Checks

Treating repetition as accidental; thinking rhythm matters only if a poem rhymes; naming a repeated word without explaining its effect.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l02_q01_repeated_words
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the lines and identify the repetition.
passageTitle: Homeward
passage: |
  Step by step, the road grows bright.
  Step by step, we leave the night.
question: Which words are repeated?
choices:
  - Step by step
  - road grows
  - leave the night
  - bright night
correctAnswer: Step by step
explanation: The phrase appears at the beginning of both lines.
hint: Look for words that appear more than once.
```

  - Rhythm is a pattern of beats or movement in language, even when lines do not rhyme.

  - Rhythm is the same thing as a moral.
