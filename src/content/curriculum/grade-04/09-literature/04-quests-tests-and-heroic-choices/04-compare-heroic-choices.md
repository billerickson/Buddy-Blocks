## Teaching Goal

Compare two heroic choices using one criterion such as motive, risk, help, consequence, or restraint.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose a focused comparison and support it with details from both episodes.

## Misconception Checks

Comparing fame or surface details; using evidence from only one story; assuming all heroic choices are equally wise.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l04_q01_comparison_focus
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which focus would make a strong comparison of two heroic choices?
choices:
  - The risk each character accepts
  - The number of letters in each hero's name
  - Whether both stories mention sandals
  - Which summary is printed first
correctAnswer: The risk each character accepts
explanation: Risk is connected to the meaning of heroic choices.
hint: Choose a focus that helps explain action and consequence.
```

```question
key: u04_l04_q02_paired_choice
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the paired summaries and choose the best comparison.
passageTitle: Two Heroic Choices
passage: |
  Story A: Leos fights the serpent alone because he wants all the praise.
  Story B: Pera waits for her companions because the map shows three paths must be watched at once.
question: Which focused comparison is best?
choices:
  - Leos chooses prideful risk, while Pera chooses patient teamwork.
  - Both heroes make the same choice for the same reason.
  - Pera wants all the praise, while Leos waits patiently.
  - Neither story includes a motive.
correctAnswer: Leos chooses prideful risk, while Pera chooses patient teamwork.
explanation: The comparison focuses on motive and choice in both stories.
hint: Compare why each character acts.
```

```question
key: u04_l04_q05_weak_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the paired summaries and choose the weak claim.
passageTitle: Two Helpers
passage: |
  Story A: A hero thanks the old woman for the map and follows it safely.
  Story B: A hero laughs at the old woman's map and becomes lost.
question: Which claim is weak because it ignores the evidence?
choices:
  - Both heroes show equal wisdom with the helper.
  - One hero accepts help, while the other rejects it.
  - The helper's map affects both stories.
  - The two choices lead to different consequences.
correctAnswer: Both heroes show equal wisdom with the helper.
explanation: The second hero rejects the map and gets lost, so the heroes do not show equal wisdom.
hint: Reject the claim that does not fit both summaries.
```

```question
key: u04_l04_q06_best_evidence_both
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the paired summaries and choose the evidence pair.
passageTitle: Two Acts Of Restraint
passage: |
  Story A: Dana leaves the jeweled cup on the altar and carries only the medicine home.
  Story B: Milo returns the golden key after opening the prison door.
question: Which evidence pair best supports the comparison 'Both heroes show restraint'?
choices:
  - Dana leaves the jeweled cup; Milo returns the golden key.
  - Dana sees an altar; Milo sees a door.
  - Dana carries medicine; Milo walks through a prison.
  - Both stories have objects made of metal.
correctAnswer: Dana leaves the jeweled cup; Milo returns the golden key.
explanation: Both details show characters not keeping tempting or powerful objects.
hint: Choose one detail from each story that shows self-control.
```
