## Teaching Goal

Explain how difficulty, aid, and cost shape a heroic episode.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify the obstacle, helper, and cost and explain their story functions.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking a helper is unimportant; treating cost as the same as failure; noticing the monster but not the challenge it creates.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l02_q01_role_sort
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each quest detail to its role.
pairs:
  - left: A cliff with no path
    right: obstacle
  - left: A raven carrying a rope
    right: helper
  - left: Giving up the silver shield
    right: cost
  - left: Reaching the far temple
    right: outcome
explanation: Obstacles block, helpers aid, costs are what success requires, and outcomes show what happens.
hint: Ask whether the detail blocks, helps, costs, or results.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q02_helper_function
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the quest moment and answer.
passageTitle: The Wind Bridge
passage: |
  Niko reached a canyon too wide to cross.
  A small wind spirit taught him to tie his cloak like a sail.
  The wind carried him safely to the other side, but the cloak tore apart.
question: What is the helper's function?
choices:
  - The wind spirit teaches Niko a way across the canyon.
  - The wind spirit makes the canyon wider.
  - The cloak becomes the main villain.
  - Niko refuses all help.
correctAnswer: The wind spirit teaches Niko a way across the canyon.
explanation: The helper gives aid that makes crossing possible.
hint: Look for what the helper makes possible.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q03_cost_not_failure
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A hero completes the task but loses the cloak that helped him cross the canyon. Why is this a cost, not a failure?
choices:
  - The hero succeeds, but success requires giving up something.
  - The hero does not complete the task.
  - The cloak was never useful.
  - A cost means nothing important happened.
correctAnswer: The hero succeeds, but success requires giving up something.
explanation: A cost can come with success; it shows what the victory required.
hint: Ask whether the task was completed.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q04_obstacle_effect
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What does an obstacle usually do in a quest episode?
choices:
  - It makes the task difficult and forces a choice or strategy.
  - It proves the story has no task.
  - It replaces the hero with the narrator.
  - It gives the moral before anything happens.
correctAnswer: It makes the task difficult and forces a choice or strategy.
explanation: Obstacles create the challenge that the hero must respond to.
hint: Think about what blocks the hero's progress.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q05_obstacle_helper_cost_chain
type: order-items
prompt: Order the obstacle-helper-cost chain.
items:
  - The hero gives the pearl to the river.
  - A river blocks the road.
  - A fish shows where the water is shallow.
  - The hero crosses safely.
correctOrder:
  - A river blocks the road.
  - A fish shows where the water is shallow.
  - The hero gives the pearl to the river.
  - The hero crosses safely.
explanation: The obstacle appears first, the helper gives aid, the cost is paid, and the hero crosses.
hint: Start with what blocks the way.
```
