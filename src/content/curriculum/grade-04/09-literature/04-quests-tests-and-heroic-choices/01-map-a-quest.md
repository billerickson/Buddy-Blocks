## Teaching Goal

Teach the quest pattern as a flexible story map.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify task, journey, obstacle, helper, choice, cost, and return in a quest episode.

## Misconception Checks

Treating quest as simply "a trip"; ignoring cost or return; forcing every quest into exactly the same parts.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l01_q01_quest_element_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each quest element to the story example.
pairs:
  - left: Task
    right: Bring back the healing water.
  - left: Obstacle
    right: A sleep-bringing mist covers the road.
  - left: Helper
    right: A crane shows a path above the mist.
  - left: Cost
    right: The hero must give up the golden cup.
  - left: Return
    right: The hero brings the water to the village.
explanation: A quest map helps readers see each part's job in the episode.
hint: Ask what role each event plays in the journey.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q02_quest_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the quest events in order.
items:
  - The hero crosses the dark river.
  - The hero returns with the healing herb.
  - The queen sends the hero to find the herb.
  - A ferryman gives the hero a warning.
correctOrder:
  - The queen sends the hero to find the herb.
  - A ferryman gives the hero a warning.
  - The hero crosses the dark river.
  - The hero returns with the healing herb.
explanation: The quest begins with a task, continues through help and obstacle, and ends with return.
hint: Start with the task that begins the quest.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q03_task_identify
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the quest summary and identify the task.
passageTitle: The White Branch
passage: |
  A city had no light after the sacred lamp went out.
  The elder sent Ione beyond the western hill to bring back a white branch that could relight it.
  On the road, Ione met a silent wolf and a river of smoke.
question: What is Ione's quest task?
choices:
  - Bring back the white branch to relight the lamp.
  - Name the silent wolf.
  - Count the hills near the city.
  - Build a river of smoke.
correctAnswer: Bring back the white branch to relight the lamp.
explanation: The elder sends Ione to get the branch for a clear purpose.
hint: Look for what the character is sent to do.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q04_not_all_quests_same
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why should readers avoid forcing every quest into exactly the same parts?
choices:
  - Quest maps are tools, but each story may use the parts differently.
  - Quest stories never have tasks.
  - Helpers and obstacles mean the story is not a quest.
  - A quest must always have seven identical events.
correctAnswer: Quest maps are tools, but each story may use the parts differently.
explanation: The map helps you read, but the story's details still matter.
hint: A pattern guides reading; it does not replace reading.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q05_cost_or_reward
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In a quest, the hero saves the village but must leave the magic harp behind. What quest element is this?
choices:
  - Cost
  - Task
  - Genre label
  - Stanza
correctAnswer: Cost
explanation: The hero succeeds but gives up something valuable, so this is a cost.
hint: A cost is what success requires or takes away.
```
