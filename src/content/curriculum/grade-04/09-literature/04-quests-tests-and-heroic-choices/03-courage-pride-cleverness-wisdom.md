## Teaching Goal

Interpret heroic qualities in context rather than as simple labels.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose whether a story detail shows courage, pride, cleverness, wisdom, restraint, or loyalty.

## Misconception Checks

Calling all brave actions wise; calling all clever actions good; treating pride and confidence as identical.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l03_q01_quality_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each quality to the best story action.
pairs:
  - left: Courage
    right: Entering the cave to rescue a trapped child despite fear
  - left: Pride
    right: Refusing advice because no one else seems worthy
  - left: Cleverness
    right: Using a mirror to avoid looking directly at the monster
  - left: Wisdom
    right: Waiting until dawn because the signs show the path is safer then
  - left: Loyalty
    right: Returning for a companion instead of taking the prize alone
explanation: Character qualities must be tied to specific actions.
hint: Choose the action that best shows each quality.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q02_courage_not_wisdom
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which action is courageous but not wise?
choices:
  - Running into a monster's cave without listening to the guide's warning
  - Studying the map before entering the cave
  - Waiting for the sleeping guard to pass
  - Sharing food with a tired companion
correctAnswer: Running into a monster's cave without listening to the guide's warning
explanation: The action may be brave, but ignoring a useful warning is not wise.
hint: Courage faces danger; wisdom uses good judgment.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q03_pride_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the heroic moment and answer.
passageTitle: The Bronze Mirror
passage: |
  The guide said, "Use the mirror. Do not face the monster directly."
  Damos laughed. "I need no tricks," he said, and strode forward with uncovered eyes.
question: Which quality is best supported by Damos's words and action?
choices:
  - Pride
  - Patience
  - Gratitude
  - Mercy
correctAnswer: Pride
explanation: Damos dismisses the guide and overtrusts himself.
hint: Look at how Damos responds to advice.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q04_clever_or_selfish
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the moment and choose the best interpretation.
passageTitle: The Shared Rope
passage: |
  Mira tied knots in the rope so every climber could grip it.
  She climbed last, checking that each knot held.
question: What does Mira's cleverness show here?
choices:
  - Cleverness used to help the group.
  - Cleverness used to steal the prize.
  - Pride that refuses all advice.
  - Fear that stops the journey.
correctAnswer: Cleverness used to help the group.
explanation: Mira's strategy makes the climb safer for everyone.
hint: Ask who benefits from the clever plan.
```
