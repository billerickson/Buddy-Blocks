## Teaching Goal

Transfer Grammar 2 knowledge by choosing the relevant tool for a mixed sentence or mini-passage.

## Student Outcome

The student can decide whether a prompt requires noun form, pronoun clarity, verb tense, expansion, punctuation, diagram reasoning, or revision for clarity.

## Key Ideas

Mixed review begins with diagnosis: name the grammar job before choosing the answer.

## Misconception Checks

The newest rule is always the one being tested; a familiar-looking mark such as apostrophe or comma always has the same job; longer answers are automatically better revisions.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l04_q01_choose_tool_possessive
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which grammar tool helps show ownership in this sentence? The teachers desk was covered with papers.
choices:
  - possessive noun
  - linking verb
  - reflexive pronoun
  - quotation marks
correctAnswer: possessive noun
explanation: The desk belongs to one or more teachers, so the writer needs a possessive noun such as teacher's or teachers'.
hint: Ask whether the sentence shows ownership.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q02_choose_tool_pronoun
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentences and answer the question.
passageTitle: Mixed Review
passage: Ava gave Mia the shell after she found it on the beach.
question: Which grammar tool should a writer use first to make the sentence clearer?
choices:
  - pronoun clarity
  - commas in a series
  - plural nouns
  - prepositional phrases
correctAnswer: pronoun clarity
explanation: She could mean Ava or Mia, so the pronoun reference needs to be made clear.
hint: Look for a pronoun that could point to more than one noun.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q03_mixed_tense_edit
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the grammar problem in the sentence.
sentence: Tomorrow, we visited the museum.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Tomorrow, we will visit the museum.
explanation: Tomorrow points to future time, so will visit matches the time clue.
hint: Use the verb phrase for something that has not happened yet.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q04_concept_to_clue
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each clue to the grammar tool it suggests.
pairs:
  - left: shows ownership
    right: possessive noun
  - left: could point to two nouns
    right: pronoun clarity
  - left: names three or more items
    right: commas in a series
  - left: connects subject to description
    right: linking verb
explanation: Mixed review starts by noticing the clue that points to the grammar tool.
hint: Use the clue before choosing a correction.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q05_best_revision_clarity
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "A writer wants to add useful, clear details to this sentence: The bird moved. Which revision is best?"
choices:
  - The small bird moved quietly across the wet grass.
  - The bird moved moved moved.
  - The bird was bird and moved.
  - The moving moved bird.
correctAnswer: The small bird moved quietly across the wet grass.
explanation: The revision adds precise details that make the sentence clearer without becoming repetitive or confusing.
hint: Choose the sentence with useful details, not repeated or confusing words.
```

```question
key: u06_l04_q06_mixed_dialogue_edit
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the dialogue sentence.
sentence: Nina said "we are early".
acceptedAnswers:
  - Nina said, "We are early."
explanation: The sentence needs a comma after said, a capital letter at the start of the quotation, and the period inside the quotation marks.
hint: Check speaker tag comma, capital letter, and end punctuation.
```
