## Teaching Goal

Build a simple diagram for familiar sentence patterns.

## Student Outcome

The student can place subject, verb, direct object or complement, and one modifier into a simple diagram structure.

## Key Ideas

Diagramming begins by finding the main subject and verb, then placing completing or describing words where they belong.

## Misconception Checks

Start with modifiers before the main line; place every word in sentence order; treat direct objects and complements as the same relationship.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l02_q01_diagram_steps
type: order-items
prompt: Put the diagramming steps in a useful order.
items:
  - Place modifiers with the words they describe
  - Find the subject
  - Find the verb
  - Ask whether the verb has an object or complement
correctOrder:
  - Find the subject
  - Find the verb
  - Ask whether the verb has an object or complement
  - Place modifiers with the words they describe
explanation: Start with the main sentence frame before placing describing words.
hint: The main subject and verb come before the smaller branches.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q02_word_to_role
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word from The small dog chased the ball to its diagram role.
pairs:
  - left: dog
    right: subject
  - left: chased
    right: verb
  - left: ball
    right: direct object
  - left: small
    right: modifier of dog
explanation: Dog is the subject, chased is the action verb, ball receives the action, and small describes dog.
hint: Ask who did the action, what action, what received it, and what describes a noun.
```

```question

type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which diagram pattern fits The soup is hot?
choices:
  - subject | linking verb | complement
  - subject | action verb | direct object
  - direct object | verb | subject
  - modifier | subject | direct object
correctAnswer: subject | linking verb | complement
explanation: Is links soup to the describing word hot.
hint: Ask whether the verb shows action or connects a description.
```


```question
key: u06_l02_q06_correct_diagram_label
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the diagram label.
sentence: In The soup is hot, hot is the direct object.
acceptedAnswers:
  - In The soup is hot, hot is the complement.
  - In The soup is hot, hot is a complement.
explanation: Hot describes the subject after a linking verb. It is a complement, not a direct object.
hint: A direct object receives action, but is is a linking verb.
```
