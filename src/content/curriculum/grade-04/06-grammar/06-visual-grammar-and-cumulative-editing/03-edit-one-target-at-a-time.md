## Teaching Goal

Use a named grammar target to make focused sentence corrections without changing unrelated correct parts.

## Student Outcome

The student can correct one target type, such as possessive noun, pronoun clarity, verb tense, or punctuation, in a short sentence.

## Key Ideas

Focused editing starts by naming the target, finding the evidence, and changing only what needs to change.

## Misconception Checks

Editing means rewriting everything; any sentence with an error must have many errors; a correction is better if it changes more words.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l03_q01_name_target
type: multiple-choice
prompt: One dog owns the bowl. The sentence is The dogs bowl is empty. Which target should you check?
choices:
  - possessive noun
  - verb tense
  - quotation marks
  - compound predicate
correctAnswer: possessive noun
explanation: "The bowl belongs to one dog, so the sentence needs a singular possessive noun: dog's bowl."
hint: Ask what kind of grammar mark shows ownership.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q02_correct_named_possessive
type: error-correction
prompt: "Target: possessive noun for one dog. Correct only that target."
sentence: The dogs bowl is empty.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The dog's bowl is empty.
explanation: Dog's shows that the bowl belongs to one dog.
hint: Change the noun that owns the bowl.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q03_pronoun_clarity_target
type: fill-blank
prompt: "Target: pronoun clarity. Choose the word that makes the sentence clear."
sentenceBefore: "Maya told Lily that "
sentenceAfter: " had won the game. The winner was Maya."
choices:
  - Maya
  - she
  - Lily
  - they
correctAnswer: Maya
explanation: Naming Maya makes the winner clear.
hint: The clue says the winner was Maya.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q04_tense_target
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer the question.
passageTitle: "Target: Verb Tense"
passage: Yesterday, he walks to the creek and skipped stones.
question: Which word should change to match the target?
choices:
  - walks
  - creek
  - skipped
  - stones
correctAnswer: walks
explanation: Yesterday and skipped show past time, so walks should become walked.
hint: Find the verb that does not match the past-time clue.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q05_series_comma_target
type: error-correction
prompt: "Target: commas in a series. Correct only that target."
sentence: The box held chalk erasers and tape.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The box held chalk, erasers, and tape.
  - The box held chalk, erasers and tape.
explanation: Chalk, erasers, and tape are three items in a series.
hint: Count the items and separate them.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q06_change_only_target
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Target: object pronoun. Which correction changes only the target? The coach thanked we after practice."
choices:
  - The coach thanked us after practice.
  - The coach will thank we after practice.
  - The coaches thanked we after practice.
  - The coach thanked our after practice.
correctAnswer: The coach thanked us after practice.
explanation: Us is the object pronoun after thanked. The rest of the sentence can stay the same.
hint: Change only the pronoun receiving the action.
```
