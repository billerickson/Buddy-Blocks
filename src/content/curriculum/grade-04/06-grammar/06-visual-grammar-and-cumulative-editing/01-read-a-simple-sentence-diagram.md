## Teaching Goal

Interpret simple sentence diagrams as visual models of sentence relationships.

## Student Outcome

The student can match a simple diagram to a sentence and name the subject, verb, direct object or complement, and one modifier.

## Key Ideas

A simple diagram places the main subject and verb on the main line, then shows objects, complements, and modifiers in their own positions.

## Misconception Checks

A diagram is unrelated to meaning; the longest word must be the subject; modifiers belong on the main line.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l01_q01_diagram_roles
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each diagram part to its sentence job.
pairs:
  - left: main subject
    right: names who or what the sentence is about
  - left: main verb
    right: shows action or links the subject
  - left: direct object
    right: receives the action
  - left: modifier branch
    right: describes another word
explanation: A simple diagram shows how sentence parts relate to one another.
hint: Think about the job each part does in a sentence.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q02_match_diagram_sentence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which sentence matches this diagram? subject: cat | verb: chased | direct object: yarn"
choices:
  - The cat chased the yarn.
  - The yarn chased the cat.
  - The cat was yarn.
  - The chased cat yarn.
correctAnswer: The cat chased the yarn.
explanation: The diagram shows cat as the subject, chased as the verb, and yarn as the direct object.
hint: Read the diagram from subject to verb to object.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q03_find_diagram_verb
type: fill-blank
prompt: "In the diagram subject: painter | verb: mixed | direct object: colors, choose the verb."
sentenceBefore: "The verb is "
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - painter
  - mixed
  - colors
  - direct
correctAnswer: mixed
explanation: Mixed is the action on the main verb line.
hint: The verb tells what the subject did.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q04_main_line_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the main diagram parts in the usual order for an action sentence.
items:
  - direct object
  - subject
  - verb
correctOrder:
  - subject
  - verb
  - direct object
explanation: A simple action diagram usually shows subject, then verb, then direct object on the main line.
hint: Start with who or what the sentence is about.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q05_modifier_connection
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the diagram description and answer the question.
passageTitle: Modifier Branch
passage: "Diagram: subject: bird | verb: sang | modifier branch from bird: small"
question: Which word does small describe?
choices:
  - bird
  - sang
  - diagram
  - branch
correctAnswer: bird
explanation: The modifier branch comes from bird, so small describes bird.
hint: Follow the branch to the word it comes from.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q06_linking_diagram
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which sentence matches this diagram? subject: soup | linking verb: is | complement: hot"
choices:
  - The soup is hot.
  - The soup heats hot.
  - Hot soup is.
  - The hot soup stirred.
correctAnswer: The soup is hot.
explanation: The diagram links soup to hot with the linking verb is.
hint: The complement describes the subject after a linking verb.
```
