## Teaching Goal

Connect personal pronouns to the nouns they replace or point to.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a pronoun and its antecedent in a short sentence pair.

## Key Ideas

An antecedent is the noun a pronoun refers to; clear writing lets the reader tell which noun the pronoun means.

## Misconception Checks

A pronoun is clear if the writer knows the noun; the nearest noun is always the antecedent; pronouns can refer to any noun in the passage.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_pronoun_roles
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each term to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: pronoun
    right: a word that can stand in for a noun
  - left: antecedent
    right: the noun a pronoun points to
  - left: clear reference
    right: the reader can tell which noun is meant
  - left: unclear reference
    right: the pronoun could mean more than one noun
explanation: Pronouns help only when their antecedents are clear to the reader.
hint: Think about what each word helps a reader know.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q02_find_antecedent
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentences and answer the question.
passageTitle: Rabbit Care
passage: Mina fed the rabbit before school. It nibbled the lettuce quickly.
question: What is the antecedent of It?
choices:
  - Mina
  - rabbit
  - school
  - lettuce
correctAnswer: rabbit
explanation: It points to the rabbit, the thing that nibbled the lettuce.
hint: Ask what nibbled the lettuce.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q03_clear_or_unclear
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence has an unclear pronoun?
choices:
  - Ravi dropped the cup, and it cracked.
  - Nora put the book on the shelf because it was full.
  - The bird opened its wings.
  - Caleb found his pencil.
correctAnswer: Nora put the book on the shelf because it was full.
explanation: It could mean the book or the shelf. The reader cannot tell which one was full.
hint: Look for a pronoun that could point to two different nouns.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q04_make_reference_clear
type: error-correction
prompt: Make the pronoun clear so the sentence says Lena finished the homework.
sentence: Lena called Sara after she finished the homework.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Lena called Sara after Lena finished the homework.
explanation: Replacing she with Lena makes the antecedent clear.
hint: The reader needs to know who finished the homework.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q05_plural_antecedent
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the pronoun that clearly refers to the trees.
sentenceBefore: "The trees lost "
sentenceAfter: " leaves in the storm."
choices:
  - its
  - their
  - her
  - his
correctAnswer: their
explanation: Trees is plural, so their is the clear pronoun.
hint: The antecedent names more than one tree.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q06_best_revision
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which revision makes the pronoun clear? The backpack was under the desk, and it was blue.
choices:
  - The blue backpack was under the desk.
  - It was under the blue desk and backpack.
  - The backpack was under it, and it was blue.
  - The backpack and desk was blue.
correctAnswer: The blue backpack was under the desk.
explanation: The revision names the backpack as blue, so the reader no longer has to guess what it means.
hint: Choose the sentence that names what was blue.
```
