## Teaching Goal

Use reflexive pronouns only when they point back to the subject of the sentence.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose and correct reflexive pronouns such as myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, and themselves.

## Key Ideas

A reflexive pronoun reflects back to the same person or thing named by the subject.

## Misconception Checks

Reflexive pronouns are fancy object pronouns; `hisself` or `theirselves` are standard forms; any sentence can use myself.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l04_q01_subject_reflexive_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each subject to the reflexive pronoun that points back to it.
pairs:
  - left: I
    right: myself
  - left: she
    right: herself
  - left: we
    right: ourselves
  - left: they
    right: themselves
explanation: A reflexive pronoun points back to the subject.
hint: Say the subject and ask which reflexive form has the same person or group.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q02_choose_reflexive
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the reflexive pronoun.
sentenceBefore: "Ava taught "
sentenceAfter: " to knit."
choices:
  - herself
  - her
  - she
  - hers
correctAnswer: herself
explanation: Ava is the subject, and Ava is also the one being taught, so herself points back to Ava.
hint: The subject and the learner are the same person.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q03_reflexive_or_object
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses a reflexive pronoun correctly?
choices:
  - The puppy saw itself in the mirror.
  - Please give the form to myself.
  - Himself carried the box.
  - Sara and myself walked home.
correctAnswer: The puppy saw itself in the mirror.
explanation: Itself points back to the subject, the puppy.
hint: A reflexive pronoun should point back to the subject of the same sentence.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q04_correct_nonstandard
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the reflexive pronoun.
sentence: The twins packed theirselves for the trip.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The twins packed themselves for the trip.
explanation: The standard reflexive pronoun for they or twins is themselves.
hint: Theirselves is not a standard reflexive form.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q05_same_subject_reason
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer the question.
passageTitle: Same Subject
passage: Omar reminded himself to bring the project.
question: Why does himself fit the sentence?
choices:
  - It points back to Omar.
  - It names a new person.
  - It shows that the project owns Omar.
  - It replaces the verb.
correctAnswer: It points back to Omar.
explanation: Omar is the subject, and himself points back to Omar.
hint: Ask who did the reminding and who was reminded.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q06_replace_unneeded_reflexive
type: error-correction
prompt: Replace the unneeded reflexive pronoun with the correct object pronoun.
sentence: Please hand the paper to myself.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Please hand the paper to me.
explanation: Myself does not point back to the subject. After to, the object pronoun me is correct.
hint: The person receiving the paper is not the subject of the sentence.
```
