## Teaching Goal

Distinguish possessive pronouns and possessive adjectives from possessive nouns.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose ownership words such as my, your, his, her, our, their, mine, yours, ours, and theirs without adding apostrophes.

## Key Ideas

Possessive pronouns and possessive adjectives show ownership, but they do not use apostrophes.

## Misconception Checks

`Its`, `hers`, `ours`, and `theirs` need apostrophes; `their` and `there` do the same job; a possessive adjective can stand alone after the noun is removed.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l03_q01_possessive_before_noun
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the possessive word that fits before the noun.
sentenceBefore: "Mia zipped "
sentenceAfter: " jacket."
choices:
  - her
  - hers
  - she
  - hers'
correctAnswer: her
explanation: Her comes before the noun jacket to show ownership.
hint: Choose the possessive word that can come before a noun.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q02_no_apostrophe
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which possessive pronoun is written correctly?
choices:
  - theirs
  - their's
  - her's
  - our's
correctAnswer: theirs
explanation: Possessive pronouns such as theirs, hers, and ours do not use apostrophes.
hint: Do not copy the apostrophe pattern from possessive nouns.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q03_correct_hers
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the possessive pronoun.
sentence: That blue notebook is her's.
acceptedAnswers:
  - That blue notebook is hers.
explanation: Hers already shows ownership, so it does not need an apostrophe.
hint: Possessive pronouns do not use apostrophes.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q04_owner_to_form
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each owner idea to a possessive form.
pairs:
  - left: I own it
    right: mine
  - left: we own it
    right: ours
  - left: they own it
    right: theirs
  - left: you own it
    right: yours
explanation: Mine, ours, theirs, and yours can stand alone as possessive pronouns.
hint: "Each right-side word can finish the sentence: It is ___."
```

```question

  - my
```
