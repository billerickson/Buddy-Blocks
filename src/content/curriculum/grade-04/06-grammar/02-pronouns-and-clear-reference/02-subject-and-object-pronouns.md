## Teaching Goal

Choose subject and object pronouns by sentence job.

## Student Outcome

The student can select pronouns such as I, he, she, we, they for subjects and me, him, her, us, them for objects.

## Key Ideas

Pronoun form depends on the job in the sentence: subject forms do the action or are described; object forms receive action or follow many prepositions.

## Misconception Checks

`Me` can be a subject if it sounds familiar; `I` is always more correct than `me`; pronoun job does not matter.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l02_q01_subject_pronoun
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the subject pronoun.
sentenceBefore: ""
sentenceAfter: " carried the tray to the table."
choices:
  - He
  - Him
  - Her
  - Them
correctAnswer: He
explanation: The pronoun is doing the action, so it needs a subject form.
hint: Ask who carried the tray.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q02_object_pronoun
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the object pronoun.
sentenceBefore: "The coach thanked "
sentenceAfter: " after practice."
choices:
  - we
  - us
  - they
  - he
correctAnswer: us
explanation: The pronoun receives the action thanked, so it needs the object form us.
hint: Ask whom the coach thanked.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q03_job_of_pronoun
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the sentence, what job does she have? She planted the seeds.
choices:
  - subject
  - object
  - possessive noun
  - predicate
correctAnswer: subject
explanation: She is the subject because she planted the seeds.
hint: Ask who planted the seeds.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q04_subject_object_pair
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Choose the pronouns that fit each job.
parts:
  - ""
  - " waved to "
  - " from the bus."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: She
    choices:
      - She
      - Her
      - Them
  - correctAnswer: him
    choices:
      - he
      - him
      - they
explanation: She is the subject doing the waving. Him is the object after to.
hint: Use a subject form before the predicate and an object form after to.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q05_correct_subject_form
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the subject pronoun.
sentence: Me walked to the library.
acceptedAnswers:
  - I walked to the library.
explanation: The pronoun is the subject, so use I, not me.
hint: The pronoun is doing the walking.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q06_after_preposition
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer the question.
passageTitle: After A Preposition
passage: The librarian saved two seats for them.
question: Why is them correct?
choices:
  - It is an object pronoun after for.
  - It is the subject of the sentence.
  - It is a proper noun.
  - It shows ownership.
correctAnswer: It is an object pronoun after for.
explanation: For is a preposition, and object pronouns often follow prepositions.
hint: Look at the word right before them.
```
