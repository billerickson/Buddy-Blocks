## Teaching Goal

Distinguish linking verbs from action verbs and identify the word that describes or renames the subject.

## Student Outcome

The student can recognize common linking verbs and explain what they connect.

## Key Ideas

A linking verb links the subject to a describing word or naming word; it does not show an action done to an object.

## Misconception Checks

Every verb shows action; `is` is not a verb; the word after a linking verb is a direct object.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l03_q01_linking_verb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word is the linking verb? The soup is warm.
choices:
  - soup
  - is
  - warm
  - The
correctAnswer: is
explanation: Is links the subject soup to the describing word warm.
hint: Look for the word that connects the subject to a description.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q02_subject_complement_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each sentence to the word that describes or renames the subject.
pairs:
  - left: The sky became dark.
    right: dark
  - left: My uncle is a pilot.
    right: a pilot
  - left: The puppies were sleepy.
    right: sleepy
  - left: That sound seems loud.
    right: loud
explanation: After a linking verb, the complement describes or renames the subject.
hint: Find what the subject is or seems to be.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q03_choose_linking
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the linking verb that completes the sentence.
sentenceBefore: "The room "
sentenceAfter: " quiet after lunch."
choices:
  - was
  - carried
  - jumped
  - painted
correctAnswer: was
explanation: Was links room to quiet. The room is not doing an action to quiet.
hint: Choose the verb that connects the subject to a describing word.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q04_action_or_linking
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses smelled as a linking verb?
choices:
  - The baker smelled the bread.
  - The bread smelled fresh.
  - The dog smelled the trail.
  - I smelled smoke outside.
correctAnswer: The bread smelled fresh.
explanation: Smelled links bread to fresh. The bread is not doing the action of smelling.
hint: Ask whether smelled shows an action or connects the subject to a description.
```


```question
key: u03_l03_q06_no_direct_object
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why is happy not a direct object in The child felt happy?
choices:
  - Felt links child to happy.
  - Happy is a proper noun.
  - The child acted on happy.
  - Happy is the subject.
correctAnswer: Felt links child to happy.
explanation: In this sentence, felt is a linking verb. Happy describes the subject.
hint: A direct object receives action. Is happy receiving an action?
```
