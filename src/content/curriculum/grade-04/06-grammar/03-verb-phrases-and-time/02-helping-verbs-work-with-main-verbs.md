## Teaching Goal

Recognize helping verbs as words that work with a main verb to form a verb phrase.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a helping verb and main verb in common verb phrases.

## Key Ideas

Helping verbs such as is, are, was, were, has, have, had, will, can, and should support the main verb.

## Misconception Checks

Helping verbs can be ignored when naming the verb; the main verb alone always shows the full time or meaning; all small words before verbs are helpers.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l02_q01_helper_meanings
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each helping verb phrase to the clue it gives.
pairs:
  - left: will read
    right: the reading happens later
  - left: is reading
    right: the reading is happening now
  - left: has read
    right: the reading is already finished
  - left: can read
    right: the reading is possible
explanation: Helping verbs work with main verbs to add time or meaning.
hint: Notice the helper before read.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q02_choose_helper
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the helping verb that completes the sentence.
sentenceBefore: "The class "
sentenceAfter: " finished the science poster."
choices:
  - has
  - under
  - bright
  - quickly
correctAnswer: has
explanation: Has works with finished to make the verb phrase has finished.
hint: Choose the word that can help the main verb finished.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q03_complete_verb_phrase
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which words are the complete verb phrase? We should bring pencils.
choices:
  - We should
  - should bring
  - bring pencils
  - pencils
correctAnswer: should bring
explanation: Should is the helping verb, and bring is the main verb.
hint: Find the helper and the main verb together.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q04_helper_main_pair

parts:
  - "The class "
  - " "
  - " the play tomorrow."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: will
    choices:
      - will
      - under
      - blue
  - correctAnswer: perform
    choices:
      - perform
      - performance
      - performer

```

```question
key: u03_l02_q05_add_helper
type: error-correction
prompt: Add the missing helping verb.
sentence: The team practicing before the game.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The team is practicing before the game.
  - The team was practicing before the game.
explanation: Practicing needs a helping verb such as is or was to make a complete verb phrase.
hint: Add a helper before practicing.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q06_not_helping_verb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word is not a helping verb?
choices:
  - will
  - has
  - under
  - can
correctAnswer: under
explanation: Under is a preposition, not a helping verb.
hint: A helping verb can work with a main verb, as in will go or can see.
```
