## Teaching Goal

Combine and identify compound predicates that share one subject.

## Student Outcome

The student can recognize two or more actions or verb phrases joined for the same subject.

## Key Ideas

A compound predicate has one subject doing or being more than one thing.

## Misconception Checks

Every sentence with two verbs has two subjects; a compound predicate must repeat the subject; a list of nouns after a verb is a compound predicate.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l03_q01_find_compound_predicate
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What is the compound predicate in this sentence? The kite rose and dipped.
choices:
  - The kite
  - rose and dipped
  - and dipped
  - kite rose
correctAnswer: rose and dipped
explanation: "The kite has one subject and two predicate actions: rose and dipped."
hint: Ask what the kite did.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q02_combine_predicates_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the words in order to combine the predicates.
items:
  - jumped
  - and
  - The fox
  - landed.
correctOrder:
  - The fox
  - jumped
  - and
  - landed.
explanation: The fox is one subject doing two actions.
hint: Keep the subject once, then join the actions.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q03_not_compound_predicate
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence does not have a compound predicate?
choices:
  - The dog barked and wagged its tail.
  - Mila cooked and served dinner.
  - The rain fell and soaked the path.
  - The rain and wind shook the tent.
correctAnswer: The rain and wind shook the tent.
explanation: The rain and wind is a compound subject. The predicate is shook the tent.
hint: Look for one subject doing two actions.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q05_combine_actions
type: error-correction
prompt: Combine the two sentences with a compound predicate.
sentence: The bird chirped. The bird flew away.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The bird chirped and flew away.
explanation: The bird is the shared subject, so the actions can be joined in one predicate.
hint: Write the subject once and join the two actions.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q06_predicate_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer the question.
passageTitle: One Subject, Two Actions
passage: The teacher smiled and opened the door.
question: Why is smiled and opened the door a compound predicate?
choices:
  - One subject does two actions.
  - Two subjects share one action.
  - The sentence has two proper nouns.
  - The words after and are a fragment.
correctAnswer: One subject does two actions.
explanation: The teacher is one subject. Smiled and opened the door are two predicate parts.
hint: Ask who did both actions.
```
