## Teaching Goal

Combine and identify compound subjects that share one predicate.

## Student Outcome

The student can recognize two or more subjects joined by and or or that do the same predicate.

## Key Ideas

A compound subject has more than one subject sharing the same predicate.

## Misconception Checks

A compound subject makes two sentences; the predicate is part of the compound subject; every sentence with and has a compound subject.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l02_q01_find_compound_subject
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What is the compound subject in this sentence? Leo and Nora painted the sign.
choices:
  - Leo and Nora
  - painted the sign
  - the sign
  - Nora painted
correctAnswer: Leo and Nora
explanation: Leo and Nora are two subjects sharing the predicate painted the sign.
hint: Ask who painted the sign.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q02_combine_subjects_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the words in order to combine the subjects.
items:
  - slept.
  - and
  - The cat
  - the dog
correctOrder:
  - The cat
  - and
  - the dog
  - slept.
explanation: The cat and the dog share the predicate slept.
hint: Start with the first subject, then join the second subject before the predicate.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q03_not_compound_subject
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence does not have a compound subject?
choices:
  - Maya and Iris watered the garden.
  - The moon and stars shone brightly.
  - The rabbit hopped and hid.
  - Pencils and markers filled the cup.
correctAnswer: The rabbit hopped and hid.
explanation: "The rabbit has one subject and a compound predicate: hopped and hid."
hint: Look for two subjects sharing one predicate, not two actions.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q04_subject_conjunction
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the sentence with a compound subject.
parts:
  - ""
  - " and "
  - " planted the seeds."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: Ari
    choices:
      - Ari
      - planted
      - quickly
  - correctAnswer: Jules
    choices:
      - Jules
      - watered
      - under
explanation: Ari and Jules are both subjects sharing the predicate planted the seeds.
hint: Both blanks should name who planted.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q05_combine_sentences
type: error-correction
prompt: Combine the two sentences with a compound subject.
sentence: Sam carried the box. Zoe carried the box.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Sam and Zoe carried the box.
explanation: Sam and Zoe share the same predicate, so they can be combined as a compound subject.
hint: Keep the shared predicate once.
```
