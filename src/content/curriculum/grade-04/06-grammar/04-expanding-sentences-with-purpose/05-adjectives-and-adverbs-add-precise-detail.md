## Teaching Goal

Use adjectives and adverbs as modifiers that add precise but useful detail.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose whether a modifier describes a noun or a verb and select a detail that improves clarity.

## Key Ideas

Adjectives usually describe nouns; adverbs often describe verbs by telling how, when, or where.

## Misconception Checks

Adjectives and adverbs do the same job; more modifiers always improve a sentence; an adverb must end in `ly`.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l05_q01_modifier_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each modifier to the word it describes.
pairs:
  - left: red in red wagon
    right: wagon
  - left: softly in sang softly
    right: sang
  - left: three in three shells
    right: shells
  - left: later in arrived later
    right: arrived
explanation: Adjectives describe nouns. Adverbs often describe verbs.
hint: Ask what word each modifier gives more detail about.
```

```question
key: u04_l05_q02_choose_adjective
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the adjective that describes the noun.
sentenceBefore: "The "
sentenceAfter: " kitten slept on the blanket."
choices:
  - sleepy
  - sleepily
  - slept
  - under
correctAnswer: sleepy
explanation: Sleepy describes the noun kitten.
hint: Choose the word that tells what kind of kitten.
```

```question
key: u04_l05_q03_find_adverb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word is the adverb in this sentence? The mouse quietly slipped away.
choices:
  - mouse
  - quietly
  - slipped
  - away
correctAnswer: quietly
explanation: Quietly describes how the mouse slipped.
hint: Ask how the action happened.
```

```question
key: u04_l05_q04_adverb_form
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the word that correctly describes the action.
sentenceBefore: "The class listened "
sentenceAfter: " to the story."
choices:
  - careful
  - carefully
  - care
  - caring
correctAnswer: carefully
explanation: Carefully describes how the class listened, so the adverb form fits.
hint: Use the word that answers how.
```

```question
key: u04_l05_q05_best_detail_revision
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which revision adds the clearest useful detail? The dog ran.
choices:
  - The dog ran fastly thing.
  - The spotted dog ran quickly across the yard.
  - The dog dog ran.
  - The running dog ran running.
correctAnswer: The spotted dog ran quickly across the yard.
explanation: Spotted describes the dog, quickly describes ran, and across the yard adds a clear place detail.
hint: Choose the revision with details that make sense and add clear meaning.
```

```question
key: u04_l05_q06_correct_modifier_type
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the modifier so it describes the action.
sentence: The turtle moved slow across the path.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The turtle moved slowly across the path.

hint: The modifier tells how the turtle moved, so use the adverb form.
```
