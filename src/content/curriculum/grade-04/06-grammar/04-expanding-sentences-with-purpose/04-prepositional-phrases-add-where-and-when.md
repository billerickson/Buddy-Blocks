## Teaching Goal

Identify prepositional phrases and their objects as detail chunks that often tell where or when.

## Student Outcome

The student can find a common preposition, its object, and the whole phrase in a short sentence.

## Key Ideas

A prepositional phrase begins with a preposition and ends with a noun or pronoun object.

## Misconception Checks

Every small word starts a prepositional phrase; the object of a preposition is the sentence's direct object; a phrase can stand alone as a complete sentence.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l04_q01_find_phrase
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which words are the prepositional phrase? The keys are under the mat.
choices:
  - The keys
  - are under
  - under the mat
  - the mat
correctAnswer: under the mat
explanation: Under is the preposition, and the mat is its object. Together they make the phrase under the mat.
hint: Find the preposition and follow it to its object.
```

```question

    right: what tool is used

    right: near what place
explanation: Prepositional phrases often add where, when, or how details.
```

```question
key: u04_l04_q03_complete_phrase
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the object that completes the prepositional phrase.
sentenceBefore: "The toy rolled under "
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - the couch
  - rolled
  - quickly
  - under
correctAnswer: the couch
explanation: Under the couch is a prepositional phrase. The couch is the object of the preposition.
hint: The phrase needs a noun after under.
```

```question

  - storm
  - before the
```

```question

  - After dinner, we played a game.
  - After dinner, I washed the dishes.
  - After dinner, the family walked outside.

hint: Keep the phrase, but add who did something and what they did.
```
