## Teaching Goal

Teach singular and plural noun forms as number signals in sentences.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify and form common singular and plural nouns in short contexts.

## Key Ideas

A singular noun names one; a plural noun names more than one. Many plural nouns add `s` or `es`, while some change spelling.

## Misconception Checks

Every plural adds only `s`; any noun ending in `s` is plural; irregular forms such as children are mistakes.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_number_clue
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the noun that fits the number clue.
sentenceBefore: "Three "
sentenceAfter: " waited near the door."
choices:
  - student
  - students
  - student's
  - students'
correctAnswer: students
explanation: Three means more than one, so the plural noun students fits.
hint: Look at the number clue before the noun.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q02_singular_plural_pairs
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each singular noun to its plural form.
pairs:
  - left: box
    right: boxes
  - left: story
    right: stories
  - left: brush
    right: brushes
  - left: child
    right: children
explanation: Some plurals add s, some add es, some change y to ies, and some irregular plurals change form.
hint: Read each singular noun and think about how it changes when there is more than one.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q03_one_or_more
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses a singular noun?
choices:
  - The birds rested on the wire.
  - The fox crossed the path.
  - The leaves covered the yard.
  - The children laughed.
correctAnswer: The fox crossed the path.
explanation: Fox names one animal in this sentence, so it is singular.
hint: Singular means one.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q04_regular_plural_text
type: text-input
prompt: Type the plural form of class.
acceptedAnswers:
  - classes
answerType: text
explanation: Class ends with an s sound, so add es to form classes.
hint: For many nouns ending in s, sh, ch, or x, add es.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q05_plural_not_possessive
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence needs a plural noun, not a possessive noun?
choices:
  - The dog's leash is red.
  - Two dogs barked at the gate.
  - Maya's pencil broke.
  - The teacher's desk is neat.
correctAnswer: Two dogs barked at the gate.
explanation: Two dogs names more than one dog. It does not show ownership.
hint: Ask whether the noun means more than one or belongs to someone.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q06_fix_plural
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the plural noun.
sentence: The three fox ran into the woods.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The three foxes ran into the woods.
explanation: Three tells us there is more than one fox, so the plural noun is foxes.
hint: The number clue tells you the noun needs to name more than one.
```
