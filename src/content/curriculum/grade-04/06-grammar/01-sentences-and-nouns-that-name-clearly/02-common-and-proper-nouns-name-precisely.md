## Teaching Goal

Distinguish common nouns from proper nouns and connect proper nouns to capitalization.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose whether a noun is common or proper and capitalize proper nouns in short sentences.

## Key Ideas

Common nouns name any person, place, thing, or idea; proper nouns name a specific one.

## Misconception Checks

Any important noun is proper; all words naming people should be capitalized; a proper noun can stay lowercase inside a sentence.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l02_q01_common_or_proper
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each noun to its kind.
pairs:
  - left: city
    right: common noun
  - left: Austin
    right: proper noun
  - left: teacher
    right: general person name
  - left: Ms. Rivera
    right: specific person name
explanation: Common nouns name general people, places, things, or ideas. Proper nouns name specific ones.
hint: Proper nouns name one specific person, place, or thing.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q02_capitalize_proper
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the proper noun capitalization.
sentence: my cousin lives in denver.
acceptedAnswers:
  - My cousin lives in Denver.
explanation: The sentence begins with a capital letter, and Denver is a specific city name, so it is capitalized.
hint: Check both the first word of the sentence and the specific place name.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q03_choose_proper_noun
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses a proper noun correctly?
choices:
  - The class visited lincoln park.
  - The Class visited Lincoln park.
  - The class visited Lincoln Park.
  - The class visited park.
correctAnswer: The class visited Lincoln Park.
explanation: Lincoln Park is the specific name of a place, so both words in the name are capitalized.
hint: Look for the specific place name.
```
