## Teaching Goal

Use possessive nouns to show ownership or close relationship and distinguish possessive forms from plurals.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose and correct singular and regular plural possessive nouns in short sentences.

## Key Ideas

A possessive noun shows that something belongs to or is connected to someone or something.

## Misconception Checks

Possessive `-'s` means more than one; plural nouns always need apostrophes; every noun ending in `s` should get `-'s`.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l04_q01_find_owner
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the sentence, who owns the notebook? Jordan's notebook fell from the desk.
choices:
  - Jordan
  - notebook
  - desk
  - fell
correctAnswer: Jordan
explanation: Jordan's is possessive. It shows that the notebook belongs to Jordan.
hint: Look for the noun with the apostrophe.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q02_singular_possessive
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the possessive noun that completes the sentence.
sentenceBefore: "The "
sentenceAfter: " collar jingled."
choices:
  - cat
  - cats
  - cat's
  - cats'
correctAnswer: cat's
explanation: One cat owns the collar, so use cat's.
hint: One owner usually adds apostrophe s.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q03_plural_possessive
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the possessive noun for more than one owner.
sentenceBefore: "The "
sentenceAfter: " locker room was noisy."
choices:
  - players
  - player's
  - players'
  - playerss
correctAnswer: players'
explanation: More than one player owns or uses the locker room. For a regular plural ending in s, add the apostrophe after the s.
hint: First make the noun plural, then add the apostrophe.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q04_plural_or_possessive
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses a possessive noun?
choices:
  - The teachers met after school.
  - The teacher's notes were on the table.
  - The students carried books.
  - The pencils rolled away.
correctAnswer: The teacher's notes were on the table.
explanation: Teacher's shows that the notes belong to the teacher.
hint: A possessive noun shows ownership or a close connection.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q05_correct_apostrophe
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the possessive noun for one bird.
sentence: The birds nest sat high in the tree.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The bird's nest sat high in the tree.
explanation: One bird has the nest, so bird becomes bird's.
hint: Ask who owns or is connected to the nest.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q06_possessive_meaning
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer the question.
passageTitle: Ownership
passage: The coaches' whistles hung by the door.
question: What does coaches' show?
choices:
  - More than one coach is connected to the whistles.
  - One coach is connected to the whistles.
  - The whistles own the coaches.
  - Coaches is a proper noun.
correctAnswer: More than one coach is connected to the whistles.
explanation: Coaches is plural, and the apostrophe after s shows possession by more than one coach.
hint: Notice that the apostrophe comes after a plural noun ending in s.
```
