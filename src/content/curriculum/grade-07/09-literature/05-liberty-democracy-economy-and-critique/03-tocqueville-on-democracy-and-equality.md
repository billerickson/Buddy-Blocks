## Teaching Goal

Read democratic analysis as diagnosis rather than simple praise or complaint.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain how equality can produce both strengths and risks in a Tocqueville-style passage.

## Misconception Checks

Democracy is discussed only to praise or condemn; equality has one simple effect; diagnosis means personal preference.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l03_q01_diagnosis
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What does it mean to read democratic analysis as diagnosis?
choices:
  - Name a social condition, then trace both strengths and risks.
  - Praise everything about democracy.
  - Condemn everything about democracy.
  - Replace the passage with a campaign slogan.
correctAnswer: Name a social condition, then trace both strengths and risks.
explanation: "Diagnosis can be balanced: it observes mixed effects."
hint: Look for an answer with both benefit and danger.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q02_equality_passage
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the democratic diagnosis and answer.
passageTitle: Equal Conditions
passage: |
  When citizens see one another as equals, old deference weakens and public energy grows. Yet the same equality may make each person distrust excellence and prefer the comfort of the majority.
question: Which answer captures the diagnosis?
choices:
  - Equality can energize public life while also creating pressure toward conformity.
  - Equality has only benefits.
  - Equality has only dangers.
  - The passage is not about democracy.
correctAnswer: Equality can energize public life while also creating pressure toward conformity.
explanation: The passage names both public energy and majority comfort.
hint: Notice the word 'Yet.'
```
