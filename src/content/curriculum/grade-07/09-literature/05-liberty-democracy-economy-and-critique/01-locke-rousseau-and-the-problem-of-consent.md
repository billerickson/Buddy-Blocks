## Teaching Goal

Compare consent, freedom, political beginning, and social contract language.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify how two modern political arguments define consent or freedom differently.

## Misconception Checks

Consent and freedom mean the same thing in every text; social contract is a literal signed document in all arguments.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l01_q01_consent_first_move
type: multiple-choice
prompt: When two political writers use the word consent, what should you ask first?
choices:
  - How does each writer define the term and what problem is it meant to solve?
  - Which writer sounds more modern?

  - Which passage is shorter?

explanation: The same term can do different work in different arguments.
hint: Focus on definition and problem before evaluation.
```
