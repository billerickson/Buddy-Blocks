## Teaching Goal

Distinguish economic and moral explanation from endorsement or command.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify how a Smith-style passage explains coordination, self-interest, sympathy, or commerce.

## Misconception Checks

Self-interest always means selfishness; explaining a market process means endorsing every outcome; sympathy is irrelevant to economic thought.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l04_q01_explanation_not_endorsement
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement best distinguishes explanation from endorsement?
choices:
  - A writer can explain how commerce coordinates action without praising every outcome.
  - If a writer explains commerce, he approves of every result.
  - Explanation means the writer has no moral vocabulary.
  - Economic prose cannot make arguments.
correctAnswer: A writer can explain how commerce coordinates action without praising every outcome.
explanation: The lesson asks students to read how an explanation works before judging it.
hint: Explaining how something works is not identical to approving it.
```

choices:
  - Exchange coordinating labor and need among strangers.
  - A law forbidding bread.
  - A family meal with no commerce.
  - A manifesto calling for revolution.

explanation: The passage explains how exchange connects the baker's labor to others' needs.
