## Teaching Goal

Separate three response jobs students often collapse.

## Student Outcome

The student can classify a sentence as summary, interpretation, evaluation, or unsupported opinion.

## Misconception Checks

Interpretation is personal reaction; evaluation can happen before understanding; summary includes judgment words.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l02_q01_response_job
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is a summary rather than an interpretation or evaluation?
choices:
  - The speaker says the city needs just laws before it can educate citizens.
  - The passage probably means that law is more important than friendship.
  - The argument is unconvincing because it ignores private life.
  - This is the best passage in the whole work.
correctAnswer: The speaker says the city needs just laws before it can educate citizens.
explanation: A summary reports what the passage says without adding meaning or judgment.
hint: Look for the option that stays closest to what the text says.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q02_order_response_jobs
type: order-items
prompt: Put the response jobs in the usual order for careful reading.
items:
  - Evaluate
  - Summarize
  - Orient
  - Interpret
correctOrder:
  - Orient
  - Summarize
  - Interpret
  - Evaluate
explanation: You first locate the passage, then state what it says, explain meaning, and finally judge.
hint: Judgment should come after understanding.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q03_interpretation_choice
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the interpretation.
passageTitle: A Short Exchange
passage: |
  The teacher asks, "Is a city just because its walls are strong, or because its people know how to live together?"
  The student answers, "Strong walls help, but they do not teach anyone what is worth defending."
question: Which option interprets the passage rather than merely summarizes it?
choices:
  - The exchange suggests that justice depends on shared moral understanding, not only safety.
  - The teacher asks about walls and justice.
  - The student mentions strong walls.
  - The passage is wrong because cities need armies.
correctAnswer: The exchange suggests that justice depends on shared moral understanding, not only safety.
explanation: The correct option explains the meaning of the exchange, using the contrast between walls and what is worth defending.
hint: An interpretation explains what the details mean.
```
