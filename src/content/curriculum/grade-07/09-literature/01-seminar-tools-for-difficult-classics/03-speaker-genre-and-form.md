## Teaching Goal

Build caution around author, speaker, narrator, character, and form.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain why genre and speaker affect interpretation.

## Misconception Checks

Every sentence in a text is the author's direct opinion; genre labels are trivia; dramatic speech is the play's thesis.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_author_speaker
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In a play, a character says, 'Power is the only truth.' What is the most careful first conclusion?
choices:
  - A character expresses this view, but the play may test or challenge it.
  - The playwright certainly believes power is the only truth.
  - The statement is false because plays cannot make arguments.
  - Every other character must agree.
correctAnswer: A character expresses this view, but the play may test or challenge it.
explanation: Dramatic speech belongs first to a character. The whole play may confirm, complicate, or reject it.
hint: Separate the voice inside the work from the author who shaped the work.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q02_forms_and_cautions
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each form to a useful interpretive caution.
pairs:
  - left: dialogue
    right: Track who asks and who answers.
  - left: satire
    right: Do not take exaggeration only literally.
  - left: confession
    right: Notice how memory becomes reflection.
  - left: manifesto
    right: Watch for audience and call to action.
explanation: Different forms ask readers to notice different features.
hint: Think about what each form typically does.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q05_genre_clue
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the note and answer.
passageTitle: Form Clues
passage: |
  A text presents two speakers. One gives a confident definition of justice. The other asks questions until the definition becomes unstable.
question: Which form best fits this description?
choices:
  - dialogue
  - manifesto
  - soliloquy
  - scientific reflection
correctAnswer: dialogue
explanation: Two speakers testing a definition through questions is characteristic of dialogue.
hint: Look for more than one speaker and a movement through questions.
```
