## Teaching Goal

Practice choosing textual evidence that actually supports an interpretation.

## Student Outcome

The student can select evidence, reject weak evidence, and connect evidence to a claim.

## Misconception Checks

Any quote counts; evidence is decoration; longer evidence is automatically stronger; context can replace passage words.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l04_q01_best_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the best evidence.
passageTitle: Practice And Character
passage: |
  The speaker says, "No one becomes just by praising justice from far away. We become trained by the small actions we repeat until choice itself has a shape."
question: Which detail best supports the claim that the passage links character to repeated practice?
choices:
  - the small actions we repeat
  - from far away
  - The speaker says
  - No one
correctAnswer: the small actions we repeat
explanation: That phrase directly connects repeated actions with the formation of choice and character.
hint: Find the detail that names repetition or practice.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q02_claim_evidence_reasoning
type: order-items
prompt: Put the parts of a supported answer in order.
items:
  - Explain how the evidence supports the claim.
  - State a narrow claim.
  - Choose a passage detail.
  - Check that the claim does not overreach.
correctOrder:
  - State a narrow claim.
  - Choose a passage detail.
  - Explain how the evidence supports the claim.
  - Check that the claim does not overreach.
explanation: A supported answer connects a claim to evidence and then checks its limits.
hint: Evidence needs a claim before it can support anything.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q03_weak_evidence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A student claims, 'The passage presents habit as important.' Which evidence is weakest?
choices:
  - The passage was written a long time ago.
  - The speaker says actions are repeated.
  - The passage says choice takes shape.
  - The speaker connects training with becoming just.
correctAnswer: The passage was written a long time ago.
explanation: The age of the passage does not support the specific claim about habit.
hint: Choose the option that does not point to habit or repeated action.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q04_evidence_fit
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each claim to the evidence that best supports it.
pairs:
  - left: The speaker values repeated practice.
    right: small actions we repeat
  - left: The speaker rejects distant admiration.
    right: praising justice from far away
  - left: The speaker links action to choice.
    right: choice itself has a shape
  - left: The passage uses moral vocabulary.
    right: just
explanation: Good evidence fits the exact claim, not just the general topic.
hint: Match the words that point most directly to the claim.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q05_reasoning_sentence
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the reasoning sentence.
sentenceBefore: The phrase 'small actions we repeat' supports the claim because it shows character forming through
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - practice
  - luck
  - title
  - audience
correctAnswer: practice
explanation: Repeated small actions are practice, and the passage connects practice to character.
hint: What do repeated actions become?
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q06_overreach_check
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which claim stays within the evidence from the practice-and-character passage?
choices:
  - The speaker argues that repeated action shapes moral character.
  - The passage proves every ancient author agreed about politics.
  - The speaker rejects all education and all laws.
  - The passage shows that justice is impossible.
correctAnswer: The speaker argues that repeated action shapes moral character.
explanation: This claim is narrow and directly supported by the passage.
hint: Avoid claims that reach beyond what the short passage can prove.
```
