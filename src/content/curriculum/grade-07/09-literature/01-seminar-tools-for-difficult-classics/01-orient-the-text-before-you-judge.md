## Teaching Goal

Teach students a first-pass routine for difficult primary texts.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify form, speaker, audience, situation, and central question before judging a passage.

## Misconception Checks

Difficulty means failure; context alone gives meaning; the fastest opinion is the best answer.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l01_q01_first_move
type: multiple-choice
prompt: You open a difficult passage from an old dialogue. What is the best first move?
choices:
  - Ask who is speaking, what form this is, and what question is active.
  - Decide whether you agree before rereading.
  - Look for one famous quote and ignore the rest.
  - Assume the speaker is the author's exact opinion.
correctAnswer: Ask who is speaking, what form this is, and what question is active.
explanation: Orientation comes before judgment. It gives you the speaker, form, situation, and question.
hint: The first move should help you understand the passage before evaluating it.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q02_orientation_categories

pairs:
  - left: Who is speaking?
    right: speaker or voice
  - left: What kind of text is this?
    right: form or genre
  - left: Who is being addressed?
    right: audience
  - left: What problem is being faced?
    right: central question

```

```question
key: u01_l01_q03_passage_orientation
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage note and answer.
passageTitle: A Difficult Opening
passage: |
  A student reads a translated excerpt in which one speaker questions another about courage. The passage does not announce a final answer. Instead, the second speaker revises his definition after each question.
question: Which orientation detail matters most for reading this passage well?
choices:
  - It is a dialogue where definitions are being tested.
  - It must be a modern scientific article.
  - The first speaker must be the author's villain.
  - The passage can be judged without tracking the questions.
correctAnswer: It is a dialogue where definitions are being tested.
explanation: "The note describes dialogue movement: questions test and revise definitions."
hint: Look for clues about form and how the passage moves.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q04_order_reading_moves
type: order-items
prompt: Put these reading moves in a responsible order.
items:
  - Judge whether the argument is persuasive.
  - Identify speaker, form, audience, and question.
  - Summarize what the passage says.
  - Choose evidence for an interpretation.
correctOrder:
  - Identify speaker, form, audience, and question.
  - Summarize what the passage says.
  - Choose evidence for an interpretation.
  - Judge whether the argument is persuasive.
explanation: Orientation and summary prepare you to interpret and evaluate responsibly.
hint: The earliest move should help you understand before you judge.
```

```question

  - If we know the author's century, every sentence is easy.
```
