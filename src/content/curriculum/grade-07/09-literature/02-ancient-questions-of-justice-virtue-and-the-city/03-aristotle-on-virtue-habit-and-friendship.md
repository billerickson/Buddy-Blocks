## Teaching Goal

Introduce virtue as trained habit and practical judgment rather than simple rule following.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain how habit, choice, mean, and friendship function in an Aristotelian-style passage.

## Misconception Checks

Virtue is only feeling nice; the mean is always the middle amount; friendship is only private preference.

## Questions

```question

passageTitle: Practice And Character

  A student becomes fair not by repeating the word fairness, but by choosing fair actions until the choice becomes steady.

  - Virtue is formed through repeated practice and choice.
  - Virtue is only memorizing a definition.
  - Fairness is impossible to learn.

explanation: The passage contrasts repeating a word with practicing fair actions.
hint: Look for what changes the student over time.
```
