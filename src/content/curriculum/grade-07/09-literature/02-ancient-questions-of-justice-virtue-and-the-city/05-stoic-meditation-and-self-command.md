## Teaching Goal

Read Stoic meditation as a disciplined practice of judgment, attention, and desire.

## Student Outcome

The student can distinguish Stoic self-command from apathy or lack of love.

## Misconception Checks

Stoicism means not caring; a short maxim needs no interpretation; self-command is selfish isolation.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l05_q01_control_distinction
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which question best fits a Stoic control distinction?
choices:
  - What is up to me, and what is not?
  - How can I control every event?
  - How can I avoid caring about justice?
  - Which feeling should I pretend not to have?
correctAnswer: What is up to me, and what is not?
explanation: Stoic meditation often distinguishes events from judgments and choices.
hint: Look for a distinction between choice and external event.
```
