## Teaching Goal

Teach biography as moral comparison and attention to character, not simple hero worship.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify how a biographical anecdote invites moral judgment through contrast and detail.

## Misconception Checks

A famous life is automatically admirable; one anecdote proves everything; biography is only fact collection.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l04_q01_biography_purpose
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In a moral biography, why does a small anecdote matter?
choices:
  - It can reveal character through action, motive, and consequence.
  - It proves the person was perfect in every situation.
  - It is only trivia for memorization.
  - It removes the need for interpretation.
correctAnswer: It can reveal character through action, motive, and consequence.
explanation: Moral biography uses selected details to invite judgment about character.
hint: Think about what an action can reveal.
```

```question
  A young leader wins a prize but gives it to a rival who had lost honorably. The crowd cheers, but the narrator lingers on the leader's quiet face rather than the applause.
question: Which detail most supports the idea that the anecdote values self-command over fame?
choices:
  - the narrator lingers on the leader's quiet face
  - the crowd cheers
  - a prize exists
  - the rival had lost
correctAnswer: the narrator lingers on the leader's quiet face
explanation: The quiet face points toward inward command rather than public applause.
hint: Find the detail that turns attention away from fame.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q04_avoid_hero_worship
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which claim avoids simple hero worship?
choices:
  - The anecdote presents one action as admirable but still asks readers to judge the pattern.
  - The leader once acted well, so every action in his life was perfect.
  - Famous people cannot be morally examined.
  - If a narrator praises someone, evidence no longer matters.
correctAnswer: The anecdote presents one action as admirable but still asks readers to judge the pattern.
explanation: A careful claim notices admiration while keeping judgment evidence-based.
hint: Avoid claims that make one detail prove total perfection.
```
