## Teaching Goal

Read grotesque humor and excess as satire of learning, appetite, and social forms.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a satiric target behind an exaggerated educational or social scene.

## Misconception Checks

Grotesque humor has no serious target; satire endorses whatever it describes; strange style makes interpretation impossible.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l03_q01_grotesque_target
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the satiric scene and answer.
passageTitle: The Giant Classroom
passage: |
  A giant student is praised for reciting a thousand rules before breakfast, though he cannot explain why one rule matters or how to use it kindly.
question: What educational problem is the scene most likely satirizing?
choices:
  - Learning that prizes empty recitation over understanding and wise use.
  - Students who eat breakfast.
  - All memory work in every situation.
  - The physical size of classrooms.
correctAnswer: Learning that prizes empty recitation over understanding and wise use.
explanation: The exaggeration targets rule-reciting without meaning or application.
hint: Ask what habit the exaggerated scene mocks.
```
