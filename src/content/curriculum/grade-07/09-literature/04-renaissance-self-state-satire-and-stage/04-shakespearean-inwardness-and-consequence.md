## Teaching Goal

Analyze soliloquy, motive, action, and dramatic irony.

## Student Outcome

The student can connect a dramatic speech to motive, choice, and consequence without treating it as the play's whole thesis.

## Misconception Checks

A soliloquy is direct author commentary; motive is the same as excuse; one speech explains the whole play.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l04_q01_soliloquy_caution
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What is a careful way to read a soliloquy?
choices:
  - It can reveal a character's inner conflict, but the whole play will test that speech through action.
  - It is always the playwright's direct opinion.
  - It makes consequences irrelevant.
  - It has no connection to motive.
correctAnswer: It can reveal a character's inner conflict, but the whole play will test that speech through action.
explanation: Drama reveals thought through speech and action together.
hint: Separate character speech from the play's full design.
```
