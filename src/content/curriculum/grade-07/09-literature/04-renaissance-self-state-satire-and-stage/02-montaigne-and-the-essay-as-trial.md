## Teaching Goal

Teach the essay as a shaped experiment in judgment.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify how an essay tests a thought, shifts perspective, or qualifies a claim.

## Misconception Checks

Essay means random opinion; uncertainty means weakness; a qualified claim has no point.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l02_q01_essay_as_trial
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What does it mean to read the essay as a 'trial' of an idea?
choices:
  - The writer tests a thought through examples, turns, and qualifications.
  - The essay must be a courtroom transcript.
  - The writer has no point if he qualifies anything.
  - Every sentence is a final rule.
correctAnswer: The writer tests a thought through examples, turns, and qualifications.
explanation: The essay may explore and qualify rather than announce a rigid conclusion.
hint: Think of trying or testing an idea.
```

```question
prompt: Read the essay-style passage and answer.

passage: |
  I first thought custom was a chain only. Yet when I watch children learn courtesy, I see that custom can also train gentleness. Perhaps the danger is not custom itself, but custom left unexamined.

choices:
  - Custom can train good habits, but unexamined custom is dangerous.
  - All custom is always a chain.
  - Children cannot learn courtesy.
  - The writer refuses to think about custom.
correctAnswer: Custom can train good habits, but unexamined custom is dangerous.
explanation: The writer revises the first thought after considering an example.
hint: Look for 'Yet' and 'Perhaps.'
```
