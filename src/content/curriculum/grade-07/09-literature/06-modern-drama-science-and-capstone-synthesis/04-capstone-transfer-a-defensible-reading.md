## Teaching Goal

Apply the full reading routine to an unfamiliar excerpt and support a focused claim.

## Student Outcome

The student can orient, summarize, interpret, choose evidence, and compare a new passage in a compact transfer task.

## Misconception Checks

Transfer means guessing from background knowledge; a capstone claim can be broad and unsupported; comparison can ignore form.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l04_q01_full_routine
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sequence best describes the capstone reading routine?
choices:
  - Orient, summarize, notice form and speaker, choose evidence, make a narrow claim, compare responsibly.
  - Judge, skim, ignore evidence, and guess the author's biography.
  - Memorize titles and skip the passage.
  - Choose the longest answer first.
correctAnswer: Orient, summarize, notice form and speaker, choose evidence, make a narrow claim, compare responsibly.
explanation: The capstone uses the full routine developed across the level.
hint: The correct sequence begins with orientation and ends with supported comparison.
```
