## Teaching Goal

Read realism through public pressure, private motive, and staged consequence.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify how a modern dramatic scene reveals social norms and inner conflict.

## Misconception Checks

A play's message is one character's speech; social pressure is just background; private motive excuses public action.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l01_q01_social_stage
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What does a social stage map help you notice in modern drama?
choices:
  - Public expectation, private motive, speech, action, and consequence.
  - Only the furniture on stage.
  - Only the playwright's biography.
  - The rhyme scheme of every line.
correctAnswer: Public expectation, private motive, speech, action, and consequence.
explanation: Modern drama often reveals pressure through ordinary speech and social roles.
hint: Think of society, character, speech, action, result.
```

  A woman smiles when guests praise her perfect home. After they leave, she hides an unpaid letter and whispers that respectability has become a locked room.
question: What pressure does the scene reveal?
choices:
  - Public respectability conflicts with private fear and constraint.
  - Guests always understand the truth.
  - Letters have no dramatic function.
  - The home is perfect in every way.
correctAnswer: Public respectability conflicts with private fear and constraint.
explanation: The smile, hidden letter, and locked-room image reveal pressure beneath public appearance.
hint: Compare the public praise with the private action.
