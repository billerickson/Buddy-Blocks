## Teaching Goal

Teach students to read narrative and parable as forms that invite interpretation.

## Student Outcome

The student can distinguish story surface, image, conflict, and deeper question in a gospel or parable-style passage.

## Misconception Checks

A parable is a flat moral slogan; religious meaning removes the need for literary reading; images are decorative.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l01_q01_parable_frame
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What is a strong first move when reading a parable?
choices:
  - Notice the story situation, surprising turn, image, and question.
  - Reduce it immediately to one slogan.
  - Ignore the story because only abstract doctrine matters.
  - Treat every image as decoration.
correctAnswer: Notice the story situation, surprising turn, image, and question.
explanation: Parables use story details and turns to press interpretation.
hint: A parable works through story before summary.
```

```question
key: u03_l01_q02_parable_turn
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the parable-style passage and answer.
passageTitle: The Open Door
passage: |
  A host prepares a feast for honored guests, but they refuse to come. The host then opens the doors to strangers who never expected an invitation.
question: Which detail is the surprising turn?
choices:
  - The host invites unexpected strangers after the honored guests refuse.
  - The host prepares a feast.
  - The guests are honored.
  - The story includes a door.
correctAnswer: The host invites unexpected strangers after the honored guests refuse.
explanation: The reversal from expected guests to unexpected strangers creates the interpretive pressure.
hint: Find the moment that changes what the listener expected.
```

