## Teaching Goal

Teach numbers 0-31 through ages, dates, quantities, and short recognition tasks.

## Student Outcome

The student can recognize, match, and produce numbers 0-31 in common Level 1 contexts.

## Misconception Checks

Confusing similar forms such as `dos/doce`, `tres/trece`, `veinte/treinta`; treating number spelling as random; counting without context.

## Teaching Note

Group numbers by useful patterns, then use them immediately in ages, classroom quantities, and dates.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_number_flash
type: flash-card
mode: easy
prompt: Choose the number.
front: doce
choices:
  - "12"
  - "2"
  - "20"
  - "30"
correctAnswer: "12"
explanation: Doce means 12.
hint: Doce is not dos.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q02_number_pairs
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each Spanish number to the numeral.
pairs:
  - left: cinco
    right: "5"
  - left: diez
    right: "10"
  - left: quince
    right: "15"
  - left: veinte
    right: "20"
explanation: Number words become easier when you retrieve them in useful groups.
hint: Say each word slowly and listen for a pattern you know.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q03_quantity_context
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the sentence.
sentenceBefore: Hay
sentenceAfter: lápices.
choices:
  - tres
  - gracias
  - lunes
  - frío
correctAnswer: tres
explanation: Tres is a number, so it fits a quantity of pencils.
hint: The sentence needs a number.
```
