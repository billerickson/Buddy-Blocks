## Teaching Goal

Teach basic clock-time statements needed for schedules and routines.

## Student Outcome

The student can interpret and produce `Es la una` and `Son las...` statements for simple times.

## Misconception Checks

Using `son las una`; omitting `las`; translating "at" and "is" word by word; confusing time with date.

## Teaching Note

Start with `Es la una` as the special case, then show `Son las dos/tres...` as the repeating pattern.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l05_q01_one_oclock
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which Spanish sentence means It is one o'clock?
choices:
  - Es la una.
  - Son las una.
  - Son las dos.
  - Hoy es una.
correctAnswer: Es la una.
explanation: One o'clock uses the special singular pattern Es la una.
hint: One o'clock is the special case.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q02_time_pairs
type: match-pairs

pairs:
  - left: 1:00
    right: Es la una.
  - left: 2:00
    right: Son las dos.
  - left: 4:00
    right: Son las cuatro.
  - left: 6:00
    right: Son las seis.
explanation: Use Es la una for 1:00 and Son las for other hours.
hint: Check whether the hour is one or more than one.
```
