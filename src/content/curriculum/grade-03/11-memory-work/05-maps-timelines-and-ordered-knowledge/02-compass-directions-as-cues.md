## Teaching Goal

Teach compass directions as ordered map cues.

## Student Outcome

The student can recall north, east, south, and west in a stable clockwise order and use direction language in simple map prompts.

## Misconception Checks

Swapping east and west; treating compass words as random labels; using classroom left/right instead of map orientation.

## Teaching Note

Draw a simple compass rose and have students point through the order while saying the words.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l02_q01_compass_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the main compass directions in clockwise order starting at north.
items:
  - south
  - north
  - west
  - east
correctOrder:
  - north
  - east
  - south
  - west
explanation: Clockwise from north goes north, east, south, west.
hint: "Imagine a compass rose: top, right, bottom, left."
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q02_north_position
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the compass clue.
sentenceBefore: On a simple compass rose, north is at the
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - top
  - right
  - bottom
  - left
correctAnswer: top
explanation: North is usually shown at the top of a compass rose.
hint: Think of the N on a compass.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q03_east_position
type: multiple-choice
prompt: If north is at the top of a simple compass rose, where is east?
choices:
  - right
  - left
  - bottom
  - top
correctAnswer: right
explanation: On a compass rose, east is to the right when north is at the top.
hint: Use the order north, east, south, west.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q04_fix_compass_order
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the compass order starting at north.
sentence: North, west, south, east.
acceptedAnswers:
  - North, east, south, west.
  - north, east, south, west
explanation: Clockwise order from north is north, east, south, west.
hint: After north, move to the right side of the compass.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q05_opposite_east
type: text-input
prompt: Type the direction opposite east.
acceptedAnswers:
  - west
answerType: text
explanation: West is opposite east on a compass.
hint: Use the pair east-west.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q06_compass_cue_explain
type: multiple-choice
prompt: How can a compass rose help you remember north, east, south, and west?
choices:
  - "It gives the directions positions: north top, east right, south bottom, west left."
  - It shows that east and west are the same direction.
  - It makes the order alphabetical.
  - It removes the need to remember south.
correctAnswer: "It gives the directions positions: north top, east right, south bottom, west left."
explanation: A visual cue can support an ordered list.
hint: Think about where the four letters sit on the compass.
```
