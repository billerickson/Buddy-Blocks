## Teaching Goal

Help students move from strong cues to lighter cues and then independent recall.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose the next cue level for a target and attempt recall with reduced support.

## Misconception Checks

Keeping the strongest cue forever; dropping all cues before the target is ready; believing a cue means the target is not learned.

## Teaching Note

Demonstrate with "Well begun is half done": full saying, "Well ___," "W b i h d," and blank recall.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l02_q01_cue_ladder_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the cue ladder from most support to least support.

  - no cue
  - first-letter cue
  - full model
  - first-word cue
correctOrder:
  - full model
  - first-word cue
  - first-letter cue
  - no cue
explanation: Cue fading slowly removes support as memory grows stronger.
hint: Start with the most help.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q02_next_cue_step
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A student can recall 'Well begun is half done' with the cue 'Well.' What is a good next step?
choices:
  - Try a lighter cue such as 'W b i h d.'
  - Keep reading the full saying forever.
  - Switch to a brand-new target immediately.
  - Stop reviewing because one cue worked.
correctAnswer: Try a lighter cue such as 'W b i h d.'
explanation: A lighter cue helps the student move toward independent recall.
hint: Choose the step that removes some support but not all of it.
```

```question

  - No cue, then full saying, then 'Measure,' then no cue

hint: Use full words, then first words or letters, then no cue.
```
