## Teaching Goal

Teach high-use measurement equivalences as exact memory targets with units.

## Student Outcome

The student can recall measures such as 12 inches = 1 foot, 3 feet = 1 yard, 60 minutes = 1 hour, and 100 cents = 1 dollar.

## Misconception Checks

Dropping units; mixing measure types; treating all equivalences as base-ten.

## Teaching Note

Show each equivalence as a two-sided card and require a recall attempt before turning the card over.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l02_q01_flash_inches
type: flash-card
mode: easy
prompt: Choose the matching measure.
front: 12 inches
choices:
  - 1 foot
  - 1 yard
  - 1 hour
  - 1 dollar
correctAnswer: 1 foot
explanation: 12 inches equals 1 foot.
hint: This is a length measure.
```

```question
  - left: 12 inches
    right: 1 foot
  - left: 3 feet
    right: 1 yard

    right: 1 hour
  - left: 100 cents
    right: 1 dollar
```


```question
key: u04_l02_q04_fix_wrong_measure
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the measure statement.
sentence: 100 cents = 1 foot.
acceptedAnswers:
  - 100 cents = 1 dollar.
  - One hundred cents equals one dollar.
explanation: Cents and dollars are money units. Feet measure length.
hint: Keep money with money.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q05_wrong_unit_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement has the wrong unit?
choices:
  - 3 feet = 1 hour
  - 12 inches = 1 foot
  - 60 minutes = 1 hour
  - 100 cents = 1 dollar
correctAnswer: 3 feet = 1 hour
explanation: Feet measure length, but an hour measures time. The correct fact is 3 feet = 1 yard.
hint: Check whether both sides name the same kind of measure.
```
