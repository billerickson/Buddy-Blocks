## Teaching Goal

Connect short grammar definitions to examples so students remember both wording and meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can recall simple definitions for noun and verb and identify examples of each.

## Misconception Checks

Reciting a definition without recognizing examples; thinking every important word is a noun; thinking verbs only show visible action.

## Teaching Note

Teach the definition, then immediately test it with examples such as teacher, playground, run, and is.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l03_q01_noun_definition
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the definition.
sentenceBefore: A noun names a person, place, thing, or
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - idea
  - jump
  - because
  - quickly
correctAnswer: idea
explanation: A noun names a person, place, thing, or idea.
hint: The definition ends with something you can think but not touch.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q02_noun_example
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word is a noun?
choices:
  - teacher
  - run
  - under
  - quickly
correctAnswer: teacher
explanation: Teacher names a person, so it is a noun.
hint: "Use the definition: person, place, thing, or idea."
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q03_verb_definition
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the definition.
sentenceBefore: A verb shows action or
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - being
  - person
  - place
  - color
correctAnswer: being
explanation: A verb shows action or being.
hint: The word 'is' is a verb of this kind.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q04_terms_examples
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each item to its grammar role.
pairs:
  - left: noun
    right: names person, place, thing, or idea
  - left: verb
    right: shows action or being
  - left: playground
    right: noun example
  - left: jump
    right: verb example
explanation: Definitions become more useful when they are linked to examples.
hint: First match the definitions, then use them on the examples.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q05_being_verb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the sentence 'The seed is small,' why is 'is' a verb?
choices:
  - It shows being.
  - It names a thing.
  - It tells where something is.
  - It is a describing word.
correctAnswer: It shows being.
explanation: Some verbs show being, not visible action.
hint: Use the second part of the verb definition.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q06_explain_playground
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why is 'playground' a noun?
choices:
  - It names a place.
  - It shows an action.
  - It describes how someone plays.
  - It joins two ideas.
correctAnswer: It names a place.
explanation: A playground names a place, so it fits the noun definition.
hint: Which part of person, place, thing, or idea fits playground?
```
