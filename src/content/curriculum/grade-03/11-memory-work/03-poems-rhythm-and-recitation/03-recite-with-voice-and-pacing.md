## Teaching Goal

Teach students to recite learned lines clearly, with pauses and attention to meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose delivery choices that support a clear recitation.

## Misconception Checks

Faster is better; expression means acting wildly; quiet mumbling counts if the words are correct.

## Teaching Note

Contrast a rushed reading with a clear reading and ask which one helps the listener understand.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l03_q01_best_delivery
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which recitation choice best helps a listener understand?
choices:
  - Speak clearly and pause where the meaning changes.
  - Rush so the words are finished quickly.
  - Whisper so no one can hear mistakes.
  - Add extra words whenever you forget.
correctAnswer: Speak clearly and pause where the meaning changes.
explanation: Recitation is meant to communicate the memorized words and meaning.
hint: Choose the option that helps the listener.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q03_speak_with_pause
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which delivery choice best shows the pause between the two couplet lines?
choices:
  - Small seeds sleep in winter ground. Spring wakes green where roots are found.
  - Small seeds sleep in winter. Ground Spring wakes green where roots are found.
  - Small seeds sleep in. Winter ground Spring wakes green where roots are found.
  - Small seeds sleep in winter ground Spring wakes green where roots are found with no pause.
correctAnswer: Small seeds sleep in winter ground. Spring wakes green where roots are found.
explanation: A pause between lines helps the listener hear the two parts of the image.
hint: Breathe after 'ground' before starting 'Spring.'
```

```question
  - Understand the meaning
  - Chunk the lines
  - Check exact words

explanation: Meaning and chunks support exact words; delivery practice comes after the target is becoming stable.

```
