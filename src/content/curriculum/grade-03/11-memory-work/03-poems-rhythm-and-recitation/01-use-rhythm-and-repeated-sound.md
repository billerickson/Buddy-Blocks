## Teaching Goal

Teach students to notice rhythm, rhyme, and repeated sound as memory supports.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a sound pattern or repeated word that can help recall a poem line.

## Misconception Checks

Sound patterns are decoration only; a poem should be memorized word by word with no attention to sound; rhyme means every line must rhyme.

## Teaching Note

Use short original practice lines such as "Bright birds build by the bay" to notice repeated beginning sounds.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l01_q01_repeated_sound
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the line 'Bright birds build by the bay,' which sound repeats at the start of many words?
choices:
  - b
  - s
  - t
  - m
correctAnswer: b
explanation: Bright, birds, build, by, and bay all begin with the /b/ sound.
hint: Listen to the first sound in each word.
```
