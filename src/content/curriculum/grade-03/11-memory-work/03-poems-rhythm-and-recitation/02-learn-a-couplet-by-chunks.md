## Teaching Goal

Help students memorize a two-line poem target by phrase chunks and first-word cues.

## Student Outcome

The student can recall the couplet "Small seeds sleep in winter ground; / Spring wakes green where roots are found" with support and then with less support.

## Misconception Checks

Practicing only from the beginning; ignoring line breaks; using the first-word cue as a permanent crutch.

## Teaching Note

First read the couplet aloud for meaning, then mark chunks: "Small seeds sleep / in winter ground" and "Spring wakes green / where roots are found."

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l02_q01_couplet_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the couplet lines in order.
items:
  - Spring wakes green where roots are found.
  - Small seeds sleep in winter ground;
correctOrder:
  - Small seeds sleep in winter ground;
  - Spring wakes green where roots are found.
explanation: The seeds sleep first; then spring wakes green growth.
hint: Think about winter before spring.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q02_couplet_cloze
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the couplet chunks.
parts:
  - "Small seeds sleep in "
  - "; Spring wakes green where "
  - " are found."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: winter ground
    acceptedAnswers:
      - winter ground
  - correctAnswer: roots
    acceptedAnswers:
      - roots
explanation: The chunks are 'winter ground' and 'roots.'
hint: Think of where seeds are before spring.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q03_missing_phrase
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the first line.
sentenceBefore: Small seeds sleep in
sentenceAfter: ;
choices:
  - winter ground
  - summer sky
  - open books
  - silver rain
correctAnswer: winter ground
explanation: The first line is 'Small seeds sleep in winter ground.'
hint: The next line begins with spring, so the first line is before spring.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q04_first_word_cues
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which first-word cues fit the two lines of the couplet?

  - Small / Spring
  - Seeds / Roots
  - Winter / Green
  - Found / Ground
correctAnswer: Small / Spring
explanation: The lines begin 'Small seeds...' and 'Spring wakes...'
hint: A first-word cue uses the first word of each line.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q05_speak_couplet
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice gives the small-seeds couplet in exact order?
choices:
  - Small seeds sleep in winter ground; Spring wakes green where roots are found.
  - Spring wakes green where roots are found; Small seeds sleep in winter ground.
  - Small roots sleep in winter green; Spring wakes seeds where ground is found.
  - Small seeds wake in winter ground; Spring sleeps green where roots are found.
correctAnswer: Small seeds sleep in winter ground; Spring wakes green where roots are found.
explanation: Speaking the couplet helps link sound, meaning, and exact words.
hint: "Use the first-word cues: Small, Spring."
```
