## Teaching Goal

Teach students to make a short memory line easier by breaking it into sensible parts.

## Student Outcome

The student can divide a short line into useful parts and practice one part at a time.

## Misconception Checks

Splitting a line at random; making each part too long; thinking the parts change the words.

## Teaching Note

Use "Measure twice, cut once." Clap or tap once for each part: "Measure twice" and "cut once." Then put the parts back together.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l02_q01_best_chunks
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which part plan best fits this saying: 'Measure twice, cut once'?"
choices:
  - Measure twice / cut once
  - Measure / twice cut / once
  - Measure twice cut once all at once
  - Measure twice / cut once / do it again
correctAnswer: Measure twice / cut once
explanation: "The saying has two clear parts: check first, then act."
hint: Listen for the pause after "twice."
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q02_order_chunks
type: order-items
prompt: Put the two parts of the saying in order.
items:
  - cut once
  - Measure twice
correctOrder:
  - Measure twice
  - cut once
explanation: The full saying is "Measure twice, cut once."
hint: Careful checking comes before cutting.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q03_missing_action
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the saying.
sentenceBefore: Measure twice,
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - cut once
  - cut twice
  - run fast
  - look north
correctAnswer: cut once
explanation: The second part is "cut once."
hint: Say the first part, then add the action part.
```

```question
    right: first half of the direction line
  - left: south, west
    right: second half of the direction line

```

```question
key: u01_l02_q05_chunk_saying
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why should you learn a line in small parts?
choices:
  - Small parts are easier to practice and put back together.
  - Small parts let you change the words.
  - Small parts mean you never need to check.
  - Small parts should be said as fast as possible.
correctAnswer: Small parts are easier to practice and put back together.
explanation: You can practice a hard part, then say the whole line again.
hint: Think about making the line easier, not different.
```
