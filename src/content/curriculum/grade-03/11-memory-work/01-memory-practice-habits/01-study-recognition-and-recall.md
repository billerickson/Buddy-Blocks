## Teaching Goal

Help students tell the difference between looking again, recognizing an answer, and pulling an answer from memory.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify whether a task asks for study, recognition, cued recall, or independent recall.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking recognition is the same as recall; thinking rereading proves memory; thinking a cue is cheating.

## Teaching Note

Show the phrase "North, east, south, west." Let students look at it, choose it from options, recall it with "N, E, S, W," and then recall it without looking. Name each step.
