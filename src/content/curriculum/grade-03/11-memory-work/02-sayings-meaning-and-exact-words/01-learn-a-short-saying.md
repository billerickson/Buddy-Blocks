## Teaching Goal

Help students memorize short sayings with exact wording.

## Student Outcome

The student can recall and complete short sayings such as "Well begun is half done" and "Measure twice, cut once."

## Misconception Checks

Replacing words with near synonyms; remembering the meaning but not the wording; treating commas and pauses as unimportant for oral memory.

## Teaching Note

Start with "Well begun / is half done." Say it aloud in two chunks before asking for recall.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_well_begun_blank
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the saying.
sentenceBefore: Well begun
sentenceAfter: half done.
choices:
  - is
  - was
  - means
  - gets
correctAnswer: is
explanation: The exact saying is 'Well begun is half done.'
hint: Say the whole sentence slowly and listen for the linking word.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q02_measure_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the saying in order.
items:
  - cut once
  - Measure twice
correctOrder:
  - Measure twice
  - cut once
explanation: The saying is 'Measure twice, cut once.'
hint: The careful checking comes before the action.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q03_exact_stitch
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice gives the exact saying?
choices:
  - A stitch in time saves nine.
  - A stitch in time saves ten.
  - A thread in time saves nine.
  - A stitch today saves time.
correctAnswer: A stitch in time saves nine.
explanation: Exact wording matters when the target is a saying.
hint: Look for the choice with both 'stitch' and 'nine.'
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q04_cut_once_input
type: text-input
prompt: "Type the missing words: Measure twice, ___."
acceptedAnswers:
  - cut once
answerType: text
explanation: The second chunk is 'cut once.'
hint: The first chunk is checking; the second chunk is doing.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q05_correct_near_saying
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the saying so it uses the exact words.
sentence: Well started is half finished.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Well begun is half done.
explanation: The near words have a similar idea, but the memorized saying uses 'begun' and 'done.'
hint: "Use the exact pair: begun and done."
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q06_speak_saying
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice gives one learned saying with exact words?
choices:
  - Well begun is half done.
  - Well started is almost finished.
  - Measure once, cut twice.
  - A stitch saves time in nine.
correctAnswer: Well begun is half done.
explanation: Short sayings are good oral memory targets because they have clear chunks.
hint: Say the first chunk silently, then speak the full saying.
```
