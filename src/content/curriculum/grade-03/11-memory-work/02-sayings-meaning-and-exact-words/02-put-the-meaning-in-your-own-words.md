## Teaching Goal

Show that meaning supports memory and that paraphrase is different from exact recall.

## Student Outcome

The student can paraphrase a short saying without losing its main idea.

## Misconception Checks

Copying the saying as a paraphrase; changing the meaning while changing the words; thinking meaning is unnecessary for memorization.

## Teaching Note

Compare exact recall of "A stitch in time saves nine" with the paraphrase "Fix a small problem early before it grows."

## Questions


```question
key: u02_l02_q02_best_paraphrase_measure
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which is the best paraphrase of 'Measure twice, cut once'?
choices:
  - Check carefully before you do something hard to undo.
  - Cutting is always better than measuring.
  - Do every job two times.
  - Never use tools.
correctAnswer: Check carefully before you do something hard to undo.
explanation: The saying teaches careful preparation before action.
hint: A paraphrase should keep the idea, not the exact words.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q03_paraphrase_stitch

prompt: Which paraphrase best fits 'A stitch in time saves nine'?
choices:
  - Fix a small problem early so it does not become a bigger problem.
  - Sewing is always faster when nine people help.
  - Counting to nine helps you remember time.
  - Do the final step first so you can skip checking.
correctAnswer: Fix a small problem early so it does not become a bigger problem.
explanation: The saying means that early correction can prevent bigger trouble later.
hint: Think of a tiny tear in cloth before it becomes a big tear.
```
