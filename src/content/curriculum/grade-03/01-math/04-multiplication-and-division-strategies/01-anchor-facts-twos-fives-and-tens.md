## Teaching Goal

Establish reliable multiplication and division anchor facts for 2s, 5s, and 10s.

## Student Outcome

The student can solve and explain facts involving 2, 5, and 10 using patterns, skip counting, or arrays.

## Key Ideas

Anchor facts are easy-to-see structures that support harder facts later.

## Misconception Checks

Reciting patterns without connecting them to groups; confusing 5s and 10s; treating division facts as unrelated.

## Teaching Note

Show arrays and skip-count sequences for 2s, 5s, and 10s side by side, then name why they are useful anchors.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l01_q01_twos_fact
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which fact helps solve 2 x 9?
choices:
  - Double 9
  - Add 2 + 9 only
  - Count by 10s
  - Subtract 2 from 9
correctAnswer: Double 9
explanation: Multiplying by 2 means doubling the other factor, so 2 x 9 = 18.
hint: A 2s fact makes two equal groups.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q04_related_division_anchor
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which fact helps solve 50 / 10?
choices:
  - 10 x 5 = 50
  - 10 + 5 = 15
  - 50 x 10 = 500
  - 50 - 10 = 40
correctAnswer: 10 x 5 = 50
explanation: 50 divided by 10 asks, 10 times what equals 50?
hint: Use a related multiplication fact.
```

```question

  - "8"

```

