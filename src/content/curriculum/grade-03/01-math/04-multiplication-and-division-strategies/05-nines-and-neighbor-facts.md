## Teaching Goal

Derive nines and other harder facts from tens or nearby known facts.

## Student Outcome

The student can use 10 times a number minus one group, or another neighbor fact, to find a product.

## Key Ideas

A difficult fact can be close to an easier fact, and the difference can be adjusted.

## Misconception Checks

Subtracting 1 instead of one group; using a neighbor fact without adjusting; treating patterns as magic rules.

## Teaching Note

Show 10 rows of 6 with one row crossed out to make 9 rows of 6.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l05_q01_nine_from_ten
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which strategy helps solve 9 x 7?
choices:
  - 10 x 7 minus 7
  - 10 x 7 plus 9
  - 9 + 7
  - 7 x 7 minus 1
correctAnswer: 10 x 7 minus 7
explanation: 9 groups of 7 is one group of 7 less than 10 groups of 7.
hint: Start with 10 groups, then remove one group.
```

```question
type: match-pairs

  - left: 9 x 8
    right: 10 x 8 - 8
  - left: 6 x 7
    right: 5 x 7 + 7
  - left: 8 x 4
    right: 10 x 4 - 2 x 4
  - left: 7 x 6
    right: 6 x 6 + 6
explanation: Neighbor facts use a close known fact and adjust by whole groups.
hint: Check what group is added or removed.
```
