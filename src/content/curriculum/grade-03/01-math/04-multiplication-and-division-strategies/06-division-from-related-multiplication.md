## Teaching Goal

Use multiplication facts and unknown-factor reasoning to solve division.

## Student Outcome

The student can solve division facts by naming the related multiplication fact.

## Key Ideas

A division fact asks for a missing factor in a related multiplication equation.

## Misconception Checks

Recalling division as isolated facts; swapping dividend and divisor; forgetting what the quotient represents.

## Teaching Note

Place a division fact under its multiplication fact and circle the missing factor.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l06_q01_related_fact_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which fact helps solve 72 / 8?
choices:
  - 8 x 9 = 72
  - 8 + 9 = 17
  - 72 x 8 = 576
  - 9 - 8 = 1
correctAnswer: 8 x 9 = 72
explanation: 72 divided by 8 asks, 8 times what equals 72?
hint: Look for a multiplication fact with product 72.
```

```question
key: u04_l06_q02_fill_related_division
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the related multiplication fact for 63 / 7.
sentenceBefore: 7 x
sentenceAfter: = 63
choices:
  - "6"
  - "7"
  - "8"
  - "9"
correctAnswer: "9"
explanation: 7 x 9 = 63, so 63 / 7 = 9.
hint: Count by 7s to 63.
```

```question
key: u04_l06_q03_match_division_products
```
