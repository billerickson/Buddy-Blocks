## Teaching Goal

Extend rounding to the nearest hundred and connect rounding to reasonableness.

## Student Outcome

The student can round to the nearest hundred and use the rounded number to judge approximate size.

## Key Ideas

Hundreds benchmarks help estimate and check whether an answer is in the right range.

## Misconception Checks

Rounding 349 to 400 because 9 is large; ignoring the midpoint between hundreds; treating rounding as exact computation.

## Teaching Note

Use 300, 350, and 400 on a number line to discuss which side of halfway a number lies on.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l02_q01_round_hundred
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Round 362 to the nearest hundred.
choices:
  - "300"
  - "360"
  - "400"
  - "500"
correctAnswer: "400"
explanation: 362 is past the halfway point 350, so it is closer to 400.
hint: Compare 362 to 350.
```
