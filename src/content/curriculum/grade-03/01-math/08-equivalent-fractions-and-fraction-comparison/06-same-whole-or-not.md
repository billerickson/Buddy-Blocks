## Teaching Goal

Ensure students check whether fraction comparisons use the same whole.

## Student Outcome

The student can decide if a fraction comparison is valid and explain the same-whole condition.

## Key Ideas

Fractions can be compared only when they refer to the same-size whole or a clearly equivalent whole.

## Misconception Checks

Any two shaded diagrams can be compared; same symbols always mean same size; larger drawing always represents a larger fraction.

## Teaching Note

Compare 1/2 of a small cake and 1/3 of a large cake, asking what must be known before judging amount.

## Questions

```question

prompt: Which comparison is fair?

  - 2/3 and 1/3 of the same rectangle
  - 1/2 of a tiny circle and 1/2 of a huge circle
  - 3/4 of two different-size bars
  - one shaded part from unequal pieces
correctAnswer: 2/3 and 1/3 of the same rectangle
explanation: A fair comparison uses the same whole.
hint: Look for the same object or same-size whole.
```
