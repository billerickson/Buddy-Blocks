## Teaching Goal

Compare fractions with the same numerator by reasoning about unit fraction size.

## Student Outcome

The student can decide which same-numerator fraction is greater and explain the unit-size reasoning.

## Key Ideas

If the numerator is the same, the fraction with fewer equal parts in the whole has larger parts.

## Misconception Checks

Larger denominator means larger fraction; same numerator means equal amount; ignoring part size.

## Teaching Note

Show 1/4 and 1/8 first, then compare three of each on same-size wholes.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l04_q01_compare_same_num
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which fraction is greater, 3/4 or 3/8?
choices:
  - 3/4
  - 3/8
  - They are equal
  - Cannot compare
correctAnswer: 3/4
explanation: Fourths are larger parts than eighths, so 3 fourths is greater than 3 eighths.
hint: Same numerator means compare the size of each part.
```
