## Teaching Goal

Introduce division as sharing a total into a known number of equal groups.

## Student Outcome

The student can model a sharing division problem and identify how many are in each group.

## Key Ideas

When the number of groups is known, division can find the size of each equal group.

## Misconception Checks

Making unequal shares; treating the number of groups as the quotient; ignoring all objects must be used in basic cases.

## Teaching Note

Show 12 counters shared into 3 plates and ask how many are on each plate.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l01_q01_sharing_model
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 12 counters are shared equally onto 3 plates. How many counters go on each plate?
choices:
  - "3"
  - "4"
  - "9"
  - "15"
correctAnswer: "4"
explanation: 12 shared into 3 equal groups gives 4 in each group.
hint: Put one counter on each plate until all 12 are used.
```

```question
key: u03_l01_q02_equal_share_equation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which equation matches 20 apples shared equally among 5 baskets?
choices:
  - 20 / 5 = 4
  - 20 x 5 = 100
  - 20 - 5 = 15
  - 5 / 20 = 4
correctAnswer: 20 / 5 = 4
explanation: The total 20 is divided into 5 equal groups.
hint: Start with the total amount, then divide by the number of groups.
```
