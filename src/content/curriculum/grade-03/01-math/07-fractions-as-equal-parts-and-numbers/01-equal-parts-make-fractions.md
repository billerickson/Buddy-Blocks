## Teaching Goal

Establish that fractions require equal-size parts of the same whole.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify equal and unequal partitions and explain whether a diagram can represent a fraction.

## Key Ideas

Fraction parts must be equal in size, even if the shapes of the parts can look different in later examples.

## Misconception Checks

Any cut shape makes fractions; equal number of pieces means equal-size parts; unequal shares can still use fraction names.

## Teaching Note

Compare two rectangles cut into four parts, one equal and one unequal, before naming fourths.
