## Teaching Goal

Distinguish measurement attributes and choose appropriate units.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify whether a situation measures length, mass, liquid volume, or time and select a reasonable unit.

## Key Ideas

Measurement depends on the attribute being measured and the unit used.

## Misconception Checks

Units are interchangeable; bigger objects always need bigger unit names; measurement labels can be added after calculating.

## Teaching Note

Show an object and ask whether students would measure how long, how heavy, how much it holds, or how much time.

## Questions

```question
key: u09_l01_q01_attribute_length
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which attribute would you measure to find how long a pencil is?
choices:
  - length
  - mass
  - liquid volume
  - elapsed time
correctAnswer: length
explanation: How long something is describes length.
hint: Think about what a ruler measures.
```

```question
key: u09_l01_q02_best_unit
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which unit is reasonable for the mass of a backpack?
choices:
  - kilograms
  - liters
  - minutes
  - inches
correctAnswer: kilograms
explanation: Mass can be measured in grams or kilograms.
hint: Mass tells how heavy something is.
```
