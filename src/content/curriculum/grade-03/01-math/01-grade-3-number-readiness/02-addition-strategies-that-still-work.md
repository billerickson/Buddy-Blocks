## Teaching Goal

Refresh flexible addition strategies for sums within 100 and supported sums within 1,000.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose a place-value, make-ten, compensation, or number-line strategy to add accurately.

## Key Ideas

Addition can be made easier by decomposing addends and preserving the total.

## Misconceptions

Counting all from 1 for every sum; adding digits without place value; changing one addend without compensating.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_choose_strategy
type: multiple-choice

  - "Add 40 + 20, then 8 + 5"
  - "Subtract 25 from 48"
  - "Ignore the tens"

questionGoal: "Choose a place-value addition strategy."
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q05_compensation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which expression has the same value as 39 + 26?"
choices:
  - "40 + 25"
  - "40 + 26"
  - "39 + 25"
  - "30 + 20"
correctAnswer: "40 + 25"
explanation: "If 39 increases by 1, 26 must decrease by 1 to keep the same total."
hint: "Compensation changes both addends but keeps the total the same."
questionGoal: "Recognize compensation in addition."
misconception: "Changing one addend without balancing the other."
```
