## Teaching Goal

Build translation among multiplication contexts, visual models, and symbolic equations.

## Student Outcome

The student can match a story, array, repeated addition expression, and multiplication equation that represent the same situation.

## Key Ideas

The same multiplication structure can appear as words, pictures, repeated addition, or an equation.

## Misconceptions

Matching by numbers alone rather than relationships; ignoring group size in the story; choosing an array with the right total but wrong structure.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l06_q01_story_equation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "There are 6 tables with 4 seats at each table. Which equation matches?"
choices:
  - "6 x 4 = 24"
  - "6 + 4 = 10"
  - "24 x 4 = 96"
  - "6 - 4 = 2"
correctAnswer: "6 x 4 = 24"
explanation: "There are 6 equal groups of 4 seats."
hint: "Find the number of groups and the size of each group."
questionGoal: "Match an equal-groups story to a multiplication equation."
misconception: "Adding the two numbers from the story."
```
