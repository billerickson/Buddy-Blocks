## Teaching Goal

Introduce multiplication equations as compact notation for repeated equal addends.

## Student Outcome

The student can translate between repeated addition and multiplication equations for equal groups.

## Key Ideas

4 + 4 + 4 can be written as 3 x 4 because there are 3 equal addends of 4.

## Misconceptions

Writing multiplication for unequal addends; using the total as a factor; thinking multiplication and repeated addition are unrelated.

## Questions

key: u02_l04_q01_addition_to_multiplication

prompt: "Which multiplication equation matches 9 + 9 + 9 + 9?"

  - "4 x 9 = 36"
  - "9 x 9 = 81"
  - "4 + 9 = 13"
  - "9 x 3 = 27"
correctAnswer: "4 x 9 = 36"
explanation: "There are 4 equal addends of 9."
hint: "Count how many 9s are being added."
questionGoal: "Translate repeated addition into multiplication."
misconception: "Using the addend as both factors or counting addends incorrectly."
