## Teaching Goal

Reason from side lengths to rectangular area without every square drawn.

## Student Outcome

The student can find area from whole-number side lengths by imagining or using the implied array.

## Key Ideas

Side lengths tell how many rows and columns of unit squares fit in a rectangle.

## Misconception Checks

Adding side lengths for area; treating side length and area as the same measure; ignoring units.

## Teaching Note

Show a 5 by 7 rectangle first fully tiled, then only with side labels, and connect the two.

## Questions

```question
key: u10_l04_q01_side_lengths_area
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A rectangle is 6 units long and 4 units wide. What is its area?
choices:
  - 10 square units
  - 20 square units
  - 24 square units
  - 64 square units
correctAnswer: 24 square units
explanation: The side lengths mean 6 by 4 unit squares, so 6 x 4 = 24.
hint: Imagine the rectangle filled with rows and columns.
```
