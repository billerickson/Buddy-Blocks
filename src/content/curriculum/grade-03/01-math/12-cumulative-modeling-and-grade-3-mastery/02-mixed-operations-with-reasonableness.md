## Teaching Goal

Demonstrate retained arithmetic skill and reasonableness checking across all four operations.

## Student Outcome

The student can solve mixed operation problems and judge whether answers fit the situation.

## Key Ideas

Operation meaning, estimation, units, and inverse relationships work together in mixed problem solving.

## Misconception Checks

Choosing operations by key words; trusting every computed answer; forgetting units or intermediate quantities.

## Teaching Note

Use a quick routine: choose operation, estimate, solve, check.

```question
key: u12_l02_q03_reasonable_answer
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which answer is reasonable for 602 - 298?
choices:
  - "304"
  - "900"
  - "104"
  - "2980"
correctAnswer: "304"
explanation: 602 is about 600 and 298 is about 300, so the difference is about 300.
hint: Estimate before choosing.
```

```question
key: u12_l02_q05_two_step_reasonable
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the story and answer.
passageTitle: Supply Boxes
passage: There are 6 boxes with 8 glue sticks each. The class uses 9 glue sticks.
question: How many glue sticks are left?
choices:
  - "39"
  - "48"
  - "57"
  - "69"
correctAnswer: "39"
explanation: 6 x 8 = 48, and 48 - 9 = 39.
hint: Find the total first.
```

```question
key: u12_l02_q06_explain_reasonableness
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why is 7 x 6 = 76 not reasonable?
choices:
  - 7 x 6 should be less than 7 x 10 = 70, so 76 is too large.
  - 76 is reasonable because it starts with 7 and ends with 6.
  - 7 x 6 should be more than 100.
  - Products are never checked for reasonableness.
correctAnswer: 7 x 6 should be less than 7 x 10 = 70, so 76 is too large.
explanation: Estimation and known facts can catch unreasonable products.
hint: Compare with 7 x 10.
```
