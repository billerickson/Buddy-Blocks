## Teaching Goal

Introduce the visible and audible shape of a poem through lines, stanzas, and sound.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify lines and stanzas and notice one sound feature in a short poem.

## Misconception Checks

A poem is just a paragraph broken oddly; every line is a complete sentence; sound does not affect meaning.

## Teaching Note

Show a four-line poem beside the same words in a paragraph and ask what changes when it is arranged as lines.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l01_q01_line_identify
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the short poem and answer.
passageTitle: Morning Bell
passage: |
  The morning bell is ringing,
  Its silver voice is clear.
  The sleepy streets are waking,
  And daylight gathers near.
question: How many lines are in the poem?
choices:
  - "4"
  - "2"
  - "1"
  - "8"
correctAnswer: "4"
explanation: Each row of words is one line, so this poem has four lines.
hint: Count each row of poem words.
```

  - A group of lines in a poem
  - The problem in a fable
  - A character's flaw
  - A quest reward

explanation: A stanza is a group of poem lines, like a paragraph is a group of prose sentences.
