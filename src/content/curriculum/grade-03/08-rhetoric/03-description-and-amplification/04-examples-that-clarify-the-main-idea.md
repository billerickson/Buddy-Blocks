## Teaching Goal

Teach students to add examples that explain or support a main idea.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose or write an example that makes a main idea clearer.

## Misconception Checks

Examples are extra decorations; an example can be unrelated if it is interesting; a general restatement is the same as an example.

## Teaching Note

Show "Our class helped the community" and compare vague support with a specific example.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l04_q01_best_example
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Main idea: Our class used teamwork during cleanup. Which example best clarifies it?"
choices:
  - Two students stacked chairs while two others wiped tables.
  - The chairs were brown.
  - Cleanup happened after math.
  - Cleanup happened on a Tuesday.
correctAnswer: Two students stacked chairs while two others wiped tables.
explanation: The example shows classmates working together.
hint: Choose the example that shows teamwork in action.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q02_main_idea_to_example
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each main idea to the best example.
pairs:
  - left: The directions were clear.
    right: They showed each step with a picture.
  - left: The lunch line moved quickly.
    right: Everyone had trays in less than five minutes.
  - left: The group shared fairly.
    right: Each person used the markers for the same amount of time.
  - left: The room was quiet.
    right: Students could hear the clock tick.
explanation: A good example shows the main idea in a specific way.
hint: Match the idea to the example that proves or explains it.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q03_example_in_paragraph
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the paragraph and answer.
passageTitle: Helpful Directions
passage: |
  The directions were easy to follow.
  They numbered each step.
  They also showed a picture of each piece.
question: Which sentence is an example that clarifies the main idea?
choices:
  - They numbered each step.
  - The directions were easy to follow.
  - The paper had a neat heading.
  - The folder was on the desk.
correctAnswer: They numbered each step.
explanation: Numbering each step is a specific example of why the directions were easy to follow.
hint: Look for a specific detail that shows the main idea.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q04_complete_example_frame

  - "Main idea: The group was prepared. For example, they "

      - brought their notes

  - correctAnswer: checked the directions
```
