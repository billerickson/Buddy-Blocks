## Teaching Goal

Help students choose specific verbs and sensory details that fit the meaning and purpose.

## Student Outcome

The student can replace vague words with more precise words and add a fitting sensory clue.

## Misconception Checks

The fanciest word is the best word; any sensory word improves a sentence; precise words can ignore tone or audience.

## Teaching Note

Compare "went" with "tiptoed," "hurried," and "stomped," and ask what each verb shows.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l03_q01_precise_verb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which verb best replaces 'went' in this sentence? 'Nina went quietly across the stage.'
choices:
  - tiptoed
  - hurried
  - stomped
  - stood
correctAnswer: tiptoed
explanation: "'Tiptoed' shows quiet movement and fits the sentence."
hint: Choose the verb that matches 'quietly across the stage.'
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q02_sensory_clue
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the sensory detail that best fits.
sentenceBefore: The fresh bread smelled
sentenceAfter: as it cooled on the counter.
choices:
  - warm and sweet
  - round and brown
  - loud and quick
  - hard and cold
correctAnswer: warm and sweet
explanation: "'Warm and sweet' gives a smell clue that fits fresh bread."
hint: Pick words that connect to smell.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q03_replace_vague_word
type: error-correction
prompt: "Revise the sentence by replacing 'good' with a more precise word: 'The good directions helped us build the model.'"
sentence: The good directions helped us build the model.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The clear directions helped us build the model.
  - The helpful directions helped us build the model.
  - The step-by-step directions helped us build the model.
explanation: A precise word such as clear or step-by-step tells why the directions were good.
hint: Choose a word that explains what kind of good the directions were.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q04_purpose_fit_word
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the purpose and answer.
passageTitle: Lost Notebook Notice
passage: |
  Purpose: Help classmates identify a lost notebook.
  Draft: I lost a notebook.
question: Which added detail best fits the purpose?
choices:
  - It has a red cover and my name inside.
  - Notebooks are useful for many subjects.
  - I like writing with sharp pencils.
  - The school has many classrooms.
correctAnswer: It has a red cover and my name inside.
explanation: That detail helps classmates identify the notebook.
hint: Choose the detail that helps the audience recognize the item.
```
