## Teaching Goal

Teach students to decide whether support actually fits the claim and audience.

## Student Outcome

The student can classify support as relevant or irrelevant and explain the mismatch in simple language.

## Misconception Checks

True details are always relevant; funny or personal details are strong support; all reasons are equally useful.

## Teaching Note

Use a simple claim and show one reason that fits and one that changes the topic.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l03_q01_relevant_support
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Claim: The hallway sign should be bigger. Which support is relevant?"
choices:
  - Some students cannot read it from the end of the hall.
  - The sign has black letters.
  - The hallway floor is shiny.
  - The sign is near the office.
correctAnswer: Some students cannot read it from the end of the hall.
explanation: This support explains why a bigger sign would help.
hint: Relevant support connects directly to the claim.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q02_support_jobs
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each support statement to its job.
pairs:
  - left: Students cannot hear the speaker in the back row.
    right: supports a claim about speaking louder
  - left: The directions skip step three.
    right: supports a claim about confusing directions
  - left: The poster colors are bright.
    right: supports a claim about easy-to-see design
  - left: The table has four legs.
    right: off topic for a claim about fair turns
explanation: Relevant support fits the claim being made. Off-topic details do not help.
hint: Ask which claim each support could actually prove.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q03_irrelevant_in_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the claim and support.
passageTitle: Quiet Corner
passage: |
  Claim: The reading corner should have another lamp.
  Support 1: It is hard to see the words in the afternoon.
  Support 2: The rug has stripes.
question: Which support is irrelevant?
choices:
  - The rug has stripes.
  - It is hard to see the words in the afternoon.
  - The reading corner should have another lamp.
  - Support 1
correctAnswer: The rug has stripes.
explanation: The rug's stripes do not explain why another lamp is needed.
hint: Look for the support that does not connect to light or reading.
```
