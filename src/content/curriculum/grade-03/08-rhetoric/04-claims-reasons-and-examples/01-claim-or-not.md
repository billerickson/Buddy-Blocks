## Teaching Goal

Teach students to recognize a claim or opinion as a statement that can be supported.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify which statements are claims and which are facts, questions, or details.

## Misconception Checks

Every sentence is a claim; questions are claims; facts and claims are the same.

## Teaching Note

Compare "The library opens at nine" with "The library is the best quiet place to read."

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l01_q01_find_claim
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is a claim?
choices:
  - Recess should be a little longer because students need movement.
  - The clock is above the door.
  - What time is recess?
  - The playground has a slide.
correctAnswer: Recess should be a little longer because students need movement.
explanation: This sentence gives an opinion that can be supported with reasons.
hint: A claim says what someone thinks or wants the audience to accept.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q02_statement_types
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each type to the statement.
pairs:
  - left: claim
    right: Our class should plant more flowers.
  - left: fact
    right: The garden has six planters.
  - left: question
    right: Who watered the garden?
  - left: detail
    right: The watering can is beside the fence.
explanation: Claims can be supported. Facts, questions, and details have different jobs.
hint: Look for the statement that someone could argue for with reasons.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q03_claim_in_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the response and answer.
passageTitle: Class Job Response
passage: |
  I think the line leader job should rotate each day.
  That way, more students get a turn.
question: Which part is the claim?
choices:
  - The line leader job should rotate each day.
  - More students get a turn.
  - That way
  - each day
correctAnswer: The line leader job should rotate each day.
explanation: The claim says what the writer thinks should happen.
hint: Find the opinion the rest of the response supports.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q04_claim_definition
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the sentence.
sentenceBefore: A claim says what someone
sentenceAfter: and can be supported with reasons.
choices:
  - thinks
  - whispers
  - counts
  - erases
correctAnswer: thinks
explanation: A claim states an opinion, position, or idea that can be supported.
hint: A claim often starts with 'I think' or names what should happen.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q05_write_claim
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is a supportable claim about a classroom job?
choices:
  - The supply helper is a helpful job for our class.
  - The supply helper carries a bin.
  - Who is the supply helper today?
  - The bin is beside the shelf.
correctAnswer: The supply helper is a helpful job for our class.
explanation: This sentence states an opinion or position that could be supported with reasons.
hint: A claim says what someone thinks or wants the audience to accept.
```

```question
key: u04_l01_q06_not_claim
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is NOT a claim?
choices:
  - How many books are on the shelf?
  - The reading corner is the best quiet place.
  - Students should save extra paper for later.
  - Our class should choose the science project.
correctAnswer: How many books are on the shelf?
explanation: A question asks for information. It does not state a position to support.
hint: Look for the sentence that asks instead of states.
```
