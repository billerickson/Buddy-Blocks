## Teaching Goal

Distinguish a reason from a claim and connect it with because.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose a reason that tells why a claim may be true or worth accepting.

## Misconception Checks

Repeating the claim is a reason; any sentence after because is support; emotional volume makes a reason stronger.

## Teaching Note

Ask "Why?" after a claim and test whether the answer actually gives support.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l02_q01_best_reason
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Claim: The class should use the larger table for the project. Which reason supports the claim?"
choices:
  - The larger table has room for every poster piece.
  - The table is near a blue wall.
  - The table is next to the poster shelf.
  - The table has smooth corners.
correctAnswer: The larger table has room for every poster piece.
explanation: The reason explains why the larger table fits the project.
hint: Ask which answer tells why the claim makes sense.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q02_because_frame
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the best reason to complete the sentence.
sentenceBefore: The class should practice the song again because
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - some students are still unsure of the words
  - the song has a title
  - the music room has a door
  - the music room has a piano
correctAnswer: some students are still unsure of the words
explanation: This reason explains why more practice would help.
hint: The words after because should answer why.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q03_claim_to_reason
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each claim to a supporting reason.
pairs:
  - left: We should label the supply bins.
    right: Students could find materials faster.
  - left: The story should include the ending.
    right: Readers need to know how the problem turns out.
  - left: The group should lower its voices.
    right: Other students are trying to read nearby.
  - left: The directions need pictures.
    right: Pictures can show what each step looks like.
explanation: A reason should explain why the claim is useful or true.
hint: Match each claim with the reason that answers why.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q04_repeated_claim
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Claim: The class should have a cleanup plan. Which choice only repeats the claim instead of giving a reason?"
choices:
  - The class should have a cleanup plan because we should have one.
  - A plan would help everyone know their job.
  - A plan could make cleanup faster.
  - A plan would keep supplies from getting lost.
correctAnswer: The class should have a cleanup plan because we should have one.
explanation: Repeating the claim does not tell why the plan would help.
hint: A reason should add support, not say the same idea again.
```
