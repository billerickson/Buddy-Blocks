## Teaching Goal

Help students identify or infer the point, lesson, or moral of a short fable.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose a lesson that fits the events and outcome of a fable.

## Misconception Checks

The lesson is always directly stated; any nice-sounding sentence can be the lesson; the lesson is the same as the setting or character name.

## Teaching Note

Ask, "What does this story want the listener to learn?" after retelling the key events.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l04_q01_lantern_lesson
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the fable and answer.
passageTitle: The Lantern
passage: |
  A student carried a lantern for the class walk.
  He rushed ahead to show that he was fastest.
  The group could not see the path, so he came back and walked with them.
question: Which lesson best fits the fable?
choices:
  - A tool is most useful when it helps others.
  - Fast walking is always the most important skill.
  - Lanterns should never be carried.
  - Paths are always easy to see.
correctAnswer: A tool is most useful when it helps others.
explanation: The student learns that carrying the lantern matters because the group needs light.
hint: Look at what the student changes after seeing the problem.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q02_lesson_not_setting
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice sounds like a story lesson, not just a setting detail?
choices:
  - Working together can be wiser than showing off.
  - The path was near the school.
  - The lantern had a handle.
  - The walk happened after lunch.
correctAnswer: Working together can be wiser than showing off.
explanation: A lesson tells what the story shows about actions or choices.
hint: A lesson can often help someone make a better choice later.
```

```question
    right: Honesty builds trust.
```


```question
key: u02_l04_q05_explain_lesson_evidence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A story says that Kai waited his turn and everyone got to play. Which lesson and clue fit best?
choices:
  - "Lesson: Patience can be fair. Clue: Kai waited, and everyone got a turn."
  - "Lesson: Winning is all that matters. Clue: Kai waited quietly."
  - "Lesson: Toys should be new. Clue: everyone got to play."
  - "Lesson: Lunch should be longer. Clue: Kai waited his turn."
correctAnswer: "Lesson: Patience can be fair. Clue: Kai waited, and everyone got a turn."
explanation: The lesson should match both the action Kai chose and the outcome that followed.
hint: Use the character's action and what happened because of it.
```

```question
key: u02_l04_q06_reject_mismatch_lesson
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A story is about a student who tells the truth about breaking a ruler and helps replace it. Which lesson does NOT fit?
choices:
  - Winning every race is most important.
  - Honesty can help solve a problem.
  - Taking responsibility matters.
  - Fixing a mistake is better than hiding it.
correctAnswer: Winning every race is most important.
explanation: The story is about honesty and responsibility, not racing.
hint: Choose the lesson that changes the topic.
```
