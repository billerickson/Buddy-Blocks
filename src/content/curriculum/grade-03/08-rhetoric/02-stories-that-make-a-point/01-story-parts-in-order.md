## Teaching Goal

Use beginning, middle, and end to organize a short story or fable.

## Student Outcome

The student can place story events in a clear order and name whether each event belongs at the beginning, middle, or end.

## Misconception Checks

Events can be retold in any order; the end is just the last sentence, even if it does not show an outcome.

## Teaching Note

Use a three-event story and physically move cards into beginning, middle, and end.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_order_events
type: order-items
prompt: Put the story events in the clearest order.
items:
  - Mia saw that the class plant was dry.
  - Mia watered the plant before lunch.
  - The leaves stood up again by afternoon.
  - Mia walked into the classroom.
correctOrder:
  - Mia walked into the classroom.
  - Mia saw that the class plant was dry.
  - Mia watered the plant before lunch.
  - The leaves stood up again by afternoon.
explanation: A clear story order starts with the situation, then the problem, action, and outcome.
hint: Find what must happen first before the other events make sense.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q02_beginning_event
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which event most likely belongs in the beginning of a story?
choices:
  - Lena opened the classroom door and saw a note on her desk.
  - Lena solved the problem and smiled.
  - Because of her choice, the group finished on time.
  - At last, everyone understood what happened.
correctAnswer: Lena opened the classroom door and saw a note on her desk.
explanation: A beginning often introduces the person, place, or first situation.
hint: The beginning usually sets up the story before the main action.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q03_fable_order
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the short fable and answer.
passageTitle: The Bell And The Helper
passage: |
  A class bell fell from its hook before morning meeting.
  Sam noticed it and told the teacher.
  The teacher fixed the hook, and the meeting started on time.
question: Which event is the middle of the story?
choices:
  - Sam noticed the bell and told the teacher.
  - The meeting started on time.
  - A class bell fell from its hook.
  - The story has no middle.
correctAnswer: Sam noticed the bell and told the teacher.
explanation: The middle shows the main action that responds to the problem.
hint: Look for the action between the setup and the outcome.
```
