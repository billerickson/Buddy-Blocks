## Teaching Goal

Show how a character's choice and outcome support the point of a story.

## Student Outcome

The student can explain how an action in a story helps show the lesson.

## Misconception Checks

A lesson floats apart from the story; only the ending matters; a character's feelings are enough evidence without actions.

## Teaching Note

Use a two-column chart: "choice" and "what it shows."

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l05_q01_choice_to_outcome
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each choice to its outcome.
pairs:
  - left: Nora labeled the supply boxes.
    right: The class found materials quickly.
  - left: Ben hid the missing scissors.
    right: The group lost time searching.
  - left: Ari asked before borrowing paint.
    right: The partner felt respected.
  - left: Leah practiced her lines each day.
    right: She spoke with more confidence.
explanation: A choice often leads to an outcome that helps reveal the lesson.
hint: Ask what happened because of each choice.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q02_choice_supports_lesson
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the story and answer.
passageTitle: The Shared Map
passage: |
  Two students had one map for a scavenger hunt.
  Tessa kept it to herself and read quietly.
  Her partner missed two clues.
  Tessa finally held the map between them, and they solved the last clue together.
question: Which choice best supports the lesson that sharing tools can help a team?
choices:
  - Tessa held the map between them.
  - The hunt had clues.
  - The map had folded corners.
  - The students were outside.
correctAnswer: Tessa held the map between them.
explanation: That choice shows sharing the tool so both partners can work together.
hint: Look for the action that changes the team problem.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q03_how_action_supports
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A story's lesson is 'Listen before deciding.' Which event best supports that lesson?
choices:
  - Mara heard both teammates explain before choosing a plan.
  - Mara chose a plan before her teammates finished talking.
  - Mara picked the plan with the brightest cover.
  - Mara asked what color folder to use.
correctAnswer: Mara heard both teammates explain before choosing a plan.
explanation: The event directly shows listening before making a decision.
hint: Choose the action that matches the lesson words.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q04_choice_outcome_lesson_frame
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the choice-outcome-lesson frame.
parts:
  - "The character chose to "
  - ". Because of that, "
  - . This supports the lesson.
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: share the map
    choices:
      - share the map
      - keep the map hidden
      - choose a new team name
  - correctAnswer: the team solved the clue together
    choices:
      - the team solved the clue together
      - the partner missed more clues
      - the map stayed folded
explanation: The frame connects a choice to an outcome, then to the lesson.
hint: The second blank should happen because of the first blank.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q05_choice_to_lesson_explain
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A character admits, 'I lost the marker,' and then helps look for it. Which explanation best shows how the choice supports the lesson 'Honesty helps solve problems'?
choices:
  - The character tells the truth, so everyone can start looking and fix the problem.
  - The marker is probably a classroom supply.
  - The character talks, so the story has dialogue.
  - The lesson is true even without the character's action.
correctAnswer: The character tells the truth, so everyone can start looking and fix the problem.
explanation: The choice supports the lesson because telling the truth makes it possible to solve the problem.
hint: Choose the explanation that connects the honest choice to the outcome.
```

```question
key: u02_l05_q06_steps_to_explain_lesson

  - By sharing the map, Tessa let both partners work, so the team solved a clue together.
  - The map had folded corners, so the story has a lesson.
  - The hunt was outside, so sharing tools is always easy.
  - Tessa had a partner, but the choice does not matter.
```
