## Teaching Goal

Teach students to choose events needed for a clear retelling and leave out extra details that do not affect the point.

## Student Outcome

The student can distinguish key events from extra details in a short story.

## Misconception Checks

Every detail belongs in a retelling; favorite or funny details are always key; short retellings are weak.

## Teaching Note

Show a retelling that includes a distracting detail and ask whether it helps the listener understand the story.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l02_q01_key_event
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the story and answer.
passageTitle: The Missing Card
passage: |
  Priya made a thank-you card for the custodian.
  The card slipped behind the bookshelf.
  Priya searched carefully and found it before dismissal.
question: Which detail is a key event for retelling the story?
choices:
  - The card slipped behind the bookshelf.
  - The bookshelf was brown.
  - Priya wore striped socks.
  - The classroom clock was round.
correctAnswer: The card slipped behind the bookshelf.
explanation: The lost card creates the story problem, so it is key to the retelling.
hint: Choose the detail that affects what happens next.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q02_detail_jobs
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each detail to its job in the retelling.
pairs:
  - left: The card slipped behind the bookshelf.
    right: creates the problem
  - left: Priya searched carefully.
    right: shows the main action
  - left: She found it before dismissal.
    right: shows the outcome
  - left: The bookshelf was brown.
    right: adds extra description
explanation: Key events carry the problem, action, and outcome. Extra description may be true but less necessary.
hint: Ask whether the story would still make sense without the detail.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q03_best_short_retelling
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which retelling keeps the key events and leaves out extras?
choices:
  - Priya made a card, lost it behind the bookshelf, searched, and found it.
  - Priya had striped socks, and the clock was round.
  - The bookshelf was brown, tall, and beside a window.
  - Priya made a card and found it later.
correctAnswer: Priya made a card, lost it behind the bookshelf, searched, and found it.
explanation: This retelling includes the important setup, problem, action, and outcome.
hint: Choose the retelling that would help someone understand what happened.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q04_remove_extra
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "A retelling says: 'Jay dropped the class tickets, the tickets were yellow, he picked them up, and the line moved on.' Which detail could be removed first?"
choices:
  - the tickets were yellow
  - Jay dropped the class tickets
  - he picked them up
  - the line moved on
correctAnswer: the tickets were yellow
explanation: The color does not change the main events of the retelling.
hint: Look for the detail that does not affect the problem, action, or outcome.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q05_why_key_event_matters
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why is 'Jay picked up the tickets' a key event in the retelling?
choices:
  - It shows how Jay fixed the problem.
  - It tells what color the tickets were.
  - It describes where the clock was.
  - It adds a funny detail but does not affect the story.
correctAnswer: It shows how Jay fixed the problem.
explanation: A key event helps the audience understand the action that changes the story.
hint: Ask what would be missing if that event were removed.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q06_order_key_events
type: order-items
prompt: Order the key events for a clear retelling.
items:
  - Noah forgot the group poster.

  - The group finished with a smaller poster.
correctOrder:
  - Noah forgot the group poster.
  - The group made a quick plan.
  - The group finished with a smaller poster.
explanation: A clear retelling uses the problem, action, and outcome in order.
```
