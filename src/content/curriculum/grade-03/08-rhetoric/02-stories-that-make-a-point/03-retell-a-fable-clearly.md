## Teaching Goal

Combine order and key events into a concise fable retelling.

## Student Outcome

The student can retell a short fable with key events and a clear outcome.

## Misconception Checks

Retelling means copying the whole story; a retelling can skip the outcome; extra adjectives matter more than key actions.

## Teaching Note

Model a "too long," "too short," and "just right" retelling of the same fable.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l03_q01_best_fable_retell
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the fable and answer.
passageTitle: The Lantern
passage: |
  A student carried a lantern for the class walk.
  He rushed ahead to show that he was fastest.
  The group could not see the path, so he came back and walked with them.
question: Which is the clearest retelling?
choices:
  - A student rushed ahead with the lantern, saw the group needed light, and came back to walk with them.
  - A student had a lantern. Lanterns can be shiny.
  - The class walked, and something happened with a path.
  - A student was fast and liked carrying the lantern.
correctAnswer: A student rushed ahead with the lantern, saw the group needed light, and came back to walk with them.
explanation: The retelling keeps the key events and outcome in order.
hint: Choose the answer that would help someone understand the whole fable.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q02_order_retelling
type: order-items
prompt: Put the retelling sentences in order.
items:
  - The group could not see the path.
  - A student rushed ahead with the lantern.
  - He returned and walked with the group.
  - The class began a walk.
correctOrder:
  - The class began a walk.
  - A student rushed ahead with the lantern.
  - The group could not see the path.
  - He returned and walked with the group.
explanation: This order shows the setup, action, problem, and changed choice.
hint: Start with the setup before the student rushes ahead.
```
