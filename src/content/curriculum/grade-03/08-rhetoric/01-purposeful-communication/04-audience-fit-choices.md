## Teaching Goal

Teach students that word choice, detail, and tone should fit the audience and purpose.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose the best message or revision for a simple audience-purpose situation.

## Misconception Checks

The same words fit every audience; being loud or bossy makes a message stronger; polite language is only about manners, not effect.

## Teaching Note

Compare two requests for the same thing, one to a friend and one to a teacher, and ask what changes and what stays respectful.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l04_q01_teacher_request
type: multiple-choice
prompt: You need to ask a teacher for help. Which sentence best fits the audience and purpose?
choices:
  - Could you please help me understand this direction?
  - Hey, fix this now.
  - This direction is confusing.
  - Help?
correctAnswer: Could you please help me understand this direction?
explanation: The sentence is respectful, clear, and fits the purpose of asking for help.
hint: Look for a polite request that tells what help is needed.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q02_wait_turn_dialogue
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Ari says, "I am using the blue marker right now." Which reply from Nina best fits the audience and purpose?
choices:
  - May I use it when you are finished?
  - Give it to me this second.
  - I like that marker best.
  - I wanted that color first.
correctAnswer: May I use it when you are finished?
explanation: Nina's line is respectful and fits the purpose of asking for a turn.
hint: Choose the line that respects the listener and still asks clearly.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q03_revise_harsh_request
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which revision is respectful and clear: 'Move. I need that seat.'"
choices:
  - Could I please sit there?
  - Move. I need that seat.
  - Give me that seat now.
  - You have to move.
correctAnswer: Could I please sit there?
explanation: A respectful request fits the audience better than a command.
hint: Turn the command into a polite question.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q04_detail_for_younger_audience
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the situation and answer.
passageTitle: Explaining A Game
passage: |
  Jalen is teaching first graders how to play a new game.
  He wants them to know the first step.
question: Which detail best fits his young audience and purpose?
choices:
  - First, choose one game piece and place it on Start.
  - The game was invented many years ago.
  - The box has three small scratches.
  - Some people prefer outdoor games.
correctAnswer: First, choose one game piece and place it on Start.
explanation: The first graders need a clear first step, not extra background or unrelated details.
hint: Pick the detail that helps the audience do the first step.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q05_audience_purpose_message
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each audience and purpose to the best message.
pairs:
  - left: Ask a friend to play fairly
    right: Let's take turns so everyone gets a chance.
  - left: Tell visitors where coats go
    right: Please hang coats on the hooks by the door.
  - left: Invite grandparents to a concert
    right: We hope you can come hear our class sing Friday.
  - left: Explain cleanup to classmates
    right: Put markers in the cup and papers in the blue bin.
explanation: A fitting message gives the audience what they need for the purpose.
hint: Match the words to the people who need them.
```
