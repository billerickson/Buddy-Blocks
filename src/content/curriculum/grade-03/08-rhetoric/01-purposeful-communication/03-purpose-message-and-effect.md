## Teaching Goal

Help students distinguish what a communication says from what it tries to do and how it may affect an audience.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify the message, purpose, and likely effect of a short communication.

## Misconception Checks

Purpose and message are the same; effect is always exactly what the speaker wanted; every communication has only one possible effect.

## Teaching Note

Use a simple sign such as "Wet floor. Please walk around." Discuss what it says, why it says it, and what it asks people to do.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_purpose_examples
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each purpose to the message.
pairs:
  - left: warn
    right: "Careful: the steps are wet."
  - left: invite
    right: Please come to our class poetry reading.
  - left: explain
    right: Fold the paper in half, then cut along the line.
  - left: entertain
    right: The silly poem made everyone laugh.
explanation: Purpose tells what the message is trying to do.
hint: Ask what action or response each message is trying to create.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q02_wet_floor_message_purpose
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sign and answer.
passageTitle: Hallway Sign
passage: |
  Wet floor.
  Please walk slowly.
question: Which choice correctly names the message and purpose?
choices:
  - "Message: the floor is wet. Purpose: warn people to be careful."
  - "Message: walking is fun. Purpose: entertain people."
  - "Message: people should run. Purpose: make the hallway exciting."
  - "Message: the floor is shiny. Purpose: describe the building."
correctAnswer: "Message: the floor is wet. Purpose: warn people to be careful."
explanation: The sign says the floor is wet and tries to help people avoid slipping.
hint: The message is what it says. The purpose is why it says it.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q04_message_not_purpose
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice is a message, not a purpose?
choices:
  - The bake sale starts at noon.
  - To persuade people to buy cookies
  - To warn the class
  - To entertain the audience
correctAnswer: The bake sale starts at noon.
explanation: That sentence tells what the words say. The other choices explain why someone might communicate.
hint: A message can often be written as the exact information being sent.
```

