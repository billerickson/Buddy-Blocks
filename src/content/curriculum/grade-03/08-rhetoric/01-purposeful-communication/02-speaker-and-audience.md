## Teaching Goal

Teach students to identify who is communicating and who the communication is for.

## Student Outcome

The student can name the speaker or writer and the intended audience in a short scenario.

## Misconception Checks

The audience is always everyone; the speaker is always the main character; the reader of a prompt is automatically the intended audience.

## Teaching Note

Show the same message changing audience: "Please walk quietly" from a teacher to a class versus from a librarian to visitors.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l02_q01_find_speaker
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Read the note: 'Class, please line up quietly for the library. - Mrs. Lee' Who is the speaker or writer?"
choices:
  - Mrs. Lee
  - the class
  - the library
  - the librarian
correctAnswer: Mrs. Lee
explanation: Mrs. Lee wrote the note, so she is the speaker or writer.
hint: Look for who is sending the message.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q03_messages_to_audiences
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each message to its most likely audience.
pairs:
  - left: Please return your books by Friday.
    right: library visitors
  - left: Remember to bring shin guards to practice.
    right: soccer teammates
  - left: Your child will need a signed permission slip.
    right: parents
  - left: Open your notebooks to page 12.
    right: students in class
explanation: Audience clues often come from what the message asks people to do.
hint: Think about who could act on each message.
```
