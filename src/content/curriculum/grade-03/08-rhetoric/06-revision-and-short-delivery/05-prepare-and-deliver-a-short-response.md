## Teaching Goal

Prepare and deliver a brief oral response with clear voice, complete thought, and listener awareness.

## Student Outcome

The student can use a simple note frame to produce a short spoken response that fits the audience and purpose.

## Misconception Checks

Speaking well means memorizing perfectly; faster is better; listeners do not affect delivery choices.

## Teaching Note

Model a short response with a note card: point, one reason or event, final sentence.

## Questions

prompt: Which delivery choice helps listeners understand a short response?

  - Speak clearly and pause between ideas.

  - Look only at the floor the whole time.
  - Mumble the most important words.

hint: Choose the action that helps the audience hear and understand.
