## Teaching Goal

Revise word choice so a message fits its audience and purpose.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose words that are clear, respectful, and effective for a simple audience-purpose situation.

## Misconception Checks

The strongest word is the fanciest word; harsh words make a message more persuasive; word choice is only about correctness.

## Teaching Note

Compare "Give me that now" and "Could I use that after you are done?" for audience and purpose.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l04_q01_best_word_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Audience: a younger student. Purpose: explain where to put scissors safely. Which sentence fits best?"
choices:
  - Please place the scissors in the blue cup with the handles up.
  - Deposit the instruments in the receptacle.
  - Put the scissors over there carefully.
  - Clean up now before anything else.
correctAnswer: Please place the scissors in the blue cup with the handles up.
explanation: The sentence is clear, respectful, and useful for the audience.
hint: Choose wording a younger student could understand and follow.
```

```question

type: multiple-choice
```

```question
choices:
  - Your idea needs a clearer reason.
  - Your idea is bad.
  - I do not like your idea.
  - Add stuff to it.

explanation: The revision focuses on the idea and gives a helpful direction.
```

```question

pairs:
  - left: Ask a teacher for another copy

  - left: Explain a rule to a first grader

  - left: Thank a guest speaker
    right: Thank you for teaching us about your work.
  - left: Suggest a revision to a partner
    right: This reason would be clearer with an example.
```
