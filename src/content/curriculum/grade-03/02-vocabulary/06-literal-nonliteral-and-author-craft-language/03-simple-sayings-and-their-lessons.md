## Teaching Goal

Introduce simple sayings as whole ideas that often teach a lesson or give advice.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose the meaning of a common saying from a short situation.

## Misconception Checks

Taking the saying word by word; treating every saying as a command; choosing a meaning unrelated to the situation.

## Teaching Note

Use "Practice makes progress" or "Many hands make light work" in a short class situation.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l03_q01_saying_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What does "Practice makes progress" mean?
choices:
  - Practicing helps you improve
  - Practicing means you are already perfect
  - Progress means going backward
  - Only games need practice
correctAnswer: Practicing helps you improve
explanation: The saying means repeated effort helps you get better.
hint: Think about what happens when you practice a skill.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q02_saying_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each saying to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: Many hands make light work
    right: A job is easier when people help
  - left: Look before you leap
    right: Think before you act
  - left: Better late than never
    right: Doing it late is better than not doing it
  - left: Honesty is the best policy
    right: Telling the truth is best
explanation: Sayings often give advice or a lesson.
hint: Match the whole idea, not each separate word.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q03_situation_saying
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the situation and choose the saying.
passageTitle: Cleaning The Park
passage: The park looked messy, but ten classmates picked up trash together. Soon the job was done.
question: Which saying best fits?
choices:
  - Many hands make light work
  - Better late than never
  - Look before you leap
  - The early bird catches the worm
correctAnswer: Many hands make light work
explanation: The job became easier because many classmates helped.
hint: Choose the saying about people helping with a job.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q04_lesson_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A student rushes into a project without reading directions and makes mistakes. Which saying gives the best advice?
choices:
  - Look before you leap
  - Many hands make light work
  - Practice makes progress
  - A penny saved is a penny earned
correctAnswer: Look before you leap
explanation: Look before you leap means think or check before acting.
hint: The problem is rushing without thinking.
```
