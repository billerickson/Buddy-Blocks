## Teaching Goal

Teach simple author-craft words that help students describe how language makes meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify an image, sound word, simple simile, or dialogue cue when it supports understanding.

## Misconception Checks

Every comparison with like or as is meaningful; sound words are only fun noises; dialogue is separate from meaning.

## Teaching Note

Compare "The leaves moved" with "The leaves whispered like paper" and ask what the reader can picture or hear.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l04_q01_simile_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence has a simile?
choices:
  - The snow sparkled like tiny diamonds.
  - The snow fell all morning.
  - The snow covered the steps.
  - The snow was cold.
correctAnswer: The snow sparkled like tiny diamonds.
explanation: A simile compares two things using like or as.
hint: Look for a comparison using like.
```
