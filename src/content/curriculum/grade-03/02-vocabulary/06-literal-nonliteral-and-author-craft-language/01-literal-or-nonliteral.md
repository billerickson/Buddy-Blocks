## Teaching Goal

Teach students to decide whether words mean exactly what they say or have a phrase-level meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can classify a phrase as literal or nonliteral and explain the meaning in simple words.

## Misconception Checks

Nonliteral means meaningless; every silly sentence is an idiom; literal meaning always fits if every word is known.

## Teaching Note

Compare "The backpack is heavy" with "The homework was a heavy load" in a supported conversation.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l01_q01_literal_choice
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses literal language?
choices:
  - The ice melted in the cup.
  - My brain is on fire with ideas.
  - Time flew during recess.
  - She has a heart of gold.
correctAnswer: The ice melted in the cup.
explanation: The ice really melted, so the words mean exactly what they say.
hint: Choose the sentence that could happen exactly as written.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q02_nonliteral_meaning

prompt: 'Read: "Time flew during the fun game." What does time flew mean?'
choices:
  - The game seemed to pass quickly
  - A clock grew wings
  - The game was outside
  - No one knew the time

explanation: Time did not really fly; the phrase means the game felt quick.
hint: Think about how fun activities can feel.
```
