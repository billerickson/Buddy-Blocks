## Teaching Goal

Teach students to infer common idiom meanings from context.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose the meaning of an idiom and reject a literal distractor.

## Misconception Checks

Defining each word separately; choosing the funniest literal picture; missing emotional or situation clues.

## Teaching Note

Use idioms such as `spill the beans`, `under the weather`, and `hit the books` in brief realistic scenes.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l02_q01_idiom_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "Maya was under the weather, so she stayed home from school." What does under the weather mean?'
choices:
  - feeling sick
  - standing in rain
  - studying clouds
  - playing outside
correctAnswer: feeling sick
explanation: Staying home from school is a clue that under the weather means feeling sick.
hint: Use what Maya did because of how she felt.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q02_idiom_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each idiom to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: hit the books
    right: study
  - left: spill the beans
    right: tell a secret
  - left: lend a hand
    right: help
  - left: piece of cake
    right: very easy
explanation: Idioms are phrases with meanings that usually are not word-by-word.
hint: Think about how people use each phrase in conversation.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q03_literal_distractor
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The math quiz was a piece of cake for Jonah because he had practiced." Which answer is the literal mistake?'
choices:
  - The quiz was an actual dessert
  - The quiz was very easy
  - Practice helped Jonah
  - Jonah understood the math
correctAnswer: The quiz was an actual dessert
explanation: Piece of cake is an idiom meaning very easy, not a real cake.
hint: Find the answer that takes the phrase word by word.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q04_idiom_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the dialogue and choose the idiom meaning.
passageTitle: The Surprise
passage: Tara almost told Dad about the surprise party. "Don't spill the beans!" her brother whispered.
question: What does spill the beans mean?
choices:
  - tell the secret
  - drop food on the floor
  - cook dinner
  - clean the kitchen
correctAnswer: tell the secret
explanation: The surprise party is a secret, so spill the beans means tell the secret.
hint: Use the clue surprise party.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q05_idiom_fill
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the idiom that fits.
sentenceBefore: When the shelf was too heavy, Dad asked me to
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - lend a hand
  - hit the books
  - spill the beans
  - under the weather
correctAnswer: lend a hand
explanation: Lend a hand means help, which fits lifting a heavy shelf.
hint: Choose the phrase that means help.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q06_idiom_explain
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "After dinner, I hit the books to study for science." What does hit the books mean?'
choices:
  - study
  - knock books off a shelf
  - eat dinner
  - play outside
correctAnswer: study
explanation: The phrase means study, and the science context confirms it.
hint: It does not mean really hitting books.
```
