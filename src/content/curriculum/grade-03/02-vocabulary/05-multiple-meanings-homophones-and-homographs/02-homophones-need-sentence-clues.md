## Teaching Goal

Teach students to use sentence meaning and spelling to choose among homophones.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose the sound-alike word that fits the sentence.

## Misconception Checks

Choosing by sound only; ignoring spelling; using the more common word even when the meaning does not fit.

## Teaching Note

Contrast `there`, `their`, and `they're` only if the sentence clues are strong; otherwise use simpler pairs such as `see/sea` and `hear/here`.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l02_q01_homophone_choice
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the word that fits the sentence.
sentenceBefore: I can
sentenceAfter: the ocean from the hill.
choices:
  - see
  - sea
  - seed
  - seen
correctAnswer: see
explanation: See means look at with your eyes. Sea is the ocean itself.
hint: Choose the action word.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q02_homophone_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each homophone to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: hear
    right: use your ears
  - left: here
    right: in this place
  - left: flour
    right: powder used for baking
  - left: flower
    right: blooming part of a plant
explanation: Homophones sound alike but have different spellings and meanings.
hint: Match by meaning, not sound.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q03_error_homophone
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the homophone mistake.
sentence: Please put your coat over their.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Please put your coat over there.
  - Put your coat over there.
explanation: There means in that place. Their shows belonging.
hint: The sentence needs the word for a place.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q04_homophone_context
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses right to mean correct?
choices:
  - You got the right answer.
  - Turn right at the corner.
  - My right shoe is untied.
  - The bird flew to the right.
correctAnswer: You got the right answer.
explanation: Right means correct when it describes an answer.
hint: Look for the sentence about an answer.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q05_passage_homophone
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the homophone.
passageTitle: Baking Day
passage: Nora measured two cups of flour before mixing the dough. She saved the flower from the garden in a vase.
question: Which word names the baking ingredient?
choices:
  - flour
  - flower
  - vase
  - garden
correctAnswer: flour
explanation: Flour is the powder used in baking. Flower is the plant part.
hint: Use the clue mixing the dough.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q06_homophone_explain
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the homophone that fits the place clue.
sentenceBefore: We waited over
sentenceAfter: by the door.
choices:
  - there
  - their
  - they're
  - hear
correctAnswer: there
explanation: There names a place, and by the door is a place clue.
hint: Ask whether the sentence shows place or belonging.
```
