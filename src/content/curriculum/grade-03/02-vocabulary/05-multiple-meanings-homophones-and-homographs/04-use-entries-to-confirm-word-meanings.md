## Teaching Goal

Combine context and reference entries to confirm confusing word meanings.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose the fitting definition from an entry after reading a sentence or short passage.

## Misconception Checks

Reading the entry without returning to context; picking a known definition; confusing homophones with multiple definitions of one word.

## Teaching Note

Show a two-definition entry for `scale` and ask which definition fits a map sentence.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l04_q01_entry_confirm
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and entry.
passageTitle: Scale
passage: |-
  Sentence: The map scale showed that one inch stood for five miles.
  Entry: scale
  1. small plates on a fish or reptile
  2. a tool or mark system used to measure or compare
question: Which definition fits scale?
choices:
  - definition 2
  - definition 1
  - both definitions equally
  - neither definition
correctAnswer: definition 2
explanation: The map clue shows scale is used to measure or compare distance.
hint: Use the word map.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q02_entry_sentence_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each sentence to the fitting definition of current.
pairs:
  - left: The current carried the leaf downstream.
    right: moving water
  - left: Our current book is about space.
    right: happening now
  - left: The wire carried an electric current.
    right: flow of electricity
  - left: The current news is on the board.
    right: latest information
explanation: A reference entry can list several meanings, and context selects one.
hint: "Use the subject: water, book, wire, or news."
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q03_context_entry_cloze
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the explanation.
parts:
  - 'In "The bat hung upside down," bat means '
  - " because the clue is "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: an animal
    choices:
      - an animal
      - a baseball stick
      - a kind of hat
  - correctAnswer: hung upside down
    choices:
      - hung upside down
      - a baseball game
      - a kind of hat
explanation: The clue hung upside down fits the animal meaning.
hint: Choose the meaning and the clue that supports it.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q04_homophone_entry
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which note would help a student choose between here and hear?
choices:
  - Here means in this place; hear means use your ears.
  - Both words mean exactly the same thing.
  - Here is always a verb.
  - Hear means a place near you.
correctAnswer: Here means in this place; hear means use your ears.
explanation: The note gives the meanings needed to choose the correct spelling.
hint: A helpful note should show the difference in meaning.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q05_entry_wrong_fit
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The seal balanced a ball on its nose." Entry: seal 1. an ocean animal 2. to close tightly. Which definition fits?'
choices:
  - an ocean animal
  - to close tightly
  - both definitions
  - neither definition
correctAnswer: an ocean animal
explanation: An animal can balance a ball on its nose.
hint: Ask which meaning can have a nose.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q06_verify_explanation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The judge was fair to both teams." An entry says fair can mean "light-colored," "a festival," or "treating people equally." Which explanation is best?'
choices:
  - '"Treating people equally" fits because the judge was fair to both teams.'
  - '"Light-colored" fits because teams are always pale.'
  - '"A festival" fits because the sentence is about a judge.'
  - All three meanings fit equally well.
correctAnswer: '"Treating people equally" fits because the judge was fair to both teams.'
explanation: The clue both teams shows fair means treating people equally.
hint: Think about what a judge should do for two teams.
```
