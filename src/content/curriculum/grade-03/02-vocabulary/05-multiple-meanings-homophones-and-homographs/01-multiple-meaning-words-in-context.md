## Teaching Goal

Teach students to choose the intended meaning of a word with more than one meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can use sentence clues to select the fitting meaning of a multiple-meaning word.

## Misconception Checks

Choosing the most familiar meaning; assuming a word has one real meaning; ignoring clues after the target word.

## Teaching Note

Use `light` in "not heavy" and "lamp light" contexts to show how sentence fit changes meaning.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l01_q01_light_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The empty backpack felt light on my shoulders." Which meaning of light fits?'
choices:
  - not heavy
  - not dark
  - to turn on a lamp
  - a flame
correctAnswer: not heavy
explanation: The clue backpack felt tells you light means not heavy.
hint: Ask what a backpack can feel like on your shoulders.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q02_sentence_meanings
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each sentence to the meaning of ring that fits.
pairs:
  - left: The phone began to ring.
    right: make a sound
  - left: She wore a ring on her finger.
    right: a piece of jewelry
  - left: The children sat in a ring.
    right: a circle shape
  - left: The tree had a dark ring inside the trunk.
    right: a circular growth mark
explanation: Context decides which meaning of ring is intended.
hint: Use the object or action in each sentence.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q03_fill_meaning
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the meaning that fits the sentence.
sentenceBefore: In "Please park the car beside the curb," park means
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - to stop and leave a vehicle
  - a place with grass and swings
  - to run quickly
  - a kind of animal
correctAnswer: to stop and leave a vehicle
explanation: The clue car shows park is an action with a vehicle.
hint: Ask what you can do with a car.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q04_passage_meaning
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and choose the fitting meaning.
passageTitle: At The Bank
passage: We walked along the river bank and watched ducks swim near the muddy edge.
question: What does bank mean in this passage?
choices:
  - the land beside a river
  - a place to keep money
  - to turn an airplane
  - a row of buttons
correctAnswer: the land beside a river
explanation: River and muddy edge show bank means land beside water.
hint: Use the words river and edge.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q05_wrong_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The pitcher poured lemonade into each cup." Why does pitcher not mean a baseball player?'
choices:
  - The sentence says it poured lemonade
  - A pitcher is always a player
  - The sentence is about a game
  - Lemonade is a kind of ball
correctAnswer: The sentence says it poured lemonade
explanation: A container can pour lemonade; a baseball player does not fit this sentence.
hint: Ask which meaning can hold lemonade.
```

key: u05_l01_q06_explain_meaning

  - "Fall means "
  - ". The clue is "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: autumn
    choices:
      - autumn
      - drop down
      - trip
  - correctAnswer: leaves change
    choices:
      - leaves change
      - the class watched
