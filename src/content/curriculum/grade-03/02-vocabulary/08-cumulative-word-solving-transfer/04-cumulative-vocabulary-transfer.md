## Teaching Goal

Assess and deepen end-of-level vocabulary transfer in mixed new contexts.

## Student Outcome

The student can solve and use vocabulary across context clues, references, word parts, relationships, multiple meanings, nonliteral language, and academic words.

## Misconception Checks

Treating the unit as separate review questions; choosing familiar meanings without evidence; using a new word in an unclear sentence.

## Teaching Note

Frame the lesson as a word-detective challenge: the student decides which evidence solves each word best.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l04_q01_mixed_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the garden note and infer the meaning.
passageTitle: Garden Notes
passage: The seedlings were fragile, so we carried them gently to the garden bed.
question: What does fragile most likely mean?
choices:
  - easy to damage
  - full of seeds
  - ready to eat
  - covered with mud
correctAnswer: easy to damage
explanation: Carried them gently shows fragile means easy to damage.
hint: Ask why someone would carry them gently.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q02_mixed_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each item to the vocabulary skill it uses.
pairs:
  - left: preview means see before
    right: prefix
  - left: piece of cake means very easy
    right: idiom
  - left: table means data chart in math
    right: multiple meaning
  - left: tiny, small, miniature
    right: shades of meaning
explanation: The level's skills can be recognized in mixed review.
hint: Ask what kind of word problem each item shows.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q03_transfer_reference
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The current in the river was strong." Entry: current 1. happening now 2. moving water 3. flow of electricity. Which definition fits?'
choices:
  - moving water
  - happening now
  - flow of electricity
  - all definitions fit equally
correctAnswer: moving water
explanation: The river clue shows current means moving water.
hint: Use the subject of the sentence.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q04_academic_transfer
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the academic word that fits.
sentenceBefore: Use text
sentenceAfter: to support your answer.
choices:
  - evidence
  - furniture
  - weather
  - volume
correctAnswer: evidence
explanation: Evidence supports an answer with clues from the text.
hint: Choose the word that means proof or support.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q05_precise_production
type: text-input
prompt: Type a more precise word for very hungry.
acceptedAnswers:
  - famished
  - starving
answerType: text
explanation: Famished and starving are precise words that mean very hungry.
hint: Think of a stronger word than hungry.
```

```question
key: u08_l04_q06_final_transfer_response
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the word that fits the sentence and shows careful action.
sentenceBefore: The
sentenceAfter: hiker stepped slowly across the slippery rocks.
choices:
  - cautious
  - careless
  - joyful
  - ancient
correctAnswer: cautious
explanation: A strong sentence makes the word's meaning clear from context.
hint: Think of a person being careful to avoid danger.
```
