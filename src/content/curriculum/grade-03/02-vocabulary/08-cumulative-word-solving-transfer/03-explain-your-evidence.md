## Teaching Goal

Strengthen students' ability to explain the evidence behind a vocabulary answer.

## Student Outcome

The student can write or select a short explanation connecting the meaning to a clue, word part, reference definition, or phrase meaning.

## Misconception Checks

Repeating the answer without evidence; naming an unrelated clue; writing a long response that does not explain meaning fit.

## Teaching Note

Compare three explanations for the same word and discuss which one actually proves the meaning.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l03_q01_best_explanation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The pond was tranquil; the water did not ripple." Which explanation is best?'
choices:
  - Tranquil means calm because the water did not ripple.
  - Tranquil means pond because pond is nearby.
  - Tranquil means loud because I like loud ponds.
  - Tranquil has two syllables, so it means water.
correctAnswer: Tranquil means calm because the water did not ripple.
explanation: The best explanation gives the meaning and the clue.
hint: Choose the answer with both meaning and evidence.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q02_fix_weak_explanation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Context: "Only one apple was left for the whole class, so fruit was scarce." Which explanation uses the clue well?'
choices:
  - Scarce means not enough because only one apple was left.
  - Scarce means food because food is near the word.
  - Scarce means sweet because the sentence mentions an apple.
  - Scarce means class because the word class is nearby.
correctAnswer: Scarce means not enough because only one apple was left.
explanation: A strong explanation uses the real clue and gives the meaning, not just a nearby topic.
hint: Explain the meaning and use the clue only one apple left.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q03_explanation_frame
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the evidence explanation.
parts:
  - "The word rugged means "
  - ". The contrast clue is "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: rough or uneven
    choices:
      - rough or uneven
      - smooth and flat
      - bright and shiny
  - correctAnswer: but the first trail was smooth
    choices:
      - but the first trail was smooth
      - the first trail was short
      - the trail had trees
explanation: The contrast with smooth supports rugged as rough or uneven.
hint: Choose the clue that proves the meaning.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q04_evidence_or_not
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence gives useful evidence for the meaning of exhausted?
choices:
  - After running three miles, Lina collapsed onto the bench.
  - Lina wore a red shirt.
  - The bench was beside a tree.
  - Lina started at noon.
correctAnswer: After running three miles, Lina collapsed onto the bench.
explanation: Running three miles and collapsing are clues that exhausted means very tired.
hint: Evidence should help explain the word.
```
