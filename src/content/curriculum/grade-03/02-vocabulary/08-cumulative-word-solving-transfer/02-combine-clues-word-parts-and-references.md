## Teaching Goal

Teach students to combine evidence sources when one clue is not enough.

## Student Outcome

The student can use context plus word parts or a reference entry to choose and verify a meaning.

## Misconception Checks

Stopping after one weak clue; ignoring an entry because a word part seems familiar; choosing a definition that does not fit the sentence.

## Teaching Note

Use `misplace` in a sentence plus a mini-entry to show how prefix and context agree.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l02_q01_combined_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and entry.
passageTitle: Misplace
passage: |-
  Sentence: I misplaced my library card, so I had to search my backpack.
  Entry: misplace: to put something in the wrong place and lose track of it for a while.
question: Which evidence supports the meaning of misplaced?
choices:
  - mis- can mean wrongly, and search my backpack fits the entry
  - library card means the same as backpack
  - misplaced means read again
  - search means the card was easy to find
correctAnswer: mis- can mean wrongly, and search my backpack fits the entry
explanation: The prefix, context, and entry all support the meaning.
hint: Use more than one clue.
```
