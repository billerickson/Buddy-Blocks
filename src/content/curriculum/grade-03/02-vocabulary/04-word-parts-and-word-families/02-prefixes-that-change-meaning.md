## Teaching Goal

Teach common prefixes as meaning parts at the beginning of words.

## Student Outcome

The student can use `un-`, `re-`, `pre-`, and `mis-` to infer meaning in familiar and near-transfer words.

## Misconception Checks

Seeing a prefix where none exists; assuming a prefix gives the whole definition; confusing `pre-` and `re-`; missing context fit.

## Teaching Note

Build a quick chart: `un-` not, `re-` again, `pre-` before, `mis-` wrong or badly.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l02_q01_prefix_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each prefix to its common meaning.
pairs:
  - left: un-
    right: not
  - left: re-
    right: again
  - left: pre-
    right: before
  - left: mis-
    right: wrongly
explanation: Prefixes are meaningful parts added to the beginning of words.
hint: Think of words like unhappy, reread, preview, and misspell.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q02_prefix_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What does reread mean?
choices:
  - read again
  - not read
  - read before
  - read wrongly
correctAnswer: read again
explanation: The prefix re- means again, so reread means read again.
hint: Use the meaning of re-.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q03_choose_prefix
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the prefix that means not.
sentenceBefore: If something is
sentenceAfter: safe, it is not safe.
choices:
  - un-
  - re-
  - pre-
  - mis-
correctAnswer: un-
explanation: The prefix un- can mean not, as in unsafe.
hint: Think of unhappy or unfair.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q04_prefix_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and use the prefix.
passageTitle: Before The Show
passage: The class got a preview of the play before opening night. They watched one short scene.
question: What does preview mean?
choices:
  - a look before the main event
  - a look after everything is over
  - a mistake in a play
  - a book with no pictures
correctAnswer: a look before the main event
explanation: The prefix pre- means before, and the class saw it before opening night.
hint: Use both pre- and before opening night.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q05_mis_prefix
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "I misspelled the word and had to fix it." What does misspelled mean?'
choices:
  - spelled wrongly
  - spelled before
  - spelled again
  - did not spell at all
correctAnswer: spelled wrongly
explanation: The prefix mis- can mean wrongly, and the sentence says the word had to be fixed.
hint: Use the clue had to fix it.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q06_prefix_explain
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "We unpacked the suitcase after the trip." What does unpacked mean?'
choices:
  - took things out of the suitcase
  - packed the suitcase again
  - looked at the suitcase before the trip
  - spelled suitcase wrongly
correctAnswer: took things out of the suitcase
explanation: The prefix un- can show reversing an action, and the suitcase clue helps.
hint: Ask what you do after a trip with a suitcase.
```
