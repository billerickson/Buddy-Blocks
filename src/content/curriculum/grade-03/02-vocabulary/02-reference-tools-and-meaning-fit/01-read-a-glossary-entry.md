## Teaching Goal

Teach students how a glossary entry gives a word, definition, and sometimes an example connected to a text.

## Student Outcome

The student can read a simple glossary entry and match the entry to a context sentence.

## Misconception Checks

Treating a glossary as an unrelated word list; skipping the example sentence; assuming a glossary word cannot appear in other subjects.

## Teaching Note

Show a mini-glossary for a short animal passage and connect one entry to a sentence in the passage.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_glossary_definition
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read the glossary entry: "habitat: the place where a plant or animal lives." What is the definition?'
choices:
  - the place where a plant or animal lives
  - habitat
  - plant or animal
  - a word in a book
correctAnswer: the place where a plant or animal lives
explanation: The definition tells what the word habitat means.
hint: In a glossary entry, the definition comes after the word.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q02_match_glossary
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each glossary word to its definition.
pairs:
  - left: caption
    right: words that explain a picture
  - left: diagram
    right: a labeled drawing
  - left: observe
    right: look carefully
  - left: estimate
    right: make a close guess
explanation: A glossary pairs important words with short definitions.
hint: Match each school word to what it helps you do or understand.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q03_entry_to_sentence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the entry and sentence.
passageTitle: Glossary Check
passage: |-
  Glossary entry: migrate: to move from one place to another at a certain time of year.
  Sentence: Many birds migrate south before winter.
question: What does migrate mean in the sentence?
choices:
  - Move from one place to another
  - Build a nest
  - Sing loudly
  - Sleep all winter
correctAnswer: Move from one place to another
explanation: The glossary entry defines migrate as moving from one place to another.
hint: Use the entry, then check that it fits birds going south.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q04_entry_parts
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each part of a glossary entry to its job.
pairs:
  - left: word
    right: the term being explained
  - left: definition
    right: what the term means
  - left: example
    right: a sentence or case that shows use
  - left: alphabet order
    right: how entries are usually arranged
explanation: Knowing entry parts helps you use a glossary quickly.
hint: Think about what each part helps a reader do.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q05_choose_glossary_word
type: fill-blank
prompt: Use the glossary meaning to complete the sentence.
sentenceBefore: A
sentenceAfter: is a labeled drawing that shows parts of something.
choices:
  - diagram
  - caption
  - summary
  - setting
correctAnswer: diagram
explanation: A diagram is a labeled drawing.
hint: Which word from a glossary often names a labeled drawing?
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q06_glossary_explanation
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'A glossary says, "scarce: hard to find or not enough." Which sentence shows scarce?'
choices:
  - Only two pencils were left for the whole class.
  - The shelf was full of pencils.
  - The pencils were yellow.
  - The class sharpened pencils after lunch.
correctAnswer: Only two pencils were left for the whole class.
explanation: Only two pencils for the whole class shows there are not enough pencils.
hint: Look for a sentence that shows something is hard to find or not enough.
```
