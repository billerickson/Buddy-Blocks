## Teaching Goal

Teach students to choose among multiple definitions by using the sentence context.

## Student Outcome

The student can select the definition that fits and reject a tempting definition that does not.

## Misconception Checks

Picking the first definition; choosing the meaning they already know; ignoring the sentence after reading the entry.

## Teaching Note

Use `bark` or `seal` to show how the same word can fit different sentences.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l02_q01_bark_definition
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The bark on the old oak tree felt rough." Which definition of bark fits?'
choices:
  - the outer covering of a tree
  - the sound a dog makes
  - to shout loudly
  - a small boat
correctAnswer: the outer covering of a tree
explanation: The sentence mentions an oak tree, so bark means the tree covering.
hint: Use the clue old oak tree.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q02_sentence_to_definition
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each sentence to the definition of scale that fits.
pairs:
  - left: The map scale shows one inch equals ten miles.
    right: a tool for comparing distance on a map
  - left: The fish had shiny scales.
    right: small flat plates on an animal
  - left: Dad stepped on the scale.
    right: a tool for measuring weight
  - left: The singer practiced the scale.
    right: a set of musical notes
explanation: The same word can have different definitions, and the sentence chooses the fit.
hint: "Look for the subject: map, fish, weight, or music."
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q03_reject_wrong_definition
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "Please close the book and place it on the shelf." Which definition of close fits?'
choices:
  - shut
  - near
  - careful
  - friendly
correctAnswer: shut
explanation: You can shut a book, so close means shut in this sentence.
hint: Ask what action you can do to a book.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q04_entry_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the entry and sentence.
passageTitle: Definition Fit
passage: |-
  Entry: draft
  1. a first version of writing
  2. a flow of air
  Sentence: I read my draft and added details before turning in my story.
question: Which definition fits draft?
choices:
  - a first version of writing
  - a flow of air
  - a type of lunch
  - a loud sound
correctAnswer: a first version of writing
explanation: The sentence is about writing and adding details to a story.
hint: Use the clues read, added details, and story.
```

```question
key: u02_l02_q05_definition_fit_cloze
type: multi-blank-cloze

parts:
  - 'In "The pitcher threw the ball," pitcher means '
  - " because the clue is "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: a baseball player
    choices:
      - a baseball player
      - a water container
      - a tall plant
  - correctAnswer: threw the ball
    choices:
      - threw the ball
      - a water container
      - the word pitcher
explanation: The clue threw the ball shows pitcher means a player, not a container.
hint: Choose the meaning and the clue that proves it.
```
