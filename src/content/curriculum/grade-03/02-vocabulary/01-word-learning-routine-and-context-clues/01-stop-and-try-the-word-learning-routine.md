## Teaching Goal

Establish a repeatable routine for approaching an unknown word instead of guessing immediately.

## Student Outcome

The student can put the routine steps in order and use them to make a supported meaning guess.

## Misconception Checks

Guessing from the first nearby word; skipping rereading; treating the first guess as final; thinking a reference must be used before looking at context.

## Teaching Note

Model meeting the word `drenched` in "After the rainstorm, my drenched socks dripped on the floor" and think aloud through the routine.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l01_q01_routine_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the word-learning routine in a useful order.
items:
  - Try a meaning
  - Pause at the unknown word
  - Check if the meaning fits
  - Reread the sentence
  - Look for clues
correctOrder:
  - Pause at the unknown word
  - Reread the sentence
  - Look for clues
  - Try a meaning
  - Check if the meaning fits
explanation: A strong reader slows down, rereads, finds evidence, tries a meaning, and checks it.
hint: Start with what you do before making any guess.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q02_best_first_step
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'You read: "The timid puppy hid behind the chair when the doorbell rang." You do not know timid. What is the best first step?'
choices:
  - Reread the sentence and look for clues
  - Skip the word forever
  - Pick the meaning of puppy
  - Guess from the first nearby word
correctAnswer: Reread the sentence and look for clues
explanation: The routine starts by rereading and looking for clues in the sentence.
hint: A first step should help you use the sentence, not avoid it.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q03_meaning_from_clue
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer.
passageTitle: Wet Socks
passage: After the rainstorm, Ava's drenched socks dripped water onto the floor.
question: What does drenched most likely mean?
choices:
  - Very wet
  - Very clean
  - Full of holes
  - Brightly colored
correctAnswer: Very wet
explanation: The socks dripped water, so drenched means very wet.
hint: Look at what the socks did after the storm.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q04_clue_identification
type: multiple-choice
prompt: In the sentence "The exhausted runner sat down and breathed hard after the race," which words best help you understand exhausted?
choices:
  - sat down and breathed hard
  - the runner
  - after the race
  - the sentence
correctAnswer: sat down and breathed hard
explanation: Sitting down and breathing hard are clues that exhausted means very tired.
hint: Choose the words that show how the runner felt.
```

```question
key: u01_l01_q05_fit_check
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the meaning that fits the sentence.
sentenceBefore: The fragile cup cracked when it fell, so fragile means
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - easy to break
  - hard to find
  - full of water
  - very loud
correctAnswer: easy to break
explanation: The cup cracked when it fell, so fragile means easy to break.
hint: Ask which meaning explains why the cup cracked.
```
