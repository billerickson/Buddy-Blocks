## Teaching Goal

Teach students to use examples to infer a category or general meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify examples in a sentence and use them to choose a reasonable meaning.

## Misconception Checks

Treating one example as the full definition; missing example clue words; choosing a meaning that fits only one example but not all.

## Teaching Note

Use a familiar sentence such as "Utensils, such as forks, spoons, and chopsticks, help people eat."

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_example_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The basket held citrus fruits, such as oranges, lemons, and limes." What does citrus mean?'
choices:
  - A kind of fruit
  - A kind of basket
  - A color of cloth
  - A cooking tool
correctAnswer: A kind of fruit
explanation: Oranges, lemons, and limes are examples of citrus fruits.
hint: Use the examples after such as.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q02_example_signal
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the example clue signal.
sentenceBefore: Protective gear,
sentenceAfter: helmets and knee pads, helps keep skaters safe.
choices:
  - including
  - although
  - instead
  - because
correctAnswer: including
explanation: Including signals that helmets and knee pads are examples of protective gear.
hint: Which word tells you examples are coming?
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q03_match_examples
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each target word to the example clue that would fit it.
pairs:
  - left: instruments
    right: drums, flutes, and guitars
  - left: vehicles
    right: buses, bikes, and trains
  - left: mammals
    right: dogs, whales, and bats
  - left: landforms
    right: hills, valleys, and mountains
explanation: Examples help reveal the group or category meaning.
hint: Ask what group all the examples belong to.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q04_too_narrow
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "Maria packed utensils, such as a fork, spoon, and chopsticks." Which meaning is best for utensils?'
choices:
  - Tools used for eating
  - Only forks
  - Food packed for lunch
  - Bags with pockets
correctAnswer: Tools used for eating
explanation: The examples are all eating tools, so the meaning should fit all of them.
hint: Do not choose a meaning that fits only one example.
```

```question
  - A group of animals
  - A kind of grass

  - A hiding place

```
