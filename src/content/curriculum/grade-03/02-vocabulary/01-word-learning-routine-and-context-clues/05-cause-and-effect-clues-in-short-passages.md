## Teaching Goal

Teach students to use cause/effect relationships across short passages to infer word meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can use one or two nearby sentences to explain why a word means what it does.

## Misconception Checks

Looking only inside the target sentence; confusing cause with effect; choosing a meaning that fits one word but not the whole passage.

## Teaching Note

Use a short science-style passage where an observable result reveals the target word, such as a plant `wilted` after days without water.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l05_q01_effect_clue
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and answer.
passageTitle: Dry Plant
passage: No one watered the classroom plant during vacation. When the class returned, the leaves had wilted and hung low.
question: What does wilted most likely mean?
choices:
  - Drooped from lack of water
  - Grew taller overnight
  - Turned into flowers
  - Made a loud sound
correctAnswer: Drooped from lack of water
explanation: The cause is no water, and the effect is leaves hanging low.
hint: Ask what happened because the plant had no water.
```

```question
key: u01_l05_q02_cause_effect_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the events in cause-and-effect order.
items:
  - The sidewalk became slick.
  - Rain froze overnight.
  - Students walked carefully.
correctOrder:
  - Rain froze overnight.
  - The sidewalk became slick.
  - Students walked carefully.
explanation: The frozen rain caused the sidewalk to become slick, which caused students to walk carefully.
hint: Start with what happened first and caused the problem.
```

```question
key: u01_l05_q03_cause_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The baby rubbed her eyes and yawned, so she was drowsy." What does drowsy mean?'
choices:
  - Sleepy
  - Angry
  - Very loud
  - Ready to eat
correctAnswer: Sleepy
explanation: Rubbing eyes and yawning are clues that drowsy means sleepy.
hint: Think about why someone yawns.
```

```question
key: u01_l05_q04_find_effect_clue
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The sink was left on for a long time. Water overflowed. A puddle spread across the floor." Which effect clue best helps explain overflowed?'
choices:
  - A puddle spread across the floor.
  - The sink was left on for a long time.
  - Water overflowed.
  - The water went down the drain.
correctAnswer: A puddle spread across the floor.
explanation: The puddle shows the effect of water spilling over the sink.
hint: Look for what happened because the water overflowed.
```

```question
key: u01_l05_q05_context_not_full_definition
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "Lena was famished, so she ate two sandwiches and an apple after soccer." What can the context tell you about famished?'
choices:
  - It means very hungry.
  - It means she played soccer.
  - It gives no clue at all.
  - It means she dislikes apples.
correctAnswer: It means very hungry.
explanation: Eating a lot after soccer shows that famished means very hungry.
hint: "Use the effect: she ate a large snack."
```

```question
key: u01_l05_q06_explain_cause_effect
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the cause/effect explanation for "The ice cream melted in the sun, so it became runny."
parts:
  - "Runny means "
  - ". The cause was "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: thin and liquid
    choices:
      - thin and liquid
      - frozen solid
      - full of crumbs
  - correctAnswer: the ice cream melted in the sun
    choices:
      - the ice cream melted in the sun
      - the ice cream was in a bowl
      - the sentence used so
explanation: The cause, melting in the sun, helps explain the effect, runny ice cream.
hint: Choose what runny means, then choose what caused it.
```
