## Teaching Goal

Teach students to use contrast words and opposite ideas as clues to unfamiliar meanings.

## Student Outcome

The student can infer a word meaning when a sentence contrasts it with a familiar word or idea.

## Misconception Checks

Ignoring contrast words; choosing the same meaning as the familiar word; treating every but sentence as a direct opposite.

## Teaching Note

Compare "The first trail was smooth, but the second trail was rugged" and ask how but changes the meaning.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l04_q01_contrast_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The first path was smooth, but the second path was rugged." What does rugged most likely mean?'
choices:
  - Rough or uneven
  - Flat and easy
  - Painted blue
  - Very short
correctAnswer: Rough or uneven
explanation: But signals a contrast with smooth, so rugged means rough or uneven.
hint: Use the word but to look for an opposite idea.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q02_contrast_signal
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the contrast signal word.
sentenceBefore: The desert is dry,
sentenceAfter: the rainforest is damp.
choices:
  - while
  - because
  - such as
  - called
correctAnswer: while
explanation: While can show a contrast between two ideas.
hint: Which word shows the two places are different?
```

```question

    right: not new
  - left: timid
    right: not bold

    right: not shrink
explanation: Contrast clues often point to an opposite or different idea.
hint: Use the word not to find the contrast.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q04_contrast_passage
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer.
passageTitle: Two Kittens
passage: Unlike her playful brother, the shy kitten stayed under the couch.
question: What does shy most likely mean?
choices:
  - Nervous around others
  - Ready to jump and play
  - Hungry for dinner
  - Covered in spots
correctAnswer: Nervous around others
explanation: Unlike shows a contrast between playful and shy.
hint: The word unlike tells you the kitten is different from her brother.
```
