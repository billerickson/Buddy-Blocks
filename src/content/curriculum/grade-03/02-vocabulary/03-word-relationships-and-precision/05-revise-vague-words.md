## Teaching Goal

Teach students to replace vague words with more precise vocabulary in short sentences.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose or produce a clearer word that improves a sentence while keeping the meaning accurate.

## Misconception Checks

Using a fancy word even if it does not fit; changing the meaning while revising; thinking precise words must be long.

## Teaching Note

Compare "The dog went across the yard" with "The dog dashed across the yard" and ask what changed.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l05_q01_precise_revision
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Choose the best revision for the vague word went: "The rabbit went across the grass."'
choices:
  - hopped
  - thought
  - slept
  - was
correctAnswer: hopped
explanation: Hopped gives a clearer action for a rabbit than went.
hint: Choose a word that shows how a rabbit moves.
```

```question
key: u03_l05_q02_error_correction_vague
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which revision replaces big with a more precise sound word?
choices:
  - The thunder made a booming sound.
  - The thunder made a tall sound.
  - The thunder made a big sound.
  - The thunder made a blue sound.
correctAnswer: The thunder made a booming sound.
explanation: Loud, booming, or rumbling is more precise than big for a sound.
hint: Use a word that describes sound, not size.
```

```question
key: u03_l05_q03_best_word_fit
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the precise word.
sentenceBefore: The soup was too
sentenceAfter: ", so I waited before taking another bite."
choices:
  - scalding
  - tiny
  - silent
  - curly
correctAnswer: scalding
explanation: Scalding means very hot, which explains why the speaker waited.
hint: Choose the word that fits soup you cannot eat yet.
```

```question
key: u03_l05_q04_revision_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each vague phrase to a more precise phrase.
pairs:
  - left: went quickly
    right: raced
  - left: said softly
    right: whispered
  - left: looked for a long time
    right: stared
  - left: ate quickly
    right: gobbled
explanation: Precise words can replace vague word groups with clearer meaning.
hint: Match by the action and how it is done.
```

```question
key: u03_l05_q05_revise_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the race sentence and choose a precise verb.
passageTitle: The Race
passage: When the whistle blew, Ana moved very fast toward the finish line.
question: Which word could replace moved very fast?
choices:
  - sprinted
  - napped
  - balanced
  - wondered
correctAnswer: sprinted
explanation: Sprinted means ran very fast, which fits a race.
hint: Use the finish line clue.
```
