## Teaching Goal

Teach students to compare related words by strength, feeling, or precision.

## Student Outcome

The student can order or choose near-synonyms based on intensity and sentence fit.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking all synonyms are equal; always choosing the strongest word; ignoring clues about feeling or amount.

## Teaching Note

Build a word line with `walk`, `hurry`, `race` and act or imagine the difference.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l04_q01_intensity_order
type: order-items
prompt: Order the words from weakest to strongest.
items:
  - scorching
  - warm
  - hot
correctOrder:
  - warm
  - hot
  - scorching
explanation: Warm is mild, hot is stronger, and scorching is very hot.
hint: Start with the word that means only a little hot.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q02_best_strength
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word best fits? "The mouse made a ___ sound under the cabinet."
choices:
  - tiny
  - thunderous
  - gigantic
  - ancient
correctAnswer: tiny
explanation: A mouse under a cabinet would likely make a tiny sound, not a thunderous one.
hint: Choose the word whose strength fits a mouse.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q03_shade_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word to its strength.
pairs:
  - left: stroll
    right: walk slowly
  - left: dash
    right: run quickly
  - left: peek
    right: look briefly
  - left: stare
    right: look for a long time
explanation: Shades of meaning show small differences in action or strength.
hint: Think about how the action is done.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q04_feeling_fit
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the word that best fits the feeling.
sentenceBefore: When Sam won the contest, he felt
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - proud
  - damp
  - crooked
  - silent
correctAnswer: proud
explanation: Proud fits the feeling of winning a contest.
hint: Choose a feeling word that matches winning.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q05_shades_passage
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the passage and answer about word strength.
passageTitle: In The Library
passage: The sign said, "Please speak quietly." Jada needed to ask a question, so she whispered to the librarian.
question: Why is whispered a better word than shouted?
choices:
  - It fits the quiet library setting
  - It means the same as shouted
  - It tells Jada was outside
  - It means Jada was angry
correctAnswer: It fits the quiet library setting
explanation: Whispered is a quiet way to speak, so it fits the library.
hint: Use the sign as a clue.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q06_explain_intensity
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which explanation best compares hungry and starving?
choices:
  - Starving is stronger than hungry; it means very hungry.
  - Hungry and starving mean exact opposites.
  - Hungry is stronger than starving.
  - Starving tells how fast someone walks.
correctAnswer: Starving is stronger than hungry; it means very hungry.
explanation: Hungry and starving are related, but starving is more intense.
hint: Use the word stronger in your explanation.
```
