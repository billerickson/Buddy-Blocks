## Teaching Goal

Help students transfer high-utility words across reading, math, science, and social studies contexts.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose and use academic/content words in different subject settings.

## Misconception Checks

A word has the same subject meaning every time; math and science vocabulary do not connect to reading vocabulary; using the word without sentence fit.

## Teaching Note

Track a word like `evidence` across a reading question, a science observation, and a social studies source.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l05_q01_subject_word
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'In math, "estimate the answer" means to:'
choices:
  - make a close and reasonable guess
  - write every detail in a story
  - draw a map
  - copy the exact question
correctAnswer: make a close and reasonable guess
explanation: Estimate means make a close guess, often before or instead of exact calculation.
hint: An estimate is close, not exact.
```

```question
key: u07_l05_q02_cross_subject_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word to a subject use.
pairs:
  - left: evidence
    right: a sentence from a story that supports an answer
  - left: measure
    right: find length or amount in math or science
  - left: map
    right: show where places are in social studies
  - left: observe
    right: look carefully during a science investigation
explanation: Academic words become useful when students know how they work in subjects.
hint: Use the subject clue in each right-side meaning.
```

```question
key: u07_l05_q03_same_word_subject
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "The table shows the number of sunny days each month." What does table mean here?'
choices:
  - a chart with rows and columns
  - furniture for eating
  - a kind of weather
  - a map of a city
correctAnswer: a chart with rows and columns
explanation: In a data sentence, table means information arranged in rows and columns.
hint: Use the clue shows the number.
```

```question
key: u07_l05_q04_subject_passage
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the science notebook and choose the meaning.
passageTitle: Science Notebook
passage: We observed the mealworms for five minutes. Then we recorded that they moved away from the light.
question: What does observed mean in this science context?
choices:
  - watched carefully
  - guessed quickly
  - measured with a ruler
  - drew a map
correctAnswer: watched carefully
explanation: Observed means watched carefully to notice what the mealworms did.
hint: Use the science notebook context.
```

```question
key: u07_l05_q05_academic_stem
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the academic sentence stem.
parts:
  - "My "
  - " is that the plant needs water. My "
  - " is the drooping leaves."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: inference
    choices:
      - inference
      - caption
      - sequence
  - correctAnswer: evidence
    choices:
      - evidence
      - map
      - estimate
explanation: The inference is the idea, and the evidence is the clue that supports it.
hint: One blank names the idea; the other names the clue.
```
