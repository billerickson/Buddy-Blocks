## Teaching Goal

Teach academic words that connect a claim or answer to text clues.

## Student Outcome

The student can use words such as infer, evidence, support, clue, and because in short reading responses.

## Misconception Checks

Evidence is any sentence nearby; inference means wild guess; support means agreeing without a clue.

## Teaching Note

Use a two-sentence mini-passage and model "I infer ___ because the text says ___."

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l02_q01_evidence_meaning
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What is evidence in a reading answer?
choices:
  - a clue from the text that supports an answer
  - a random sentence nearby
  - a guess with no reason
  - the title of any book
correctAnswer: a clue from the text that supports an answer
explanation: Evidence supports an answer with something from the text.
hint: Evidence should help prove the answer.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q02_inference_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the clues and choose the inference.
passageTitle: Rainy Morning
passage: Jules grabbed an umbrella before walking outside. Dark clouds covered the sky.
question: Which inference is best supported?
choices:
  - Jules thinks it may rain
  - Jules is going swimming
  - Jules lost the umbrella
  - Jules dislikes clouds
correctAnswer: Jules thinks it may rain
explanation: The umbrella and dark clouds support the inference that it may rain.
hint: "Use both clues: umbrella and dark clouds."
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q03_sentence_stem
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the evidence sentence.
parts:
  - "I infer that Jules expects rain because the text says "
  - " and "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: he grabbed an umbrella
    choices:
      - he grabbed an umbrella
      - he ate lunch
      - he rode a bike
  - correctAnswer: dark clouds covered the sky
    choices:
      - dark clouds covered the sky
      - the sun was bright
      - the grass was dry
explanation: Both clues support the inference about rain.
hint: Choose the clues that point to rain.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q04_support_match

  - left: infer
    right: figure out using clues
  - left: evidence
    right: proof or clue
  - left: support
    right: help prove an answer
  - left: because
    right: shows a reason

```

```question
key: u07_l02_q05_best_evidence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Claim: "The soup is hot." Which sentence is the best evidence?'

  - Steam rose from the bowl.
  - The spoon was silver.
  - The table was round.
  - Lunch was at noon.

hint: Choose the sentence that proves the claim.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q06_inference_explain
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "Mia zipped her coat and pulled on mittens before recess." Which inference uses evidence?'
choices:
  - It is cold because Mia zipped her coat and wore mittens.
  - It is hot because Mia went to recess.
  - Mia is hungry because she has mittens.
  - Mia dislikes school because she has a coat.
correctAnswer: It is cold because Mia zipped her coat and wore mittens.
explanation: The coat and mittens support the inference that the weather is cold.
hint: "Use the frame: I infer ___ because ___."
```
