## Teaching Goal

Teach relationship words that organize informational reading and subject-area tasks.

## Student Outcome

The student can use cause, effect, sequence, classify, and related words to understand short contexts.

## Misconception Checks

Confusing cause and effect; thinking sequence means importance; classifying by appearance rather than shared feature.

## Teaching Note

Use a seed-growing sequence and ask which sentence names the cause, effect, and order.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l04_q01_relation_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each academic word to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: cause
    right: why something happens
  - left: effect
    right: what happens as a result
  - left: sequence
    right: order of events
  - left: classify
    right: sort into groups
explanation: These words name relationships among ideas, events, or objects.
hint: Think about why, result, order, and sort.
```

```question
key: u07_l04_q02_cause_effect
type: multiple-choice
prompt: 'Read: "Because the sidewalk was icy, Lena walked slowly." What is the cause?'
choices:
  - the sidewalk was icy
  - Lena walked slowly
  - Lena wore a hat
  - the school was open
correctAnswer: the sidewalk was icy
explanation: The icy sidewalk caused Lena to walk slowly.
hint: The cause tells why something happened.
```

```question
key: u07_l04_q03_effect_choice
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the effect.
sentenceBefore: The vase fell, so the effect was that it
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - broke
  - was tall
  - sat on a shelf
  - held flowers earlier
correctAnswer: broke
explanation: Broke is what happened as a result of the vase falling.
hint: The effect happens because of the fall.
```

```question
key: u07_l04_q04_sequence_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the sequence in order.
items:
  - The seed sprouted.
  - The plant grew leaves.
  - The seed was planted.
  - The flower opened.
correctOrder:
  - The seed was planted.
  - The seed sprouted.
  - The plant grew leaves.
  - The flower opened.
explanation: Sequence means the order in which events happen.
hint: Start with what happens to the seed first.
```
