## Teaching Goal

Separate personal preference and feeling from claims that can be supported.

## Student Outcome

The student can classify a sentence as an opinion/preference, a feeling, or a supportable claim.

## Key Ideas

A claim says something someone can try to support; a preference or feeling may be real without needing proof.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking every opinion needs evidence; treating feelings as false because they are not provable; assuming "I think" always makes a sentence weak.

## Teaching Note

Contrast "I feel nervous," "I like graphic novels," and "Graphic novels can help readers understand a story."

prompt: Which sentence is a claim someone could support with reasons or evidence?

  - Our class should read outside today.
  - I feel tired today.
  - I like blue notebooks best.
  - Wow, that is bright!

explanation: Someone could give reasons for or against reading outside today.
hint: Look for the sentence that someone could try to prove or support.
