## Teaching Goal

Assess cumulative Grammar 1 transfer in compact mixed editing and explanation tasks.

## Student Outcome

The student can apply sentence, word, and mechanics knowledge to a short mixed review and explain selected choices.

## Misconception Checks

Editing mechanically without reading meaning; fixing only punctuation while ignoring word forms; changing correct sentences because a mixed set feels suspicious.

## Teaching Note

Use a compact student-writing-style passage and model a checklist: sentence, word job, word form, capitalization, punctuation.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l03_q01_capstone_target
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the draft and answer.
passageTitle: Garden Draft
passage: |
  on Sunday, maya planted seeds. The seeds growed near the fence.
question: Which two targets need attention?
choices:
  - capitalization and verb form
  - dialogue and quotation marks
  - commas in a series and superlatives
  - pronoun reference and ownership
correctAnswer: capitalization and verb form
explanation: On and Maya need capitals, and growed should be grew.
hint: Check sentence beginnings, names, and past-tense verbs.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q02_capstone_sentence_fix
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the sentence.
sentence: on Tuesday, liam said, I found three leafs.
acceptedAnswers:
  - On Tuesday, Liam said, "I found three leaves."
explanation: Capitalize the sentence beginning, day, and name; use quotation marks for exact words; and change leafs to leaves.
hint: Check capitals, dialogue marks, and the plural noun.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q03_capstone_cloze
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Complete the edited sentence.
parts:
  - "Yesterday, the "
  - " "
  - " quietly beside the pond."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: children
    choices:
      - childs
      - children
      - child's
  - correctAnswer: sat
    choices:
      - sits
      - sat
      - will sit
explanation: Children is the plural of child, and yesterday needs the past-tense verb sat.
hint: Use the plural noun and match the verb to yesterday.
```


```question
key: u07_l03_q05_explain_capstone_edit
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which correction fits one boy owning the lunch: the boys lunch spilled."
choices:
  - The boy's lunch spilled.
  - The boys' lunch spilled.
  - The boys lunch spilled.
  - the boy's lunch spilled.
correctAnswer: The boy's lunch spilled.
explanation: A capstone edit should fix the target and explain why the correction fits the meaning.
hint: Show that the lunch belongs to one boy.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q06_best_final_revision
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the draft and choose the best final revision.
passageTitle: Final Check
passage: |
  sara packed apples crackers and cheese. She was hungry because the hike was long.
question: Which revision is best?
choices:
  - Sara packed apples, crackers, and cheese. She was hungry because the hike was long.
  - sara packed apples crackers and cheese. she was hungry because the hike was long.
  - Sara packed apples, crackers and cheese she was hungry because the hike was long.
  - Sara packed apples, crackers, and cheese. Hungry because the hike was long.
correctAnswer: Sara packed apples, crackers, and cheese. She was hungry because the hike was long.
explanation: The best revision fixes capitalization and the series commas while keeping the second sentence complete.
hint: Check the name, sentence beginning, list commas, and complete thoughts.
```
