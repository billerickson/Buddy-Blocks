## Teaching Goal

Teach students to choose and apply a specific editing target instead of trying to fix everything at once.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify the likely grammar target in a short sentence or passage and make the targeted correction.

## Misconception Checks

Guessing the newest rule; changing meaning while editing; overcorrecting sentences that are already correct.

## Teaching Note

Give a short sentence and three possible targets; model checking only one target at a time.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l01_q01_choose_target
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the draft and choose the best editing target.
passageTitle: One Target
passage: |
  last Saturday, I helped Dad wash the car.
question: What should you check first?
choices:
  - capitalization
  - plural nouns
  - dialogue punctuation
  - comparative adjectives
correctAnswer: capitalization
explanation: The sentence begins with a lowercase word, and Saturday and Dad need capitalization.
hint: Look for lowercase words that should be capitals.
```

```question
key: u07_l01_q02_error_type
type: multiple-choice
prompt: What kind of error is in this sentence? The girl run to the bus.
choices:
  - subject-verb agreement
  - comma in a series
  - proper noun capitalization
  - quotation marks
correctAnswer: subject-verb agreement
explanation: The singular subject girl needs the verb runs.
hint: Find the subject and verb.
```

```question
key: u07_l01_q03_focused_fix
type: error-correction
prompt: Edit only the plural noun error.
sentence: The three childs carried lunch boxes.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The three children carried lunch boxes.
explanation: Children is the correct plural of child. No other change is needed.
hint: Find the noun that means more than one child.
```

```question
key: u07_l01_q04_already_correct
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which sentence is already correct for the target: end punctuation?"
choices:
  - Where is my pencil.
  - Please close the window.
  - What a loud sound?
  - Did you finish your drawing.
correctAnswer: Please close the window.
explanation: This calm command is complete and correctly ends with a period.
hint: The target is only end punctuation.
```

```question
key: u07_l01_q05_target_and_fix
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Name the target and choose the correction.
parts:
  - "Sentence: The dogs bowl is empty. The target is "
  - ", and the corrected phrase is "
  - .
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: possessive
    choices:
      - possessive
      - future tense
      - dialogue
  - correctAnswer: dog's bowl
    choices:
      - dog's bowl
      - dogs bowl's
      - dogs' bowled
explanation: The bowl belongs to one dog, so dog's bowl shows ownership.
hint: Ask whose bowl it is.
```

```question
key: u07_l01_q06_explain_edit
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which correction and explanation fit: i saw three mouse."
choices:
  - I saw three mice. Capitalize I and use the plural mice.
  - i saw three mice. Only the noun needs fixing.
  - I saw three mouse's. Add an apostrophe because mouse ends with s.
  - I saw three mouse. No change is needed.
correctAnswer: I saw three mice. Capitalize I and use the plural mice.
explanation: Focused editing means naming what you changed and why.
hint: You can fix capitalization, plural form, or both.
```
