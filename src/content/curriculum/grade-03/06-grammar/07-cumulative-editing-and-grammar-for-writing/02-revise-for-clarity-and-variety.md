## Teaching Goal

Teach revision as improving clarity, detail, and sentence variety rather than merely lengthening.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose or produce a revision that improves a sentence while preserving meaning.

## Misconception Checks

The longest revision is best; adding details always helps; combining sentences automatically improves them.

## Teaching Note

Compare three revisions of the same base sentence and discuss clarity, precision, and meaning.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l02_q01_best_revision
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which revision is clearest? The dog ran. The dog chased the ball.
choices:
  - The dog ran the dog chased the ball.
  - The dog ran, and it chased the ball.
  - Because the dog ran.
  - The dog ran, it chased the ball.
correctAnswer: The dog ran, and it chased the ball.
explanation: This combines the related ideas without creating a run-on.
hint: Choose the revision that keeps both ideas and has a clear connector.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q02_write_revision
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which revision adds a useful where detail: The children read quietly."
choices:
  - The children read quietly under the oak tree.
  - The children read very quietly.
  - The children read quietly.
  - The children read quietly and carefully.
correctAnswer: The children read quietly under the oak tree.
explanation: A useful where detail makes the sentence more specific without changing its meaning.
hint: Try a phrase beginning with in, under, near, beside, or at.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q03_order_varied_sentence
type: order-items
prompt: Put the parts in order to make a clear revised sentence.
items:
  - before breakfast.
  - The sleepy cat
  - stretched on the rug
correctOrder:
  - The sleepy cat
  - stretched on the rug
  - before breakfast.
explanation: The sentence starts with the subject, then the predicate, then a time detail.
hint: Build the complete sentence core first.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q04_fix_faulty_revision
type: error-correction
prompt: Fix this faulty revision.
sentence: The storm was loud it shook the windows.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The storm was loud, and it shook the windows.
  - The storm was loud. It shook the windows.
explanation: The revision needs a clear boundary between the two complete thoughts.
hint: Find where the first complete thought ends.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q05_revision_strategy
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the draft and answer.
passageTitle: Choppy Draft
passage: |
  The class planted beans.
  The class watered them.
question: Which revision strategy would reduce repetition?
choices:
  - Combine the ideas and use a pronoun clearly
  - Add a describing word to beans
  - Repeat The class at the start of both sentences
  - Add a where phrase to only the first sentence
correctAnswer: Combine the ideas and use a pronoun clearly
explanation: The class planted beans and watered them keeps the meaning and reduces repetition.
hint: Look for repeated words and related ideas.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q06_precise_word
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the precise word that improves the sentence.
sentenceBefore: The soup smelled
sentenceAfter: after an hour on the stove.
choices:
  - savory
  - thing
  - under
  - quickly
correctAnswer: savory
explanation: Savory is a precise adjective that describes the smell of the soup.
hint: The blank should describe what kind of smell.
```
