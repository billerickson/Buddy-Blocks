## Teaching Goal

Teach students to repair incomplete and overconnected sentences in straightforward cases.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a fragment or run-on and choose a correction that creates one or two complete sentences.

## Misconception Checks

Fixing a fragment by adding punctuation only; fixing a run-on with a comma alone; combining ideas without checking each complete thought.

## Teaching Note

Place `The rain stopped we went outside.` beside `The rain stopped. We went outside.` and ask what helped the reader.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l04_q01_identify_fragment
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word group is a fragment?
choices:
  - The thunder rumbled.
  - Ava opened her umbrella.
  - Because the sidewalk was wet.
  - The bus arrived late.
correctAnswer: Because the sidewalk was wet.
explanation: This word group gives a reason, but it does not finish the complete thought.
hint: Ask whether the words can stand alone as a complete idea.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q02_identify_run_on
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence is a run-on?
choices:
  - The timer rang, and we washed our hands.
  - The timer rang we washed our hands.
  - When the timer rang, we washed our hands.
  - The timer rang.
correctAnswer: The timer rang we washed our hands.
explanation: Two complete thoughts are pushed together without correct punctuation or a connector.
hint: Find whether two complete thoughts are stuck together.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q03_fix_run_on
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct this run-on sentence.
sentence: The rain stopped we went outside.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The rain stopped. We went outside.
  - The rain stopped, and we went outside.
explanation: The two complete thoughts need to be separated or joined with a comma and conjunction.
hint: Find the two complete thoughts before you choose the fix.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q04_fix_because_fragment
type: error-correction
prompt: Make this fragment into a complete sentence.
sentence: Because the puppy was tired.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The puppy slept because it was tired.
  - Because the puppy was tired, it slept.
  - Because the puppy was tired, it rested.
explanation: The correction completes the idea by telling what happened because the puppy was tired.
hint: A because part gives a reason. Add the main idea.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q05_repair_steps
type: order-items
prompt: Put the run-on repair steps in order.
items:
  - Choose punctuation or a connector
  - Find the two complete thoughts
  - Read the sentence again
  - Check that the meaning stayed the same
correctOrder:
  - Find the two complete thoughts
  - Choose punctuation or a connector
  - Check that the meaning stayed the same
  - Read the sentence again
explanation: A run-on fix starts by finding the thoughts that need a clear boundary.
hint: Before fixing, you need to know where the complete thoughts are.
```

```question
key: u01_l04_q06_comma_not_enough
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Why is this not a good run-on fix: The bell rang, we lined up."
choices:
  - A comma by itself does not correctly join two complete thoughts.
  - A comma can join any two complete thoughts by itself.
  - The sentence is wrong because it has only one complete thought.
  - The sentence needs no punctuation at all.
correctAnswer: A comma by itself does not correctly join two complete thoughts.
explanation: Two complete thoughts need a period or a comma with a conjunction such as and.
hint: Ask what separates the two complete thoughts.
```
