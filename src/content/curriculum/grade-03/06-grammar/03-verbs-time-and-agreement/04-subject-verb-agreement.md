## Teaching Goal

Teach simple agreement between subjects and present-tense verbs.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose a verb that fits a singular or plural subject in clear sentences.

## Misconception Checks

Matching the verb to the closest noun; omitting `s` with singular present-tense verbs; adding `s` with plural subjects.

## Teaching Note

Circle the subject first, then test the verb aloud: `The bird sings`; `The birds sing`.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l04_q01_singular_agreement
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the verb that agrees with the subject.
sentenceBefore: The bird
sentenceAfter: every morning.
choices:
  - sing
  - sings
  - are singing
  - were sing
correctAnswer: sings
explanation: The singular subject bird agrees with sings.
hint: Check whether the subject names one bird or more than one bird.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q02_plural_agreement
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence has subject-verb agreement?
choices:
  - The dogs runs fast.
  - The dogs run fast.
  - The dog run fast.
  - The dog are fast.
correctAnswer: The dogs run fast.
explanation: Dogs is plural, so run agrees with it.
hint: Find the subject first, then choose the verb that fits.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q03_fix_agreement
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the subject-verb agreement.
sentence: The kittens sleeps in the basket.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The kittens sleep in the basket.
explanation: Kittens is plural, so the verb should be sleep.
hint: More than one kitten needs the plural verb form.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q04_agreement_pairs
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Choose the verbs that agree.
parts:
  - "The apple "
  - " red, and the apples "
  - " red."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: is
    choices:
      - is
      - are
      - were
  - correctAnswer: are
    choices:
      - is
      - are
      - was
explanation: "One apple is red. The plural subject apples uses are: the apples are red."
hint: Check one noun, then more than one noun.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q05_find_subject_first
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer.
passageTitle: Subject First
passage: |
  The two kittens are asleep beside the chair.
question: Why is are the correct verb?
choices:
  - The subject kittens is plural.
  - The word chair is singular.
  - The sentence has a period.
  - The word asleep is an adjective.
correctAnswer: The subject kittens is plural.
explanation: The verb agrees with the subject kittens, not with another noun later in the sentence.
hint: Find who or what the sentence is about.
```

```question
key: u03_l04_q06_explain_agreement
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why is The flowers bloom correct but The flowers blooms is not?
choices:
  - Flowers is plural, so it agrees with the base verb bloom.
  - Flowers is singular, so it needs blooms.
  - Plural subjects always need verbs ending in s.
  - Both are wrong because flowers cannot be a subject.
correctAnswer: Flowers is plural, so it agrees with the base verb bloom.
explanation: In present tense, plural subjects usually use the base verb form.
hint: Compare one flower blooms with many flowers bloom.
```
