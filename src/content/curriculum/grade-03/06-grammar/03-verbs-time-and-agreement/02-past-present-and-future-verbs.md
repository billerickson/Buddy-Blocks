## Teaching Goal

Connect verb forms and time words to past, present, and future meaning.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose or identify verb forms that show when an action happens.

## Misconception Checks

Using present forms with past time words; assuming future time always needs `will` plus any verb form; ignoring context clues.

## Teaching Note

Build a three-column timeline: yesterday, today, tomorrow.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l02_q01_past_time
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the verb that fits the time clue.
sentenceBefore: Yesterday, we
sentenceAfter: to the park.
choices:
  - walk
  - walked
  - will walk
  - walking
correctAnswer: walked
explanation: Yesterday is a past-time clue, so walked fits.
hint: Find the time clue before choosing the verb.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q02_time_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the verb-time labels in order from earliest to latest.
items:
  - "future: will play"
  - "present: play"
  - "past: played"
correctOrder:
  - "past: played"
  - "present: play"
  - "future: will play"
explanation: Past happened before now, present happens now, and future happens later.
hint: Think yesterday, today, tomorrow.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q03_identify_tense
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence tells about the future?
choices:
  - Nora packed her bag.
  - Nora packs her bag.
  - Nora will pack her bag.
  - Nora is a careful packer.
correctAnswer: Nora will pack her bag.
explanation: Will pack tells about something that has not happened yet.
hint: Look for the future helper will.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q04_fix_time_mismatch
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which correction matches the future-time clue?
choices:
  - Tomorrow, we will visit the science museum.
  - Tomorrow, we visited the science museum.
  - Yesterday, we visited the science museum.
  - Tomorrow, we had visited the science museum.
correctAnswer: Tomorrow, we will visit the science museum.
explanation: Tomorrow is a future-time clue, so will visit fits better than visited.
hint: The trip has not happened yet.
```
