## Teaching Goal

Teach adjective forms for comparing two or more nouns.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose comparative and superlative forms in common cases.

## Misconception Checks

Using `most` for two things; using both `more` and `-er`; using `gooder` or `bestest`.

## Teaching Note

Place two pencils and then three pencils in order to show how comparison changes.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l02_q01_two_compare
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the adjective form for comparing two.
sentenceBefore: This hill is
sentenceAfter: than that hill.
choices:
  - steep
  - steeper
  - steepest
  - most steepest
correctAnswer: steeper
explanation: Use steeper when comparing two hills.
hint: Two things usually use the comparative form.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q02_three_compare
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence correctly compares one runner among three?
choices:
  - Luis is faster of the three runners.
  - Luis is fastest of the three runners.
  - Luis is the fastest of the three runners.
  - Luis is more faster of the three runners.
correctAnswer: Luis is the fastest of the three runners.
explanation: For one among three or more, use the superlative form the fastest.
hint: Three runners means one is the fastest.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q03_match_forms
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each base adjective to a correct comparison form.
pairs:
  - left: small
    right: smaller
  - left: careful
    right: more careful
  - left: good
    right: better
  - left: happy
    right: happiest
explanation: Different adjectives use different comparison forms, and some common ones are irregular.
hint: Say each form in a sentence to check whether it sounds standard.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q04_fix_double_comparison
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the comparison form.
sentence: This puzzle is more easier than the last one.
acceptedAnswers:
  - This puzzle is easier than the last one.
explanation: Use easier, not more easier. Do not use both more and -er.
hint: Choose one comparison form.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q05_best_or_better
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the adjective form.
sentenceBefore: Of the two plans, this one is
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - good
  - better
  - best
  - bestest
correctAnswer: better
explanation: Use better when comparing two.
hint: Count how many plans are being compared before choosing the form.
```

```question
key: u04_l02_q06_explain_superlative
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why does tallest fit in Mina is the tallest of the four students?
choices:
  - Tallest compares one student with a group of four students.
  - Tallest is used only when comparing exactly two people.
  - Tallest fits because Mina is a proper noun.
  - Tallest should be changed to taller because there are four students.
correctAnswer: Tallest compares one student with a group of four students.
explanation: Use a superlative form when one is compared with a group of three or more.
hint: Count how many students are being compared.
```
