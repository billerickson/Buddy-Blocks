## Teaching Goal

Teach adverbs as words that tell how, when, or where about verbs in clear examples.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify adverbs, tell what verb they modify, and choose an adverb that fits the action.

## Misconception Checks

Believing all adverbs end in `-ly`; using adjectives in adverb jobs; confusing where-phrases with single-word adverbs in first exposure.

## Teaching Note

Compare `The turtle moved slowly` with `The slow turtle moved` to show different modifier jobs.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l03_q01_find_adverb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word is the adverb in this sentence? The snail moved slowly.
choices:
  - snail
  - moved
  - slowly
  - The
correctAnswer: slowly
explanation: Slowly tells how the snail moved.
hint: Ask how the action happened.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q02_how_when_where
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each adverb to the detail it adds.
pairs:
  - left: carefully
    right: how the action is done
  - left: today
    right: when the action happens
  - left: nearby
    right: where the action happens
  - left: loudly
    right: how the action sounds
explanation: Adverbs can tell how, when, or where about a verb.
hint: Put the adverb after an action and ask what detail it adds.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q04_fix_adverb
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the modifier.
sentence: The runner moved quick around the track.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The runner moved quickly around the track.
explanation: Quickly is the adverb form that tells how the runner moved.
hint: You need a word that describes the action moved.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q05_adjective_or_adverb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses slow as an adjective?
choices:
  - The turtle walked slowly.
  - The slow turtle crossed the path.
  - The turtle crossed slowly.
  - Slowly, the turtle moved.
correctAnswer: The slow turtle crossed the path.
explanation: Slow describes the noun turtle, so it is an adjective in that sentence.
hint: Find whether the word describes a noun or an action.
```

```question
key: u04_l03_q06_add_adverb
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which sentence adds an adverb and tells what it adds: The dog barked."
choices:
  - The dog barked loudly. Loudly tells how the dog barked.
  - The brown dog barked. Brownly tells how the dog barked.
  - The dog barked at the gate. Gate tells how the dog barked.
  - The dog barked. No detail was added.
correctAnswer: The dog barked loudly. Loudly tells how the dog barked.
explanation: A useful adverb adds action detail without changing the sentence core.
hint: Try asking how the dog barked.
```
