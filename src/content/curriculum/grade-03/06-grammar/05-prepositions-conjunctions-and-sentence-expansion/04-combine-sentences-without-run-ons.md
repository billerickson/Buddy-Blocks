## Teaching Goal

Teach students to combine related sentences while preserving sentence boundaries.

## Student Outcome

The student can combine two short related sentences with a fitting conjunction or punctuation choice.

## Misconception Checks

Joining sentences with a comma alone; using an unfitting conjunction; changing meaning while combining.

## Teaching Note

Show two choppy sentences and three possible combinations, then ask which one keeps meaning and reads smoothly.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l04_q01_best_combination
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which is the best way to combine these sentences? The sky grew dark. We hurried home.
choices:
  - The sky grew dark we hurried home.
  - The sky grew dark, we hurried home.
  - We hurried home because the sky grew dark.
  - The sky grew dark hurried home.
correctAnswer: We hurried home because the sky grew dark.
explanation: Because connects the action to its reason.
hint: Avoid sticking two complete thoughts together with no proper connector.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q02_fix_combined_run_on
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which correction fixes the run-on?
choices:
  - Because the recipe was simple, I made it after school.
  - The recipe was simple, I made it after school.
  - The recipe was simple I made it after school.
  - Because the recipe was simple.
correctAnswer: Because the recipe was simple, I made it after school.
explanation: The two complete thoughts need a clear boundary or a fitting connector.
hint: Find both complete thoughts before joining them.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q03_order_combined_sentence
type: order-items
prompt: Put the parts in order to make a clear combined sentence.
items:
  - but
  - the library was closed.
  - We wanted new books,
correctOrder:
  - We wanted new books,
  - but
  - the library was closed.
explanation: But connects the two contrasting ideas.
hint: The conjunction should sit between the two ideas.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q04_choose_connector
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the connector that keeps the meaning.
sentenceBefore: Mina wore a jacket
sentenceAfter: the wind was cold.
choices:
  - because
  - or
  - but
  - and
correctAnswer: because
explanation: Because gives the reason Mina wore a jacket.
hint: Ask why Mina wore a jacket.
```

```question
key: u05_l04_q05_combine_production
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence combines the ideas with because?
choices:
  - The team practiced because the game was tomorrow.
  - The team practiced, the game was tomorrow.
  - Because the team practiced. The game was tomorrow.
  - The team practiced, but the game was tomorrow.
correctAnswer: The team practiced because the game was tomorrow.
explanation: Because connects the practice to its reason.
hint: Put because between the action and the reason.
```
