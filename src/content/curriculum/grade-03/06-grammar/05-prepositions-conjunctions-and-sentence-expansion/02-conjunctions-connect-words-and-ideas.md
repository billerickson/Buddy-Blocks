## Teaching Goal

Teach conjunctions as connectors with different meanings.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose `and`, `but`, `or`, or `because` to connect words or simple ideas clearly.

## Misconception Checks

Using `and` for every relationship; using `because` without a complete main idea; treating connectors as punctuation replacements.

## Teaching Note

Compare `I wanted tea, but the kettle was empty` with `I wanted tea because I was cold`.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l02_q01_choose_but
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the conjunction that fits the contrast.
sentenceBefore: I wanted to play outside,
sentenceAfter: it started raining.
choices:
  - and
  - but
  - or
  - because
correctAnswer: but
explanation: But shows a contrast between wanting to play and rain stopping the plan.
hint: Look for a change or contrast between the ideas.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q02_match_conjunction_job
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each conjunction to its job.
pairs:
  - left: and
    right: adds
  - left: but
    right: contrasts
  - left: or
    right: shows a choice
  - left: because
    right: gives a reason
explanation: Different conjunctions show different relationships.
hint: Think about what the connector tells the reader.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q03_relationship_reason
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses because to give a reason?
choices:
  - We packed snacks because the hike was long.
  - We packed snacks but the hike was long.
  - We packed snacks or the hike was long.
  - We packed snacks and the hike was long.
correctAnswer: We packed snacks because the hike was long.
explanation: Because connects the action to the reason for it.
hint: Find the sentence that answers why.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q04_fix_connector
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the conjunction so the sentence makes sense.
sentence: You may read a poem but draw a picture.
acceptedAnswers:
  - You may read a poem or draw a picture.
explanation: Or shows a choice between two activities.
hint: The sentence offers two choices.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q05_connector_cloze
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: Choose connectors that fit each relationship.
parts:
  - "I wore boots "
  - " the trail was muddy. I packed water "
  - " a sandwich."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: because
    choices:
      - because
      - or
      - but
  - correctAnswer: and
    choices:
      - but
      - and
      - because
explanation: Because gives the reason for the boots. And adds water and a sandwich.
hint: First blank answers why; second blank adds another item.
```

```question
key: u05_l02_q06_explain_connector
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why does but fit in The box was small, but it was heavy?
choices:
  - But shows a contrast between small and heavy.
  - But shows that the two ideas mean the same thing.
  - But lists three items in a series.
  - But shows that the box belongs to someone.
correctAnswer: But shows a contrast between small and heavy.
explanation: But prepares the reader for a contrast.
hint: Ask how the second idea is different from what you might expect.
```
