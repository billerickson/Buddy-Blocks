## Teaching Goal

Teach common prepositional phrases as word groups that add location or time information.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a prepositional phrase and explain what where/when detail it adds.

## Misconception Checks

Treating a prepositional phrase as a complete sentence; choosing the noun alone as the phrase; confusing every small word with a preposition.

## Teaching Note

Move an object and describe it: `on the desk`, `under the desk`, `beside the desk`.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l01_q01_find_phrase
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which words make a prepositional phrase in this sentence? The cat slept under the chair.
choices:
  - The cat
  - slept under
  - under the chair
  - the chair slept
correctAnswer: under the chair
explanation: Under begins the phrase, and the chair completes it.
hint: Look for the word that shows where, then include the noun after it.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q02_match_preposition_meaning
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each phrase to the detail it adds.
pairs:
  - left: after lunch
    right: when
  - left: beside the fence
    right: where next to something
  - left: under the blanket
    right: where below something
  - left: during the storm
    right: when something happens
explanation: Prepositional phrases often add where or when information.
hint: Ask whether the phrase tells a place or a time.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q03_complete_phrase
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the preposition that completes the phrase.
sentenceBefore: The shoes were
sentenceAfter: the bed.
choices:
  - under
  - ran
  - soft
  - they
correctAnswer: under
explanation: Under the bed is a prepositional phrase that tells where the shoes were.
hint: The blank should show where.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q04_phrase_fragment
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice makes this phrase fragment a complete sentence?
choices:
  - After the game, we ate dinner.
  - After the game.
  - The after game.
  - After the game and dinner.
correctAnswer: After the game, we ate dinner.
explanation: After the game gives time information, but a complete sentence still needs a main idea.
hint: Add who did something after the game.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q05_where_when_context
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and answer.
passageTitle: Phrase Detail
passage: |
  The hikers rested near the river.
question: What does the phrase near the river tell?
choices:
  - where the hikers rested
  - when the hikers rested
  - who rested
  - what action happened
correctAnswer: where the hikers rested
explanation: Near the river tells the place of the resting.
hint: Ask where did they rest?
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q06_add_phrase
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence adds a where phrase to The backpack sat?
choices:
  - The backpack sat beside the door.
  - Beside the door.
  - The backpack beside the door.
  - The backpack sat quietly.
correctAnswer: The backpack sat beside the door.
explanation: A where phrase such as beside the door adds location to a complete sentence.
hint: Try a phrase that begins with on, under, beside, near, or in.
```
