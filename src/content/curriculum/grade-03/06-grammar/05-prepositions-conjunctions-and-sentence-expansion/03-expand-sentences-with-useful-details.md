## Teaching Goal

Teach students to expand a base sentence with relevant details.

## Student Outcome

The student can add a where, when, how, or describing detail that makes a sentence clearer without making it cluttered.

## Misconception Checks

Adding random details; making fragments by adding only a phrase; thinking the longest sentence is always best.

## Teaching Note

Begin with `The bird sang.` Add where, when, or how details and discuss which one helps most.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l03_q01_best_expansion
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which expansion adds useful detail without changing the meaning? The bird sang.
choices:
  - The bird sang in the maple tree.
  - Because the bird sang in the maple tree.
  - The bird sang because it was a bird.
  - The bird in the maple tree.
correctAnswer: The bird sang in the maple tree.
explanation: This keeps the complete sentence and adds a where detail.
hint: Choose the sentence that is complete and clearer.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q02_order_expanded_sentence
type: order-items
prompt: Put the parts in an order that makes a clear sentence.
items:
  - after dinner
  - The family
  - played a board game
correctOrder:
  - The family
  - played a board game
  - after dinner
explanation: The subject and predicate make the core sentence; after dinner adds a time detail.
hint: Start with who the sentence is about.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q03_add_how_detail
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which expansion adds one how detail: The girl opened the door."
choices:
  - The girl opened the door carefully.
  - The girl opened the blue door.
  - The girl opened the door near the hallway.
  - The girl opened.
correctAnswer: The girl opened the door carefully.
explanation: A how detail tells the reader more about the action.
hint: Ask how she opened the door.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q04_fix_clutter
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which revision removes the repeated clutter?
choices:
  - The tiny kitten slept.
  - The small tiny little kitten slept.
  - The small tiny little kitten slept quietly softly gently.
  - The kitten small tiny little slept.
correctAnswer: The tiny kitten slept.
explanation: One clear adjective is enough here. Too many similar details can clutter the sentence.
hint: Keep one useful describing word.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q05_context_detail
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the sentence and choose the best added detail.
passageTitle: Useful Detail
passage: |
  The class planted seeds.
question: Which detail best tells where?
choices:
  - in the garden bed
  - carefully
  - green
  - after lunch
correctAnswer: in the garden bed
explanation: In the garden bed is a where phrase.
hint: A where detail tells the place.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q06_detail_type
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the detail type.
sentenceBefore: In The horse trotted slowly, slowly tells
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - how
  - where
  - who
  - ownership
correctAnswer: how
explanation: Slowly tells how the horse trotted.
hint: Find the action word, then ask which question slowly answers.
```
