## Teaching Goal

Teach nouns as naming words and distinguish common nouns from specific proper nouns.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify common and proper nouns and capitalize proper nouns in simple sentences.

## Misconception Checks

Capitalizing any important noun; thinking only people are nouns; missing place and idea nouns.

## Teaching Note

Pair `city` with `Dallas`, `girl` with `Maya`, and `month` with `April` to show general versus specific naming.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l01_q01_find_proper_noun
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word is a proper noun in this sentence? On Friday, the class visited the museum.
choices:
  - Friday
  - visited
  - museum
  - the
correctAnswer: Friday
explanation: Friday is the specific name of a day, so it is a proper noun.
hint: Look for a specific name, not a general thing.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q02_match_common_proper
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each common noun to a proper noun example.
pairs:
  - left: city
    right: Houston
  - left: month
    right: October
  - left: girl
    right: Priya
  - left: river
    right: Mississippi River
explanation: A common noun names a general kind. A proper noun names a specific one.
hint: Proper nouns are specific names and usually begin with capital letters.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q03_capitalize_proper
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the capitalization.
sentence: my uncle ben lives near lake michigan.
acceptedAnswers:
  - My uncle Ben lives near Lake Michigan.
explanation: The sentence begins with a capital, and Ben and Lake Michigan are specific names.
hint: Find the sentence beginning and the specific names.
```

```question
key: u02_l01_q04_noun_sort
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which list contains only nouns?
choices:
  - teacher, park, kindness
  - jump, bright, slowly
  - under, the, because
  - happy, louder, careful
correctAnswer: teacher, park, kindness
explanation: Teacher names a person, park names a place, and kindness names an idea.
hint: Nouns can name people, places, things, or ideas.
```
