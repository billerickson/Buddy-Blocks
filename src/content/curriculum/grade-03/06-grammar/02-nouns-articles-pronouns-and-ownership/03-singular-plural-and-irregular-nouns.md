## Teaching Goal

Teach plural nouns as forms that show more than one, including common regular and irregular patterns.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose singular, regular plural, and common irregular plural forms in short contexts.

## Misconception Checks

Adding `s` to every plural; using apostrophes for plurals; using plural determiners with singular nouns.

## Teaching Note

Sort plural patterns by sound and spelling before asking students to type any forms.

## Questions

```question
key: u02_l03_q01_choose_plural
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the plural noun.
sentenceBefore: Three
sentenceAfter: sat on the fence.
choices:
  - cat
  - cats
  - cat's
  - cats'
correctAnswer: cats
explanation: Three means more than one, so the plural noun cats fits. No apostrophe is needed.
hint: Plural means more than one; ownership is not being shown.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q02_match_singular_plural
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each singular noun to its plural form.
pairs:
  - left: box
    right: boxes
  - left: baby
    right: babies
  - left: child
    right: children
  - left: foot
    right: feet
explanation: Some plurals add -s or -es, some change y to ies, and some irregular plurals change another way.
hint: Look for the plural that sounds like standard English.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q03_irregular_plural
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses an irregular plural correctly?
choices:
  - The childs played outside.
  - The children played outside.
  - The childrens played outside.
  - The childes played outside.
correctAnswer: The children played outside.
explanation: Children is the standard plural form of child.
hint: Child does not become childs.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q04_fix_plural_error
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the plural noun.
sentence: Two mouses ran behind the cabinet.
acceptedAnswers:
  - Two mice ran behind the cabinet.
explanation: The plural of mouse is mice.
hint: This noun changes its spelling in the plural.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q05_type_plural
type: text-input
prompt: Type the plural of story.
acceptedAnswers:
  - stories
answerType: text
explanation: "Story changes y to ies in the plural: stories."
hint: For many nouns ending in consonant plus y, change y to ies.
```

```question
key: u02_l03_q06_explain_no_apostrophe
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why does The dogs barked not need an apostrophe?
choices:
  - Dogs means more than one dog; it does not show ownership.
  - Every word ending in s needs an apostrophe.
  - Dogs is a verb, so it cannot use punctuation.
  - The sentence needs an apostrophe because the dogs own barked.
correctAnswer: Dogs means more than one dog; it does not show ownership.
explanation: Plural nouns usually do not use apostrophes. Apostrophes can show ownership.
hint: Ask whether anything belongs to the dogs.
```
