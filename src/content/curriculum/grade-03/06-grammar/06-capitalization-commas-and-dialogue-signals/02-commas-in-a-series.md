## Teaching Goal

Teach commas as separators in lists of three or more.

## Student Outcome

The student can place commas in a simple series and explain that each comma separates list items.

## Misconception Checks

Putting commas in two-item lists; putting commas after every word including after `and`; using a comma wherever the reader pauses.

## Teaching Note

Place list items in boxes, then put commas between boxes, not randomly inside them.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l02_q01_correct_series
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence uses commas correctly in a series?
choices:
  - We packed pencils erasers and paper.
  - We packed pencils, erasers, and paper.
  - We packed pencils, erasers and, paper.
  - We packed, pencils, erasers, and paper.
correctAnswer: We packed pencils, erasers, and paper.
explanation: "Commas separate the three list items: pencils, erasers, and paper."
hint: Put commas between the list items.
```

  - The trail had rocks, roots, and puddles.

```question
key: u06_l02_q03_two_item_no_comma
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence does not need a comma between the listed items?
choices:
  - We saw clouds, rain, and lightning.
  - Bring a notebook and pencil.
  - The flag was red, white, and blue.
  - She drew stars, moons, and planets.
correctAnswer: Bring a notebook and pencil.
explanation: Notebook and pencil is a two-item list, so it usually does not need a comma between the items.
hint: Count the items in the list.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q04_list_items_order
type: order-items
prompt: Put the parts in order for a correctly punctuated list.
items:
  - bananas,
  - and grapes.
  - We bought apples,
  - oranges,
correctOrder:
  - We bought apples,
  - bananas,
  - oranges,
  - and grapes.
explanation: Commas separate the items before the final and.
hint: Start with the subject and verb, then list the items.
```
