## Teaching Goal

Consolidate high-frequency capitalization rules.

## Student Outcome

The student can capitalize sentence beginnings, the pronoun `I`, proper nouns, days, months, titles, and familiar place names.

## Misconception Checks

Capitalizing any exciting word; missing `I`; lowercasing days and months; capitalizing all common nouns in a title-like phrase.

## Teaching Note

Sort examples into `sentence beginning`, `specific name`, and `not capitalized`.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l01_q01_fix_capitals
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the capitalization.
sentence: on monday, i met aunt rosa at the park.
acceptedAnswers:
  - On Monday, I met Aunt Rosa at the park.
explanation: Capitalize the sentence beginning, Monday, I, and the title and name Aunt Rosa.
hint: Check the sentence beginning, the day, I, and specific names or titles.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q02_choose_not_capital
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which word should usually not be capitalized in the middle of a sentence?
choices:
  - Tuesday
  - I
  - river
  - Dr. Lee
correctAnswer: river
explanation: River is a common noun unless it is part of a specific name such as Colorado River.
hint: Look for the word that is general, not a specific name.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q03_capital_form
type: fill-blank
prompt: Choose the correctly capitalized word.
sentenceBefore: We visited
sentenceAfter: in July.
choices:
  - grandma sue
  - Grandma Sue
  - Grandma sue
  - grandma Sue
correctAnswer: Grandma Sue
explanation: Grandma Sue is a title used with a specific name, so both words are capitalized.
hint: A family title before a name is part of the name.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q04_passage_capitals
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the draft and answer.
passageTitle: Draft Sentence
passage: |
  i saw mr. patel at lincoln park.
question: Which correction is best?
choices:
  - I saw Mr. Patel at Lincoln Park.
  - I saw mr. Patel at lincoln Park.
  - i saw Mr. patel at Lincoln park.
  - I saw Mr. patel at lincoln park.
correctAnswer: I saw Mr. Patel at Lincoln Park.
explanation: I, Mr. Patel, and Lincoln Park all need capital letters.
hint: Check the pronoun I and each specific name.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q05_capital_rules_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word or phrase to the reason it is capitalized.
pairs:
  - left: I
    right: pronoun that is always capitalized
  - left: March
    right: month name
  - left: Ms. Chen
    right: title and specific name
  - left: Austin
    right: specific place name
explanation: Capital letters mark specific names, sentence starts, and the pronoun I.

```

```question
key: u06_l01_q06_explain_capital
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Which correction and reasons fit: i visited dallas in april."
choices:
  - I visited Dallas in April. I is capitalized, and Dallas and April are specific names.
  - i visited Dallas in april. Only place names need capitals.
  - I visited dallas in april. Only the first word needs a capital.
  - I Visited Dallas In April. Every word should be capitalized.
correctAnswer: I visited Dallas in April. I is capitalized, and Dallas and April are specific names.
explanation: Capitalization rules help readers see sentence starts and specific names.
hint: Look for the pronoun, the place, and the month.
```
