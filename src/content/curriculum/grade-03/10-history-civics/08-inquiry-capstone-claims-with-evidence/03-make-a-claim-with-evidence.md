## Teaching Goal

Bring the level to mastery by choosing or completing a claim supported by relevant evidence.

## Student Outcome

The student can answer a historical or civic question with a clear claim and one relevant evidence detail.

## Misconception Checks

Writing a detail without a claim; making a claim the evidence does not support; adding unrelated facts; thinking longer is always better.

## Teaching Note

Model two answers to the same question: one with an unsupported claim and one with a claim plus source detail.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l03_q01_best_claim
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the source packet and answer.
passageTitle: Town Well
passage: |
  Source: A photo shows people standing near a public well with buckets.
  Map: The well is marked in the center of town.
question: Which claim is best supported?
choices:
  - The well was an important shared place in town.
  - No one needed water.
  - The well was outside the town.
  - Every family owned a private well.
correctAnswer: The well was an important shared place in town.
explanation: The photo shows people using the well, and the map places it in the town center.
hint: Choose the claim supported by both the photo and map.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q02_best_evidence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Claim: The bridge helped people travel across the river. Which evidence best supports it?"
choices:
  - A photo shows wagons crossing the bridge.
  - A motto says fairness matters.
  - A chart lists needs and wants.
  - A diary mentions a holiday meal.
correctAnswer: A photo shows wagons crossing the bridge.
explanation: Wagons crossing the bridge directly support the travel claim.
hint: Look for evidence about crossing or travel.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q03_fix_weak_support
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the answer so the evidence supports the claim.
sentence: "Claim: The market was near the river. Evidence: The source says people bought bread."
acceptedAnswers:
  - "Claim: The market sold bread. Evidence: The source says people bought bread."
  - "Claim: People bought bread at the market. Evidence: The source says people bought bread."
  - The evidence supports a claim about buying bread, not a claim about the river.
explanation: Evidence about bread does not support a claim about location near a river.
hint: Make the claim match what the evidence actually says.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q04_claim_evidence_write
type: multi-blank-cloze
prompt: "Complete the claim with evidence. Question: How did people cross the river? Evidence: A photo shows wagons on a bridge."
parts:
  - "People crossed the river by "
  - " because the photo shows "
  - " on it."
blanks:
  - correctAnswer: bridge
    acceptedAnswers:
      - bridge
      - a bridge
  - correctAnswer: wagons
    acceptedAnswers:
      - wagons
explanation: The claim answers how people crossed, and the evidence explains how we know.
hint: "Use the frame: People crossed by ___ because the source shows ___."
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q05_claim_evidence_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each claim to the evidence that supports it.
pairs:
  - left: The town valued learning.
    right: The seal shows an open book.
  - left: People traded at the market.
    right: A diary describes exchanging eggs for cloth.
  - left: The bridge was repaired after it was built.
    right: A timeline lists built in 1905 and repaired in 1930.
  - left: The town was near a river.
    right: A map shows the river beside the town.
explanation: A claim needs evidence that directly supports it.
hint: Match the idea in the claim to the source clue that proves it.
```

```question
key: u08_l03_q06_best_complete_answer
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Question: What does the source show about the town's travel? Source: A map shows a road from the town to the river bridge. Which answer is strongest?"
choices:
  - The town had a road to the river bridge because the map shows the route.
  - The town had no travel.
  - The map is old, so it proves every detail about the town.
  - The road proves every person crossed the bridge every day.
correctAnswer: The town had a road to the river bridge because the map shows the route.
explanation: The strongest answer makes a claim and supports it with the map evidence.
hint: Choose the answer that includes both a claim and relevant evidence.
```
