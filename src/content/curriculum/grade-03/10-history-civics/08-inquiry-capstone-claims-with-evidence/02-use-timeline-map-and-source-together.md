## Teaching Goal

Integrate multiple representations to answer one question.

## Student Outcome

The student can combine a timeline clue, a map clue, and a source detail in a guided inquiry task.

## Misconception Checks

Treating maps and timelines as decoration; using only the easiest source; combining details that answer different questions.

## Teaching Note

Use a three-part display: a small timeline, a tiny map description, and a source sentence. Ask what each part tells us.

## Questions

```question
key: u08_l02_q01_tool_to_clue
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each tool to the kind of clue it often gives.
pairs:
  - left: timeline
    right: when events happened
  - left: map
    right: where places are located
  - left: photograph
    right: what visible details looked like
  - left: document
    right: words people wrote or shared
explanation: Different inquiry tools answer different parts of a question.
hint: Think when, where, what it looked like, and what words say.
```

```question
key: u08_l02_q02_bridge_packet
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the mini source packet to answer.
passageTitle: Bridge Inquiry Packet
passage: |
  Timeline: 1905 bridge built; 1930 bridge repaired.
  Map: The bridge crossed the river north of town.
  Source note: A photo shows wagons using the bridge.
question: Which answer uses more than one tool correctly?
choices:
  - The bridge crossed a river north of town, and wagons used it.
  - The bridge was south of town and never used.
  - The timeline shows what wagons looked like.
  - The map proves the bridge was repaired in 1930.
correctAnswer: The bridge crossed a river north of town, and wagons used it.
explanation: The answer uses the map for location and the photo note for use.
hint: Look for an answer that combines map and source details accurately.
```

  - correctAnswer: "1905"

      - "1905"
  - correctAnswer: north
    acceptedAnswers:
      - north
