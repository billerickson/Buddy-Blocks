## Teaching Goal

Compare daily life using time, place, source, civic, and economic clues.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify one similarity and one difference in daily life across time or place using evidence.

## Misconception Checks

Ranking past and present as simply better/worse; comparing without using the same category; treating one example as everyone.

## Teaching Note

Compare one familiar feature, such as travel or school, before asking for broader daily-life comparisons.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l02_q01_similarity
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the paired descriptions and answer.
passageTitle: Going To School
passage: |
  In one past community, many children walked to a one-room school.
  In Maya's community today, many children ride a bus to a larger school.
question: Which detail shows a similarity?
choices:
  - Children in both communities went to school.
  - Both schools had one room.
  - Both groups traveled by bus.
  - Neither group learned.
correctAnswer: Children in both communities went to school.
explanation: The transportation and school size differ, but both descriptions include children going to school.
hint: Look for what is true in both descriptions.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q02_comparison_categories
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each comparison category to a clue.
pairs:
  - left: transportation
    right: walked or rode a bus
  - left: school size
    right: one room or many rooms
  - left: work
    right: farmed, built, taught, or sold
  - left: home life
    right: where and how families lived
explanation: Using the same category makes comparisons clearer.
hint: Ask what kind of daily-life detail each clue describes.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q03_transport_evidence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which evidence best supports the claim that transportation changed?
choices:
  - Children once walked, but many children today ride a bus.
  - Children in both communities went to school.
  - Both communities had families.
  - The passage uses the word community.
correctAnswer: Children once walked, but many children today ride a bus.
explanation: Walking and riding a bus are different transportation methods.
hint: Look for a detail about how people moved from place to place.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q04_similarity_sentence

  - Both groups went to school.
  - Both groups rode buses.
  - Past travel was bad because it was different.
  - Neither group went anywhere.

```

```question
key: u06_l02_q05_compare_contrast
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the sentence.
sentenceBefore: To compare is to tell how things are alike; to contrast is to tell how they are
sentenceAfter: .
choices:
  - different
  - primary
  - north
  - scarce
correctAnswer: different
explanation: Compare often looks for similarities, while contrast looks for differences.
hint: Alike and different are the pair.
```

```question
key: u06_l02_q06_fix_ranking
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the sentence so it compares respectfully.
sentence: The old way of traveling was bad because it was different from today.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The old way of traveling was different from today.
  - People traveled in different ways in the past and today.
  - In the past, people used different transportation than many people use today.
explanation: Different does not automatically mean bad. A respectful comparison names the difference without ranking unfairly.
hint: Remove the judgment and keep the comparison.
```
