## Teaching Goal

Teach students to describe historical people and cultures accurately, respectfully, and with context.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose or complete a respectful description supported by source evidence.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking respectful means never naming differences; thinking present-day ideas automatically explain past choices; writing broad claims from one detail.

## Teaching Note

Compare "They were strange" with "Their tools were different because they used local materials" and discuss why the second is stronger.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l03_q01_respectful_description
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which description is most accurate and respectful?
choices:
  - The source shows people using local clay to make pots.
  - Those people were weird because they made pots by hand.
  - Everyone in the past was less clever.
  - One pot proves everything about the whole culture.
correctAnswer: The source shows people using local clay to make pots.
explanation: The best description uses evidence and avoids insults or overgeneralizing.
hint: Choose the statement that stays close to the source.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q02_fix_stereotype
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the sentence so it avoids a stereotype.
sentence: One old tool shows that everyone in the community did the same work.
acceptedAnswers:
  - One old tool shows one kind of work in the community.
  - The tool is evidence that some people may have done that work.
  - The old tool gives a clue about work, but it does not show everyone did the same work.
explanation: One source can give a clue, but it does not prove what everyone did.
hint: Limit the claim to what one tool can support.
```

```question
key: u06_l03_q03_supported_description
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the source note and answer.
passageTitle: Fishing Village Source
passage: |
  A map shows a village beside the sea. A photo shows several fishing boats.
question: Which description is supported by the sources?
choices:
  - The village was near the sea, and fishing may have been important.
  - No one in the village worked.
  - The village was in the mountains.
  - Every person in the village owned a boat.
correctAnswer: The village was near the sea, and fishing may have been important.
explanation: The map supports the sea location, and the photo supports fishing as a possible important activity.
hint: Use both the map and photo details.
```
