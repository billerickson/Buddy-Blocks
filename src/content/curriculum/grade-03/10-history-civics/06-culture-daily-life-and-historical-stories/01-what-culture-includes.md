## Teaching Goal

Broaden students' understanding of culture.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify multiple parts of culture and avoid reducing culture to food or holidays.

## Misconception Checks

Equating culture only with food, clothing, or holidays; thinking every person in a culture does the same things; using stereotypes as evidence.

## Teaching Note

Build a culture web with many branches, then ask which branches a source can actually support.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l01_q01_culture_parts
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each part of culture to an example.
pairs:
  - left: language
    right: words people use to greet each other
  - left: art
    right: patterns painted on pottery
  - left: work
    right: jobs people do for the community
  - left: family life
    right: ways relatives live and help
  - left: tradition
    right: a practice passed down over time
explanation: Culture includes many parts of life, not only food and holidays.
hint: Think about different ways people live, make meaning, and work together.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q02_more_than_holidays
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which sentence best explains culture?
choices:
  - Culture includes language, beliefs, arts, work, family life, and traditions.
  - Culture only means holiday foods.
  - Culture means everyone in a group is exactly the same.
  - Culture has nothing to do with work or family life.
correctAnswer: Culture includes language, beliefs, arts, work, family life, and traditions.
explanation: Culture is a broad way of life, not just one celebration or food.
hint: Choose the answer with many parts of life.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q03_tradition_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the description and answer.
passageTitle: Community Festival
passage: |
  Each spring, families gather to sing old songs, tell stories, and teach children a dance their grandparents learned.
question: Which detail is evidence of tradition?
choices:
  - children learn a dance their grandparents learned
  - spring comes after winter
  - families stand outside
  - the passage has one sentence
correctAnswer: children learn a dance their grandparents learned
explanation: A tradition is a practice passed down over time.
hint: Look for something passed from older people to younger people.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q04_culture_blank
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the sentence.
sentenceBefore: Culture is a way of
sentenceAfter: shared by a group or community.
choices:
  - life
  - food only
  - holiday only
  - object only
correctAnswer: life
explanation: Culture is often described as a way of life.
hint: Think about language, beliefs, art, work, and family life together.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q05_avoid_stereotype
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement is most careful and respectful?
choices:
  - This source shows one family's holiday meal.
  - Everyone in that culture eats the same meal every day.
  - The people were strange because their meal was different.
  - The meal tells us everything about the culture.
correctAnswer: This source shows one family's holiday meal.
explanation: The careful statement stays close to what the source shows and avoids broad stereotypes.
hint: Choose the statement that does not claim more than the source can prove.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q06_culture_source_detail
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A source says a community built fishing boats and told stories about the sea. Which two parts of culture are shown?
choices:
  - work or technology, and stories or tradition
  - food and holidays only
  - a map key and a compass rose
  - scarcity and money
correctAnswer: work or technology, and stories or tradition
explanation: Boat building can show work or technology, and stories can show tradition or belief.
hint: Look for what people do and what they pass on.
```
