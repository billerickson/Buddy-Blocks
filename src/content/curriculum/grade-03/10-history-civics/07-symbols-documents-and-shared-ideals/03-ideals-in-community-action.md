## Teaching Goal

Connect shared ideals to responsible civic choices.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify an ideal in action and connect it to a citizen or leader responsibility.

## Misconception Checks

Treating ideals as words only; assuming an ideal has no tradeoffs; choosing actions that help one person while ignoring the shared community question.

## Teaching Note

Use a community problem and ask which action best shows fairness or responsibility and why.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l03_q01_ideal_action_pairs
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each ideal to an action that could show it.
pairs:
  - left: fairness
    right: taking turns with shared equipment
  - left: safety
    right: repairing a broken sidewalk
  - left: respect
    right: listening during a meeting
  - left: responsibility
    right: returning borrowed supplies
explanation: Ideals become visible through choices and actions.
hint: Ask what action shows each value.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q02_playground_fairness
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scenario and answer.
passageTitle: Playground Plan
passage: |
  The playground has one basketball hoop. Students make a schedule so each group gets time to play.
question: Which ideal is the schedule trying to support?
choices:
  - fairness
  - safety
  - liberty
  - responsibility
correctAnswer: fairness
explanation: The schedule helps groups share the hoop fairly.
hint: Look for the value connected to taking turns.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q03_safety_responsibility
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which action best shows responsibility for safety?
choices:
  - Reporting a broken crossing sign to an adult or town worker
  - Hiding the sign so no one sees it
  - Pretending roads do not need rules
  - Waiting for someone else while people may be unsafe
correctAnswer: Reporting a broken crossing sign to an adult or town worker
explanation: Reporting the problem helps protect people who use the crossing.
hint: Choose the action that helps solve the safety problem.
```

```question
key: u07_l03_q05_action_sequence
type: order-items
prompt: Put these responsible community action steps in order.
items:
  - Help carry out the fair plan
  - Notice that some students never get a turn
  - Discuss possible schedules
  - Choose a schedule everyone understands
correctOrder:
  - Notice that some students never get a turn
  - Discuss possible schedules
  - Choose a schedule everyone understands
  - Help carry out the fair plan
explanation: Responsible action often starts by noticing a problem, discussing it, choosing a plan, and helping.
hint: Start with the problem before the plan.
```
