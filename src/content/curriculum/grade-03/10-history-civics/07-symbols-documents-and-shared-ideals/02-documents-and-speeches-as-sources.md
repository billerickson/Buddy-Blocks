## Teaching Goal

Treat short civic document and speech excerpts as sources.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify an idea or ideal in a short civic line and cite the words that support it.

## Misconception Checks

Believing only pictures and artifacts are sources; quoting words without explaining their meaning; thinking all civic text is too hard to read.

## Teaching Note

Read one short line aloud, then ask what word or phrase gives the clue.

## Questions

```question
key: u07_l02_q01_ideal_in_line
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the civic line and answer.
passageTitle: Community Promise
passage: |
  We promise to share the park fairly so every family can enjoy it.
question: Which ideal is shown most clearly?
choices:
  - fairness
  - safety
  - liberty
  - responsibility
correctAnswer: fairness
explanation: The line says people should share the park fairly.
hint: Look for the value word in the sentence.
```

```question
key: u07_l02_q02_text_as_source
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why can a short speech line be a source?
choices:
  - It can preserve words and ideas people shared.
  - It is never evidence.
  - It is too short to provide evidence.
  - It tells every fact without context.
correctAnswer: It can preserve words and ideas people shared.
explanation: Documents and speeches can be sources because their words provide evidence.
hint: Think about what written or spoken words can show.
```
