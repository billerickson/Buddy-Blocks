## Teaching Goal

Teach students to read a short timeline as an ordered representation of events.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify first, next, last, before, after, earliest, and latest events on a simple timeline.

## Misconception Checks

Treating timeline spacing as always exact; choosing the event with the biggest number or longest sentence as most important; confusing earliest with latest.

## Teaching Note

Use three events from a school day before moving to a community or historical sequence.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l02_q01_order_town_events
type: order-items
prompt: Put the town events in timeline order from earliest to latest.
items:
  - "2021: A new walking trail opened"
  - "2016: The park was planned"
  - "2018: Trees were planted"
  - "2023: A picnic area was added"
correctOrder:
  - "2016: The park was planned"
  - "2018: Trees were planted"
  - "2021: A new walking trail opened"
  - "2023: A picnic area was added"
explanation: Timeline order moves from the earliest year to the latest year.
hint: Compare the years before reading the event details.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q02_after_event
type: passage-question
prompt: Use the timeline notes to answer.
passageTitle: School Garden Timeline
passage: |
  2019: Students planted seeds.
  2020: The garden grew vegetables.
  2022: The school added a rain barrel.
question: What happened after the garden grew vegetables?
choices:
  - Students planted seeds.
  - The school added a rain barrel.
  - The garden began before 2019.
  - The timeline has no later event.
correctAnswer: The school added a rain barrel.
explanation: The rain barrel was added in 2022, which is after 2020.
hint: Find the event with a later year than 2020.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q03_earliest_latest
type: multiple-choice
prompt: On a left-to-right timeline, where is the earliest event usually placed?
choices:
  - on the left
  - on the right
  - under the title
  - where the longest label fits
correctAnswer: on the left
explanation: Simple timelines usually move from earlier on the left to later on the right.
hint: Think about how the timeline moves from first to last.
```

```question
key: u01_l02_q04_timeline_word
```

