## Teaching Goal

Introduce simple explanatory time relationships: cause/effect, change, and continuity.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify a likely cause, effect, change, or continuity in a short scenario.

## Misconception Checks

Assuming any earlier event caused a later event; thinking change means everything changed; thinking continuity means time stopped.

## Teaching Note

Contrast sequence with cause: "I tied my shoes, then it rained" is sequence, not a cause.

## Questions

```question
key: u01_l03_q01_storm_effect
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Heavy rain flooded the road, so the bus took a different route. What was the effect?
choices:
  - Heavy rain fell.
  - The road flooded.
  - The bus took a different route.
  - The road existed before the rain.
correctAnswer: The bus took a different route.
explanation: The bus route changed because the road flooded. That result is the effect.
hint: Look for what happened because of the flooded road.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q02_terms_examples
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each word to the best meaning.
pairs:
  - left: cause
    right: why something happened
  - left: effect
    right: what happened because of it
  - left: change
    right: what became different
  - left: continuity
    right: what stayed the same
explanation: These words help explain events, not just list them.
hint: Cause and effect go together; change and continuity go together.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q03_school_change
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the short scenario and answer.
passageTitle: A School Moves
passage: |
  The old school had three classrooms. The new school has ten classrooms.
  The school kept the same name and mascot.
question: Which detail shows a change?
choices:
  - The school has ten classrooms now.
  - The school kept the same name.
  - The school kept the same mascot.
  - The passage does not mention classrooms.
correctAnswer: The school has ten classrooms now.
explanation: The number of classrooms changed from three to ten.
hint: Find the detail that is different in the new school.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q04_not_cause
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which pair shows sequence but not a clear cause?
choices:
  - The class planted seeds, so plants grew.
  - The bridge washed out, so people used a ferry.
  - I tied my shoes, then the sun rose.
  - The town built a road, so travel became easier.
correctAnswer: I tied my shoes, then the sun rose.
explanation: Tying shoes happened before the sunrise, but it did not cause the sun to rise.
hint: Ask whether the first event really made the second event happen.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q05_fix_bad_cause
type: error-correction
prompt: Correct the sentence so it does not claim a false cause.
sentence: The museum opened after the storm, so the museum caused the storm.
acceptedAnswers:
  - The museum opened after the storm, but the museum did not cause the storm.
  - The storm happened before the museum opened, but the museum did not cause the storm.
  - The museum opened after the storm.
explanation: After does not always mean because. The sentence needs to avoid a false cause.
hint: Keep the time order, but remove the wrong cause claim.
```

```question
key: u01_l03_q06_change_continuity
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A town replaced horse carts with buses, but people still traveled to the market. Which answer names one change and one continuity?
choices:
  - "Change: people used buses; continuity: people still traveled to the market."
  - "Change: people still went to the market; continuity: horse carts disappeared."
  - "Change: nothing changed; continuity: buses replaced horse carts."
  - "Change: the town stopped traveling; continuity: the market vanished."
correctAnswer: "Change: people used buses; continuity: people still traveled to the market."
explanation: The transportation changed from horse carts to buses, while traveling to the market stayed the same.
hint: Ask what became different and what stayed the same.
```
