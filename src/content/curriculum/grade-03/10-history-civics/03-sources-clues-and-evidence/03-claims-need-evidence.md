## Teaching Goal

Connect source details to claims.

## Student Outcome

The student can choose relevant evidence for a simple claim and explain why it supports the claim.

## Misconception Checks

Treating opinions or guesses as evidence; choosing an interesting but irrelevant detail; making a claim that is broader than the source supports.

## Teaching Note

Use the frame "I know ___ because the source shows ___" and discuss what happens when the evidence does not match.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l03_q01_photo_evidence
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the source description and answer.
passageTitle: Street Photo
passage: |
  A photograph from 1910 shows a busy street with horses, wagons, and a few early cars.
question: Which claim is best supported by this source?
choices:
  - People used more than one kind of transportation on that street.
  - Everyone owned a car in 1910.
  - The street was empty.
  - No one used horses in 1910.
correctAnswer: People used more than one kind of transportation on that street.
explanation: The photo shows horses, wagons, and a few cars, which supports the claim.
hint: Choose the claim that matches the visible details.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q02_relevant_evidence
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Claim: The town had a river crossing. Which evidence best supports the claim?"
choices:
  - A map shows a bridge over the river.
  - A photo shows a school desk.
  - A diary says the weather was cold.
  - A timeline lists a festival date.
correctAnswer: A map shows a bridge over the river.
explanation: A bridge over a river is evidence of a river crossing.
hint: Look for the detail about crossing a river.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q03_claim_evidence_match
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each claim to the evidence that supports it.
pairs:
  - left: The town had a train station.
    right: A map labels a station by the tracks.
  - left: Children attended school there.
    right: A photo shows students outside a classroom.
  - left: People traded at the market.
    right: A diary says families exchanged eggs for cloth.
  - left: The bridge was repaired later.
    right: A timeline lists repairs in 1932.
explanation: Evidence must connect directly to the claim it supports.
hint: Match what the claim says to the source detail that shows it.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q04_fix_unsupported_claim
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "Source: A photo shows two children carrying lunch pails. Which claim is supported?"
choices:
  - The photo shows two children carrying lunch pails.
  - Every child in town carried a lunch pail.
  - No children carried lunch pails.
  - All families in town used lunch pails.
correctAnswer: The photo shows two children carrying lunch pails.
explanation: The source supports what the photo shows, not a claim about every child in town.
hint: Limit the claim to what the source actually shows.
```

```question
key: u03_l03_q05_evidence_frame
type: fill-blank
prompt: Complete the evidence sentence.
sentenceBefore: I know this because the source
sentenceAfter: a bridge on the river.
choices:
  - shows
  - suggests without evidence
  - hides
  - contradicts
correctAnswer: shows
explanation: The word shows connects the claim to visible source evidence.
hint: Choose the verb that tells what the source provides.
```
