## Teaching Goal

Distinguish clear primary and secondary source examples.

## Student Outcome

The student can decide whether a source was made during an event or later about the event in simple cases.

## Misconception Checks

Believing primary means "best" or always true; believing secondary means "wrong" or useless; classifying by format instead of relationship to the event.

## Teaching Note

Compare a photo taken at a parade with a paragraph written later describing the parade.

## Questions

```question
key: u03_l02_q01_diary_primary
type: multiple-choice
prompt: A diary entry written by a child on the day of a parade is what kind of source about the parade?
choices:
  - primary source
  - secondary source
  - later summary
  - source with no time clue
correctAnswer: primary source
explanation: The diary was written during the event by someone connected to it.
hint: Ask whether it was made during the event or later about the event.
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q02_source_category_examples
type: match-pairs

pairs:
  - left: photo taken at the parade
    right: primary photograph
  - left: letter mailed during the trip
    right: primary letter
  - left: textbook paragraph about the trip
    right: secondary explanation
  - left: museum sign written years later
    right: secondary label
explanation: Primary sources come from the time or people connected to the event; secondary sources explain later.
hint: Look for during the event or years later.
```

```question
  - Source B, because it was written later about the parade
  - Both, because all sources are secondary
```

```question
key: u03_l02_q05_primary_complete
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement about primary sources is best?
choices:
  - A primary source can give evidence, but it may not tell the whole story.
  - A primary source is always completely true.
  - A primary source is never useful.
  - A primary source must be a map.
correctAnswer: A primary source can give evidence, but it may not tell the whole story.
explanation: Primary sources are valuable, but they still need context and careful reading.
hint: A source can be useful without being complete.
```
