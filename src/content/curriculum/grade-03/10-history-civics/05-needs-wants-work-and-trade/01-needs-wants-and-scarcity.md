## Teaching Goal

Introduce scarcity as the reason people make economic choices.

## Student Outcome

The student can distinguish needs and wants and explain that scarcity means limited resources.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking wants are always bad; thinking scarcity means none exists; confusing a person's preference with a need.

## Teaching Note

Compare "water to drink" and "a new game," then ask what happens when time, money, or materials are limited.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l01_q01_need_or_want
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which choice is usually a need?
choices:
  - clean water to drink
  - a second toy wagon
  - a fancy ribbon
  - a larger dessert
correctAnswer: clean water to drink
explanation: Clean water is needed for health and survival. The other choices may be wanted but are not usually needs.
hint: A need is something people require to live or stay healthy.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q02_economic_words
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each economics word to its meaning.
pairs:
  - left: need
    right: something people require
  - left: want
    right: something people would like to have
  - left: scarcity
    right: limited resources for choices
  - left: resource
    right: something people can use
explanation: These words help explain why people make economic choices.
hint: Need and want are about what people value; scarcity and resource are about limits.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q03_scarcity_choice
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scenario and answer.
passageTitle: Limited Wood
passage: |
  A small town has enough wood to repair the bridge or build a new picnic shelter, but not both.
question: What does this scenario show?
choices:
  - scarcity, because the wood is limited
  - abundance, because there is enough wood for every project
  - trade, because the town exchanged wood with another town
  - a want, because a bridge can never be useful
correctAnswer: scarcity, because the wood is limited
explanation: Scarcity means resources are limited, so people must choose.
hint: Look for what the town does not have enough of.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q05_wants_not_bad
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement about wants is best?
choices:
  - Wants can matter, but they are not the same as needs.
  - Wants are always bad.
  - Wants are always more important than needs.
  - Wants mean there is no scarcity.
correctAnswer: Wants can matter, but they are not the same as needs.
explanation: People can value wants, but needs are things people require.
hint: Do not treat wants as either evil or the same as needs.
```

```question
key: u05_l01_q06_choice_explain
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scenario and answer.
passageTitle: One Afternoon
passage: |
  A family has time to visit either the library or the park today, but not both.
question: Which answer best explains the scarcity and choice?
choices:
  - Time is limited, so the family must choose one place.
  - Water is limited, so the family cannot read.
  - There is no scarcity because the family has two ideas.
  - The family can do everything because choices have no costs.
correctAnswer: Time is limited, so the family must choose one place.
explanation: Scarcity can involve time, money, materials, or other limited resources.
hint: Ask what the family does not have enough of to do both.
```
