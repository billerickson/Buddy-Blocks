## Teaching Goal

Explain trade and money as ways people exchange goods and services and make choices.

## Student Outcome

The student can describe a simple exchange and identify the choice or tradeoff involved.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking money and trade are the same; thinking a trade must involve money; ignoring what is given up when a choice is made.

## Teaching Note

Use a barter example first, then show how money can stand between producer and consumer.

## Questions

```question
key: u05_l03_q01_barter_trade
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scenario and answer.
passageTitle: Trading At The Market
passage: |
  Ana gives a basket of eggs to Ben. Ben gives Ana a piece of cloth.
  No money is used.
question: What is happening?
choices:
  - trade
  - a gift with nothing exchanged
  - saving money
  - a tax
correctAnswer: trade
explanation: Ana and Ben exchange goods, so they are trading.
hint: Look for what each person gives and gets.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q02_trade_money
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Which statement is true?
choices:
  - Trade can happen with or without money.
  - Trade and money are exactly the same thing.
  - Money can only be used for maps.
  - People never exchange services.
correctAnswer: Trade can happen with or without money.
explanation: Trade means exchange. Money can help exchange, but some trades do not use money.
hint: Remember the eggs-for-cloth example.
```

```question

  - left: trade
    right: exchange goods or services
  - left: money
    right: a common tool used to buy things
  - left: cost
    right: what someone gives up

    right: what someone gains

```

```question
key: u05_l03_q05_tradeoff
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Mia spends her only coin on a pencil, so she cannot buy a snack. What is the tradeoff?
choices:
  - She gives up the snack to get the pencil.
  - She gets both items.
  - The coin becomes a map symbol.
  - There is no choice because money is used.
correctAnswer: She gives up the snack to get the pencil.
explanation: A tradeoff is what someone gives up when choosing one option.
hint: Ask what Mia cannot get after buying the pencil.
```

```question
key: u05_l03_q06_exchange_explain
type: multiple-choice
prompt: Why can money make trade easier?
choices:
  - People can buy without finding someone who wants their exact trade item.
  - Money means no one gives anything up.
  - Money makes goods and services the same.
  - Money means trade cannot involve services.
correctAnswer: People can buy without finding someone who wants their exact trade item.
explanation: Money can make exchange easier because people do not have to want exactly what the other person offers.
hint: Think about why eggs-for-cloth might be harder than using coins.
```
