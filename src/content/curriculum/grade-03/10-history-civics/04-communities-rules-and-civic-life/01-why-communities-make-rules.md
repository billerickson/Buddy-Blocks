## Teaching Goal

Explain why communities create rules and laws.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify the purpose of a rule or law in a concrete scenario.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking rules exist only to punish; treating rules and laws as identical; assuming every community rule has the same purpose.

## Teaching Note

Start with a classroom or playground rule and ask what problem it helps solve before naming formal civic vocabulary.

## Questions

```question
key: u04_l01_q01_rule_purpose
type: multiple-choice
prompt: "A library has a rule: Use quiet voices. What purpose does the rule most likely serve?"
choices:
  - It helps people read and study.
  - It helps people talk over readers.
  - It makes a classroom rule into a law.
  - It helps only the loudest person.
correctAnswer: It helps people read and study.
explanation: Quiet voices help the library community share the space for reading and study.
hint: Ask what problem the rule helps solve.
```

```question
  - to help people cross streets safely
  - to let cars ignore people walking
  - to make crosswalks only for adults
  - to remove the need for street safety
correctAnswer: to help people cross streets safely
explanation: The law helps protect people using the crosswalk.
hint: Look for the safety problem in the scenario.
```
