## Teaching Goal

Clarify civic roles and responsibilities in a community.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify actions of leaders and citizens that support a shared need or common good.

## Misconception Checks

Thinking a leader is anyone famous; believing citizens only participate by voting; confusing popularity with responsibility.

## Teaching Note

Use a school or town problem, then identify what a leader might do and what citizens might do.

```question
key: u04_l02_q02_cleanup_common_good
type: passage-question
prompt: Read the scenario and answer.
passageTitle: Park Cleanup
passage: |
  A neighborhood park has litter near the playground. A group leader organizes a cleanup.
  Families volunteer so everyone can enjoy a safer park.
question: Which action supports the common good?
choices:
  - Families volunteer to clean the park.
  - One person hides the litter.
  - Everyone ignores the playground.
  - The leader becomes famous.
correctAnswer: Families volunteer to clean the park.
explanation: The cleanup helps the shared park, so it supports the common good.
hint: Look for the action that helps the community, not just one person.
```
