## Teaching Goal

Students use il y a to say that someone or something exists in a family, room, or picture.

## Student Outcome

The student can interpret and build sentences such as Il y a deux frères and Il y a une trousse.

## Misconception Checks

Translating il y a as "he has"; using a singular/plural English distinction that French il y a does not show; omitting the article after il y a.

## Teaching Note

Compare "J'ai un frère" with "Il y a un frère dans la photo" only as meaning, not as a full grammar contrast.
