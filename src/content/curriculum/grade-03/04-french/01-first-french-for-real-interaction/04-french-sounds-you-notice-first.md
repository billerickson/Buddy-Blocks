## Teaching Goal

Students notice several high-frequency French sound-spelling patterns in words they already know.

## Student Outcome

The student can identify that some final consonants are silent, accents affect spelling and sound, ç sounds like /s/, and liaison can link words.

## Misconception Checks

Pronouncing every final letter; ignoring accents; reading ç like English c in cat; assuming liaison means every final letter is always pronounced.

## Teaching Note

Use known phrases only. Ask, "What do you see? What do you hear? What letter is quiet or changed?"

## Questions
