## Teaching Goal

Students identify school objects and subjects with articles and use preference language in a school context.

## Student Outcome

The student can match common school nouns to meanings and say whether they like a subject.

## Misconception Checks

Dropping articles; confusing object nouns with subject nouns; using the wrong article because English has one "the."

## Teaching Note

Sort words into "things in a bag" and "classes on a schedule" before asking for preferences.

## Questions

```question
key: u06_l01_q01_match_school_objects
type: match-pairs
prompt: Match each school object to English.
pairs:
  - left: le stylo
    right: the pen
  - left: le cahier
    right: the notebook
  - left: le livre
    right: the book
  - left: la trousse
    right: the pencil case
explanation: School objects should be learned with their articles.
hint: Look for cognates or classroom words you have seen in color phrases.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q03_article_trousse

sentenceAfter: " trousse"

  - la
  - le
  - les
  - un
correctAnswer: la
explanation: The phrase is la trousse.
hint: Trousse is learned with la.
```

```question
key: u06_l01_q04_subject_preference

choices:

  - le stylo
  - la trousse
  - il pleut

explanation: Le français is the school subject French.
hint: Choose the school subject, not an object or weather phrase.
```
